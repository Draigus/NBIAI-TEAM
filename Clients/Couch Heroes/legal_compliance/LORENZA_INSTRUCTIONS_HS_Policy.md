I've put together the Health and Safety Policy for CH Game Development UK Ltd. It's attached as a Word document (CH_Health_and_Safety_Policy.docx). It covers everything we're legally required to have under UK law for a fully remote employer. Nothing more, nothing less.

There are a few things you need to do to make it live:

## 1. Fill in the names

- CEO field: Vardis
- COO field: Aris
- Competent Person field: This is the person responsible for helping manage H&S. It can be you, it can be an external H&S consultant, or it can be someone else internally. This must be named before our first UK employee starts work.

## 2. Get Vardis to sign it

The policy needs to be signed by the most senior person in the company. That's Vardis. Digital signature is fine. Once signed, it's a live legal document.

## 3. Set the effective date and review date

Effective date is whatever day Vardis signs it. Review date is 12 months later.

## 4. Send the DSE self-assessment to every UK-based employee

This is the one piece of ongoing work. Appendix A in the document is a self-assessment form. Each UK employee fills it in about their home workstation. Takes about 5 minutes. Their manager reviews it, and if anything needs fixing (bad chair, screen too low, whatever), we provide or fund the equipment.

**Important: this only applies to employees employed by CH Game Development UK Ltd and working in the UK.** Employees based in Greece or any other country are covered by their own country's health and safety legislation, not UK law. The DSE assessment, the HSE leaflet, the accident reporting procedures, all of it is UK-only.

## 5. Provide the HSE leaflet to each UK employee

UK law requires every employee to receive a copy of the HSE-approved health and safety law leaflet. You can download it free here: https://www.hse.gov.uk/pubns/lawleaflet.pdf

Send it digitally to each UK employee. This should be part of induction for any new UK hire.

## 6. Make the EL insurance certificate accessible

Once we have Employers' Liability Insurance (which we need before employing anyone in the UK), the certificate needs to be accessible to employees electronically. Shared drive, intranet, wherever they can find it.

## 7. Keep records

- Completed DSE assessments on file
- Accident/incident reports if anything ever happens
- Training records (who received the H&S induction and when)

The induction checklist in Appendix C of the policy walks through everything a new UK starter needs to be told on day one. Use it as a tick-box for onboarding.

That's it. No external consultants needed, no expensive programmes, no site visits. The policy is written specifically for a fully remote studio. There's nothing in there about offices, fire drills, or first aid kits because we don't have premises. Where we hire a venue for an off-site or meeting, the policy covers checking the venue has the right arrangements.
