# Saybrook Legal Engagement — Feedback Points for Aris
2026-06-12 — Glen

## Headline

I'm not against Saybrook. The shape is right: a senior fractional GC one day a week, pulling in specialists when we need them. But this letter is not signable as it stands. There are four things that need fixing before anyone signs, and two bigger questions about whether this actually covers what we need.

## The four fixes — non-negotiable before signature

**1. The fee clause doesn't say what we're paying.**
It says £1,500 ex VAT but never says per what. Per month? For a day a week? And "time in excess of this amount" bills at £350 an hour, but excess of what is never defined. £1,500 at £350 an hour is about four hours. A day a week is about 35 hours a month. If the retainer only covers four hours, the real bill could be £10k+ a month and we'd have signed up to it. This is probably just sloppy drafting, but a lawyer selling us drafting services has sent us an ambiguous fee clause. Make them rewrite it: £X per month, covering one day per week, anything beyond that needs written approval first.

**2. We haven't seen the actual contract.**
The letter says the Terms and Conditions govern everything: liability cap, termination, their right to switch off services if an invoice is late. None of that was attached. We are being asked to sign up to a document we haven't read. Get the Terms, get them reviewed, then decide.

**3. The company has nothing behind it.**
I checked Companies House. Saybrook Legal Ltd was incorporated in June 2025, one director, virtual office address. It's also not SRA regulated, which the letter admits: no compensation fund, no required minimum insurance, and the Legal Ombudsman can't touch the company, only Riley personally. None of that kills the deal, but it means the only thing standing behind their advice is their insurance. So we see the insurance certificate before signing, minimum £3M cover, and we verify Riley's SRA number on the register. Two minutes' work, has to be done.

**4. The Gmail address has to go.**
Our privileged legal correspondence going to graebner@gmail.com is not acceptable. It contradicts their own letter, which promises all data stays in the UK, and it's exactly how invoice fraud happens. Proper firm domain before we send them anything confidential.

## The two bigger questions

**5. This doesn't cover our two biggest legal needs.**
There is no corporate or fundraising work in the scope. The Series B is the most important legal work we'll do in the next twelve months and it's not in here. Fine if Mishcon owns it, but then let's say so in writing: Saybrook does day-to-day, Mishcon does corporate. And there's nothing on Greece, where half our people are. Calling this our "primary legal force" only works for the UK half of the company. We need a named Greek counsel relationship alongside this or the gap just sits there.

**6. The real cost isn't the retainer, it's the panel.**
The specialists bill us directly at rates that are "indicative only", with no caps and no reporting. Saybrook coordinates but takes no responsibility for their work. So: confirmed rate card per specialist, a monthly legal budget with pre-approval above a threshold, and a consolidated spend report from Saybrook every month — that's part of what the retainer pays for. And one direct question for Riley: does Saybrook take any referral fee from the panel firms? If yes, we want it disclosed and knocked off our bills.

## Operational points for us, not for them

- One instruction channel. If everyone can email Riley directly, the hourly meter runs unsupervised. Dino instructs, you as backup. Tell the team.
- Templates currently have three cooks: Dino, Mishcon, now Saybrook. Pick one owner or we get a third conflicting set.
- We need turnaround commitments. We're making 18 hires with Lorenza on her own. Contract review in two business days needs to be in the scope, in writing.
- Ask what happens to the weekly day when Riley is ill or away. One person, no cover. Get the answer in writing.
- Payment terms: push 14 days to 30, and no suspension of services without 14 days' notice. We don't want legal switched off mid-termination over an invoice quibble.

## What good looks like

Treat the three months as a paid trial. First deliverable: a 90-day legal roadmap sequenced against the vertical slice — player terms and EULA for closed beta, the partner portal contracts, the terminations and FTE conversions. If Riley produces a sharp roadmap in week one, we've got the right person. If we get a shrug, we've learned that for £1,500.

One last thing: we've apparently been working with them since April on an unsigned letter. Whatever we decide, that gets regularised either way.
