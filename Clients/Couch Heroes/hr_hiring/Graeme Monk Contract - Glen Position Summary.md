# Graeme Monk Employment Contract — Glen's Position Summary

**Date:** 12 May 2026
**Contract:** Graeme Peter Monk, Executive Producer, CH Game Development UK Ltd
**Decision makers:** Glen Pryer and Ari

---

## Accepted Changes

### 1. Deed Scope Limitation
**Graeme's request:** Limit deed execution to IP assignment, moral rights waiver, and power of attorney provisions only. Everything else treated as standard employment contract.
**Glen's position:** Agreed. This is what Sheridans intended. The deed exists to bulletproof the IP power of attorney, not to extend limitation periods on holiday disputes.

### 2. Start Date
**Graeme's request:** Change from 18th May 2026 to 1st June 2026.
**Glen's position:** Confirmed.

### 3. IP Carve-Out for Personal Work
**Graeme's request:** IP created wholly outside working hours, without company equipment or confidential information, belongs to the employee.
**Glen's position:** Accepted with one addition — personal IP cannot overlap with the same genre, platform, or product category as active CH projects. The right of first refusal mechanism Graeme proposed works for both sides.

### 4. Power of Attorney Limitation
**Graeme's request:** Power of attorney applies only to Employment IPRs that validly belong to the company, not to employee-retained IP.
**Glen's position:** Accepted. Logical — you wouldn't want power of attorney over IP you don't own.

### 5. Prohibited Business Definition
**Graeme's request:** Narrow from "any business in direct competition" to "only specific products, services, or projects the employee was materially involved with in the final 12 months."
**Glen's position:** Agreed. A narrower covenant is more enforceable in court. The 6-month restricted period with specific project scope gives real protection where a broad, vague covenant might get struck down entirely.

### 6. Mandatory Holiday Provisions
**Graeme's request:** New clause specifying which days the company can designate as mandatory holidays (bank holidays, Christmas/New Year closure, studio shutdowns), with advance notice required.
**Glen's position:** Agreed. Lorenza to confirm the number of additional shutdown days so it can go in before signing.

### 7. IP Clause Duplication
**Graeme's request:** Two overlapping definitions of Employment Inventions should be reconciled into one.
**Glen's position:** Agreed. Sheridans to clean this up. One definition, not two.

## Glen's Own Modifications

### 8. Working Hours
**Graeme's suggestion (comment only):** Core hours model, possibly reducing from 40 to 37.5 or 35 hours per week.
**Glen's position:** 40 hours per week stays. Core hours 10am to 4pm. We are a globally staffed company and must hold equality in hours across all territories. The flexibility is in when you work the remaining hours around that core.

### 9. Holiday Entitlement
**Graeme's suggestion (comment only):** Reword as "28 days plus bank holidays" or increase to "30 days plus bank holidays."
**Glen's position:** 28 days plus bank holidays. Same total as the current 36 inclusive figure, cleaner wording.

## Noted but No Contract Change Required

### 10. Remote Work
**Graeme's point:** Contract says "may work remotely from time to time with prior agreement" but CH positions itself as remote first. These contradict each other.
**Glen's position:** Noted. We are remote first. Contract wording should reflect that in the final version.

### 11. Probation Process
**Graeme's point:** If the company doesn't confirm probation completion, the employee sits in perpetual probation by default.
**Glen's position:** Fair point. The clause stays, but HR must have a process to confirm probation completion in writing.

### 12. Double Signature
**Graeme's query:** Why sign twice?
**Glen's position:** Standard for deed execution. One signature accepts employment terms, the other executes the deed. No change needed.
