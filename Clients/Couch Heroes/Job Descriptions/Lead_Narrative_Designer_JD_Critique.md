# Lead Narrative Designer JD — Line-by-Line Critique

Companion to `Lead_Narrative_Designer_JD.docx`. Created 2026-06-10.
For each line: what it is for, what response it is designed to provoke in the candidate, and what expectation it sets. Benchmarked against live and recent postings: CD Projekt Red Lead Narrative Designer (Project Sirius, the multiplayer Witcher game), Firesprite/PlayStation Studios Lead Narrative Designer (UK, hybrid/remote), Lunacy Studios Lead Narrative Designer (remote), and industry references on MMO narrative roles.

---

## Title: "Lead Narrative Designer"

**Purpose:** Standard industry title, searchable on LinkedIn/job boards. "Lead" signals people management plus craft ownership, distinct from "Narrative Director" (vision only) and "Senior Narrative Designer" (IC).
**Response sought:** Self-selection by candidates who want to manage AND write. Pure writers and pure managers should both hesitate.
**Benchmark:** Matches CDPR, Firesprite, and Lunacy exactly. This is the correct title for the seniority band; "Narrative Lead" is the common variant but designer-suffixed titles attract design-literate applicants rather than screenwriters, which is what an MMO needs.

## About Couch Heroes (2 paragraphs, fixed boilerplate)

**Purpose:** Identical across all 29 CH JDs. Establishes genre (MMO), setting (fantasy + ancient technology, shattered realms, corruption), tech (Unreal Engine), stage (early production), and culture (remote-first, UK, autonomy).
**Response sought:** For this role specifically, this section works harder than usual: "shattered realms", "corruption", and "bridge between the gamers of the 80s and today" are narrative premises. A good narrative candidate will arrive at interview with ideas about them. Candidates who only want photoreal AAA cinematic storytelling self-filter out.
**Expectation set:** Live-service, multi-year commitment, distributed team. No surprises at offer stage.

## About the Job — paragraph 1

> "A world only matters if players care what happens in it. The history of the shattered realms... are the domain of narrative design. We need a Lead Narrative Designer who can own the story of our MMO and lead the team responsible for worldbuilding, quest narrative, dialogue, and the systems that deliver story to players at MMO scale."

**Purpose:** House pattern: every CH JD opens About the Job with a one-line thesis about why the discipline matters, then "We need a [Role] who can own X and lead Y". Defines the four content domains (worldbuilding, quest narrative, dialogue, delivery systems) so scope is unambiguous.
**Response sought:** The phrase "systems that deliver story" is a deliberate filter. Writers think in scripts; narrative designers think in systems. Candidates who light up at that phrase are the right ones.
**Benchmark:** CDPR's posting leads with "narrative systems to deliver a player-driven story experience" — same framing. Aligned.

## About the Job — paragraph 2

> "You will report to the Game Director and take ownership of a narrative discipline that is just beginning: one narrative designer is in place today. You will set creative direction... stay hands-on... and grow the function by hiring and managing narrative designers and writers as production ramps."

**Purpose:** Reporting line, the true state of the team, and the hands-on expectation in one paragraph. Follows the Audio Lead function-builder variant rather than the Game Design Lead team-inheritor variant, per Glen's confirmation (2026-06-10) that the studio has exactly one narrative person.
**Response sought:** "A discipline that is just beginning" plus "grow the function" attracts function-builders, who are a different candidate from team-inheritors. "Stay hands-on" warns off candidates seeking a pure management seat — with a team of one, the lead writes most of the words for a while.
**Expectation set:** One direct report from day one; hiring is part of the job, not a someday possibility. Honest about scale, which prevents the senior candidate who expects to inherit a writers' room from discovering reality at interview.

## About the Job — paragraph 3

> "We are in early production. The world's history, factions, characters, tone of voice... are still being defined and iterated. You will be making narrative decisions that define the player experience for years to come."

**Purpose:** House pattern (every CH JD has this stage-setting paragraph). Honest about ambiguity; sells influence as the compensation for it.
**Response sought:** Excitement from world-shapers; self-deselection by candidates who want an established lore bible handed to them.
**Benchmark:** Neither CDPR nor Firesprite is this honest about production stage. This is a genuine differentiator for senior candidates who want authorship, and it is consistent with how CH has positioned every other lead role.

## Your Role — "Worldbuilding and Narrative Design" (5 bullets)

- *Bullet 1 (own narrative vision, lore bible):* The core craft mandate. "Keeps the world coherent as the team and content scale" sets the real job: consistency management, not just invention. Expectation: candidate has built or maintained a lore bible before.
- *Bullet 2 (story structures: arcs, zone narratives, side content, vignettes):* Defines the content taxonomy. "Vignettes that reward exploration and discovery" ties directly to the game pillar in the boilerplate (progression driven by discovery) — the JD is internally coherent, which strong candidates notice.
- *Bullet 3 (hands-on writing and editing, style guide):* Repeats the player-manager expectation at craft level. "Every writer who touches the game" implies quest designers and others write too; the lead owns voice consistency, a real MMO problem.
- *Bullet 4 (narrative at MMO scale: out of order, in groups, multi-year persistence):* **The most important bullet in the JD.** This is the single biggest filter between single-player narrative designers and MMO-capable ones. Interview probe: "how do you tell a story to a player who does the zones in the wrong order, in a group, three years after launch?"
- *Bullet 5 (environmental storytelling with art and level design):* Signals narrative is embedded in world-construction, not bolted on. Mirrors the cross-discipline collaboration bullet every CH JD carries.
**Benchmark:** Industry references confirm worldbuilding/lore, quest content, and systemic narrative as the three pillars of MMO narrative work. All three are covered. CDPR adds "prototyping mechanics and conducting playtests" — covered in the next section.

## Your Role — "Systems and Collaboration" (5 bullets)

- *Bullet 1 (narrative and gameplay systems reinforce each other):* Two-directional framing ("story gives systems their stakes") matches the Game Design Lead JD, which names narrative as a collaborator. The two JDs interlock — a candidate reading both sees a studio that has thought about the seam.
- *Bullet 2 (pipeline and tooling: dialogue systems, quest scripting, localisation-ready text):* Expectation that the lead owns workflow, not just words. "Localisation-ready from day one" is a hard-won live-service lesson; senior candidates recognise it as a sign the studio has shipped before. Firesprite explicitly lists "overseeing tools, systems, and pipelines" and "managing written and VO localisation" — aligned.
- *Bullet 3 (VO planning with audio):* Deliberately mirrors the Audio Lead JD's "work with narrative... on voice implementation planning" — the two JDs reference each other, again signalling internal coherence. "Even if voice recording is a later milestone" honestly manages expectations about production stage.
- *Bullet 4 (playtests and player feedback validate narrative):* House evidence-over-assumption value, translated for narrative ("what players actually feel over what the story says"). Filters out writers who treat playtest feedback as an attack on the text.
- *Bullet 5 (production: scope, milestones, capacity):* Standard CH leads bullet. Narrative is notorious for unscopeable work; this says it gets scoped here. Lunacy's posting includes "budget and timeline management" — aligned.

## Your Role — "Team Building and Leadership" (5 bullets)

**Purpose:** The leadership block, positioned THIRD rather than first — deliberately mirroring the Audio Lead JD (the house function-builder pattern) rather than the Game Design Lead JD (the team-inheritor pattern, where People Leadership comes first). Craft leads in a discipline of one; management grows with the team.
- *Bullet 1 (manage and develop the existing narrative designer from day one):* States the truth precisely: one report exists now. Concrete management verbs (priorities, 1:1s, honest feedback) set the expectation that even a team of one gets real management. Interview probe: "walk me through how you run a 1:1."
- *Bullet 2 (build the team: define roles, JDs, writing tests, lead interview loops):* The function-builder mandate. "Define the roles the discipline needs" gives the lead org-design authority over their own function, which senior candidates value highly. "Writing tests" tells candidates assessment is craft-based, which good writers respect.
- *Bullet 3 (performance reviews, goal setting):* Signals real HR machinery exists (relevant: Lorenza will lean on leads for this).
- *Bullet 4 (represent discipline in leads meetings):* Narrative gets a seat at the table — historically the discipline that gets steamrolled by design and production. Strong attraction signal.
- *Bullet 5 (culture of craft rigour, honest critique, player-first storytelling, "everyone who writes for the game"):* Culture-setting duty extending beyond the narrative team — quest designers and others write too. "Honest critique" warns that writing gets edited here; precious writers self-filter.
**Benchmark:** Firesprite lists "leading the Narrative Design team day-to-day including line management" — aligned, and the CH version is more honest about the starting size and more concrete about what management means.

## Must-Haves (6 bullets)

- *"7+ years, at least one shipped title in a narrative design or game writing role":* House standard for leads (identical bar to Game Design Lead, Lead Concept Artist, Audio Lead). "Or game writing" deliberately widens the funnel — many strong narrative designers carry writer titles. Benchmark: CDPR asks 3+ years *as a lead* on AAA; Lunacy asks 5+ total. CH's 7+ total with 2+ managing is the same effective seniority expressed the house way. Consistent and defensible.
- *"2+ years of direct people management":* House standard, verbatim. Non-negotiable screen: managed humans, not just projects.
- *"Strong portfolio or writing samples... documentation that shows design thinking as well as prose quality":* Tells candidates exactly what to submit and tells reviewers what to reject: beautiful prose with no design thinking, or dry systems docs with no voice. Benchmark: CDPR requires "examples of narrative design features or systems... branching dialogue"; Lunacy requires an online portfolio. Aligned.
- *"Multiplayer or online games... non-linearly, socially, and at scale":* The MMO filter at must-have level. Deliberately "multiplayer or online" rather than "MMO" — requiring shipped MMO experience at must-have level would shrink the UK/Greece-compatible candidate pool to nearly zero. MMO experience sits in nice-to-haves instead, mirroring exactly how the Game Design Lead JD splits this.
- *"A lore entry an artist can build from and a quest spec an engineer can implement":* House pattern (Game Design Lead: "a design spec an engineer can implement without constant clarification"), adapted with a narrative-specific double test. Documentation is the lead's primary output at scale.
- *"Collaborating across disciplines to ship integrated narrative content":* House closing must-have, verbatim pattern. "Integrated" is the load-bearing word: not a writers' room that throws scripts over a wall.

## Nice-to-Haves (5 bullets)

- *Shipped MMO/persistent world through live service:* The dream profile, correctly positioned as bonus not bar. Identical placement to Game Design Lead.
- *Branching dialogue tools or narrative middleware (articy:draft, Ink):* Tool fluency signal. Naming real tools shows the JD was written by someone who knows the discipline — candidates notice generic JDs.
- *Quest scripting in Unreal Blueprints:* Mirrors the Game Design Lead's prototyping nice-to-have and matches the engine named in the boilerplate. CDPR also lists Unreal as nice-to-have. Aligned.
- *VO production and localisation planning at volume:* Live-service MMO reality; pairs with the Systems bullet. Firesprite has this as a responsibility; for CH's stage, nice-to-have is the honest level.
- *Live-service cadences (seasonal arcs, world events, evolving world states):* Connects to "multi-year live-service roadmap" in the boilerplate. Tests for candidates who understand narrative as an ongoing service, not a one-shot script.

## About You (6 bullets)

House-pattern section: second person, values, deliberately unverifiable on a CV — these are interview-calibration lines, not screening lines.
- *"Lead by investing in people, and you are comfortable starting with a team of one":* House opener for leads, merged with the Audio Lead's "comfortable being one of the first hires and building the function from scratch". One line covers both the management creed and the honest starting scale.
- *"You write for the player, not for the lore bible":* Narrative adaptation of the Game Design Lead's "design for the player, not for the design document". The single most useful interview calibration line: lore-first candidates will visibly bristle at it.
- *"Best game stories are told through play. Dialogue and cutscenes are tools, not the destination":* The one bespoke line in the section. States CH's narrative philosophy so candidates can disagree before applying rather than after joining. Aligned with Firesprite's "integrate storytelling seamlessly with gameplay".
- *Ambiguity / ambition-vs-pragmatism / remote autonomy:* House standards, near-verbatim. The remote line carries weight: a narrative lead who goes quiet for two weeks "while writing" is a known remote failure mode.

## Footer

> Location: Remote-first / Type: Full-time / Start: ASAP / Salary: Competitive, based on experience

House standard, identical to all CH JDs. **Note:** CDPR publishes a salary band ($151k–$210k for the US role). UK postings increasingly publish bands and some boards downrank listings without them. CH has chosen consistency over disclosure across all 29 JDs; that is a client policy decision, not a defect in this JD, but worth raising with Lorenza if narrative applications come in thin.
