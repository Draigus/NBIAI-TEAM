## Why we are doing this

We are mid-production with a full feature manifest, a hiring plan, an investor track running, a UK company being stood up, partner content being negotiated, and a studio that has been running on momentum more than on cadence. The offsite is the place we replace momentum with structure for the next twelve months.

We walk out of Day 3 with:

1. A clean roadmap our leads can take, size, subtask, true up, and roll into the production tracker with no rework.
2. Gate-passing criteria for every milestone gate so estimates have a yardstick.
3. Studio pipelines documented with named owners at every stage.
4. A staff plan that matches the new roadmap, with the highest-pain-point names called out and a path forward for each.
5. The studio's twelve-month goal cascaded so every IC can see what success looks like for their feature.

Studio leadership in the room is the delivery vehicle. There is no homework after, only the work we do inside the three days. If we slip, we extend the day.

## The three-day arc

### Day 1, Tuesday Apr 28 - Foundation and feature sweep

Open with the rules and the goal. Each of you commits verbally to the rules. We then walk the consolidated backlog so everyone is oriented on what's there. Most of the day is feature-sweep work: we go through the Game Features and Game Systems lists, agree owners across tech / content / design for each one, set rough sizing, and place every feature in a gate column on a wall. By end of day we have a gate wall with every Game Feature and every Game System placed, and an owner triplet against each.

We do **not** review every row individually. We focus on the rows the consolidated backlog has already flagged for review (around 180 lower-confidence classifications and a smaller pile of duplicates and assignment gaps). The leads will fill the body of the rest after. We are getting coverage and frame, not perfection.

### Day 2, Wednesday Apr 29 - Gates, live services, GTM, community, goals cascade

This is the most leveraged day. We define the entry criteria, exit criteria, gatekeeper, and required evidence for every gate from Concept through Launch. Once those are in place we walk the gate wall again and move features that don't actually meet criteria. After that we attack what is not in the backlog yet: live services tooling and rhythm, monetisation hooks, the GTM lane (segments, positioning, demo strategy, partnerships), the community plan (how, when, why at that time), and the studio-level twelve-month goal cascaded down so every feature has a one-line dream of success.

### Day 3, Thursday Apr 30 - Pipelines, RACI, staff, wrap

We map every pipeline in the studio: art, code, design, audio, QA, content, live ops, build, marketing. Steps, tools, handoffs, what triggers the next step. Then RACI for each pipeline: who is responsible, accountable, consulted, informed at each stage, and which consultations are mandatory versus optional. Late morning we drop into a C-level only session for staff assessment against the new roadmap (leads will be excused for that hour); we walk the previous quad review against the new pressure points, name the three to five highest-pain-point situations and a path forward for each. Afternoon is decisions walkthrough, naming Confluence article owners, and wrap.

## The rules

These are conditions of being in the room, not aspirations. We will state each on Day 1 morning and ask each of you to verbally agree.

1. **Verbal interaction from everybody.** Silent attendance is non-attendance. If we do not hear from you on a topic, we assume you have nothing to say and move on; that's a worse outcome than friction.

2. **Lean in. Active. Proactive. Thinking ahead.** Read the next two agenda items, not just the current one. The offsite only works if everyone is participating in shaping it.

3. **Disagree. Must disagree.** If you disagree with something we are about to lock, you have to say so. Silent disagreement that surfaces later is the failure mode that has hurt this studio before.

4. **Take breaks without announcing.** If you need ten minutes, walk out and come back when you are ready. Don't disrupt the room verbally to do it.

5. **Everything happens in the room.** Laptops for the room only. Phones for breaks only. No Slack on the side, no parallel emails, no "I'll come back to that".

6. **No "we'll figure that out later".** If we hit something we cannot resolve in session, it goes to the parking lot with an owner and a deadline, and we keep moving.

## Six pre-decisions I am laying down before Day 1

These are decided. The reasoning behind each is solid; if any is fundamentally unworkable for you, ping me in Slack before Sunday evening so we can talk before we land. If you want to disagree at the offsite during the relevant session, that is fine and you must do it openly. If you stay silent here and silent in the room, you have agreed.

1. **Pricing model: F2P with cosmetic monetisation, plus a Founder's Pack tier pre-launch.** Charges for the aspirational currency the design pillars already centre on. Aligns the business model with the no-stat-gear pillar.

2. **Publisher direction: self-publish core, targeted capability partnerships only.** Preserves freedom on partner-game integrations and on live ops. Glen has the BD network for targeted deals (one platform-aligned, one regional, one IP cross-promo as the starter pattern).

3. **Demo, beta, early access: closed beta at vertical slice / mid-production, no public early access, public demos at major beats only.** Closed beta gets us server load and social system testing without burning launch energy. Founder's Pack carries the early-access value as a perk.

4. **Studio name versus game name: separate.** Couch Heroes stays as the game name. Studio name is TBD and resolves at the offsite or in the week after. Avoids capping studio identity at one game's lifecycle.

5. **Cross-play and unified account: unified account is mandatory by design. Cross-play across PC and mobile at launch, consoles when first-party negotiations land.** The Platform layer (web, mobile) needs a single identity spine; partner game integration funnels demand cross-platform play.

6. **Studio twelve-month goal: close the institutional funding round and ship an internal vertical slice the leadership room is proud of. Plus sustainable production cadence, plus core hiring to working capacity per the roadmap.** Without funding close, every other goal is contingent. Vertical slice is what funding closes against. Cadence and hiring are the two supporting outcomes that prove we can execute against the roadmap we leave the offsite with.
