Player Attributes
Attributes are the different skill sets every player in GOALS has. Each attribute has several
underlying stats, often referred to as “sub-stats”. The value of each sub-stat determines how
well a player will perform in-game. The value of a sub-stat ranges from 1-99, and the
performance increase for stats is usually linear.
Below is a description of each sub-stat in GOALS, and how it affects the playerʼs performance
during gameplay 
Note:All stats and their impact on gameplay are subject to change. Furthermore, stats
balancing is done regularly, meaning the importance of a certain sub-stat can vary over
time. Feedback on stats logic and balancing is much appreciated!

Height & Weight
Height: Affects the total jumping reach together with the jumping stat.
For Agility, there is a height multiplier ranging from 0,9-1 acting on top of the stat. This means
that a player of maximum height will have their agility stat multiplied by 0,9, and a player of
minimum height will have their agility stat multiplied by 1. This means that the average agility of
tall players will be slightly lower than for short players. The reasoning behind this multiplier is to
balance the advantages between short and tall players since tall players inevitably will have an
advantage for headers due to a better total jumping reach on average.
Weight: When determining the weight of a player upon generation, the height and the strength
stat are the determining factors. The taller the player is, and the stronger the player is, the
heavier they will be. Weight is being used to determine how much force a player can exert and
withstand during collisions and duels.
