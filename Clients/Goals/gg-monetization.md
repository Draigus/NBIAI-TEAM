Currencies:
Hard currency (coins) - Used to purchase items in the in-game store. Can only be attained by
cash payments.
Soft currency (points) - Used to purchase player packs in the in-game store, unlock player
upgrades, legend conversions, and trading players on the marketplace. Can be earned
through gameplay rewards and by discarding/selling players on the marketplace.
Note: While soft currency cannot be directly purchased by users, the soft currency demand is
directly tied to hard currency demand, as hard currency is a faster way to get hold of soft
currency by purchasing players and discarding/selling them on the marketplace for soft
currency. Therefore, while some game features are soft currency exclusive, they still have a
direct effect on hard currency demand.

Asset descriptions
Included in launch:
Player packs - Includes a number of unique players. Packs come with different types of
guarantees - tier, potential, nationality, position, age, stats, etc. The number of players and the
pack guarantees determines the price of a pack. Time-limited packs generally offer better value
than the regular packs that are available at all times.
Kits - The clothing all players in a userʼs team will wear during a game and in the lobby. Users
can assign any kit to either of their active slots (home/away). Each kit comes with a separate
design for the goalkeeper. In the lobby, the user can select between their active kits for their
own team, and select between the opponentʼs active kits for the opposing team. 
Celebrations - Goal celebrations that can be triggered after scoring a goal. Users can set x
celebrations to be active in team customization. Celebrations live on a team level, i. e. all active
celebrations can be triggered by any player on the team after scoring a goal.
Originals packs - Includes one Original resembling a real-life footballer. Visuals for Originals are
fixed to resemble the real-life footballer. Stats are unique just as for all other players, but are
generated within ranges to ensure real-life characteristics are kept intact. Originals will generally
have a fairly high starting rating, ~70-85+ depending on the player, and are subject to
progression and aging just like all other players.
Originals packs could either guarantee a specific Original in the pack, or pick from a pool of
Originals with different drop rates (e. g. Messi very low drop rate compared to Busquets). Ideally
weʼd prefer the pool-based approach, but that depends on the number of Originals we have
available, and what the rev-share requirements look like. With the approach of having a specific
Original guaranteed in a pack, the price point should differ based on the player.

Post-launch:
Footballs - Footballs that can be selected to be used in-game whenever the user plays a home
game. The user can set one active football in team customization.
Stadiums - Stadiums that can be selected for the userʼs home games. The user can set one
active stadium in team customization.
Penalty run-ups - Penalty run-ups that can be selected to be used in-game when one of the
userʼs players takes a penalty. (TBD whether this should be part of player generation instead)
Free kick run-ups - Free kick run-ups that can be selected to be used in-game when one of the
userʼs players takes a free kick. (TBD whether this should be part of player generation instead)
Stadium customization assets - Visual customization of stadiums. Nothing concrete planned
yet, but could be things like tifos, grass, chants, goal music, etc.
Celebrity packs - Includes one player resembling a real-life celebrity. Similar to Originals, but
with the difference that rating guarantees will generally be lower, and the stats are completely
randomized as we donʼt have any real-life characteristics that we need to maintain. For certain
celebrities a few sub-stats could be guaranteed (e. g. The Rock = 95+ strength guaranteed, or
iShowSpeed = 85+ pace guaranteed or similar). Celebrities are subject to aging and progression
just like all other players.
Similar to Originals, depending on the number we have available, and what rev-share guidlines
allow, clebrity packs could either guarantee a specific celebrity, or pick from a pool of celebrities
with different drop rates. To make celebrity packs more appealing, they could come with
different rating guarantees at different price points. E. g. Basic+ being the cheapest alternative
and Uncommon+ the most expensive.

Swaps - Increases the demand for players to be able to meet the swap criteria and get a
reward in return. With time-limited swaps and custom requirements being added, more
specific player types will be required to complete certain swaps, to further increase the
demand. Any increase in demand for players increases the demand for soft and hard currency
to get hold of new players.
Tournaments - Tournaments with custom entry requirements increases the demand for
players beyond your regular starting XI, as youʼll need different types of players (nationality,
age, Legend/active, team rating etc.) to enter tournaments.
Challenges - Certain challenges have player-specific requirements to complete. For users
who donʼt have a fit for the challenge, this results in a bigger demand for player packs to get
hold of a player to complete the challenge(s).
Marketplace - Increases the demand for soft currency to be able to purchase desirable
players on the marketplace. Each trade on the marketplace also results in a 10% fee in soft
currency, draining the ecosystem on soft currency.
An increase in soft currency demand translates to a bigger demand for hard currency as well.
While users cannot directly purchase soft currency, they can get hold of it by using hard
currency to purchase players to then discard, swap, or sell on the marketplace for soft
currency.
Progression system - To unlock player upgrades, users must pay with soft currency. The price
depends on the current rating of the player, where higher rated players are more expensive to
upgrade. This is a soft currency sink that helps to increase the demand for soft currency,
leading to a bigger demand for hard currency as well.
Legend conversion - Once a player retires they are no longer eligible to be used in gameplay.
However, by converting a retired player to a legend, users can once again use the player in
game modes where the entry requirements allow it. The legend conversion comes at a soft
currency cost based on the playerʼs rating, once again draining the ecosystem on soft
currency and increasing the demand for hard currency to get hold of more soft currency.

Brand partnerships:
While not entirely my territory, the idea behind brand partnerships is to give exposure to brands
that fit well with the GOALS identity in our game. Fortunately, having brands as part of the game
is very natural in a football context, and there are several avenues that we could explore:
Stadium Branding
Billboards & Banners: Brands on LED screens or billboards by the pitch side.
Stadium naming rights: A brand sponsoring the name of a stadium in the game, e. g. “Adidas
Stadium” or similar.
Scoreboard: Animations or logos appearing during half-time or goal replays, either on the
scoreboard or on the pitch.
Kits and Accessories
Kits: Branded kits as part of the store offering - E. g. “Manchester United Home” or “Adidas
Gold” kits.
Accessories: Branded accessories as part of player generation - boots, gloves, wristbands, etc.
Footballs - Branded footballs as part of the store offering
Branded player packs and player cards
Player packs: Branded player packs that come at a discount, e. g. “Adidas Premium”
Player cards: Player cards that come in a branded player pack also comes with a custom player
card design with the brand on it (still tier-based).
Broadcasts
Replay Sponsorships: “Replay brought to you by” or similar.
Half-time: Graphics and/or commercials related to the brand.
