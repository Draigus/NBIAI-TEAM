## Introduction and Summary

Following the success of the March open beta—headlined by a 50% day-one retention rate on PlayStation—the transition to the May 14 global launch requires a shift from technical validation to commercial durability. This proposal outlines set of steps designed to better inform monetization and pricing decisions, and establish a sustainable live-service foundation.
Our approach is guided by the underlying principle that the first 30–60 days post-launch must prioritize player stability and sentiment. By establishing a fair, market-aligned pricing framework now, PlayGOALS earns the credibility required to further develop monetization as the game matures. NBI is the ideal partner for this pre-launch phase, offering implementation-ready strategies that respect your non-seasonal design philosophy and the realities of multi-platform publishing.
**Core Objectives:**

Optimizing Pricing Strategies Prior to the Launch Window: Eliminating pricing volatility and potential resulting player friction before the first transaction occurs.
Live-Ops Foundation: Building the "pulse and tempo" necessary to sustain beta momentum through the high-stakes June World Cup period.
Operational Readiness: Empowering a team new to live services with battle-tested templates and decision intelligence.
**Summary of Workstreams:**

This effort will include two separate but related workstreams:
Pricing Benchmarking and Monetization Strategy: A global analysis and regional framework to ensure day-one revenue efficiency and player fairness.
Live Service Consulting: A tactical roadmap for engagement, focusing on content cadences and internal team alignment.
These workstreams help to ensure that PlayGOALS launches not just as a great game, but as a resilient business.



## Background

The PlayGOALS March beta provided more than just data; it provided strong evidence of market potential. Surpassing industry benchmarks with 50% D1 retention on PlayStation confirms that the core loop is resonant and the technical architecture is multi-platform ready.
However, while User Acquisition (CPI) and player retention are proven, the monetization framework remains unvalidated. Launching without a rigorous, benchmarked pricing structure introduces significant risks in terms of player sentiment and the game’s financial stability. Because pricing changes are notoriously difficult to implement post-launch—particularly within the rigid ecosystems of console platforms, immediate action is required to ensure the financial foundation is as stable as the gameplay.

## Proposed Scope of Work: Overview

We recognize that with six weeks to launch, major architectural shifts are not feasible. Instead, our approach will focus on high-leverage strategic adjustments and operational frameworks that can be deployed immediately.
PlayGOALS has prioritized Pricing Benchmarking and Live Service Consulting as the "first packages" for three reasons:
Platform Lead Times: Sony and other console platforms require pricing finalization well in advance of launch.
The World Cup Pulse: The June World Cup begins only weeks after launch; the live-ops cadence must be finalized by May 14 to capitalize on this window.
Team Empowerment: Providing your new Live Ops Specialist and the broader team with shareable templates reduces the "cognitive load" during the high-stress launch period.
This initial set of activities promotes a post-launch "stabilization phase" (Days 1–30) while preparing the ground for deeper monetization expansion at Day 60+.

### Work Stream 1: Pricing Benchmarking and Monetization Strategy

Upfront pricing accuracy is a cornerstone of player trust. Mistakes in pricing policies such as foreign exchange translation or a failure to account for regional purchasing power can lead to accusations of "price gouging" and significant revenue leakage.
**Methodology & Objectives**

We will establish a market-aligned, resilient pricing structure that maximizes conversion while maintaining defensive positioning against 3–5 direct competitors.
Hard-Currency Logic: We will validate your base price points, ensuring that currency bundle tiers (e.g., the "Path to Purchase") are optimized for conversion and platform-specific SKU structures.
Regional Strategy: Move beyond simple currency conversion to a localized strategy that respects regional market norms, preventing the player backlash common in global launches.
Platform Navigation: Specifically addressing the "Sony bottleneck." Since PlayStation is less flexible with mid-stream pricing changes, we will focus on pre-emptive pricing submissions to ensure Day 1 stability.
**Key Activities**

Analysis of 3–5 competitive sets across North America, EU, and LATAM.
Internal conversion rate optimization (CRO) review of current draft pricing tables.
Strategic guidance on SKU "bundling" to increase Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) without damaging sentiment.
**Deliverables**

**Regional Pricing Framework: A comprehensive guide for all launch territories.**

**Competitive Benchmark Summary: A "battle card" detailing how PlayGOALS sits within the market.**

White Paper: The "Our Creators" Risk (Optional): A detailed analysis of how the project "Our Creators" suffered massive player backlash and revenue loss by ignoring regional pricing, and how PlayGOALS will avoid this.
**Timeline**

**Weeks 1-2: Competitive Analysis & Data Intake (CPI, draft tables).**

**Weeks 3-4: Framework Development & Regional Logic.**

**Weeks 5-6: Implementation Support & Platform Submission Guidance.**

**Inputs Required**

Current draft pricing and currency conversion tables.
Historical CPI data and retention metrics from the March Beta.
Proposed SKU structures and platform-specific store configurations.

### Work Stream 2: Live Service Consulting

Maintaining the momentum of the beta requires a transition from launching a game to operating a service. This workstream focuses on the strategic pulse and tempo of the game post-launch.
**Strategy & Alignment**

We focus on a dual perspective: generating revenue and maintaining the 50% D1 retention levels. Close collaboration with appropriate PlayGOALS personnel is critical to ensure that all engagement strategies respect the non-season-based design philosophy.
Engagement Pulse: Designing a cadence of content and events that avoid player burnout while providing constant reasons to return.
Internal Education: We will provide "shareable examples"—including a Live Plan Simulation—to train a team that is largely new to live-service environments.
Milestone Synchronization: Deep-dive planning for the May 14 Launch, the June World Cup peak, and August content planning.
**Key Activities**

Develop a "Content Release Schedule" that aligns with technical capabilities (e.g., Unreal Engine’s season triggering).
Establish an "Asset Warehousing Philosophy" to ensure the pipeline can support the live cadence.
Create recommendations for a data-driven A/B testing framework for rapid optimization of player spend and engagement.
**Deliverables**

Live Plan Simulation: A mock 90-day roadmap (based on NBI’s recent studio success) demonstrating a high-performance cadence.
**Content Release & Scheduling Framework: A tactical guide for the first three months.**

**A/B Testing Playbook: Standardized structures for optimizing engagement.**

**Timeline**

**Weeks 1-2: Design Alignment (Frans) & Technical Constraint Mapping.**

**Weeks 3-4: Live Plan Simulation & Template Creation.**

**Weeks 5-6: Internal Team Workshop & Hand-off to Live Ops Specialist.**
