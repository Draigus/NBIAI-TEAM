## 3. Longitudinal Trends (EA FC Points 2018-2026)

- **FIFA 19:** 8 tiers, max $39.99 (4,600 pts)
- **FIFA 20-22:** 9 tiers, added 12,000 tier at $99.99
- **FIFA 23-FC 26:** 8 tiers, current structure (max $149.99 for 18,500 pts)

**EA has NEVER increased the USD price of an existing tier.** Their strategy:
1. Add higher-ceiling tiers (pushing max from $39.99 → $99.99 → $149.99)
2. Restructure mid-range tiers (removed 250/750/2200/4600, added 2800/5900/18500)
3. Raise non-USD currencies when FX moves against them (GBP, AUD, CAD adjusted June 2023)

**Implication for Goals:** "Start slightly high" philosophy is validated. EA demonstrates you CAN add new tiers later but you CANNOT raise existing tier prices.

## 4. UFL — Closest Direct Competitor

UFL's pricing is **deliberately benchmarked against EA FC:**
- 6 LP tiers: 100/$0.99 through 9,500/$79.99
- Tier structure maps 1:1 to EA FC's lower 6 tiers
- Volume discount curve (18%) slightly below EA FC (22%)
- UFL has no equivalent to EA FC's $100 or $150 tiers (lower ceiling)

**UFL's economy changed significantly in Season 25-26** (Sep 2025):
- Moved from skin upgrades to pack-based card acquisition
- Now closer to EA FC Ultimate Team model
- Added Legendary tier cards, Mastery progression
- Team Pass (battle pass): 1,000 LP (~$9.99) standard, 2,000 LP (~$19.99) elite

**Implication for Goals:** UFL priced at or slightly below EA FC. Goals should price at parity or slightly above UFL (since Goals has the "new, non-P2W" positioning advantage).

## 5. F2P Cosmetic Pricing Ceiling (Valorant)

Valorant represents the **upper bound** of what competitive F2P players accept for cosmetics:
- Individual skins: $15-25 (Select to Deluxe editions)
- Premium skins: $25-50 (Premium/Exclusive editions)
- Ultra-tier bundles: $70-100+
- Volume discount on VP: only 13.3% at max tier (deliberately NOT rewarding bulk buys)

**Implication for Goals:** If Goals' cosmetics (kits, celebrations) are priced in the $3-7 range (as their internal model shows), they are positioned well BELOW the proven ceiling. Room exists to go higher if cosmetic quality warrants it.

## 6. Industry Pricing Change Patterns

| Publisher | Strategy for Price Increases | Example |
|---|---|---|
| EA | Raise ceiling, restructure tiers, adjust non-USD currencies | 18,500 tier added FIFA 23; GBP raised June 2023 |
| Epic (Fortnite) | Keep $ price, reduce HC quantity | March 2026: $8.99 went from 1000 to 800 V-Bucks |
| Riot (Valorant) | No changes to VP pricing; raise item prices in VP instead | Skin bundle prices have increased over time |
| Respawn (Apex) | Adjust non-USD only; decouple Battle Pass from premium currency | June 2023 regional; Season 22 pass change |
| 2K (NBA) | No documented USD changes; rely on new modes to expand spend surface | VC consistent but cross-mode spending grows |

**Universal rule:** Nobody raises the USD sticker price on currency packs. They find other ways.

**Implication for Goals:** Once you set a USD price tier, it's permanent. Jonas's instinct ("cannot raise prices post-launch") is correct and supported by every competitor's behaviour.

## 9. Recommendations Preview (for deliverable)

Based on this data, the preliminary direction for Goals:

1. **Goals' 7 HC tiers should price at EA FC parity** — the market anchor. UFL already does this.
2. **Volume discount curve: target 20-25%** at max tier (matching EA FC/Madden, slightly above UFL's 18%)
3. **Launch with a ceiling of $99.99** (12,000-tier equivalent). Add $149.99 tier later if demand supports it.
4. **Entry tier at $0.99** for 100 HC — industry standard, proven conversion optimiser
5. **Do NOT plan to raise USD prices.** Ever. Set them right the first time. Jonas's instinct is correct.
6. **Regional pricing:** Follow EA FC's regional matrix as the template (it's the most tested in the genre)

These are preliminary directions pending full deliverable analysis. The data supports them but the final recommendations should be more specific (exact HC amounts per tier, exact regional multipliers).
