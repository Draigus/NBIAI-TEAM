## Page 1

Knockout
Knockout is a weekly game mode only available in the 1v1 format. The objective of Knockout is
to progress through as many matches and divisions as possible, with limited “attempts” at
hand. This creates an exciting feeling that every game truly matters. 
Knockout Weekly Reset
Knockout is only playable during a specific time window (for example, Thursday-Sunday) each
week, and resets on a weekly basis. 
Users should be able to tell how long it is left until the next Knockout becomes available when
none is currently available, and how long is left until the ongoing one expires.
Knockout Divisions
Knockout games are divided into 4 regular Divisions (not counting the final Knockout Division),
each split into 3 subdivisions. These are Bronze, Silver, Gold and Elite.
Users starts each week in Bronze III, and work their way upwards to Bronze II, then Bronze I,
then Silver III etc.
Each Division is made up of a set number of max games, and thresholds for each subdivision.
The number of max games and thresholds can vary per Division.

## Page 2

As an Example, the Bronze division gives the user a total of 15 games maximum. To get from
Bronze III to Bronze II, the user needs to win 1 game. To get from Bronze II to Bronze I, they
need to win an additional time (for a total of 2 Bronze wins) and to get promoted into the next
Division (Silver III) they need to win a total of 3 games.
Each game removes 1 from the total of remaining games, whether it was a win or a loss.
Getting Knocked Out
If users fail to reach a new Division within the max number of games, they are eliminated and
can no longer play this weeks rotation of Knockout.
They can then immediately claim the reward for the Division/Subdivision they reached (unless
they are in the Knockout Leaderboard, detailed further down on this page).

## Page 5

Users have a limited number of attempts before getting knocked out, but they do not progress
through subdivisions. Instead, they can play as many games as possible to reach the highest
amount of wins they are able to before running out of attempts. Each loss removes 1 attempt,
whereas a win leaves the number of attempts remaining unaffected.
When reaching this Division, we start tracking their position on a global leaderboard across all
platforms, and if they are within the top 10,000 users this is displayed in a badge that is
displayed similar to rank badges.
Users that reach the top 500 win streaks within the Division across all platforms are displayed in
an in-game leaderboard.
Users here are displayed in order of highest to lowest win-streak. Whichever user achieved the
streak first is considered higher on the leaderboard in the case of ties.
If users shown on the leaderboard have an account connected to the platform the local user is
currently viewing the leaderboard on, that platform username is displayed. Otherwise their
GOALS username is displayed.

Top 500 Leaderboard Rewards
The weekly reward for users that are in the Knockout Division works differently in that it
depends on the outcome of their final position on the leaderboard when this Knockout window
closes.
That means users can not claim their reward until that occurs, as they can potentially move
down in position without playing.
Users are rewarded based on how they end the week on the leaderboard, and only earn the
specific reward position they were placed within (a user can not get both the top 500 and top
100 rewards for example).

Leaderboard History (MVP+)
The result of the top 500 leaderboard should be stored each week, so that we can allow users
to reference it in the future.
Regional Leaderboards
While the rewards for the Knockout Leaderboards are exclusive to the global top 500 users
across all platforms, we want to allow users to displayed leaderboards specific to regions as
well. These regional leaderboards display up to 500 users that have accounts based in each
specific region.
