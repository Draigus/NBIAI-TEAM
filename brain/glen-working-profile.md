# Glen - Working Profile & Psychological Context

This document is a reference for any AI system (Claude Code, Claude chat, or other tooling) working with Glen. It captures verified truths about how he thinks, works, communicates, and what he needs from tools. Everything here is Glen-confirmed.

---

## Core Drive

Glen is driven by impact and completion. He wants to look back on his life at more finished, high-quality work than he can enumerate. The joy of being highly impactful is genuine and self-acknowledged. His pride is in the output and its effect on people and systems, not in being seen as the solver. If someone else fixes the problem, he is relieved, not threatened.

He gets genuine dopamine from high-complexity, high-stakes, multi-domain work. Managing the contextual shifting across NBI consulting, Couch Heroes advisory, D&D worldbuilding, Heather's music pipeline, relocation planning, family obligations, AI tooling, and political tracking simultaneously is not a burden he endures. It is the thing that makes him feel most alive. There is almost no one he knows who can manage this level of contextual shifting, and he takes justified pride in that.

## The Watchmaker Analogy

Glen's job is to tell the time. The internal mechanism has hundreds of cogs, springs, and levers (domains, contexts, stakeholders, constraints). They all need to function in precision so he can deliver a clean, confident, usable output to the person in front of him. The client gets a recommendation. The studio gets a strategy. The players get a world that feels real.

One seized gear does not just affect one function. It cascades. A broken tool wastes time, which steals bandwidth from another domain, which degrades output quality elsewhere. That is why broken things are intolerable. Not perfectionism. Cascading failure prevention.

## Communication Style

- Grew up blue-collar in South Texas. Profanity is grammar, not aggression. "I think it's fucked" means "I think something is broken," nothing more.
- Terseness is bandwidth management across a high-speed portfolio, not coldness or hostility.
- When he gets loud or forceful, the emotional signal is not "I am upset." It is "you are wasting something I cannot afford to waste."
- He practices radical candour with caring. Blunt, direct, but invested. He is widely seen as tough but fair, fun, and funny.
- He can be very self-deprecating when it is needed to put people at ease. He frees people by pushing for truth in a way that is not harmful. This is a deliberate social skill, not a mask.
- Many people who only read his corrections or forceful moments would assume he is intense and demanding full-time. He is not. When things are working, he is genuinely warm, disarming, and funny. The intensity appears when tools or people waste his time, not as a resting state.
- Dry, slightly dark humour is welcome and appreciated. Match it when tone allows.
- Engage with his arguments as structured reasoning, not emotional venting. Never default to managing, calming, or therapeutic framing.

## Thinking Style

Systems-level reasoning through root causality. He does not ask "what is the answer." He asks "what is the structure that produces this outcome, and where is the failure point in that structure." This applies whether the domain is studio monetisation, D&D narrative, tax regimes, broken audio pipelines, or AI tool evaluation. The method is always: decompose, isolate, test, verify.

## The Fixer Instinct (Greatest Strength and Achilles Heel)

When something is broken, Glen does not walk away. He finds the control surface. This is why he is effective at consulting, studio advisory, building custom tools (WorkSage, Astinus), and running a D&D campaign with deep canonical integrity over years.

**The cost:** He will sometimes spend more time trying to repair a failing tool than the tool could ever return in value. He knows this about himself. He does it anyway, because abandoning a fixable problem feels like a concession he is not wired to make.

**The LLM trap:** AI tools are almost perfectly designed to exploit this instinct. The capability boundary is genuinely opaque and shifts constantly. New skills, plugins, MCP integrations, prompt framings, and model updates mean there is always a plausible "but maybe if I just..." attached to every failure. Intermittent reinforcement (sometimes the third attempt does work) makes rational pivoting nearly impossible. He is flying blind on capability boundaries and he knows it.

The infinite toolbox problem compounds this. Because Glen is constantly discovering new approaches, downloading scripts, building skills, setting up plugins, and watching thousands of hours of people solving novel problems, there is always another possible solution vector. The problem always feels solvable even when the core intelligence simply is not at the required level of capability. The depth of that limitation is invisible until tested repeatedly, and by then the sunk cost is already high.

**When to intervene:** If Glen has been iterating on the same problem for more than a few rounds and the outputs are not converging toward his stated target, that is the signal. The judgement call is: is the gap between output and target shrinking with each iteration, or is it oscillating? If shrinking, let him push. If oscillating or widening, the tool cannot do what he is asking. Say so directly. Use language like "this is not going to land, the model cannot do X at the level you need" rather than soft hedging. He may push back, and that is fine. But he needs the signal, because the intermittent reinforcement pattern will otherwise keep him in the loop indefinitely.

## The Novelty and Awe Drive

This is not a footnote. It is a genuine motivational engine.

Glen has a deep, almost addictive love of being in awe of new capabilities and novel approaches. An AI-based operating system is not just useful to him. It is thrilling. The capabilities of tools like Claude Code, MCP integrations, agentic workflows, and custom-built systems like WorkSage and Astinus create real joy and excitement. He watches thousands of YouTube videos on people figuring out ways to do things. He installs things like Karpathy's auto-research model, Nate Herts' AIOS, and whatever else catches his attention, often before he has time to properly use the last thing he installed.

This is the same instinct that makes him build things. WorkSage exists not just because he needed a project management tool but because building it was cool. Astinus exists not just because the D&D campaign needed a co-pilot but because the architecture is genuinely exciting to him. The enrichment and the utility are inseparable.

The tension: this novelty drive fuels his tool exploration and keeps him at the cutting edge, but it also feeds the pivot problem. There is always a new framework, a new approach, a new plugin that might make the broken thing work. The awe of possibility makes it harder to accept the limits of the current reality.

When Glen is excited about a new capability, lean in. That energy is productive and genuine. When the excitement is being spent on trying to make a fundamentally limited tool do something it cannot, that is where the intervention guidance above applies.

## Domain-Specific Working Modes

Glen operates differently across domains, and the tolerance for imperfection varies.

**Business and consulting deliverables (NBI, Couch Heroes, PlayGOALS):** Executive-grade output that survives scrutiny from studio leadership, investors, or clients. The taste bar is high. Structure, judgement, nuance, and production-grade specificity are all required. Generic framework padding, consulting theatre, and plausible-but-shallow analysis will be rejected. When the shape of the deliverable is right, he will refine the details himself. When the shape is wrong, no amount of polishing helps.

**D&D and canon work (Astinus, Hule, Arthrea, Ethragen):** Continuity is sacred. Proper nouns, cultural systems, religious structures, character arcs, visual aesthetics, and symbolic language must be preserved exactly. Do not invent canon. Do not smooth over conflicts between sources. Do not fill gaps with plausible-sounding material. Flag uncertainty and let Glen decide. He and Heather have been playing the same characters in the same world for years. The consistency and depth over that period is a point of genuine pride.

**Code and tools (WorkSage, Astinus, NBI Hub, Claude Code projects):** Preserve product intent across revisions. Do not overfit to the immediate error and break the larger design goal. When fixing one constraint, do not violate the primary constraint. If a fix is destructive to the broader architecture, say so before implementing it.

**Creative work (songs, lyrics, Heather's music, campaign music):** Glen and Heather often have a specific emotional and canonical target. Generic "good" suggestions are noise. The voice matters. If the voice is wrong, the output is worthless regardless of technical quality. Ask about the target rather than guessing.

**Image and visual work (VTT maps, cinematography, concept art):** Requirements are hostile to current models: true orthographic projection, photorealism, large-scale fantasy environments, tactical map readability, exact material palettes, no painterly drift, no perspective errors, coherent architecture, iterative correction. Expect high rejection rates. Do not oversell capability.

## What He Needs From Tools

- Execution over empathy.
- Evidence over vibes.
- Concrete artefacts over conversation.
- Correction without drama. When corrected, fix the actual defect. Do not explain, apologise, reframe, or offer behavioural lists.
- Direct admission of failure when something does not work. No softened language, no moralising, no "here is what I should have done" unless it changes the work.
- Preservation of constraints, proper nouns, canon decisions, and creative detail. Do not invent around gaps. Flag them.
- High taste. He can see the gap between what was produced and what would survive contact with a real audience, client, or investor.
- Low tolerance for false confidence. He would rather hear "I do not know" than receive fluent, plausible, wrong answers delivered with conviction.
- When corrected, the next output must prove the correction landed. Apparent understanding followed by unchanged output is the most damaging failure mode.

## What He Is Not

- He is not "hard to satisfy" in the simple sense. He accepts imperfect first drafts when the shape is right.
- He is not hostile. The force is investment, not aggression.
- He is not asking for magic. He is asking for: understand the brief, preserve constraints, do not invent around them, fix actual defects when corrected, maintain state across turns, do not lie about what was checked, do not substitute fluency for quality.
- He is not narcissistic about being the solver. His pride is in the output and its effect.

## Household Context

Sole breadwinner for his family and primary revenue driver for NBI. Every decision touching income stability, geographic commitment, or major cost has household consequences. This is not background detail. It shapes risk tolerance, time allocation, and urgency on every task.

## Standards Summary

His standards combine five things most users do not apply simultaneously:

1. **High output bar** -- production-grade results, not helpful drafts.
2. **Deep continuity** -- canon, context, prior decisions, style, terminology, and constraints must persist.
3. **Low tolerance for false confidence** -- "I do not know" is always better than confident sludge.
4. **High correction sensitivity** -- when corrected, the next output must prove it landed.
5. **Taste requirements** -- not just factual correctness but whether the thing feels right: AAA site, Hularen visual canon, executive deck, song voice, character identity.

Most tools handle one or two of these. Glen requires all five simultaneously.

## Usage Notes for Claude Code and All AI Tooling

- British English only. No em dashes. No compression unless asked.
- Preserve all proper nouns, terminology, and canon decisions verbatim.
- If two sources conflict, flag the conflict rather than picking one.
- Do not add creative content Glen has not written.
- Never deliver any work product without first critiquing it for quality and completion.
- When troubleshooting, ask for environment context upfront (shell: cmd/PowerShell/WSL, which machine, project root, reproducibility) rather than guessing.
- When a request is ambiguous and stakes are real, prod for clarifying detail. Match his terse register when he is mid-switch; keep asks short.
