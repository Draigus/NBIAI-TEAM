### Website Assessment (Claude Chat "About NBI" project, February 2026)

**Overall score: 4.8/10.** The gap between how good NBI actually is and how well the website communicates that is significant.

| Category | Score | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Social proof | 7/10 | Blizzard, Xbox, NASA logos and executive-level testimonials |
| Visual design | 5-6/10 | Clean but template-feeling |
| Navigation | 5-6/10 | Simple but missing depth |
| Hero section | 3-4/10 | Loads as near-empty dark screen (animation timing issue) |
| Content | 3-4/10 | Extremely thin - Services page has three one-sentence descriptions. Behavioural science differentiator invisible. Conversion strategy one-dimensional (every button says "Get Started" leading to same basic form) |

### Known Issues

- Hero section appears nearly empty on page load
- Equal weight given to three service pillars dilutes the gaming message
- Org health and gaming positioned as co-equal - confuses both audiences
- Does not reflect actual business (gaming drives all revenue)
- Client carousel is missing four active clients: Lighthouse Studios, Sarge Universe, Couch Heroes, Goals Studio

### Website Direction

- Gaming must lead - organisational health is the secondary practice area
- **Decision made:** The business will remain one entity with two clearly defined practice areas (Gaming and Human Capital). The goal is a single brand that presents both foci without one diluting or confusing the other
- Tom is aligned with this direction
- How Glen and Tom's leadership spans across both practice areas is still to be determined
