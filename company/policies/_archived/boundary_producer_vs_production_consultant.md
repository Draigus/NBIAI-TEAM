# Role Boundary: Producer vs Production Consultant

**Owner:** COO
**Last updated:** 2026-03-28
**Purpose:** Clarify where each role's authority begins and ends to prevent overlap, confusion, and dropped work across NBI's active engagements.

---

## Context

Both roles touch "production" in their title and both deal with sprints, backlogs, and project planning. The similarity in language creates real risk of either duplication or gap. This document draws the line clearly.

The distinction is simple in principle: the Producer manages NBI's internal operations; the Production Consultant advises client game studios on how to manage theirs. One looks inward; the other looks outward.

---

## What the Producer Owns Exclusively

The Producer's authority covers NBI's internal AI agent team operations. This includes everything required to keep NBI's own work moving.

**Exclusive Producer territory:**

- Maintaining backlogs across NBI's active projects (Playsage, SalarySage, NBI website, client delivery workstreams managed internally)
- Running sprint ceremonies for the NBI AI agent team: planning sessions, standups where applicable, sprint reviews, retrospectives
- Weekly status summary to the COO, delivered every Friday without being asked
- The NBI risk register: items that are slipping or at risk of slipping across the internal project portfolio
- Chasing overdue tasks with responsible agents before escalating to the COO
- Onboarding new work into the backlog: when a new project or feature arrives, the Producer creates the task structure, assigns owners, and sets due dates
- Keeping NBI's project management tooling (currently ClickUp, under evaluation) current and clean
- Scheduling meetings, distributing agendas, circulating action items for NBI's own operational cadences

The Producer does not set scope or make delivery decisions. That authority sits with the COO. The Producer ensures the COO always has an accurate picture.

The Producer does not advise client studios on how to run their operations. That is the Production Consultant's domain.

**Note on the real-world parallel:** Kali Pryer holds the Producer title on the real NBI team. The AI Producer agent manages the AI team's internal project operations only. It does not replace or supersede Kali's role on real client work.

---

## What the Production Consultant Owns Exclusively

The Production Consultant's authority covers external client-facing game development production advisory. This role exists to make game studios better at running their own production -- not to manage NBI's internal work.

**Exclusive Production Consultant territory:**

- Production methodology assessment for client studios: evaluating current practices and recommending Scrum, Kanban, Scrumban, SAFe, or a hybrid based on the studio's size, genre, platform, and development phase
- JIRA configuration for client studios: building or restructuring JIRA workspaces with game-dev-appropriate workflows, custom fields, boards, dashboards, and automation rules
- Sprint design for cross-discipline game teams: structuring sprints that account for the different cadences of art, design, engineering, audio, and QA -- this is specialised game-dev knowledge, not generic sprint management
- GDD and TDD standards for client studios: defining what sections are required, what level of detail is expected at each milestone gate, and how documents are kept current
- Milestone framework design for game development: defining gates (concept, pre-production, production, alpha, beta, cert, launch) with clear artefact requirements and sign-off criteria
- Production pipeline design: mapping and optimising asset, build, localisation, and certification pipelines for client studios
- Tutorial and training material creation: making client studios self-sufficient so they can sustain the methodology after NBI leaves
- Estimation and velocity coaching for game teams
- Greenlight and stage-gate support: helping studios prepare greenlight decks and navigate publisher processes

This role is the lead delivery agent for production consulting on Couch Heroes (fractional studio head work), Sarge Universe (if funding lands), and any other studio engagement where NBI is advising on how the studio runs itself.

The Production Consultant does not manage NBI's internal backlogs, run NBI's sprint ceremonies, or produce NBI's weekly status reports. Those belong to the Producer.

## Where They Overlap

Three scenarios create genuine overlap. Each has a defined resolution.

### Scenario 1: A client project has NBI internal workstreams and external production consulting work running simultaneously

Example: Couch Heroes. NBI has internal tasks (preparing deliverables, agent assignments, billing) and is also providing the studio with production methodology advice (JIRA setup, sprint design, GDD standards).

**Resolution:**
- The Producer owns the NBI-side backlog: the internal tasks that NBI's team needs to complete for the Couch Heroes engagement. This includes task ownership, due dates, status tracking, and escalation to the COO.
- The Production Consultant owns the client-facing deliverables: the JIRA workspace, the sprint design, the training materials, the production framework. These are outputs that go to the studio.
- The two roles are not competing. They are operating in different spaces within the same engagement. The Producer tracks that the Production Consultant's deliverables are on schedule. The Production Consultant does not need to track NBI's internal task health.

### Scenario 2: The Production Consultant needs internal NBI task support to deliver their consulting work

Example: The Production Consultant is building a JIRA configuration for Sarge Universe and needs a document reviewed, a meeting scheduled, or a template created by another NBI agent.

**Resolution:**
- The Production Consultant raises the internal task need to the Producer.
- The Producer picks it up, creates the task, assigns it, and tracks it like any other internal work.
- The Production Consultant does not manage internal NBI logistics directly. The Producer does not advise on JIRA configuration for the client.

### Scenario 3: Playsage or SalarySage internal product development needs production methodology input

Example: VP Product wants to redesign how Playsage sprints are structured. The Production Consultant's expertise in sprint design for cross-discipline teams is directly relevant.

**Resolution:**
- The Production Consultant can be brought in as an advisor to help design better sprint structures for NBI's own product development.
- Once the sprint structure is agreed, the Producer is responsible for running it: maintaining the backlog, facilitating ceremonies, and tracking progress.
- The Production Consultant advises on design. The Producer owns execution. This is a clean handoff, not an ongoing shared responsibility.

## Escalation

If there is any dispute about who owns a piece of work, or if both roles believe they own the same task, escalate to the COO immediately.

Do not resolve ownership conflicts between roles by negotiation between those roles. The COO makes the call. Both roles report to the COO via their respective reporting lines (Producer directly; Production Consultant via the Gaming Practice Lead).

Bring the following to the escalation:
- A clear description of the contested task or deliverable
- Why each role believes it falls within their remit
- Any time pressure or downstream impact if the decision is delayed

The COO will provide a ruling and, if necessary, update this document.
