# CH Art Leads Bypassed Production Chain -- Escalated Directly to CEO

## Key Content

Art leads went directly to Vardis (CEO) to complain that the Vertical Slice scope is "too big/scary," bypassing the production chain (Glen as EP, Valeria as producer). This is a classic chain-of-command bypass that undermines production authority. Glen is meeting with the art leads directly to address it.

Counterbalancing signal: Alon (art lead) is showing improved morale -- less frustrated, acknowledging progress. This suggests the escalation may be driven by a subset of art leadership rather than universal dissatisfaction.

## Decisions / Insights

- **Bypass pattern:** when leads feel unheard by production, they escalate to the founder/CEO. This is common in founder-led studios where the CEO has historical relationships with department heads that predate the production structure
- **Glen's response is correct:** meet directly, don't punish the escalation but redirect the channel. If he routes it back through Valeria without the face-to-face, the art leads will feel dismissed twice
- **"Too big/scary" is a scope confidence issue,** not necessarily a scope problem. The estimation work (art team already completed theirs) should provide the data to either validate or refute the concern
- **Alon's improving morale** is a leading indicator that the production structure is working -- the bypass may be a lagging behaviour from before things improved

## Context

This is a common pattern in studios transitioning from flat/founder-led to structured production. Department leads who previously had direct CEO access resist the new layer. Glen needs to validate production authority without alienating the art team.

## Applicability

- Client pattern: founder-bypass escalation is predictable when introducing production structure to previously flat studios. Worth documenting as a standard risk in production consultancy engagements
- Personal insight: Glen's instinct to meet directly rather than enforce hierarchy through intermediaries is the right call at this maturity stage. Enforcement comes later once trust is established
