# CH Contract Templates: Glen Rejected Over-Prescriptive Structure from Dino

## Key Content

Dino (General Counsel) created contract templates for incoming hires (VFX lead and lead engineering roles) that were too prescriptive -- specifying milestones, deadlines, and fees per milestone. Glen kicked the templates back, directing that contracts need to be "more general and flexible" rather than locking in specific deliverables and timelines at the contract stage.

Separately, Daniel Casadevall's offer is progressing -- scheduling call for Tuesday, with Daniel travelling with kids on Monday.

## Decisions / Insights

- **Glen's directive: consulting/contractor contracts should not embed project-level specificity.** Milestones and deadlines belong in project plans, not employment/engagement contracts. Over-specifying creates legal exposure when (not if) timelines shift
- **Dino's instinct as GC is to de-risk through specificity** -- this is textbook legal thinking but incompatible with game development reality where scope and timelines are inherently uncertain during pre-production
- **Pattern:** legal counsel in game studios consistently over-constrains contracts because they apply standard commercial contract logic to creative/iterative work. The EP needs to push back on this every time
- **Two senior hires in pipeline** (VFX lead, lead engineering) -- contract templates need to be right before these close

## Context

This is a recurring tension in studios with formal legal counsel. Dino is doing his job correctly from a legal risk perspective, but game development contracts need flexibility clauses rather than rigid milestone structures. Glen's pushback establishes the precedent for all future CH contracts.

## Applicability

- Client pattern: GC over-specification of creative contracts is a repeatable friction point. Worth having a standard "creative engagement contract" template that balances legal protection with delivery flexibility
- CH-specific: the template outcome will set the standard for all future CH hires, so getting it right now has compounding value
