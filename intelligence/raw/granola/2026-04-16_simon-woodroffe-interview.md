# Interview — Simon Woodroffe, Head of Design

## Candidate Profile
- 30+ years in games. Early work on Simon the Sorcerer, Call of Cthulhu: Dark Corners of the Earth.
- Career: Midway, Ubisoft, Sega, Rare, Improbable, Epic.
- Key achievement: co-designed Sea of Thieves at Rare (8 years). Built incubation function and recipe for emergent social gameplay.
- Currently at Epic working on Disney project with spatial audio and avatar systems.

## Design Philosophy — Exceptionally Strong Alignment
- Core principle: "Does this produce meaningful shared moments?" vs individual quality.
- Social games are 8x more sticky due to friend connections.
- Players choose games based on "good time with friends" over traditional quality metrics.
- Spatial voice chat foundational to social experiences — built custom solutions 4 times.
- Recipe analogy: games need the right 5 features, not the 10 best features.
- Prototype early and often vs massive design documents.
- Vision must cascade through entire team for independent decision-making.

## Sea of Thieves Insights
- Original concept: players creating stories together, not piracy focus.
- Had to remove crew betrayal feature — fun in office, toxic online.
- Anchor handbrake turn: 0.5 days coding, 99.5 days art polish.

## CH Vision Alignment
- 3-5M players treating game as primary social space.
- Cultural emergence: guilds, weddings, player-generated content.
- Portal system connecting to other games.
- Fan behaviour: tattoos, fan fiction, character art, AI builders.

## Studio Assessment Shared
- Glen told Simon studio was in prototype, not mid-production as believed internally.
- Head of Design role critical for vision clarity and feature filtering.

## Risks
- Highly opinionated, unlikely to execute vague directives without pushback.
- May have low tolerance for weak strategy or unclear leadership.

## Intelligence Value
Strongest design leadership candidate seen. Sea of Thieves pedigree directly applicable to CH's social MMO vision. The "recipe" philosophy and social-first design thinking are exactly what the studio lacks. Currently at Epic — availability and comp expectations are key unknowns.
