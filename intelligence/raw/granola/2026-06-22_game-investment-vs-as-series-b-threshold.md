# Game Investment Market State: Vertical Slice = Effective Series B Threshold

## Key Content
Game investment has dropped sharply in the past year. The market has effectively shifted the VS from a Series A proof point to a Series B prerequisite.

**Source:** Riley Graebner, Magna Capital partner, 2026-06-22.

**Current market state:**
- No investor is funding game development without a vertical slice as of mid-2026
- Vertical slice is now the de facto gate for gaming-specific VC (previously it was a Series A deliverable)
- Riley's framing: "vertical slice = Series B" — studios raising their first meaningful institutional round must have one

**Real funding sources for pre-VS studios:**
1. Grants (e.g. UK Games Fund up to £100k)
2. Publisher advances or rev-share arrangements
3. Founder capital / family offices
- VC and gaming funds are not a realistic source at pre-VS stage in the current environment

**Strategy for a studio without a VS:**
- Rev share to vertical slice → grants → self-publish and reinvest
- The VS is the unlock for all other capital sources

**AI and market convergence note:** democratisation of AI tools is making game dev cheaper but monetisation harder as models converge. This doesn't change the VS threshold — it likely increases investor scrutiny, not reduces it.

## Decisions / Insights
- Riley Graebner observed: game investment is down sharply YoY; no institutional fund is writing cheques without a VS
- Glen agreed: grants and publisher dollars are the realistic path for pre-VS studios
- Pattern recognised: studios approaching investors pre-VS are 1-2 years early for the current market; the advice is to build the VS first, not pitch earlier
