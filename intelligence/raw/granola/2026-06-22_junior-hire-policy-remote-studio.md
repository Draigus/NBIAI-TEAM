# Junior Hire Policy for Remote Studios

## Key Content
Structured onboarding policy for junior hires in a fully remote game studio, written into the studio's hiring framework:

**Required support structure per junior hire:**
- Dedicated training hours per week (formal, scheduled)
- Separate mentoring hours per week (1:1 with a senior)
- Senior buddy for osmosis and ad hoc questions (always-available informal support)
- Director check-ins every 3 weeks (structured progress review)

**Design rationale:** a remote studio cannot rely on ambient learning (overhearing conversations, watching seniors work, being in the same physical space). All of the learning pathways that happen organically in an office must be engineered deliberately.

**Core hiring philosophy:** hire veterans who can do three things, not one. Veterans justify the senior overhead; juniors need structural support to develop; a team of juniors with one veteran creates a bottleneck.

**Target team shape (level design example):** ~3 veterans, 2 mids, 1 junior. A team of 6 juniors bogs down the one veteran; the support policy only works at low junior density.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen wrote the junior hire policy into the studio's hiring framework as non-negotiable for remote studios
- Glen decided: dedicated training + separate mentoring hours + senior buddy + director check-ins every 3 weeks is the minimum viable support structure
- Pattern recognised: remote studios that hire juniors without structured support create retention problems and slow senior output
- Glen observed: "remote studio makes all of this non-negotiable" — the structure that offices provide for free must be designed in
