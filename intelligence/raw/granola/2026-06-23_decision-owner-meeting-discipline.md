# Decision Owner Model for Studio Meeting Discipline

## Key Content

Without named decision owners per pipeline stage, meetings expand to include everyone and collapse into non-decisions. A 12-person, 45-minute meeting (9 person-hours) to review a level layout that produced no decisions is the failure pattern.

**Root cause:** no one knows who is authorised to decide, so everyone attends to protect their position and no one commits.

**Fix:**
1. Assign a named decision owner for each process/pipeline stage (e.g. level design handoff: Level Design Director; character pipeline: Art Director)
2. Meeting attendee list = decision makers for that pipeline stage only; observers not invited
3. Every meeting requires: named objective, explicit agenda, clear owner of the outcome
4. PVP, scope, and unresolved design questions must not be introduced into a meeting whose pipeline stage hasn't confirmed them -- attendees from unrelated stages cannot derail scope

**Starting point:** map the handoff pipeline first (location review → level design → environment art → characters → other workstreams), assign owners at each handoff, and set attendee lists from that map.

**Extend to all cross-department pipelines** using the same model once the first pipeline is clean.
