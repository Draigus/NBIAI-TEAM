# Live Service vs Box Game Mindset Gap in Studio Leadership

## Key Content

A critical strategic misalignment can exist between studio heads of development and the game's intended commercial model. The failure pattern: leadership builds and plans as if shipping a contained product, while the game requires live service architecture from day one.

The signal: head of development has perfected the base product but has no plan for post-launch content, live operations, or systems that sustain a player economy. "Perfecting the mains with no dessert menu and no front-of-house."

**Consequences if unaddressed:**
- Only the final 3 months before ship available to build live service infrastructure -- insufficient
- Base game locks in without a cutoff date for new systems, so the product keeps expanding
- Publishers or investors probing live service readiness (e.g. post-Alpha check-in) will surface the gap publicly

**Fix:**
- Establish a hard cutoff date: no new systems added to base game after that point
- Everything unfinished by cutoff ships as live content post-launch, not in the base product
- Align head of development explicitly on the two-phase model (base + live), preferably before hiring

**Tencent context:** publishers with live service ambitions will probe this gap; it becomes their leverage point.

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen observed: the head of development was building a box game mentally while the game is designed as a live service
- Glen decided: raise a hard cutoff date for base game systems with the development lead to force alignment
- Pattern recognised: six months of "start-stop" cycles often trace back to this strategic misalignment, not team execution
- Glen concluded: this must be surfaced and addressed before producers are in place, not after

## Context

Production meeting between Glen, Valeria (Head of Production), and Graham (g.monk) at a ~65-person MMO studio in pre-production, June 2026. Studio in vertical slice phase targeting September milestone. Meeting followed a 1:1 that revealed the gap.
