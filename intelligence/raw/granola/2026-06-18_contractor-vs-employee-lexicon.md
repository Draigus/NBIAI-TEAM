# Contractor vs Employee Termination: Lexicon Confusion Creates Operational and Legal Risk

## Key Content
Studios managing a mix of employees and contractors frequently apply "terminate" to both groups. This is a category error with operational and legal implications: employees are terminated (statutory protections, notice periods, employment law obligations apply); contractors have their contracts ended (governed by contract terms only, not employment law).

Consequences of the confusion: HoDs believe contractors have the same protections as employees, creating hesitation to end underperforming contracts or unnecessary process overhead. Using "terminate" language for contractors can also imply employment status in a dispute context.

**UK probation law note (current as of June 2026):** Unfair dismissal does not apply during probation under current law. From 1 January 2027 onward, unfair dismissal rights begin after 6 months of service (reduced from the previous 2-year threshold). Probation terminations before that date carry negligible unfair dismissal risk if the paper trail is clean.

Simple correction pattern agreed: one nominated person flags incorrect "terminate/end contract" language every time it arises in HR discussions. Low-overhead, consistent reinforcement.
