# Jira + Perforce Rollout Sequencing for Mid-Size Studio

## Key Content
Transition from ClickUp + GitHub to Jira + Perforce at a ~70-person studio. Rationale: lower overhead for new hires, industry-standard integrations. Jira not opened to the wider team immediately -- held until structure and controls are in place.

Five-step rollout sequence:
1. Load Jira (~3 weeks from rollout decision)
2. Art and tech producers onboard around the same time
3. ~1 week to stabilise Jira with a Jira admin hire
4. ~3 weeks debugging pipelines via sprint retrospectives
5. Flywheel starts ~6-7 weeks from rollout decision

Two Jira admin candidates in consideration at decision point. Estimation exercise completed first to identify who can and cannot estimate reliably -- this feeds Jira with credible data from day one.

Lower overhead rationale: industry-standard tools reduce onboarding friction for new hires already familiar with Jira/Perforce from previous studios.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen decided: switch from ClickUp/GitHub to Jira/Perforce; lower overhead for new hire onboarding is the primary driver.
- Glen decided: do not open Jira until controls and Jira admin structure are ready; premature access without structure creates noise.
- Glen observed: estimation exercise must precede Jira rollout to seed the system with reliable data.
- Pattern: 6-7 week total time from rollout decision to functioning sprint flywheel, assuming two producers onboard concurrently.

## Context
1:1 with a studio COO, 2026-06-19. Studio previously used ClickUp and GitHub; transitioning to industry-standard stack as studio grows toward vertical slice. Two producers (art and tech) joining around the same time as Jira rollout.
