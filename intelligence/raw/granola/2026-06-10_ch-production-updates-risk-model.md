# Production Risk Scoring Model and Headcount Efficiency Analysis

## Key Content

Production sync with Valeria and Graeme on estimation methodology. Graham's risk model: two inputs — duration buckets (day / days / week / 1–2 weeks / 2+ weeks) and complexity variance (delta between min and max estimate). Contingency by tier: 5% minor, 10% low, up to 25% extreme. Baseline assumption: 65% efficiency, full concurrency, no overlaps. Current output: ~18 months across all work at max estimates. Glen's addition: dependency chain depth should also raise complexity tier (a UI feature blocked by an unbuilt system carries higher risk than a standalone task). Key insight: task-level breakdown justified ONLY where it unlocks parallel work — environment kits are the primary example (~1,900 man-days identified). The goal is not granular breakdown of everything; it's exposing tent poles. Headcount efficiency approach at 55 people: divide work by role, overlay actual headcount per role, go back to leads with the math ("you have 5 environment artists, why is this 2,000 hours not 400?"). Outliers at <12% utilisation become 1:1 conversations. At 75+ people this per-named-individual approach stops being feasible.

## Decisions / Insights

- [Glen/Graham] decided: dependency chain depth is a valid complexity-tier modifier, add to risk model
- [Glen/Graham/Valeria] decided: task-level breakdown only for long tent poles, not entire studio scope
- [Glen] concluded: 55-person window is the only time per-individual efficiency analysis is tractable
- [Glen] observed: some staff at <20% of contracted hours — this is a leads accountability issue, not a studio ops issue
- [Glen] decided: outsourcing options (Multiplayer Guys, Roommate) to be explored for longest tent poles

## Context

Production update meeting, 10 June 2026. Glen Pryer, Valeria Trofimova (Head of Production), Graeme Monk (Executive Producer). Part of vertical slice planning process.
