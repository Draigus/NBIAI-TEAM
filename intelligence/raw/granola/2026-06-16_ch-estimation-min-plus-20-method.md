# Re-Estimation Methodology: Min+20% as Baseline

## Key Content

Design Estimations Discussion, 16 June 2026. Robin (Game Director), Glen, Gary (Environment Art Lead) reviewed the quality of the current estimate spreadsheet and agreed a corrective methodology.

**Root cause of inflated estimates:**
- All current figures use maximum estimates, not averages or medians
- Wide min/max gaps (e.g. 1 day to 20 days, 2 days to 400 days) show estimators had no real basis
- Junior team members padded heavily — some entries described as "apocalypse-scenario" level
- Game director role calculated at 16 months; game designer at 14.7 months (Robin's actual assessment: 3–4 months)
- Estimators guessed at unfamiliar systems; min/max framing was under-explained; people went to emotional extremes
- Some roles (e.g. technical designer) barely assigned tasks across 100+ line items

**Corrective methodology — Min+20%:**
1. Recalculate all estimates as: min estimate + 20%
2. Present this figure to the team as the starting point
3. Ask team members to challenge (justify why it should be higher), not defend a pre-inflated number
4. Forces upward justification rather than natural padding

**Diagnostic signal:** A wide gap (e.g. 2 days min, 400 days max) flags who does not understand the task — a gap that large means the estimator was guessing, not estimating.

**Session structure for re-estimation:** Role by role, per discipline, not in one general call. Mix of senior leads and juniors in the same session creates groupthink pressure. Robin, Valeria, and Graham to attend the mixed seniority session (Charlie/Gary + juniors) specifically to observe team dynamics.

**Longer-term:** Graham's goal is to re-educate the team on agile estimation practices over 6–8 months.

## Decisions / Insights

- [Glen/Robin/Gary] agreed: min+20% replaces max figures as the baseline for all re-estimation
- [Glen] proposed: 30 hours to 80% completion as a reasonable planning bar for design docs; Robin agreed

- [Glen/Robin] agreed: separate estimation sessions per discipline reduces groupthink and surfacing confusion from other disciplines

## Applicability

Relevant when: inheriting a studio's existing estimate spreadsheet — max-figure inflation is a near-universal problem; min+20% is a mechanically deployable corrective.
Relevant when: running estimation sessions with mixed seniority — separate per-discipline sessions prevent junior team members from anchoring to senior leads' higher (or lower) numbers.
Relevant when: a game director disputes the spreadsheet total — a gap between spreadsheet and GD's gut feel of 3–4x signals systematic max-padding, not GD overconfidence.
Relevant when: building a planning bar for design documentation — 30 hours to 80% completion (with AI-assisted layout) is a validated benchmark from an experienced game director.
Relevant when: identifying team members who don't understand their tasks — a min/max gap of 200x is a cleaner signal than any other diagnostic.
