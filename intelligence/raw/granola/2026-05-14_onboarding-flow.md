# Onboarding Flow Design -- Department Tooling, Probation, and Process Gaps

## Key Content

Comprehensive onboarding framework designed across all departments. General requirements: Slack, Google Workspace, Jira, Confluence, VPN, Hi Bob (replacing ClickUp). Role-specific machine builds defined: artist kit vs producer kit vs developer kit, hardware for FTEs only. Development needs: GitHub, Perforce, Azure, Redis. Art: Perforce, Maya, 3D Max, Photoshop, Miro. Audio: DAW, Sound Forge, F-mod -- no asset management system exists (critical gap). QA: Azure, OBS, Snag-it, Team City for automated testing (critical gap -- no test automation engine). Production additions: Claude accounts, Whisper Flow, Granola paid accounts for all producers/directors. Four-month probation with structured 30/60/90-day check-ins using templates. First week non-negotiables: 1:1 with direct manager, department training, team introductions on day one. Missing critical elements: role expectations for all positions, company culture session content, asset management for audio/art, automated test engine.

## Decisions / Insights

- [Glen] decided: four-month probation with template-based 30/60/90-day reviews across all roles
- [Team] decided: hardware provisioning for FTEs only, contractors bring own equipment
- [Glen] observed: QA has no test automation engine -- critical infrastructure gap
- [Glen] observed: audio department has no asset management system -- flagged as needed
- [David] decided: Art Bible walkthrough (30-minute call) mandatory for all art onboardees

## Context

Onboarding Flow meeting on 14 May 2026. Participants: Glen, Robin Jubber, David Luong, Mustafa Sibai, Valeria Trofimova. Designing the complete onboarding process ahead of the June hiring wave (Simon, Charlie, Michel starting 1 June).
