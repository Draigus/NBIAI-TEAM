# CH Production Meeting: Estimation Review, Environment Art Red Flag, Vertical Slice Scope

## Key Content

Valeria's spreadsheet breaks down all epics, features, and stories by role. Graham added risk analysis and rough effort sizing toward the vertical slice. Pivot table shows gross man-days by role from the resource planner. Directors set at 60% efficiency; rest of staff at 90%.

**Environment art red flag:** I53 shows 4,622 days of environment art effort for three zones (new zone, downtime zone, portal peak area). Average ~385 days per zone after three-zone split. Michael's estimates appear to double-count: environment kit creation AND zone dressing calculated as if no kit existed. Even halved, still massively heavy. WoW benchmark: 12 zones in 18 months with a team of six. Glen's verdict: environment art sandbagging at ~300%, possibly double-counting on top.

**Outsource plan:** Get an outsourced company to bid the full environment art workload as a baseline. Even doubling the outsourcer's estimate likely won't reach Michael's numbers. Gives an objective, argument-proof reference before confronting Michael directly.

**Other concerns:** Animation: 14 months estimated for ~22 characters with two junior animators (Cjal, Alon). Fix: add a senior animator and a hands-on lead (70% animating, 30% leading). UI/UX: Luca spending 80% of time as concept artist, only 20% on UX.

**Vertical slice reality:** Current VS (Robin's definition, Vardy-approved) is roughly a third of the full game. Next conversation: cut scope to a 4–6 month deliverable. Build cadence: Glen requested a daily build from Mustafa, one sprint behind, always active.

## Decisions / Insights

- [Glen] decided: get an outsourced environment art bid as a baseline before confronting Michael — objective, argument-proof reference point avoids subjective stand-off
- [Glen] observed: 4,622 days for three zones is sandbagging at ~300%; no AAA title in 27 he's shipped has taken a full year to art one zone
- [Valeria] proposed: estimation process order — share total accumulated time per team → leads distribute to staff → long conversation with each team before any changes
- [Glen] decided: he wants to be present in all team estimation review calls to observe leads leading, and has six months of context to draw on
- [Glen] decided: vertical slice must be cut from ~1/3 of the full game to a 4–6 month deliverable once estimates are cleaned up
- [Glen] committed: daily build mandate — always available, one sprint behind, every employee able to jump in at any time

## Context

Couch Heroes production planning session, 15 June 2026, with Glen Pryer, Valeria (Head of Production), and Graham (incoming production consultant/advisor). Meeting followed Graham completing two weeks in observation mode. Simon Woodroffe (Head of Design, first day) was also briefly discussed.

## Applicability

Relevant when: a studio's internal estimates for an asset type (environment art, animation) need pressure-testing before a confrontation with the lead responsible.
Relevant when: planning how to use outsourcer bids as objective benchmarks to neutralise sandbagging arguments.
