# Quad Assessment: Staff Segmentation for Quality-Gated Production

## Key Content
The "quad assessment" is a structured staff evaluation exercise used when a studio needs to identify who can and cannot deliver a specific product to a required quality standard. The core question is not "is this person a good employee?" but: "Can this person deliver high-quality [product type] content in their craft, at speed, right now?"

Two criteria for staying:
1. Good to work with, self-managing, team-positive
2. Delivers product-level quality consistently and at pace required

The exercise is run with leads and directors, not management alone. Each lead presents their team; they are pressed to give an honest answer against the two criteria, not a protective one. Saves carry personal accountability: the director who saves someone owns that person's growth or replacement if performance doesn't materialise.

Result tiers: hard cuts (red triangle -- first priority), stars (director saves with accountability), juniors (flagged J -- separate consideration), unmarked (exits but lower urgency than triangle tier). Assessment must be ratified by the full director group, not decided unilaterally.

Not about potential or trajectory ("can they get there in 12 months") -- need delivery capacity now.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen decided: saves must carry personal accountability from the director who made them -- "you own their growth or their replacement."
- Glen observed: leads who default to saving everyone required direct pressure and a cap ("you get three picks") to produce an honest assessment.
- Glen decided: assessment must be ratified by all directors so no individual can walk it back later.
- Pattern recognised: directing the question at craft delivery for the specific product type (MMO, mobile, etc.) eliminates ambiguity about the performance bar.

## Context
1:1 with Game Director, 2026-06-19. Exercise completed the prior day across all departments at a ~70-person MMO studio in vertical slice preparation. Result: ~23 people flagged across departments.

## Applicability
Relevant when: a studio needs to assess whether its current team can deliver a specific product to a specific quality bar, rather than just assessing performance in the abstract.
Relevant when: department heads are protecting underperformers and a structured accountability mechanism is needed to get honest input.
Relevant when: a studio is entering a high-stakes production phase (vertical slice, publisher milestone) and needs to know which roles have delivery risk.
Relevant when: advising on how to make director-level saves credible -- accountability assignment prevents save inflation.
