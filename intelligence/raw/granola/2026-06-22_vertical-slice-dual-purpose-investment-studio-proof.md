# Vertical Slice Dual Purpose: Investor Proof and Studio Belief

## Key Content
Vertical slice serves two purposes beyond the game itself, both of which must be designed into how the VS is built and delivered:

**Purpose 1 — Investor proof of concept:**
- Something to show existing and external investors within 2-3 months
- Demonstrates the team can build, iterate, and ship a coherent experience
- Side-by-side proxy vs. polished comparison is the narrative device for showing velocity

**Purpose 2 — Studio proof it can build a game:**
- Many studios in their first large-scale project have never shipped a full production cycle
- The VS is the first time the full studio completes an estimate, builds to it, and ships a result
- Emotional proof to the studio: "we can build a game" — this is not a soft benefit; it directly affects morale and retention across the build cycle
- Four years of start-stop cycles is a solved problem if the VS delivers a complete cycle

**Production rhythm taught by the VS:**
- Teaches the studio to estimate, scope, load, and ship in repeatable cycles
- Each sprint should produce a meaningfully different build (monthly cadence cited)
- Backlog out-of-scope items as top priority for VS2: proves the production cycle, not just the content

**Leadership position:** the mandate is binary — "build up or shut up." Team members not delivering to the production cadence are not a fit for the studio at this scale.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen and CEO aligned: the VS teaches the production muscle that the rest of the build depends on
- Glen observed: without a production rhythm, sprints slip without consequence — treated as scheduling exercises rather than commitments
- CEO decided: team members who aren't delivering need to go; the industry has good talent available
- Pattern recognised: studios that skip the VS often skip the production curriculum, producing a team that can code and design but cannot ship on a timeline

## Context
Production planning meeting between Glen, the studio CEO, and Head of Production at a ~70-person MMO studio, 2026-06-22. Studio has completed years of development without a full production cycle; vertical slice is the first structured delivery milestone.
