# Couch Heroes: Studio Operations and Process Maturity Assessment

## Key Content

Studio has run four years without formal feedback loops; bad habits are embedded. Miro vs. Excel incident: art team moved to Miro because David wanted it, not because the team needed it; should have stayed in Excel and caused unnecessary pain. Concept of "sacred cows": nothing is truly off-limits, but healthy critique needs an established structure before it can land.

Parallelising work requires guidance; guidance is the current bottleneck. Evidence: AI module committed to main branch without authorisation; an unauthorised Tripo subscription taken out by a team member. Blind parallelisation without guidance capacity does not work. Fix: add senior leadership (Graham, Simon, a CTO) so more workstreams can run safely in parallel.

Graham at day 8: instructed to look, learn, listen before pulling triggers. Eager to add retros and sprint cadence but Jira is not yet set up. Glen's framing to Graham: "you're talking about putting the tires on and we don't have a carburetor." Direction and instincts are good; notes need to get more specific to be actionable.

Simon Woodruff (Head of Design, creator of Simon the Sorcerer) starts Monday. Robin shifts to game director role; Simon covers design; Glen bridges both toward product.

Animation headcount gap: first time Glen heard animation headcount was insufficient was in this 1:1. HoDs have been conservative. Glen pushing them to state what they truly need against the vertical slice.

## Decisions / Insights

- [Glen] observed: four years without formal feedback loops has embedded bad habits that cannot be fixed by tooling alone
- [Glen] concluded: parallelising workstreams without guidance capacity leads to rogue commits and unauthorised subscriptions
- [Glen] instructed: HoDs must stop being conservative on headcount and report genuine needs against the vertical slice
- [Glen] assessed: Graham's direction is sound but feedback must get more specific to be actionable for the team

1:1 between Glen Pryer and Alon Helman (Couch Heroes Animation Lead), 12 June 2026 at 1pm. Part of Glen's regular leadership 1:1 cycle at the studio.
