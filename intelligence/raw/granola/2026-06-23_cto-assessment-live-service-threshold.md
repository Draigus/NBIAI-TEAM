# CTO Assessment Criteria: Live Service Experience as Threshold Requirement

## Key Content

For a studio building a persistent world or live service game, live service / MMO experience in the CTO role is a threshold requirement, not a preference. A CTO with an extensive single-player portfolio carries a specific structural risk: they optimise for a shipped box product, not for a game that runs and evolves for years post-launch.

**Glen's assessment framework for CTO candidates at live service studios:**

- **Live service / MMO credits required** at the lead or principal level -- not just awareness. Absence is disqualifying unless other signals compensate
- **How was the CTO role earned?** Roles filled by vacancy (stepping in because no one else was there) carry lower confidence than roles earned upward through technical leadership
- **Investor optics of the candidate** matter -- a highly charismatic CTO can support fundraising, but charisma without live service experience is a mismatch for the actual role requirements
- **Culture fit with founders** -- a technically accomplished CTO from a blunt, high-pressure engineering culture may perform well under pressure but need adjustment to a more relationship-driven founding team
- **Salary expectations calibrated to background** -- candidates from large AAA engineering organisations often have compensation expectations that don't land with Series A-stage studios

**Positive signal in interviews:** a candidate who pushes back on clearly bad ideas (Web3 hype, poor architecture choices) in an interview is demonstrating executive independence. This outweighs polish.

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen decided: single-player credits only = disqualifying risk for CTO of a live service MMO
- Glen observed: the most charismatic and investor-friendly candidate may not have the right experience for the actual role
- Glen concluded: how a CTO role was earned (vacancy vs. earned upward) is a meaningful signal about executive capability
- Pattern recognised: calibrating salary expectations to studio stage is a distinct assessment step -- large-company background correlates with unworkable expectations at Series A

## Context

CTO selection process at a ~65-person MMO studio in pre-production, June 2026. Three candidates in process; assessment revealed a fundamental split between technical credibility, investor optics, and live service experience. Glen's framework used to navigate the tension.
