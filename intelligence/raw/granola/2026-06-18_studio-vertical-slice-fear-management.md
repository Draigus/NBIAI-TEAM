# Managing Studio Fear When the Vertical Slice Reveals Full Scope

## Key Content
When a studio sees its vertical slice for the first time and full scope becomes visible to the broader team, a predictable fear response triggers scope-cut proposals from individual contributors and mid-level leads. This is normal and expected, but must be managed structurally rather than allowed to drive reactive scope decisions.

Pattern observed: staff push scope cuts from fear, not from strategic analysis. Initial VS estimate at this studio: ~2 years. Compressed to 10 months. Target: ~5 months. Fear increases as the compression gap is visible.

**Response framework that worked:**
1. Separate idea from action: all scope-cut suggestions go into a designated document for future review at the right stage. No immediate decisions. Leaders redirect in the moment: "good idea, put it in the document."
2. Clarify scope ownership explicitly: scope decisions belong to a named core group only. Individual contributors and mid-level leads are consulted, not accountable for scope.
3. Acknowledge the fear openly: if staff express concern, they are flagged to leadership for direct support -- not left to simmer.
4. Studio head pre-empts: studio head meets directly with the most affected department to reframe scope decisions as not theirs to make.
