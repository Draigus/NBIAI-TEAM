# Art Bible as Mandatory Source of Truth for Creative Direction Changes

## Key Content

Small creative direction changes communicated informally (via Slack, corridor conversations, or 1:1s) cause studio-wide confusion when they reach people outside the original conversation. The failure case: an art direction shift was surfaced to the studio lead during an unrelated call -- the lead had heard it secondhand from a colleague, and believed the full creative direction was being abandoned.

**Root issue:** no central source of truth. Creative changes live in the head of the Art Director and surface unpredictably.

**Solution: Art Bible as the mandatory update point**
- Art Director owns the Art Bible; Creative Director provides sign-off
- Every time any art direction is finalised or iterated -- even small environmental style changes -- the Art Bible must be updated before or simultaneously with the decision being communicated
- Style branches (e.g. specific area or environment type directions) exist as named branches within the Art Bible, not as separate documents or Slack decisions
- The Art Bible update is the official communication: once it's in the Bible, the studio knows it's real

**Extension:** a Lore Bible following the same discipline for narrative direction changes.

**Failure cost:** hours spent chasing clarification, demotivated art team unsure of direction, creative leads overriding work that was already correct.

Executive meeting at a ~65-person MMO studio, June 2026. An art direction shift (downtown area moving from wood-heavy to gridcore/metal aesthetic) surfaced informally and caused studio-wide confusion about whether the full fantasy direction was being abandoned. Post-incident review identified the absence of a central source of truth as the structural cause.
