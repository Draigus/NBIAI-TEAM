# Couch Heroes: MMO Positioning and Core Design Pillars (June 2026 Articulation)

## Key Content

Definitive articulation of Couch Heroes MMO positioning and design pillars, stated by Glen in a technical leadership interview context on 2026-06-24:

**Positioning:**
- Scale: between Palia (smaller, younger, casual) and vanilla WoW (too large, too demanding). Not a mass-market MMO; not a niche one.
- Systems-heavy, design-led, highly social. Not a "hostage game": players are not punished for low playtime.
- Cross-game entitlement (portals, in-world game machines) is a secondary feature, not the core product -- this was a recurring misconception that needed correcting.

**Core social mechanics:**
- Guild benefits accrue whether members are online or not -- the social contract doesn't punish real-world absence.
- Zone scale: 200 players per zone, up to 100 in open-world combat.
- Combat: tab to localise target, then aim a ground-placed AoE -- hybrid between pure tab-targeting and full action combat.

**Production state (as of June 2026):**
- Four years of pre-production before Glen joined; now entering early production (~5-6 months into advisory/fractional C-level role).
- Working toward a "proof of game": a large vertical slice containing one of every system type.
- Engineering writing TDDs on core systems to flesh out GDD and cut rework before production ramps.

**Funding:**
- Backed by a non-games funding source (~$13-14B fund, described as highly invested). Funding is not a current constraint.

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen observed: the cross-game hub framing was a persistent misconception; correcting it is important in every external conversation about CH.
- Glen stated: "not a hostage game" is a deliberate design decision -- guild benefits accruing offline is a structural feature, not a monetisation afterthought.
