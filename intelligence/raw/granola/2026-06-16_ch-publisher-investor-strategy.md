# CH Publisher Strategy and Blue-Chip Investor Model

## Key Content

Org Discussion between Glen and Aris (COO), 16 June 2026. Firm articulation of CH's publisher and investor strategy.

**Self-publishing position (decided):**
CH will not take a publisher. Vardis's goal is to build and keep the studio, not sell it — this eliminates all publisher leverage. Publishers are structurally predatory for a studio with this goal: milestone gates, 3x–7x ironclad payback, no capital left to build the next game. EA example: publishing teams are incentivised to undervalue studios for cheap acquisition.

The one genuine publisher advantage: platform landing pages. Xbox front page placement generates approximately 200x downloads vs. organic. CH's model does not depend on a launch spike — it needs a sticky, long-term playerbase — so this advantage does not override the structural cost.

**Investor profile (decided):**
Traditional gaming VC funds are flippers (exit-focused); wrong fit for CH's model. CH needs blue-chip investors who want dividend yield, not an exit event. Pitch framing: Nvidia-style macro bet, not a flip.

**Sub-studio model (floated, not decided):**
Build subsidiary studios on new IPs, sell the sub-studio, distribute proceeds as dividends. CH investors inherit a dividend uplift without the core studio being sold. Risk: running multiple studios creates high cognitive load — Blizzard North nearly destroyed Blizzard. Fundraise planning now a priority with Rania joining; deliverables needed: investor profile with named targets, pitch deck, data room.

## Decisions / Insights

- [Vardis/Glen/Aris] decided: no publisher — self-publish only; removes publisher leverage and protects studio ownership
- [Glen] articulated: publishers are structurally predatory for long-term studio-building goals (milestone gates + ironclad 3–7x payback + no capital for next game)
- [Glen] identified: Xbox front page = ~200x downloads — the one hard publisher advantage; CH's retention model is not launch-spike dependent, so this advantage doesn't change the calculus
- [Glen/Aris] agreed: investor profile should target dividend yield seekers, not flippers; Nvidia macro-bet framing preferred over exit-story framing
- [Glen] proposed: sub-studio model as dividend mechanism — subsidiary IPs sold, proceeds distributed to investors without core studio being touched
- [Glen] flagged: multi-studio risk is high (Blizzard North precedent); sub-studio model is a direction, not a decision
