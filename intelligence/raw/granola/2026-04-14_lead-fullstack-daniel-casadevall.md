# Interview — Daniel Casadevall, Lead Full Stack Developer

## Assessment: 5/10 Overall — Not a fit for this role

### Strengths
- Exceptional backend/multiplayer systems: redesigned Niantic's AR multiplayer server (1M peak users). Redis cluster to in-memory cache, UDP to WebSocket migration.
- Strong full stack web: React, JavaScript, serverless. 17 years web dev.
- Deep MMO appreciation: 20+ years starting with Final Fantasy XI. Understands progression, housing, collectibles.
- Barcelona location, proper work authorisation.

### Critical Gaps
- C++ proficiency: 4/10 (self-assessed 6/10). No recent production C++ experience.
- Unreal Engine: 2/10. Zero production Unreal experience (Unity background only).
- Leadership: 4/10. Explicitly stated "people management is not my favorite thing." Prefers IC/mentoring over formal leadership.

### Conclusion
Strong candidate for a backend-focused senior role but significant technology mismatch (C++/Unreal) and stated preference against formal leadership. Would need 3-6 months ramp-up. Studio needs immediate lead contribution.

## Intelligence Value
Adds to the hiring assessment library for CH engineering roles. Confirms the C++/Unreal requirement is a hard filter for lead-level gameplay engineering candidates.
