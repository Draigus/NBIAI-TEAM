# Scope Governance Discipline: No Cuts Before Full Estimate

## Key Content

Formal reaffirmation of CH's scope governance rule at the Directors/Leads weekly sync, 16 June 2026. The rule is non-negotiable:

**Sequence before any scope cut:**
1. Get real hours from all leads (full estimation complete)
2. Explore outsourcing and headcount options
3. Only then consider scope cuts

Ad hoc scope cuts are not acceptable at any stage before step 3. Anyone with a cut idea writes it up and sends to Glen or Robin — it does not surface in meetings or get acted on unilaterally.

**Vertical slice purpose (stated for new hires):** Three goals: (1) demonstrate depth and breadth of the game to the studio, (2) prove out pipelines, systems, and QA processes, (3) serve as investor material. These goals must be met before scope cut discussions are legitimate.

The estimation cycle in progress this week: returning to in-depth role-by-role sessions. Robin already ran a test session to validate the format. QA estimates still pending but approach is agreed.

- [Glen] decided: no scope cuts accepted until the full timeline estimate is complete — order is hours first, outsourcing/headcount second, cuts last
- [Glen/Robin] agreed: ad hoc cut ideas go in writing to Glen or Robin, not surfaced in group sessions
- [Glen] articulated: vertical slice serves three simultaneous purposes — studio demonstration, pipeline validation, and investor material — all three must remain intact
