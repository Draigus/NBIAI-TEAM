# Dino 1:1 -- EMI Options Ineligibility and Nia Compensation Strategy

## Key Content

Couch Heroes cannot provide EMI-eligible options due to connected persons holding >50% control. Vardis and related parties control majority through various holdings. Connected/associated persons definition triggers EMI exclusion under April 6, 2026 reforms. Triple-checked with tax advisor in August 2025, confirmed ineligible.

Only unapproved options available with significant tax burden. Employee pays income tax on exercise (~25%+ potentially more). Creates "benefit in kind" tax treatment. Current 3% offer effectively worth ~1.5% after tax. Total options pool: 22.5% allocated for entire studio. 15% would be substantial deduction from remaining pool.

Compensation strategy alternatives discussed. Launch bonus structure recommended over current options approach -- more aligned with 3+ year development timeline to early 2029. Revenue/profit sharing percentage as bonus alternative.

Nia's credentials justify premium compensation: 30 years industry experience, built systems for GTA 5, GTA 4, Red Dead Redemption, VP role at Unity. Paper credentials slightly ahead of Cam. Salary target: GBP 250k vs initial GBP 220k consideration. Ari's GBP 210k suggestion deemed insufficient. GBP 30k difference represents ~8 hours company burn vs expected value delivered. "Don't pass up a dollar to pick up a nickel."

Complex 20+ vehicle corporate structure creates EMI ineligibility. UK holding company with Cyprus-based corporate shareholders. Three subsidiaries: Greece, UK, Cyprus. Potential structural solutions: dual class/weighted voting rights to maintain control without economic majority. Tax advisor on retainer with full corporate picture available for fresh advice.
