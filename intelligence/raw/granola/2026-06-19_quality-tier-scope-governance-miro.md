# Quality Tier Mapping Per Department as Scope Governance

## Key Content
A Miro board was created mapping deliverable quality tiers per department, to be shared with leads and directors for sign-off. This serves as the primary scope governance document for the vertical slice: each department's expected output quality is made explicit and locked before work begins.

"Design lock" terminology introduced as a protective mechanism -- once scope is locked, rescoping attempts are handled through a formal process rather than ad hoc in meetings. Separately, the studio owner directly communicated to the art team to stop rescoping the game, providing top-down enforcement of the design lock.

Miro was also used to build a player promise and value creation framework (see separate extract), with pillars tying to player feelings and retention/revenue outcomes.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen decided: quality tier Miro board to be reviewed and signed off by all leads and directors before VS work begins.
- Pattern recognised: without explicit quality tier agreement per department, individual teams rescope independently and create conflicting expectations.
- Glen decided: design lock terminology is adopted as a formal boundary for VS scope; requests to cross it require explicit process.
- Studio owner explicitly told teams to stop rescoping -- top-down enforcement is necessary alongside the process tool.

## Context
G&G weekly catch-up, 2026-06-19. ~70-person MMO studio in VS planning. Scope drift from multiple departments was an active problem; this Miro board was created the same day.

## Applicability
Relevant when: advising a studio on how to prevent scope drift during pre-production or vertical slice phases.
Relevant when: multiple departments are independently reinterpreting scope and no single source of truth exists for quality expectations.
Relevant when: a studio owner or CEO is the primary source of scope changes and the production team needs a mechanism to manage that.
Relevant when: onboarding a new Head of Production who needs a structured handover of what "done" means per department.
