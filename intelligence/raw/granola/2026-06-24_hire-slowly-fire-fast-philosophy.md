# Glen's Hiring Philosophy: Hire Slowly, Fire Fast -- Toxicity Is an Existential Risk

## Key Content

Glen's stated hiring philosophy, articulated explicitly to a senior technical candidate on 2026-06-24:

- **Hire slowly, fire fast.** The sequence is deliberate: extended due diligence before committing, then decisiveness when the decision to exit becomes clear.
- The rationale: toxic individuals threaten the whole studio. One bad hire at a senior level can destabilise team culture across multiple squads.
- This applies with particular force to technical leadership roles -- a CTO or TD shapes engineering culture, onboarding standards, and conflict norms across the full team.
- Corollary: the studio uses a multi-conversation hiring process for senior roles (referenced as likely 3+ conversations before advancing for a CTO-level candidate).

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen stated explicitly: "hire slowly, fire fast" as the operating principle for all senior hires.
- Glen observed: fully remote studios require this discipline more acutely -- without physical colocation, cultural problems from a toxic hire are harder to detect and slower to resolve.

## Context

Stated by Glen in a CTO candidate interview on 2026-06-24, explaining the hiring process and culture at a ~55-person fully remote game studio where Glen serves in a fractional C-level advisory role. Articulated to a candidate being evaluated for a senior technical leadership role.

## Applicability

- Relevant when: advising a studio on senior hiring processes -- multiple conversations before advancing is a structural safeguard, not excessive caution.
- Relevant when: a client is considering a fast hire under pressure -- the hire slowly principle applies most strongly when urgency creates the temptation to cut due diligence.
- Relevant when: a studio has a toxic senior employee with strong technical output -- the "fire fast" side of the principle; output does not justify cultural damage.
- Relevant when: fully remote studios are scaling -- physical absence makes cultural contamination harder to observe, raising the stakes for each senior hire.
