# Vision Call — Studio Identity, Culture Values, Product Vision, and Mustafa Performance

## Key Content

Foundational vision session with Vardis, Aris, and Lorenza shaping Couch Heroes studio identity and culture. Studio framed as guild/collective (D&D analogy) not corporate hierarchy — "when you're wearing our tabard, this is what's expected of you." Core behavioural traits agreed: unit-first mentality, aggressive discipline, brotherhood through hardship, trust-based hierarchy, self-determination, craft connected to vision, pridefully humble, dark humour, firm but loving feedback. Crisis posture defined as "calm intensity" — fight collectively, not individually, never convict failures but fix them. Truth established as top-five cultural rule; lies treated as debt against trust with zero tolerance for malicious deception. Product vision centred on "belonging" as the studio's word (Blizzard owns "epic") — belonging TO something epic, with players feeling the game is home. Studio vision and product vision explicitly separated. Gamified employee progression system proposed: numbered entry, milestone rewards (signet ring/coin), scaling equity packages, "Couch Club" alumni access, titles evolving from Adventurer to Hero to Champion. Greek gaming ecosystem and headquarters identity flagged for future discussion. Appendix: Mustafa on final warning — performing at 50%, burnt out since stepping down from CTO, told interview candidate Nia he was burnt out (cost Glen an hour recovering the candidate), took vacation without informing anyone, repeat pattern from CTO step-down. Agreed accountability: regular 1:1s, direct pings to Glen when struggling, visibly different behaviour or studio parts ways.
