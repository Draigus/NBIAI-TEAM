# Milestone as Production Pressure Mechanism, Not Conference Target

## Key Content

Studios often anchor milestones to external events (conferences, publisher shows). The better use of a milestone: force internal production discipline before the studio is ready to show externally.

**The distinction matters:**
- Conference-anchored milestone = driven by external visibility requirement; teams can rationalise "not quite ready" as acceptable because the milestone is about optics
- Pressure-anchored milestone = internal deadline with no external excuse; the studio must ship the thing or face a clear internal failure

**When to use pressure-anchored milestones:**
- Studio has cash runway but lacks urgency
- Teams are productive but not learning to estimate, scope, and close
- The goal is to teach the production rhythm (estimate, build, ship in cycles), not to impress investors yet

**Secondary benefit:** once the studio ships to a pressure milestone, they have evidence they can execute. This matters for investor narrative later.

**Finance note:** a studio with stable runway has leverage -- money without urgency produces drift. A hard milestone date (even arbitrary) converts runway into productive pressure.

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen decided: September locked as the vertical slice milestone -- not to present at DICE or any conference, purely to force deadline pressure
- Glen observed: finance is stable and runway has leverage, but money without a deadline produces drift
- Pattern recognised: the act of shipping to a milestone teaches studios to estimate and close, regardless of what they're shipping to
- Glen concluded: investor readiness follows from production discipline, not the other way around

## Context

Production meeting at a ~65-person MMO studio in pre-production, June 2026. Studio has investor backing and stable cash. September milestone set during this meeting as internal discipline anchor, not a conference target.

## Applicability

Relevant when: advising a well-funded studio that is drifting without urgency -- propose a hard internal milestone with no conference attached.
Relevant when: a studio is debating whether to delay a milestone because "we're not ready to show it" -- reframe the milestone's purpose.
Relevant when: a studio has never shipped to a milestone before and needs to learn the production rhythm.
Relevant when: NBI is structuring an engagement around a studio with cash but no delivery culture -- set a pressure milestone as the first deliverable.
