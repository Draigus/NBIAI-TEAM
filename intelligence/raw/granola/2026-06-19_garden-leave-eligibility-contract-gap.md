# Garden Leave Eligibility Gap: Self-Selecting Exits

## Key Content
Employment contract gap identified in practice: no clause disqualifying garden leave entitlement when an employee effectively self-selects out of a role (declares it is not a role they can perform). The employee demanded to be terminated rather than resign specifically to trigger the garden leave payout. A resignation would have received nothing.

Agreed contract fix adopted:
- Probation exits: 1 week (statutory minimum only)
- Post-probation termination (company-initiated): full contractual garden leave
- Resignation (employee-initiated): nothing

This creates a clear three-tier eligibility structure and removes the incentive to contest classification of exit as resignation vs. termination.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen decided: adopt three-tier garden leave structure in all future contracts for this studio.
- Pattern recognised: absence of resignation-disqualification clause creates adversarial behaviour at exit where employees seek termination to maximise payout.
- Lesson: contract language must explicitly address what triggers vs. disqualifies garden leave, not just how long it lasts.
