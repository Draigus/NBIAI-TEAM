# Tech Producer Candidate: Sarah Clarke — Avalanche Studios, Buffer Planning, Culture Signals

## Key Content

Sarah Clarke (Avalanche Studios, ~7 years, Stockholm-based). Estimation approach: time-box new tasks first, then reevaluate; for repeat tasks, trust first estimate, split into chunks, check in at midpoint. Buffer planning: adds 20–30% to every epic/sprint estimate (20-day animal asset budgeted as 30–35 days; 3-day rig budgeted as 5). Pipeline breakdown patterns she's fixed: disciplines siloing (art handing off after 6 weeks with no downstream consideration — fixed by giving rigging blockouts before full art completion); QA miscommunication from undefined done criteria — her fix is a Definition of Done per epic written with directors, devs, and QA, documented in Confluence, not just Jira comments. Tech-to-leadership translation: "explain it like I'm 5" with leads, then asks devs to write a short Slack snippet for exec conversations. Culture signals: "culture is the worst acceptable behaviour" is her guiding principle; advocates buddy systems and informal onboarding over handbooks. Red flags she watches for: more goodbye posts than hello posts in Slack, cameras off/no social engagement, toxic optimism ("everyone knows things are on fire but says it's fine"). CI/CD: limited direct experience — transparent about gap. Strengths: "get it done" energy, candor without unkindness. Glen's read: strong; Mustafa's concern: limited tech depth.
