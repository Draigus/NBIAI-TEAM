# Proxy Kit as Vertical Slice Art Quality Floor

## Key Content
Art quality tiering decision for a vertical slice in a mid-size MMO studio:

**Quality tiers defined:**
- **Proxy kit + proxy props:** minimum viable quality for VS1 (first playable). Consistent, readable, intentional look without final art. Artists were defaulting to mid-poly bake; this was corrected as too high and too slow.
- **Mid-poly bake:** reserved for feature pillars only. Not the default; applied where the specific feature must demonstrate final-quality potential.
- **Character customisation space:** explicitly called out as a feature pillar warranting mid-poly bake investment.

**Set dressing:** required on the critical path even at proxy stage. Doesn't need to be extensive, but enough to show "there's stuff here." Absence of set dressing at proxy stage reads as incomplete rather than intentional.

**Artist drift risk:** without a defined quality tier, artists self-escalate to the highest quality they can produce, which creates unsustainable timelines and inconsistent output across teams.

**Art direction principle (proxy kit):** consistent and readable beats artified. A proxy kit look that is internally coherent reads better to investors than a mix of proxy and partially-polished assets.

## Decisions / Insights
- Level design lead decided: proxy kit + proxy props is the VS1 quality standard; mid-poly bake is not default
- Glen agreed: art direction should aim for controlled intentional look over mixed fidelity
- Pattern recognised: without explicit quality tier definitions, art teams self-escalate and create timeline and scope risk
- Studio lead observed: investors can pitch-sell a roughly painted image; the art fidelity required for investment is lower than studios assume

## Context
1:1 between Glen and a newly engaged level design lead at a ~70-person MMO studio, 2026-06-22. Studio is in vertical slice phase and calibrating art quality expectations across teams.
