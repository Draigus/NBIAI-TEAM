# Art Asset Management: No CMS for WIP, Biweekly Output View

## Key Content
At a ~70-person MMO studio, no centralised CMS exists for WIP art assets. ZBrush high-poly source files (up to 20 GB) sit on individual artists' personal drives. Only approved final models land in shared storage (G Drive). This creates a structural blind spot for leadership: output anomalies are invisible until approval stage.

Leadership approach: establish a biweekly view of what was built, by whom, as a non-intrusive output visibility mechanism. Framing: high output buys tolerance for rough edges; low output does not. Goal is pattern recognition without micromanagement.

Approved assets organised by team lead with a direct link to the renders folder shared with leadership.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen concluded: "high output buys tolerance for rough edges; low output does not" -- a practical framework for calibrating supervision intensity per artist.
- Glen decided: biweekly asset output summaries from team leads are sufficient for anomaly detection without requiring a formal CMS.
- Sasha (CH) observed: saving multiple asset versions preemptively protects against being blocked by bureaucratic re-approval requests.
