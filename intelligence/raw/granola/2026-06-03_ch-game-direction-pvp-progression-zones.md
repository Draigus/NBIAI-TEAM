# CH Game Direction: PVP Strategy, Weapon Mastery, and Zone Distraction Density

## Key Content

Weekly game direction alignment with Robin and Vardis. Dual wielding approved across all classes including mages/priests (off-hand = wands/orbs). Balancing strategy: accept that min-max builds emerge (dual dagger crit warrior), plan for nerf cycling as a retention mechanic, avoid auto-kill scenarios, use cooldowns on stuns/teleports. PVP: ~20% playerbase required; PVE/PVP separation through zone cloning — separate PVP instances of existing zones, unlocked after completing PVE version; cosmetic-only PVP rewards (titles, house decorations). Level cap: 60 with weapon specialisation. One primary weapon to mastery; Weapon Master perk unlocks secondary at half XP rate. Zone design philosophy: maximum 4 minutes travel without impulse activity — fishing spots, runes, POIs along all major paths ("mall experience"). Evergreen content: achievement-based exploration, rune collection with lore combinations. Distraction density is the primary defence against content-consumption vs production timeline gap.

## Decisions / Insights

- [Glen/Robin/Vardis] decided: PVP through zone cloning — no mixed PVE/PVP spaces
- [Glen/Robin/Vardis] decided: level 60 cap, weapon mastery system with staged progression
- [Glen] decided: distraction density ceiling = 4 minutes between impulse activities in any zone
- [Glen/Robin] decided: dual wielding confirmed for all classes; nerf cycling is acceptable and strategic
- [Robin] concluded: animation team communication breakdown (zero combat animation in 4 months) is unacceptable

## Context

Game direction alignment meeting, 3 June 2026. Glen Pryer, Robin Jubber (Game Director), Vardis (CEO). Part of weekly design series.
