# Hiring Pipeline Health Rules and Interview Process Governance

## Key Content
Three practices formalised at a ~70-person studio in an active senior hiring phase across multiple departments:

**Pipeline health minimum:** Any open role with fewer than 3 valid candidates = red status, reported as such. Applied to all recruiter reporting going forward. Purpose: forces the recruiter to maintain active sourcing rather than waiting on a single candidate.

**Scorecard policy tiered by seniority:** Lead-level and above require scorecards and background checks. Below lead: scorecards optional unless two candidates are in direct contention for the same role. Rationale: proportionate process overhead; below-lead hiring decisions are usually clear enough without formal scorecards.

**HR screening as the intentional first interview step:** HR lead conducts the first call to collect salary expectations, contract type preference, and relocation interest before any technical or functional evaluation begins. Explicitly confirmed as a deliberate design choice, not administrative process noise. Purpose: surface disqualifying practical mismatches before costly interview time is spent.

## Decisions / Insights
- Studio agreed: fewer than 3 valid candidates on a role = risk signal, logged as red.
- Glen and Lorenza agreed: scorecard and background check requirements scale with seniority level; below-lead screening is proportionate.
- Glen observed: collecting salary expectations and relocation intent at the first touch reduces late-stage offer failures.
