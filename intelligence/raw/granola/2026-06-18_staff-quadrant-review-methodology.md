# Staff Quadrant Review: Four-Category Team Assessment Framework

## Key Content
A structured staff capability review using four categories on a 2x2 matrix:
- **Weak link:** Low capability, low growth potential. Target for managed exit.
- **Loose cannon:** High output but unpredictable or destructive behaviour. Manage closely or exit.
- **Steady Eddie:** Reliable, meets expectations, not a growth risk. Retain and stabilise.
- **Champion:** High capability, high behaviour alignment. Protect and develop.

Effective when combining three data sources: (1) estimation data showing days-to-complete per person from the vertical slice mapping, (2) direct HOD observation after 3+ months working alongside the individual, (3) cross-checking against the published seniority matrix and role expectations -- not peer comparison.

Key design choice: evaluation is against the role standard, not against peers. Peer comparison is explicitly considered acceptable for morale management in early review cycles, but external role standards are the long-term benchmark.

Three-question overlay (for HOD facilitation): Can the person do the job at the required scale today? Do they have growth potential into the role? Can the studio accelerate that growth within a year?
