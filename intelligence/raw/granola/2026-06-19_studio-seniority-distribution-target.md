# Target Studio Seniority Distribution: 60/30/10

## Key Content
Target distribution for a quality-gated MMO studio building toward a vertical slice:
- ~60% seniors
- ~30% mids
- ~10% juniors (only with real mentorship infrastructure in place)

The current studio distribution is inverted -- too many juniors, not enough seniors to mentor them. The fundamental problem with remote juniors without senior mentors: no osmotic learning, they develop bad habits or stall entirely. Training curriculum, scheduled senior time, and checkpoints are prerequisites for any junior presence.

Replacements are product-driven, not budget-driven. Budget exists to replace flagged staff with mids and seniors. The budget goal is not cost reduction but capability elevation.

Hiring question that surfaced structural problem: "how do you handle conflict when asked to do something you disagree with?" -- used in interviews to identify cultural fit and self-management capacity.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen decided: target distribution is 60% seniors, 30% mids, 10% juniors once mentorship infrastructure exists.
- Glen concluded: juniors without mentorship infrastructure are a studio liability, not a capability, in a remote MMO environment.
- Glen decided: replacements are product-capability decisions, not headcount-cost decisions.
- Pattern recognised: too many juniors + remote work + no training curriculum = chronic underperformance and bad habits that compound over time.

## Context
1:1 with Game Director, 2026-06-19. Studio was historically junior-heavy due to earlier hiring decisions. Current restructuring targets the inversion as a core production risk for the vertical slice.
