# Glen/Jonas — PlayGoals Beta Results & Work Packages

## Beta Results
- Open beta ran throughout March, taken down to transition toward launch.
- Public launch targeted for 14 May. Multi-platform day-one: Steam, Epic, PlayStation, Xbox.
- ~50% day-one retention on PlayStation (highlighted as exceptionally strong).
- Julius (new live ops specialist) credited for driving retention.

## Package 1: Base Pricing & Regional Strategy
- Define foundational price architecture: base price points, hard-currency-to-USD conversion rates, currency pack sizes.
- Competitive alignment against relevant competitors.
- Regional pricing guidance: regional price indices, rounding, platform fee norms.
- Cautionary reference: Patrick Soderlund's "don't do any regional pricing" advice caused backlash requiring full pivot.
- Sony may be less flexible than other platforms.

## Package 2: Live Ops Planning & Team Education
- Drive engagement, create excitement, manage pulse/tempo, reinforce retention.
- Many team members new to live service — need clear reusable template.
- Narrated mock plan/simulation proposed. Milestones: 14 May launch, June World Cup, August planning.
- Not season-based by design — any seasonal elements require game design input.

## Technical Constraints
- New systems require 3-4 months development; ~6 weeks to launch means complex systems out of day-one scope.
- Prioritise first 30-60 days on stability and bug fixes to "earn the right" with players.
- Heavier monetisation around ~60-day mark once quality and trust established.

## Intelligence Value
PlayGoals is a concrete NBI client with measurable beta results (50% D1 retention on PS). Two defined work packages. The 60-day monetisation delay strategy is a sound approach worth documenting as an NBI best practice. Multi-platform day-one launch is ambitious.
