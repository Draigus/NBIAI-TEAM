# Employee Satisfaction Survey: Don't Launch Until You Can Act on the Results

## Key Content
Employee satisfaction survey at a ~70-person studio deliberately delayed until production infrastructure (project management tooling, pipelines, vertical slice) is underway. Rationale: a survey creates a commitment backlog. Launching before the studio has capacity to act on results creates expectations that will be visibly unmet -- damaging trust more than the survey helps.

Timing target: late August, after Jira, pipelines, and vertical slice are moving. Survey will be fully anonymous.

Secondary finding: morale among non-lead staff is currently low. One cause identified is communication style from leadership landing as intimidating to juniors. Response: start a full-studio 1:1 rotation (~55 staff over ~4 weeks) to connect with people who haven't yet had direct time with leadership.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen decided: delay satisfaction survey to late August; launch only once infrastructure is in place to act on responses.
- Glen observed: a survey creates a commitment backlog -- the studio must have capacity to close the loop or it backfires.
- Glen decided: all-studio 1:1 rotation (~55 people, ~4 weeks) as an immediate morale intervention rather than waiting for the formal survey.
- Glen acknowledged: leadership communication style (direct/firm framing) lands differently with juniors than with leads; adjusting tone per person is necessary.

## Context
1:1 with a studio COO, 2026-06-19. Studio recently went through large-scale staffing assessment and is mid-restructure. Morale concern raised by the COO.
