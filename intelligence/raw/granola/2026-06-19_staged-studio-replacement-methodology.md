# Staged Studio Staff Replacement Methodology

## Key Content
When a studio has identified a cohort of underperformers across multiple departments, the replacement process follows a phased, non-disruptive pattern:

**Priority tiers**: Hard cuts (tier 1, highest priority) are those whose craft is definitively below the quality bar for the product. Secondary tier (unmarked) are exits that are lower urgency -- not first wave. The distinction matters for sequencing.

**Replacement process per person**: (1) Open the replacement role. (2) Find and hire the candidate. (3) Overlap new hire with existing person for 2-3 weeks for handover. (4) Exit the existing person. Not mass exits -- staged, overlap-based.

**Wave sizing**: Groups of 3-5-7-8 replacements, not all at once. Prevents cultural shock and allows onboarding infrastructure to absorb new hires.

**Communication sequence**: Brief COO and EP (Executive Producer) first. Consolidate a step-by-step plan first. Do not brief the CEO/studio owner until the plan is fully consolidated -- prevents premature leak to the wider team and ad hoc reactions.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen decided: recruit against flagged roles first, then overlap, then exit -- never exit before replacement is ready.
- Glen observed: doing all replacements simultaneously is disruptive and unabsorbable; waves of 3-5 are the unit.
- Pattern recognised: some underperformers may self-select out when the new hires arrive, which is preferable to managed exits.
- Glen decided: COO and EP briefed before CEO to consolidate the plan before top-down communication.
