# Series A Games Funding Market: Down ~70-80%, Investors Moved to AI (June 2026)

## Key Content

Glen's characterisation of the current games investment market, stated in a technical leadership interview on 2026-06-24:

- **Series A game investment down approximately 70-80%** from prior cycle highs.
- Primary cause: major investors have rotated to AI, reducing available capital for game studios at the growth stage.
- Result: funding a game at Series A is extremely difficult without either pre-existing investor relationships or exceptional market differentiation.

**Strategic rationale for choosing an MMO in this environment:**
- Fewer competitors than crowded genres (extraction shooters, battle royale, etc.).
- Higher standout visibility at launch without requiring massive CPI (cost per install) spend.
- Unique enough to attract a dedicated segment; familiar enough to retain them.
- This is explicitly a market positioning choice, not just a design preference.

**Contrast:** the studio in question has stable funding from a non-games source (~$13-14B fund) -- this is explicitly not the norm. The market observation applies to studios without locked-in pre-production capital.

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen observed: the MMO genre choice is partially a capital efficiency play -- fewer competitors means lower customer acquisition cost at launch relative to genre-crowded alternatives.
- Glen observed: AI investment rotation has materially compressed Series A availability for game studios as of mid-2026.
