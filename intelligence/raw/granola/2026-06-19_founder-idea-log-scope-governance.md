# Managing Founder "Midnight Ideas": The Shared Idea Log

## Key Content
Pattern: studio founders/CEOs generate scope change requests ad hoc, often in late-night messages or impromptu meeting comments. These are acted on immediately by team members who don't want to push back on the CEO, bypassing production planning. The result: one-person misinformation loops, ad hoc scope pivots, and lost meeting time.

Proposed mechanism: a shared log (the "idea log") where anyone hit by an ad-hoc founder idea adds the request. The log is reviewed weekly in 1:1s or team meetings. Ideas are not acted on ad hoc -- they go through the review process. The founder sees all their own ideas in one place, which can itself be a self-regulating mechanism (founders may naturally deprioritise items when they see the volume).

For in-meeting scope pivots: the lead or director handles the interrupt in the moment ("can we take this offline, people need to move on"). Leadership coaches the founder on this pattern in their regular 1:1/mentoring session when an incident is flagged.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen decided: implement a shared idea log where anyone receiving an ad-hoc scope request from the studio owner logs it for weekly review.
- Pattern recognised: the combination of a log (visibility) + weekly review (governance) + in-meeting interrupt (immediate containment) creates three layers of protection against ad hoc scope drift.
- Glen decided: coaches the founder on scope discipline in their direct 1:1/mentoring sessions, not by confrontation in front of the team.
- Pattern recognised: showing a founder all their own ideas in one place is a natural self-regulator -- volume makes the pattern visible to the originator.
