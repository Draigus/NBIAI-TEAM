# Social Design Spectrum: Solo to Raid as a Design Framework

## Key Content
Framework from a senior game designer (Everwild/Rare background) for designing social systems in a live MMO:

**The spectrum:** solo play / ambient presence / informal group / organised group / raid. The hardest design space is the middle: ambient presence without forced interaction.

**Core philosophy:** "It's always better to be with others" should be baked into mechanics, not bolted on as a feature.

**Concrete design examples:**
- Walking through thick grass leaves a path; the next player isn't slowed
- Running alongside a friend both move 5% faster
- Valve GDC 2019 data: best-selling items were things that benefited other people, not the buyer
- Everwild shared inventory: a side pocket others in your group could pull from freely while gathering
- Stances (borrowed from Nier: Automata) communicate "not in the mood" without words; social-pressure-free opt-out

**Solo play must remain meaningful:** after 11 meetings in a day, sometimes you just want to play alone. The social spectrum must serve solo players as a first-class experience.

**Griefer design:** creating solo/ambient opt-outs reduces conflict at the soft middle of the spectrum.

## Decisions / Insights
- Designer concluded: collaboration benefits should be passive and automatic, not gated behind group mechanics
- Designer concluded: the ambient-presence middle of the spectrum is the hardest to design and most under-served by live games
- Glen observed: forced interaction mechanics alienate the large solo-at-heart player population
- Valve data: players pay for items that benefit others — social generosity is a monetisation signal, not just a design nice-to-have

## Context
Game design session between Glen and a senior game designer advising a ~70-person MMO studio, 2026-06-22. Studio is designing a large shared-world game and debating how to structure collaboration without coercing it.
