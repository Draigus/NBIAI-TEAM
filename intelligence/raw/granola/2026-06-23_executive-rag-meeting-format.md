# Executive Status Meeting Format: RAG, Time-Boxed, Action-Item First

## Key Content

Unstructured executive meetings accumulate stale status updates with no accountability and no resolution. Items reported one week are forgotten the next; scope drifts without a persistent title on each issue.

**Proposed format (Glen's design, deployed at a ~65-person MMO studio):**

1. **Start by reviewing last week's action items** -- who committed to what, who closed it. Names visible, not hidden
2. **Each area owner gets 5-7 minutes:** "Here is my red, yellow, green. Here is my plan. Here is when I will close it"
3. **No update without a timeframe.** If unsure, give a best guess. A missing date = blocked; flag it as such
4. **Do not solve heavy problems in this meeting.** Identify, assign, spin off to the right people. The meeting's job is triage and accountability, not problem-solving
5. **Persistent item titles.** Hiring pipeline, scope decisions, and red items carry the same title week-to-week so progress is trackable against a fixed reference

**Hiring pipeline specifically:** move to a consolidated list format rather than verbal updates. One document, updated before the meeting, reviewed in the meeting.

**Why it fails without RAG:** verbal status updates allow leaders to contextualise failure without admitting it. RAG forces a binary: it's red or it isn't.

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen decided: to run the first executive meeting in this format, review old notes himself to close out items, then introduce the template
- Glen observed: the existing format allowed items to be reported one week and forgotten the next with no accountability
- Valeria observed independently: same problem -- points reported, then lost, scope drifts without a persistent title
- Glen concluded: send a Google Sheets template with RAG fields and due dates for the team to populate before the next meeting
