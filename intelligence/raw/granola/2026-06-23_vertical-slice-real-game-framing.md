# Vertical Slice "Building the Real Game" Anxiety Pattern

## Key Content

Studios in vertical slice phase often develop a specific anxiety: the team suspects they are building another pitch deck rather than their actual game. Signals include:

- Assets described as "temporary" or "for the VS only" rather than production-quality
- Scope debates about features that are officially unconfirmed (e.g. PVP when only PVE is confirmed)
- Cross-department concern expressed independently by multiple heads (art, tech, production)
- Teams building to a demo standard rather than a game standard

**Why it matters:** this anxiety produces lower-quality work and scope creep simultaneously. Teams hedge their effort (because "it's just for the VS") while also trying to include everything (because "we need to impress").

**Fix: explicit framing mandate**
- Communicate directly to all staff: every asset built for the vertical slice is a piece of the real game, not a demo
- Scope that isn't confirmed cannot be discussed in pipeline meetings; PVP is not in the vertical slice until it is confirmed as in the vertical slice
- Leadership must repeat this framing consistently, not just once

**Underlying risk: PVP (or any unconfirmed feature) in the VS.** If a complex feature is in the VS and proves unexecutable to a fun standard, it costs time AND reveals the team's judgement deficit to investors. Lock the VS scope hard, communicate it clearly, and defend it.
