# Slack Messages Are Retrievable via Data Subject Access Requests

## Key Content
In a UK employment context, Slack messages sent on company-managed workspaces are retrievable by employees via Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs). Raised as a risk management reminder: informal Slack commentary about performance, personnel decisions, or management intent can surface in an employment dispute.

This cuts both ways: the same evidence trail that documents a pattern of poor employee behaviour (Granola transcripts, 1:1 notes) can also expose inconsistent management decisions or informal promises that create liability. Both parties can use discoverable communications.

Practical risk areas: verbal commitments made in Slack (e.g. promises of relocation support, contract extension, continued roles) may be construed as binding. Heads of department making informal performance comments over Slack create a discoverable record.

Action agreed: HoDs to be reminded about electronic communication hygiene at a future session. Sensitive HR discussions to be held in appropriate documented channels (formal HR tools, official email) rather than casual Slack threads.
