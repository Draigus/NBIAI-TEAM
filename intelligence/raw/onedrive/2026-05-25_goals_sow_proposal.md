# NBI Proposal Structure: PlayGOALS Monetization and Live Service Engagement

## Key Content

NBI proposed a two-workstream engagement to GOALS AB for their PlayGOALS F2P football game launching 14 May 2026. Workstream 1: Pricing Benchmarking ($17,500) covering competitive analysis of 3-5 sets across NA/EU/LATAM, CRO review, regional pricing framework, and platform-specific SKU guidance with focus on Sony pre-submission. Workstream 2: Live Service Consulting ($45,440) covering content cadence design, A/B testing playbook, 90-day roadmap simulation, and team workshop. Optional white paper on "Our Creators" pricing failure case study ($2,500). Payment: 30% upfront ($18,882), remainder on deliverable acceptance. The client signed SOW 1 only at 100,000 SEK (~$10K) for pricing benchmarking scoped at ~116 hours. The proposal leveraged beta metrics (50% D1 retention on PlayStation cited, later verified at 34-36%) as urgency drivers.

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen/NBI decided: frame proposals around client urgency windows (6-week pre-launch) rather than NBI capability
- Glen/NBI decided: price workstreams separately to allow partial buy-in, which worked (client took pricing only)
- NBI observed: "Embed and Align" positioning differentiates from report-only consultancies
- Glen observed: citing beta metrics creates urgency but must be verified (50% D1 was overstated vs actual 34-36%)

## Context

Proposal submitted 31 March 2026, MSA signed 8 April 2026 between NBI LLC (Tom Rieger signatory) and GOALS AB (Stockholm). Jonas Rundberg (CEO) is primary client contact. The engagement was NBI's first major gaming advisory engagement with a live product approaching launch.

- Relevant when: pricing future consulting proposals for game studios with imminent launch deadlines
- Relevant when: structuring workstreams for partial client buy-in on multi-phase engagements
- Relevant when: building urgency narrative around platform certification windows (Sony, Xbox)
