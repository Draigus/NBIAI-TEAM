# Offsite Pre-Read Communication Template

## Key Content

Glen's pre-read pack for a 3-day studio leadership offsite, designed for 15 minutes of careful reading. Structure: why we are doing this (replace momentum with structure), five concrete exit deliverables (clean roadmap, gate criteria, pipeline RACI, staff plan, cascaded goals), three-day arc summary (Day 1 foundation/feature sweep, Day 2 gates/GTM/community/goals, Day 3 pipelines/RACI/staff/wrap), six rules stated as conditions not aspirations (verbal interaction, lean in, must-disagree, silent breaks, everything in room, no deferral). Framing choices: "Studio leadership in the room is the delivery vehicle. There is no homework after, only the work we do inside the three days. If we slip, we extend the day." Rule 3 explicitly calls out the failure mode: "Silent disagreement that surfaces later is the failure mode that has hurt this studio before."

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen decided: pre-read is exactly 15 minutes to read (respects attendees' time, ensures compliance)
- Glen decided: "must disagree" rule explicitly names the studio's historical failure mode
- Glen decided: no homework framing removes the escape hatch of "we'll finish after"
- Glen observed: "running on momentum more than on cadence" is the diagnosis driving the offsite

## Context

Written April 2026 for Couch Heroes offsite (Apr 27-30, London). Sent to 7+ attendees three working days before the event. Blocking questions were to be raised in Slack before Sunday evening.
