# Studio Offsite Facilitation Playbook — Working Document Pattern

## Key Content

Glen's private facilitation playbook for a 3-day studio offsite with 8-9 senior attendees. Structure per session: opening line (verbatim script), outcome target, watch-fors (person-specific failure modes), standard responses (phrase bank for common deflections), and move-on criteria (green-light signals). Key facilitation patterns: force verbal commitment from each attendee on rules individually; named watch-fors per person (e.g. "Mustafa on dial-in will default to silent observer -- force him in early", "Aris is a 'let's discuss offline' person by reflex", "Vardis will try to converge too quickly"); scripted rebuttals for common derails ("No. In the room or not at all.", "If you can't decide now, name what evidence you need by end of day.", hand-up gesture to redirect monologues). The 2-minute-per-row feature sweep uses "L by default" sizing to prevent analysis paralysis. Gate-passing criteria session framed as "the single most leveraged hour."

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen decided: facilitation playbook is for facilitator's eyes only, not shared with attendees
- Glen observed: person-specific watch-fors are essential (know who defaults to silence, who converges too fast, who defers to others)
- Glen decided: scripted standard responses prevent energy-sapping real-time improvisation
- Glen decided: "move on criteria" per session prevent over-indexing on any topic
- Glen observed: having the opening line scripted verbatim ensures tone-setting is deliberate, not accidental

## Context

Written April 2026 for Couch Heroes offsite. Glen was acting as CPO (fractional) and primary facilitator. Document designed alongside the public agenda and pre-decisions pack. Attendees included CEO, COO, Game Director, Head of Production, Head of Tech (remote), Director of Art, Head of HR.
