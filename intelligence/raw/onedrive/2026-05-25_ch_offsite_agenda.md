# 3-Day Studio Leadership Offsite Methodology

## Key Content

Glen designed a 3-day offsite for a ~55-person game studio with 8-9 senior attendees. Structure: Day 1 (Foundation) covers rules commitment, goal statement, workbook walkthrough (2,216 items across 7+AI projects), feature sweep with 2-minute-per-row discipline (success factor, owner triplet, size, gate target). Day 2 (Gates/GTM) covers gate-passing criteria for 8 production gates (Concept through Launch), live services planning, GTM lane, community strategy, studio-down goals cascade. Day 3 (Pipelines/Staff) covers 9 pipeline RACI maps, maintenance cadence, staff assessment (C-level only, quad review), decisions log walkthrough, Confluence rollout commitment. Standing items: parking lot wall, decisions log wall, risks log wall, daily energy check (1-10 score). North star printed on wall: "a clean roadmap the leads can take, size, subtask, and roll into JIRA."

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen decided: 6 rules stated and verbally committed by each attendee before any content (including "must disagree" rule)
- Glen observed: feature sweep at 2 minutes per row with "L by default" sizing prevents analysis paralysis
- Glen decided: gate-passing criteria session is "the single most leveraged hour of the offsite"
- Glen decided: staff assessment is C-level only; separate from full room to enable candour
- Glen observed: biggest overrun risks are feature sweep (1,194 Game items) and pipelines (9 to map)
- Glen decided: fallback if behind by end Day 2: Day 3 pipelines collapse to "frame + assign owners"

## Context

Designed April 2026 for Couch Heroes offsite (London, Apr 27-30). Attendees included CEO, COO, CPO (Glen, fractional), Game Director, Head of Production, Head of Tech (remote due to visa), Director of Art, Head of HR, incoming Exec Producer. Supported by WorkSage workbook with 264 flagged validation items.
