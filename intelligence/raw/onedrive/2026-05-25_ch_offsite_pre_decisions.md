# Couch Heroes Strategic Pre-Decisions (Offsite Apr 2026)

## Key Content

Glen laid down six binding strategic decisions before the Couch Heroes offsite to prevent relitigating in the room. 1) F2P with cosmetic monetisation (gear has no stats; cosmetics are aspirational currency). 2) Self-publish core distribution with targeted capability partnerships (preserve partner-game integration freedom; equity raise makes publisher capital redundant). 3) Closed beta at vertical slice/mid-production, no public early access (protect launch buzz, NDA-light controls for partner reveals, Founder's Pack carries beta access as conversion path). 4) Studio name and game name are separate (protect multi-game identity, investor narrative). 5) Unified account mandatory, cross-play PC+mobile at launch, console when first-party allows (Platform is first-class product surface, not companion). 6) 12-month goal: close institutional funding round + ship vertical slice + sustainable production cadence + core hiring.

## Decisions / Insights

- Glen decided: F2P cosmetic monetisation aligns business model with design intent (no stat gear)
- Glen decided: self-publishing preserves partner-game integration freedom that publishers would constrain
- Glen decided: Founder's Pack beta access is the conversion funnel, not early access
- Glen decided: vertical slice is multi-purpose anchor (investor follow-on, publisher conversations, beta quality)
- Glen observed: CH's historical pattern has been "more excitement than cadence"; sustainable delivery named as explicit counter
- Glen decided: burden of proof at offsite shifts onto anyone disagreeing with pre-decisions
