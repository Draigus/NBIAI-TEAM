# F2P Hard Currency Pack Pricing Benchmarks (2026)

## Key Content

315 normalised price points across 12 competitors with 147 citations. Entry-level tier: $0.99/100 HC is the football genre standard (EA FC, UFL, eFootball all match). Volume discount curves cluster at 15-23% industry-wide. EA has NEVER raised USD prices on existing tiers across 8 years of FIFA/FC history; strategy is to add higher ceiling tiers and restructure mid-range. UFL (closest F2P football competitor) mirrors EA FC on first 3 tiers, caps at $79.99 vs EA FC's $149.99. NBA 2K is the outlier with 86% volume discount curve. Fortnite sits at 56% (measured against practical $8.99 base). EA FC and Madden use near-identical cross-sport pricing structures (same price points, same amounts within 50 points). F1 24 uses dramatically lower price points reflecting its smaller economy, showing EA adapts structure scale per game. Red team scored the research 93/100 post-remediation.

## Decisions / Insights

- NBI concluded: USD prices on F2P currency packs are functionally permanent across all competitors studied
- NBI concluded: entry-level tier at $0.99/100 HC is the genre floor; launching without a micro-entry tier is a gap
- NBI observed: EA's cross-sport pricing consistency suggests deliberate portfolio strategy, not per-game optimisation
- NBI concluded: volume discount curves in 15-23% range represent the safe zone; steeper curves signal different audience economics
