# Data Room Valuation Sheet Structure for Investors

## Key Content
12-tab workbook structure for investor-grade valuation model: (1) Summary with base/downside/upside headline numbers. (2) Assumptions with named ranges, source, last-updated. (3) Historical Financials with gross-to-net reconciliation, LTM and TTM. (4) Normalisations with add-backs and rationale. (5) Forecast with driver-based 36-month model. (6) DCF with unlevered FCF, WACC, terminal value, sensitivity tables. (7) Multiples with public and transaction comps. (8) EV to Equity Bridge: less net debt, plus non-operating assets, less preferred liquidation, fully diluted shares via TSM. (9) KPIs and Cohorts. (10) Evidence and Sources. (11) Checks with balance checks and audit block. (12) Change Log.

Display standards: inputs blue, outputs black, links green, checks red until passing. Every number in PDF must tie to a visible cell. Common red flags: valuing on gross receipts while collecting net, using forward revenue without showing drivers, ignoring refunds and platform fees, cherry-picked comps.

## Decisions / Insights
- Glen decided: valuation models need both DCF and revenue multiple approaches side by side
- Glen decided: 5x5 sensitivity grids (WACC vs terminal growth, multiple vs revenue) on summary tab
- Glen observed: common investor red flag is valuing on gross receipts while the business collects net
- Glen observed: comps must show range and quartile position, never cherry-picked single comparators
