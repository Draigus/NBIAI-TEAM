The cost of hiring a recruiter to find a **Technical Director** depends on several factors, including region, seniority, industry, and whether you use **contingency**, **retained**, or **in-house** recruitment. Here’s a breakdown:

---

### **1. Contingency Recruiter**
- **Fee Structure:** Typically **20% to 30%** of the Technical Director’s **first-year base salary**
- **When Used:** No upfront payment; recruiter is only paid if the hire is successful
- **Example:** If salary is £140,000, expect to pay **£28,000 to £42,000**

---

### **2. Retained Executive Search Firm**
- **Fee Structure:** Typically **25% to 35%** of total compensation (base + bonus), split across 3 installments
- **Upfront Commitment:** Yes – often 1/3 of fee paid on signing
- **Best For:** High-profile or confidential roles, or when talent is scarce
- **Example:** For a total comp of £160,000, cost may be **£40,000 to £56,000**

---

### **3. Internal Recruiter or Embedded RPO (Recruitment Process Outsourcing)**
- **Cost Model:** Monthly fee, or salary if internal. Ranges from **£4,000 to £12,000/month**
- **Pro:** Good for multiple hires or ongoing recruitment
- **Con:** Less aggressive on passive candidates vs. external headhunters

---

### **Other Factors That Affect Price**
- **Seniority & scarcity:** More niche = higher fee
- **Location:** US and UK top markets command higher fees
- **Equity packages:** Some firms base fee on total comp (incl. equity)

---

If you're hiring for a **video game company**, **tech startup**, or **high-stakes SaaS/engineering role**, expect the recruiter to lean toward the higher end of those ranges, especially if you're demanding both deep technical acumen and leadership experience.
