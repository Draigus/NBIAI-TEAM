# Assumptions
- You want the latest 2025 global estimates in USD, covering mobile, PC, and console.  
- “Players” means people who play games, not necessarily paying users.

# Answer
**Players (global, 2025):** About 3.6 billion people play video games worldwide. Newzoo’s 2025 free report cites 3.6B; press write-ups put the estimate at roughly 3.57B. citeturn3search5turn3search1

**Revenue (global, 2025):** Expected full-year consumer spend of about **$188.8–$188.9 billion**. For context, 2024 closed at **$182.7 billion**. citeturn2view0turn3search2

**By country (2025, consumer spend; excludes hardware)**  
1) China: **$49.8B**, **723.0M** players  
2) United States: **$49.6B**, **224.8M** players  
3) Japan: **$16.8B**, **74.1M** players  
4) South Korea: **$7.3B**, **34.0M** players  
5) Germany: **$6.6B**, **53.2M** players  
6) United Kingdom: **$6.3B**, **43.4M** players  
7) France: **$4.0B**, **40.2M** players  
8) Canada: **$3.1B**, **24.4M** players  
9) Brazil: **$2.7B**, **123.3M** players  
10) Mexico: **$2.6B**, **78.1M** players. Figures are Newzoo’s 2025 ranking, which defines revenues as consumer spending and excludes hardware, tax, B2B services, and gambling. citeturn1view0

- Numbers are rounded and represent the latest published estimates as of 17 September 2025. Newzoo updates these annually and quarterly. citeturn2view0
