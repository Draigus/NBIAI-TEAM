How much interest can you earn 14,000,000 pounds

[ASSISTANT]
The amount of interest you can earn on £14,000,000 depends on several factors, including:

1. **Interest Rate**: The annual interest rate offered by the bank or investment.
2. **Type of Interest**: Whether the interest is simple or compound.
3. **Term**: The duration the money is invested or deposited.
4. **Investment Type**: Bank savings, bonds, stocks, or other instruments.

### Example Calculations:

#### 1. **Simple Interest**:
Using the formula:
\[
\text{Interest} = P \times r \times t
\]
- \( P = £14,000,000 \)
- \( r = \text{interest rate (e.g., 4% = 0.04)} \)
- \( t = \text{time in years (e.g., 1 year)} \)

At 4% annually:
\[
\text{Interest} = 14,000,000 \times 0.04 \times 1 = £560,000
\]

#### 2. **Compound Interest**:
Using the formula:
\[
A = P \times (1 + r)^t
\]
Where:
- \( A = \text{final amount} \)
- \( P = \text{principal (£14,000,000)} \)
- \( r = \text{annual interest rate} \)
- \( t = \text{time in years} \)

At 4% annually, compounded yearly, for 1 year:
\[
A = 14,000,000 \times (1 + 0.04)^1 = 14,560,000
\]
Interest earned: \( A - P = 14,560,000 - 14,000,000 = £560,000 \)

At 4%, compounded yearly, for 5 years:
\[
A = 14,000,000 \times (1 + 0.04)^5 \approx 17,019,058
\]
Interest earned: \( 17,019,058 - 14,000,000 = £3,019,058 \)
