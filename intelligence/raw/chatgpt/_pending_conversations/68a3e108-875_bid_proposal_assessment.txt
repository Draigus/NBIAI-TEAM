# What is a “TRC mapping snapshot”
**TRC** stands for **Technical Requirements Checklist** used by platform holders to decide if a build is compliant. Sony uses TRC, Microsoft uses Xbox Requirements (XR), and Steam has policy and integration checks. A **mapping snapshot** is a fast, practical view of how the current game and pipelines line up against those rules.

**What it includes in this 40-hour package**
- A consolidated sheet with columns like: Requirement ID, Short name, Applies to (Xbox, PlayStation, Steam), Current status, Known gaps, **Evidence needed** (log, video, test step, config), **Owner**, and **Gate** where we will prove it.  
- Coverage of the highest-risk areas for multiplayer titles: user identity and sign-in, entitlements checks, suspend and resume, save data rules, online presence and privacy, error messaging, controller and input, network disconnect and recovery, profanity and user-generated text, crash handling and reporting, and cross-network play policies.  
- A short **pre-cert checklist**: what must be demonstrated per platform before you schedule a first submission.

**Why it matters**
- It turns “we think we are compliant” into a concrete owner-and-evidence plan.  
- It connects each requirement to a specific roadmap gate so compliance is baked into delivery, not tacked on at the end.
