[USER]
how do i change the domain in an email adress on 0365

[ASSISTANT]
To change the domain in an email address in Office 365, you'll need to update your domain settings and modify users’ email addresses. Here's a concise step-by-step:

1. **Add and Verify the New Domain**  
   - Sign in to the Office 365 admin center.
   - Navigate to **Setup** > **Domains**.
   - Click **Add Domain** and follow the prompts to add your new domain.
   - Verify ownership by updating your DNS records (e.g., TXT record) as instructed.

2. **Update DNS Records**  
   - After verification, update necessary DNS records (MX, SPF, etc.) for the new domain to ensure mail flow.

3. **Change Users’ Email Addresses**  
   - In the **Exchange admin center** or **Office 365 admin center**, update the primary email address for each user from the old domain to the new one.
   - You can do this individually or use PowerShell for bulk updates.  
     For PowerShell, you might run:
     ```powershell
     Set-Mailbox user@olddomain.com -PrimarySmtpAddress user@newdomain.com
     ```

4. **Test and Confirm**  
   - Verify that emails are routing correctly.
   - Inform users about the change if needed.

This process ensures that the new domain is fully integrated into your Office 365 environment and that user mailboxes are updated accordingly.

how di I give someone admin rights in o365

[ASSISTANT]
To grant someone admin rights in Office 365, follow these steps:

1. **Sign In**  
   - Log in to the [Office 365 admin center](https://admin.microsoft.com).

2. **Select the User**  
   - In the left-hand menu, go to **Users** > **Active users**.
   - Find and click on the user you want to grant admin rights.

3. **Assign an Admin Role**  
   - Under the user's settings, click **Manage roles**.
   - Choose the appropriate admin role (e.g., Global admin, Exchange admin, etc.) based on the level of access required.
   - Click **Save changes**.

4. **Confirmation**  
   - Inform the user of their new privileges and verify that they can access the admin features.

This approach ensures the user gets the necessary rights without over-assigning permissions.
