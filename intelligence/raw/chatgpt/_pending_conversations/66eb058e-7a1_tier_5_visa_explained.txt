A Tier 5 visa refers to a category of work visa under the UK immigration system, designed for temporary workers. It is primarily used for individuals coming to the UK for short-term employment or specific work-related purposes, such as charity work, religious roles, creative or sporting activities, or as part of a government-authorized exchange program. The Tier 5 visa is further divided into six subcategories:

1. **Creative and Sporting**: For individuals working as entertainers, athletes, or creative artists.
2. **Charity Workers**: For people involved in unpaid voluntary work for a charity.
3. **Religious Workers**: For those working in a religious community, such as missionaries.
4. **Government Authorised Exchange**: For people coming to the UK through government-approved schemes for work experience, training, or research.
5. **International Agreement**: For individuals working under international law, such as embassy or diplomatic staff.
6. **Youth Mobility Scheme**: Allows young people (typically between 18-30) from certain countries to live and work in the UK for up to two years.

Tier 5 visas are typically time-limited and often come with restrictions on the type of work or activities allowed during the stay.
