### NBI’s Addressable-Market Definition Service for Video-Game Studios  

**What the service is**  
NBI delivers a rigorous, data-driven assessment of how many players your title can realistically reach and monetize. We:

* **Aggregate authoritative industry data** to size the total pool of potential players by platform (console, PC, mobile, cloud) and by business model (premium, F2P, subscription).  
* **Map genre-specific demand** to reveal how big the audience is for games that play, look and monetize like yours.  
* **Benchmark against comparable titles** to calibrate adoption curves, engagement depth and spend patterns.  
* **Segment by geography, language, wallet-size, gender and adoption behaviour** to show which audiences move the needle first—and which require localisation or staggered launches.

**Why getting it right matters**  

1. **Strategic resourcing:** Accurate TAM & SAM numbers tell production, LiveOps and backend teams exactly how much scale to build for—preventing over-engineering or costly rework when interest outpaces capacity.  
2. **Targeted marketing dollars:** Knowing the addressable audience by channel enables smarter UA bidding, creator partnerships and regional rollouts, maximising ROAS instead of blank-cheque ad buys.  
3. **Investor confidence:** A defensible market model underpins revenue forecasts, de-risks funding rounds and can materially improve valuations.  
4. **Road-map focus:** Feature priorities become data-led—if a mechanic resonates with the largest, highest-value segment, it stays on the critical path; if not, it’s deferred or cut.  
5. **Post-launch longevity:** Understanding where future cohorts will come from guides Live-service cadence, localisation pipelines and content themes, extending LTV rather than chasing churn after it happens.  

**Bottom line:** When studios guess at market size, they either starve promising games with too little support or drown them in spend they’ll never recoup. A precise addressable-market definition is the first—and most leverageable—step toward building a hit.
