Here’s how I’d frame it so it’s true to Sarge and crisp for design, UA, and investors.

# Assumptions
- Core loop should be the shortest repeatable sequence that drives resources and power.
- Quests, events, and tournaments are progression rails layered on top of the core loop.

# Brief plan
- Separate core loop from mid-loop and meta-loop.
- Map your proposal to these layers, tighten language, remove outcomes masquerading as actions.

# Deliverable

**Recommended core loop (irreducible):**  
Build your base → Battle others → Earn resources → Upgrade to grow in power → Repeat.

**Mid-loop (progression rails):**  
Complete quests and events → Claim rewards → Unlock features, units, and tiers → Enter tournaments.

**Meta-loop (social and status):**  
Join a clan → Share replays and strategy → Climb ladders and rankings → Host or enter tournaments.

**Why not list “progress” and “reward” inside the core loop?**  
They are outcomes of actions, not actions themselves. Keep the core loop action-driven so it’s measurable and optimisable.

**Your version, mapped and tightened**  
- Build your base ✔  
- Battle others ✔  
- Complete quests → mid-loop rail  
- Progress, reward → outcomes of the above actions  
- Grow in power → captured by “Upgrade to grow in power”  
- Then loop back to Battle and Build

**Player-facing copy (store page or trailer script):**  
Build. Battle. Loot. Upgrade. Repeat.  
Crush rivals, finish quests and events, win rewards, and climb the ranks with your clan.

**Investor-facing one-liner:**  
Action loop: Build → Battle → Loot → Upgrade → Repeat, with quests/events and tournaments driving mid-term retention and monetisation.

# Self-audit
- No em dashes or dividers used.
- Core loop is action-only, minimal, and repeatable; quests moved to mid-loop; tournaments to meta-loop.
- Language is British English and aligned to Sarge’s base-build/PvP/tournament design.
