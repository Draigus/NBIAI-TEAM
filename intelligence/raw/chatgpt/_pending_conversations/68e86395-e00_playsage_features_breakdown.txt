# Foresight — features and components

## 1) Forecast scopes and horizons
- Scopes: per game, per genre, or multi-title compare.
- Horizons: 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5 years.
- Granularity controls: daily, monthly, yearly rollups with consistent time zones and platform calendars.
- Platforms: mobile, Telegram, PC, Steam, console. Multi-platform merging with per-platform splits.

## 2) KPI coverage and definitions
- Traffic and users: Installs, Organic vs Paid split, DAU, MAU, CCU, Session Count, Avg playtime per week/month/year.  
- Monetisation: Conversion to payer, ARPU, ARPPU, ARPDAU, LTV.  
- Marketing: CAC, ROAS, CPI, Click-through rate, Cost per click.  
- Retention: D1, D7, D14, D30, D60, D90, M1, M3, M6 cohorts.  
- All KPIs are forecasted natively and also available as derived metrics with formula audit.

## 3) Data ingestion and sources
- Public web harvesters by platform and store where allowed, plus Steam-adjacent sources, site traffic signals, social and community proxies, and ad market benchmarks.  
- Optional connectors: Sensor Tower, data.ai, Steamcharts, platform partner feeds, internal data drops.  
- Easy upload: CSV/Excel upload of baseline genre data or studio history; schema checked on upload with human-readable error messages.  
- Source registry: each series tagged with origin, recency, and quality score.

## 4) Genre baselines and “vibe coding”
- Genre library: up-to-date genre and sub-genre catalog per platform.  
- Baseline builder: computes genre averages, spread, seasonality, update cadence effects, platform-specific curves.  
- Title-to-genre matching: wizard asks about camera, loop, monetisation, pace, session profile, PvE/PvP and proposes 1–3 candidate genres; user confirms via drop-down.  
- Curvature assets: reusable shapes for launch peaks, update bumps, decay tails, holidays, sports calendars, and Telegram-specific adoption patterns.

## 5) Modelling engine
- Core: ensemble of time-series models and gradient models guided by genre baselines.  
- Drivers: updates, price changes, UA spend, feature flags, platform promos, DLC, content drops, live-ops cadence, reviews sentiment.  
- Cold-start flow: if a title is new or data-poor, start from genre baseline curves, then adapt as signals arrive.  
- Cross-metric coupling: installs → DAU/MAU → conversion → pay KPIs, with retention gates and session effects.  
- Constraints: sanity bounds per KPI, monotonic rules for cumulative values, non-negative spend, unit consistency.  
- Missing-data resilience: back-fill from proxies, shorten lookback, widen error bands, and flag confidence.

## 6) Auto-validation and QA
- Backtests: rolling origin backtests at D30, D60, D90 with error ribbons.  
- Cross-source checks: compare same KPI from two sources; flag deltas.  
- Identity checks: metric math reconciliation, for example ARPU × MAU ≈ monthly revenue.  
- Drift and anomaly detector: alert when live data deviates from expected shape.  
- Repeat runs: each forecast passes multiple validation passes before saving; any failure requires user acknowledgement.

## 7) Controls and scenario editing GUI
- Control panel: horizon, granularity, platform, region, currency, tax and fee presets.  
- KPI editor: per-metric sliders or inputs with live recalculation and dependency hints.  
- Spend plan: CAC or CPI targets, budget curves, and channel mix to drive installs and ROAS forecasts.  
- Live-ops schedule: planned updates, DLC, promos, price changes, bundles.  
- Genre sensitivity: dial that moves the model between conservative, base, and aggressive within genre variance.  
- Notes: rich text annotations pinned to any chart point or window.

## 8) Questionnaire engine
- Adaptive Q&A: asks only what is needed given monetisation model and known data.  
- Modes supported: premium/box, free to play live service, hybrid box + live service, freemium.  
- Examples: primary monetisation, target regions, platform mix, expected update cadence, paid UA starting month, initial CPI expectations, price points, launch window, competitor set.  
- All answers stored as scenario metadata and used to shape baselines and drivers.

## 9) Outputs and exports
- Excel pack: one workbook per scenario with tabs for inputs, assumptions, KPI forecasts by day/month/year, revenue bridge, retention cohorts, spend plan, and a change log.  
- Dashboard: interactive tiles and charts, KPI selector, timeline scrubber, filters for platform, region, genre.  
- Graph actions: export PNG/SVG, add notes, toggle bands, overlay competitors, compare scenarios.  
- API and CSV/JSON exports for automation.

## 10) Save, compare, and versioning
- Save scenarios with name, timestamp, and tags.  
- Compare up to N titles or scenarios across any KPI with aligned time bases.  
- Version history and diffs: what changed, who changed it, before/after values.  
- Snapshots: lock a scenario for investor or board packs.

## 11) Metric-by-metric behaviour
- Installs: built from CPI or CAC and spend, plus organic share model.  
- Organic vs Paid: platform and genre priors, uplift from featuring and updates.  
- DAU/MAU: cohort-based from installs and retention; MAU is rolling 30-day unique.  
- Retention: cohort curves per platform-genre, adjusted by content cadence and sentiment.  
- Conversion: probability model tied to first-purchase timing, paywall design, and country mix.  
- ARPPU and ARPU: basket model from price ladder and payer frequency; ARPU = ARPPU × payer rate.  
- LTV: discounted cash flow from cohorts with configurable discount rate.  
- Session Count and Playtime: function of engagement curve and content cadence.  
- CCU: DAU × concurrency factor from session length and overlap.  
- CAC, CPI, CTR, CPC, ROAS: channel mix model using media benchmarks; ROAS tracked at D1/D7/D30/D90.  

## 12) Multi-title comparison
- Any KPI overlay for multiple titles.  
- Normalise by days-since-launch or calendar date.  
- Benchmarks: median, top quartile, bottom quartile of chosen genre and platform.

## 13) Roles, governance, and security

- Audit: all edits and exports logged.  
- Workspace settings: currencies, taxes, fees, regional packs.  
- Provenance: each chart can show data source and last refresh.

## 14) Performance targets
