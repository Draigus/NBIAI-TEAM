## Game Design and Experience
We refine core loop, progression, difficulty curve, rewards, and UX flows. For mobile we streamline early funnels and sessions. For PC and console we protect pacing, discovery, and feel. The goal is strong engagement without friction that kills momentum.

## Economy and Monetization
Premium, F2P, or hybrid. We design sinks and sources, reward cadence, bundles, and stores. For premium we set pricing, editions, and DLC plans. For F2P we build scalable IAPs, events, and offers that respect players and grow LTV.

## User Acquisition and Go-to-Market
From pitch to launch, we plan UA tests, creatives, and channel mix. For mobile we run soft launch milestones, ASO, and store optimization. For PC and console we align platform requirements, wishlists, and retail timing. Every step ties to measurable targets.

## Analytics and Forecasting
We define KPIs, build cohorts and funnels, and create decision-grade dashboards. Forecasting links content plans and UA to revenue and margin. Causal methods show what moved retention or spend so the roadmap reflects evidence, not guesswork.

## Production and Operating Model
We shape the delivery system. Roadmaps, resourcing, vendor strategy, rituals, risk tracking, and quality bars. Teams ship with clarity and fewer surprises. Leaders see status, tradeoffs, and runway in one place.

## Platform, Compliance, and Localization
We prepare for store guidelines, ratings, accessibility, privacy, and regional content rules. Launches clear review with fewer iterations and less rework.

## Post-Launch and Ongoing Support
For live titles we plan events, releases, and offers with guardrails that protect retention and trust. For premium titles we map DLC cadence, updates, and community feedback loops. You get a sustainable content and communication rhythm.

why is there a 160 limit?

[ASSISTANT]
There isn’t a hard 160-character cap. That number is a practical guideline for search snippets.

How it actually works:
- Google and Bing truncate by pixel width, not characters. Desktop cuts off around 920 px which often lands near 150–160 characters. Mobile is tighter, roughly 100–120 characters.
- Google may ignore or rewrite your description based on the query.
- Framer’s “Page Description” is used for the HTML meta description and can also be reused for social previews, which have their own truncation.

Best practice:
- Put the core value in the first 100–120 characters so it reads clean on mobile.
- Keep total around 140–160 characters for reliable desktop display.
- Write a unique line per page, include one primary keyword naturally, avoid quotes that can trigger truncation, and keep it human, not a keyword list.

If you want to run longer, you can, but expect truncation and possible rewrites.
