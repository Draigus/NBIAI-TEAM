what project management method has epics, features, stories, tasks

[ASSISTANT]
The project management method that uses **Epics, Features, Stories, and Tasks** is **Agile**, specifically in the **Scaled Agile Framework (SAFe)** and **Scrum/Kanban** methodologies.  

### Breakdown of the Hierarchy:  

1. **Epics** – Large, strategic initiatives that deliver significant business value. Often take multiple sprints or releases to complete.  
2. **Features** – Major functionalities or services that deliver value and align with business goals, typically completed within a **Program Increment (PI)** in SAFe.  
3. **Stories (User Stories)** – Small, specific deliverables that describe a piece of functionality from an end-user perspective. Typically fit within a single sprint (Scrum) or continuous flow (Kanban).  
4. **Tasks** – The smallest unit of work, breaking down stories into actionable steps for developers, testers, or designers.  

### Common Frameworks Using This Structure:  

- **SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)** – Uses **Epics → Features → Stories → Tasks** at an enterprise level.  
- **Scrum** – Uses **Epics → Stories → Tasks**, with Features sometimes used in larger organizations.  
- **Kanban** – Uses **Epics → Features → Stories → Tasks**, though the structure is more flexible than in Scrum.  

This hierarchy is commonly implemented in **Agile tools like Jira, Azure DevOps, and Rally** for backlog management and tracking progress.
