in the usa does the goverment have the ability to revoke education visa students at a university

[ASSISTANT]
Yes. In the United States both the Department of State and the Department of Homeland Security have separate powers to end a student’s permission to study here:

1. Visa-stamp revocation by the Department of State  
   Under section 221(i) of the Immigration and Nationality Act (INA), and implementing regulation 22 CFR 41.122, a U.S. consular officer may revoke an issued nonimmigrant visa at any time if the holder is no longer eligible for that classification or for other prescribed reasons. In practice, this means that even a valid F-1 stamp in your passport can be cancelled at post—by literally stamping “Revoked” across it—if the officer concludes you don’t meet the requirements any longer citeturn0search10.

2. Termination of status in SEVIS by DHS/ICE  
   Separately, once you’re in the U.S., Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) enforces your “duration of status” under 8 CFR 214.2(f)(5). If you fail to maintain a full course of study, work without authorization, or otherwise violate the terms of your F-1 or M-1 status, your Student and Exchange Visitor Information System (SEVIS) record can be terminated. That termination effectively ends your legal right to remain or to work on-campus and may trigger removal proceedings citeturn0search13.

In short, a student can lose their visa stamp abroad and/or have their legal status revoked at school—even if one action hasn’t yet occurred, the other can. Always keep your SEVIS record current, follow your Designated School Official’s guidance, and ensure you meet all course-load and authorization requirements.

can the fed revoke the ability of a university to sponsor international students

[ASSISTANT]
Yes. The U.S. Department of Homeland Security, through its Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP), has the authority to withdraw a school’s certification to admit F-1 and M-1 students. Once SEVP withdraws that certification, the school can no longer issue the Form I-20 that international students need to apply for or maintain their visas. Key points:  

1. Regulatory basis  
   Under 8 CFR § 214.3 and § 214.4, SEVP may initiate withdrawal proceedings or automatically withdraw certification if a school fails to comply with its obligations. Triggers include failure to maintain proper accreditation or facilities, willful misrepresentation by a designated school official, failure to timely file for recertification, or inadequate recordkeeping and reporting citeturn0search0turn0search4.  

2. Process and consequences  
   • SEVP will notify the school and allow it to cure minor infractions or appeal before final withdrawal.  
   • If certification is withdrawn, the school must stop enrolling new F-1/M-1 students, and existing students must transfer to another certified school or face termination of their SEVIS records.  
   • A school whose certification has been withdrawn (voluntarily or otherwise) generally must wait at least one year and reapply via Form I-17 to regain its ability to sponsor international students citeturn0search4.  

Additionally, for J-1 Exchange Visitor programs the Department of State can suspend or withdraw a school’s or program sponsor’s designation under 22 CFR § 62.11 if it fails to meet program requirements (e.g. improper screening, insufficient oversight, or fraudulent activities). Once withdrawn, the sponsor cannot issue DS-2019 forms, effectively halting new J-1 admissions.  

In short, both DHS (for F / M students) and DOS (for J students) have clear regulatory authority to revoke a university’s ability to sponsor international students if it falls out of compliance.
