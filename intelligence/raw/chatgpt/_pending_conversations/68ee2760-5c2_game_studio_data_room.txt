## Round definitions (games-specific, short)
**Seed**  
Team, thesis, and prototype. Goal is to prove fun, team quality, and a credible path to market. Premium teams often show wishlist momentum and a vertical slice. F2P teams show early playtest data, CPI experiments, and a first economy model. Funds typically take the project from prototype to vertical slice or soft launch prep.

**Series A**  
From promising signals to product-market fit. Premium: strong wishlist trajectory, successful demo or festival performance, early partner interest, production readiness. F2P: soft launch metrics trending toward sustainable LTV/CAC with improving retention and monetisation. Funds lock production, expand team, and finance launch with measured GTM.

**Series B**  
Scale what works. Live title with predictable KPIs or a premium title approaching launch with strong preorders and platform support. Investment accelerates content cadence, UA at scale, data science, and additional titles in parallel. Focus on portfolio construction and multi-platform reach.

**Series C**  
Institutional scaling. Multiple titles or one flagship with durable revenues, platform relationships, international expansion, and M&A readiness. Funds go to global scale, new lines of business, and potential acquisitions. Governance and reporting mature to public-company standard.

## Metrics and telemetry spec
Core definitions
- Retention: D1, D7, D30, D60.  
- Monetisation: ARPDAU, ARPPU, payer conversion.  
- LTV: sum of discounted ARPDAU over horizon, or cohort revenue per user net of returns and platform fees.  
- Payback: day t where cumulative gross margin from acquired cohort ≥ CAC.  
- Premium wishlists: time series, add rate, conversion to sales at launch window, refund rate.  
- Sentiment: review score distribution, keyed to version and date.

Required cuts
- By platform, geo, channel, creative, device class, version, experiment arm.  
- Cohort keys: install_date, channel, geo, platform.  
- Time base: UTC with explicit timezone; declare week start.

Evidence of integrity
- Data dictionary with table, column, type, null handling, and calculation rules.  
- Provenance: ingestion sources, schedule, and owner.
