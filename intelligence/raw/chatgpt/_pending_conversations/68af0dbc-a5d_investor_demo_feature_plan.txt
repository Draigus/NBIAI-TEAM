# Playsage — Investor Demo Feature Plan

## Demo goal
Show that Playsage can turn industry-wide data into decisions. In one short flow, investors should see: market-level visibility, game-level diagnostics, forward-looking forecasts, and “do this next” guidance. The demo uses real public data where possible and clearly marked synthetic fill for gaps.

## Personas for the demo
- **Studio Executive:** needs market size, competitor velocity, and risk flags.
- **Live-ops Director:** needs feature-level impact, sentiment shifts, and next best action.
- **PM/Analyst:** needs configurable dashboards, exports, and API access.

## Scope: features required for the demo
1) **Interactive Dashboards (MVP)**
   - Filters: genre, platform, country, time window.
   - KPIs: DAU/MAU, revenue proxy, ARPDAU, conversion, retention curve snapshot.
   - Views: “Top 25 by genre,” “My watchlist,” “Regional split.”
   - Export: CSV and one-click PDF of the current view.
   - Acceptance: filters update in under 2 seconds on seeded dataset, exports reflect active filters, saved views persist.

2) **Competitive Landscape**
   - Head-to-head compare for any two titles with a third “genre median” baseline.
   - Feature diff: content cadence, monetization patterns, update frequency.
   - Acceptance: select two titles, see time-aligned KPI curves and a simple feature matrix that highlights differences.

3) **Sentiment Analysis**
   - Sources: app store reviews and Steam reviews for the demo set.
   - Outputs: daily sentiment index, top topics, “what changed” since last update.
   - Acceptance: pick a title and date range, see sentiment trend, click a topic to read sample verbatims.

4) **Foresight (Forecasting)**
   - 90-day engagement and revenue-proxy forecast with backtest ribbon.
   - Simple explanation layer: “drivers that most influence the next 30 days.”
   - Acceptance: toggle “show backtest,” view accuracy band, and a list of top drivers.

5) **Market Watch: Releases**
   - Release calendar with “projected impact” tags by genre and region.
   - History and projections: past 6 months and next 3 months.
   - Acceptance: select a month, see upcoming drops, and a “collision alert” if launches cluster in your genre.

6) **Alerts**
   - Create alert from any chart or KPI: threshold, spike, slope change, or competitor update.
   - Delivery for demo: in-app feed and mock email preview.
   - Acceptance: create an alert in under 30 seconds, then trigger it with a demo toggle to show the feed item.

7) **Game Brain (Recommendations)**
   - Rule-plus-model hybrid for the demo:
     - “Run a limited-time bundle” when conversion dips and sentiment is stable.
     - “Content gap: mini-event” when cadence is sparse vs peers.
   - Acceptance: for one title, show two concrete recommendations with expected lift ranges and links to evidence.

8) **API Access (read-only stub)**
   - Token, sample endpoint, and a live “try it” call that returns the same dataset behind the dashboard.
   - Acceptance: call succeeds with filtered sample payload and rate limit headers.

## Data plan for the demo
- **Seeded catalog:** 50 to 100 titles across 3 genres, iOS, Android, and Steam.  
- **Sources:** public store ranks, ratings, review text, update timestamps, press releases, and curated lists.  
- **Synthetic fill:** clear flags on estimated KPIs and revenue proxies. Backtests use held-out weeks within the same dataset.

## Architecture for the demo
- **Ingest:** scheduled pulls and CSV drops into an object store.  
- **Process:** lightweight transforms, topic modeling for reviews, simple prophet or gradient boosting for 90-day forecasts with backtests.  
- **Serve:** Postgres or DuckDB for query, FastAPI for endpoints, Next.js or Framer-wrapped React for UI.  
- **Observability:** precomputed tiles for heavy charts, request logging, and a forced-cache toggle to guarantee smooth demo playback.

- **Steam-focused suites**  
  **VGInsights** and **Steam Data Suite** provide sales estimates, tags, review analytics, release calendars, and competitor tracking for PC. Often paired with a mobile tool above.

- **Newzoo**  
  Market sizing and PC/console dashboards plus consumer research. Less granular per-title feature analytics, strong for TAM and executive views.

# Tier 2 — Adjacent competitors
Point solutions Playsage touches in the MVP, often bought alongside a Tier 1 tool.

- **AppFollow, Appbot, unitQ**  
  Review mining, sentiment, ASO monitoring.

- **GameAnalytics, Unity Analytics (deltaDNA)**  
  SDK-based in-game telemetry. Not market intel, but competes for “analytics budget.”

- **Amplitude, Mixpanel, Looker, Tableau**  
  General product analytics and BI used by studios. Not games-specific intelligence, but alternative for dashboards and alerts.

- **MobileAction, AppTweak, Similarweb**  
  ASO and web traffic intel that sometimes substitutes for basic competitive tracking.

- If they ask “why not just use Steam-only tools plus a mobile tool,” the answer is: studios want one system of truth, not two disconnected stacks with different KPI definitions and no shared alerting or action layer.

Here is what those are:

- **MAE (Mean Absolute Error):** average of absolute errors. Formula: MAE = (1/n) Σ |y − ŷ|. Units are the same as the KPI (example: DAU). Easy to read, treats all errors equally.

- **MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error):** average of absolute percentage errors. Formula: MAPE = (100/n) Σ |(y − ŷ)/y|. Intuitive percent. Problems when y is near 0 and it over-penalizes small denominators.

- **RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error):** square errors, average, then square root. Formula: RMSE = √[(1/n) Σ (y − ŷ)²]. Same units as KPI. Punishes large misses more than MAE.

Recommended for Playsage:
- Show **MAPE %** for quick comparability across titles and regions.
- Also show **MAE** so users see the miss in player units or revenue proxy.
- Put **RMSE** in the tooltip for users who care about outlier sensitivity.
