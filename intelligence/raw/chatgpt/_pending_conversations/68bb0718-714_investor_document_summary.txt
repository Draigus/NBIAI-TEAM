Here’s a structured view of what **Unleashed Games** needs to change or sharpen if they want to secure funding. I’ve broken this into **four critical categories**:  

---

## 1. **Financials & Valuation**
- **Adjust the ask vs. stage**  
  - $15–20M for a studio at 10 staff, pre-prototype, and with launch 3+ years away is a tough sell.  
  - Most VCs would prefer staged raises ($5–7M seed/Series A now, then larger Series B when a vertical slice or alpha proves traction).  

- **Revisit ARR projections**  
  - $120M ARR from 500k subs is overly aggressive. Present tiered cases: conservative, base, and optimistic.  
  - Investors want to see how the studio survives at 100k subs (~$20–25M ARR) as a floor case.  

- **Clean up the cap table**  
  - Outstanding founder exits, stock splits, and unclear option allocations will kill diligence. Must finalize ownership and option pool **before pitching**.  

---

## 2. **Go-to-Market & Traction**
- **Show early proof points**  
  - A 2027 Early Access target is too far. Need playable demo, vertical slice, or pre-alpha builds within 12 months to validate fun and loop.  
  - Run limited user tests (100–500 players) and publish retention/engagement metrics to de-risk assumptions.  

- **Clarify CAC strategy**  
  - “$0 CAC” through community virality is aspirational. Add a realistic plan for paid UA, influencer partnerships, and early marketing spend.  
  - Benchmark expected CAC vs LTV for subscription MMOs.  

- **Differentiate from competitors**  
  - Sharpen why Haven wins against Minecraft/Roblox for kids and Valheim/Genshin for parents.  
  - Consider unique IP hooks (tone, art style, safety features, cross-generational play systems).  

---

## 3. **Team & Execution**
- **Fill credibility gaps**  
  - Add a proven CTO or Engineering VP with shipped MMO experience. Investors will worry about network infra/scaling.  
  - Define missing key hires explicitly (engineers, live ops leads, backend specialists).  

- **Operational clarity**  
  - Lay out a milestone-based hiring and burn plan (what hires trigger when).  
  - Show a roadmap for managing scope—how to avoid feature creep in a cross-platform MMO.  

---

## 4. **Narrative & Pitching**
- **Refocus the investor story**  
  - Current deck leans aspirational. Investors need de-risking: why this team, why now, why this market.  
  - Highlight early community engagement experiments as **traction metrics**, not side experiments.  

- **Exit strategy**  
  - Strengthen the acquisition pitch. What KPIs or milestones make Haven irresistible to a Microsoft/Sony/EA buyout?  
  - Spell out how the IP can expand into multiple “realms” to grow LTV.  

- **Investor segmentation**  
  - Pitch “gaming specialist funds” (1Up Ventures, Makers Fund, Griffin Gaming Partners) rather than generalist VCs.  
  - Prepare a staged-funding option for angels and family offices who like the “cozy MMO” thesis.  

---

### Bottom Line
To get funded, the studio must:
1. **Stage the raise smaller and nearer-term.**  
2. **Produce playable proof of fun fast.**  
3. **Clean up equity and governance.**  
4. **Add senior hires with MMO/live ops track record.**  
5. **Recast the financial model into conservative, base, and upside cases.**  
6. **Reframe “community first” from an aspiration into a measurable traction story.**
