## Red-team (how it fails, and countermeasures)

### Failure mode A: Popularity bias masquerades as “top-rated”
Discover will tend to surface widely-linked explainers and vendor marketing. Those can score high on “recency” and “readability” but low on evidence and implementability. citeturn0search0turn0search15  
Countermeasure: add a hard rule: “No Tier A without either (a) code, (b) reproducible method, (c) production postmortem detail, or (d) primary documentation.”

### Failure mode B: Scope explosion and generic outcomes
“All areas must be covered” plus “all material possible” can push the system into generic mega-summaries, not a curated library.  
Countermeasure: enforce quotas per batch: each batch targets exactly 1 role family + 1 phase, and produces exactly 10 candidate sources, ranked.

### Failure mode C: Silent gaps (backend, build, security, legal) stay red
Even with good intentions, the system will over-index on visible areas (art, writing, ideation) and under-collect for build pipelines, infra, security, enterprise governance, compliance.  
Countermeasure: force an early sequence: Engineering/tools, QA automation, data science/live ops, governance/legal/security, then creative roles.

### Failure mode D: Source poisoning and prompt injection
Web sources can contain hostile instructions or low-trust claims. NotebookLM is not a secure sandbox.
