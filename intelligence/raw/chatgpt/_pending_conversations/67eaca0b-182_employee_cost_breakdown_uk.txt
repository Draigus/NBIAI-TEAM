To determine the **total weighted cost of headcount** for a UK startup and the **overhead as a percentage beyond base salary**, we need to consider **all statutory, optional, and practical costs** associated with employing someone.

Here's a breakdown of typical **employee cost components in the UK** (as of 2025, using updated but generalized values):

---

### **1. Base Salary**  
This is the employee's gross salary.  
**Example**: £40,000/year

---

### **2. Employer National Insurance Contributions (NICs)**  
- **13.8%** on earnings above the secondary threshold (£9,100/year)  
- **Calculation**:  
  £40,000 - £9,100 = £30,900  
  £30,900 × 13.8% = **£4,264.20**

---

### **3. Employer Pension Contributions**  
- Legal minimum: **3%** (based on qualifying earnings, £6,240 to £50,270)  
- Most offer: **3–5%**, or match up to a higher rate  
- **Example at 5%**:  
  Qualifying earnings: £40,000 - £6,240 = £33,760  
  £33,760 × 5% = **£1,688**

---

### **4. Holiday Pay (Already Included in Salary)**  
- 5.6 weeks (28 days including bank holidays) minimum  
- **No additional cost if pro-rated into salary**  
- If using day rates or contractors, then factored into pricing

---

### **5. Sick Pay & Parental Leave**  
- **Statutory Sick Pay (SSP)** is low and reclaimable in rare cases  
- Most startups absorb short absences as paid  
- **Approximate annualized buffer**: **£500–£1,000**/employee

---

### **6. Training & Development**  
- Highly variable. Startups often spend:  
  **£500–£2,000/year** per employee

---

### **7. Equipment & Software Licenses**  
- Laptop, monitor, peripherals: £1,200–£2,000 amortized over 2–3 years  
- Software (SaaS): £50–£150/month = **£600–£1,800/year**

---

### **8. Office Space / Remote Working Stipend**  
- Office: £4,000–£10,000/year per desk (London), less elsewhere  
- Remote: £300–£1,000/year stipend  

---

### **9. Other Overheads (Insurance, HR platforms, wellness, etc.)**  
- Spread cost: **£500–£1,500/year**/employee

---

## **Summary – Total Weighted Cost Example (for £40,000 Salary)**

| Cost Item                        | Value (Annual)       |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------|
| Base Salary                      | £40,000              |
| Employer NICs (13.8%)            | £4,264               |
| Employer Pension (5%)            | £1,688               |
| Training & Development           | £1,000               |
| Equipment & SaaS Licenses        | £1,200               |
| Office/Remote Stipend            | £1,000               |
| Other Overheads                  | £1,000               |
| **Total Cost**                   | **£50,152**          |

---

## **Overhead Beyond Salary**  
**Total Overhead** = £50,152 - £40,000 = **£10,152**  
**Percentage Overhead** = (£10,152 / £40,000) × 100 = **25.38%**

---

### **Conclusion:**
- The **total weighted cost** of a £40,000 employee is **~£50,152/year**
- The **average overhead** beyond salary is **~25–30%**, varying by:
  - Pension level
  - Equipment/SaaS needs
  - Office vs. remote
  - Company perks or benefits
