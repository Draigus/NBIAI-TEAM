[ASSISTANT]
It can be, depending on a few factors. The **underlying consulting work** itself doesn’t automatically cost more just because your UK company is billing a Greek company — but the **total cost to the client** can be higher due to:  

**1. VAT treatment**  
- If your UK company is VAT-registered and the Greek client is a business with a valid EU VAT number, the service is usually **zero-rated in the UK** under the reverse charge rules. The Greek company accounts for Greek VAT at their local rate. This means they pay VAT in Greece (potentially reclaimable if they’re VAT-registered).  
- If they don’t have a valid VAT number, UK VAT may need to be charged, which adds cost unless they can reclaim it.  

**2. Currency conversion**  
- If you bill in GBP and they pay from EUR, exchange rates can push up the effective cost for them.  
- International payment fees from their bank or yours may also add a few percent.  

**3. Withholding tax**  
- Greece applies withholding tax on certain cross-border service payments, unless reduced or eliminated under the UK–Greece tax treaty.  
- If withholding applies, they may deduct it from your invoice unless treaty relief is claimed in advance.  

**4. Compliance & admin costs**  
- Cross-border contracts often involve extra legal review, invoicing requirements (e.g., showing VAT IDs), and possibly translation or notarisation.  
- Some companies add a margin for these additional admin and payment delays.
