does a global talent visa cover dependents 

[ASSISTANT]
Yes, the Global Talent Visa (also known as the Global Talent Independent Program) does cover dependents. If you hold a Global Talent Visa, you can include your spouse or partner, as well as dependent children, in your application. They will be granted the same visa duration as the primary applicant, allowing them to live, work, and study in the country where the visa is issued. The specifics can vary depending on the country issuing the visa, so it's important to check the relevant immigration authority's guidelines for detailed information.

in the uk how long can a student stay inthe country after completing thier diploma

[ASSISTANT]
In the UK, after completing a diploma or degree, a student can typically stay for the following durations:

1. **Standard Tier 4 (General) Student Visa**: After completing their course, students are usually allowed to stay in the UK for up to **4 months** beyond the end date of their program. This applies to courses that last 12 months or longer.

2. **Graduate Route Visa**: Students who have successfully completed an eligible degree at a UK university can apply for a Graduate Visa. This visa allows them to stay in the UK for up to:
   - **2 years** if they completed a bachelor's degree or master's degree.
   - **3 years** if they completed a PhD or other doctoral qualification.

This allows graduates to work or look for work during that period without needing to be sponsored by an employer. 

It's important to note that these durations can vary, and you should always check the most up-to-date information from the UK government or immigration services.

if you have a work visa but loose your job how long do you have to find another one in the UK

[ASSISTANT]
If you lose your job while on a work visa in the UK, the length of time you have to find another job depends on the type of visa you hold:

### **Skilled Worker Visa (formerly Tier 2)**
If you lose your job while on a Skilled Worker Visa, your visa will usually be curtailed (shortened) by the UK Home Office. Typically, you will have **60 days** from the date of the curtailment notice or until the end of your visa, whichever is shorter, to find another job and have your new employer sponsor you. During this period, you can stay in the UK, but you must either:

- Find a new job and have your new employer apply for a new Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS) for you; or
- Make arrangements to leave the UK if you cannot find a new job.

If you don't find another job within this period, you'll need to leave the UK before your visa expires.

### **Other Work Visas**
For other work visas, such as the Tier 5 (Temporary Worker) visa, the rules are similar, but the specific time you have might vary. It's essential to check the specific conditions of your visa and seek advice if your situation is complex.
