[ASSISTANT]
No, a child over the age of 18 cannot generally be considered a dependent on a Tier 2 (now called a Skilled Worker) visa in the UK. The UK immigration rules specify that only children under the age of 18 can be included as dependents. 

However, if a child was already in the UK as a dependent before turning 18 and remains in the UK, they might be able to continue as a dependent in certain circumstances, but this is usually assessed on a case-by-case basis and may require special consideration or exceptions. does an education visa count towards ILR in the uk

[ASSISTANT]
No, time spent in the UK on a Student Visa (formerly known as a Tier 4 Visa) does not generally count towards the qualifying period for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) in the UK. 

ILR typically requires a continuous period of lawful residence in the UK under a visa category that leads to settlement, such as a Skilled Worker Visa (formerly Tier 2), Spouse Visa, or other eligible visa categories. Student visas are typically excluded from this calculation.

However, the time spent on a student visa may count towards ILR if you switch to a qualifying visa category and meet other specific requirements.

if you switch to a tier 3 visa from a tier 4 visa does your tier 4 time count towards settlement

[ASSISTANT]
There is no "Tier 3" visa in the UK immigration system. The Tier 3 category was initially intended for low-skilled workers, but it was never implemented.

If you are referring to switching from a Tier 4 (Student) visa to another visa category, such as a Skilled Worker visa (previously Tier 2), here's how it works:

When you switch from a Tier 4 visa to a Skilled Worker visa, the time spent on the Tier 4 visa **does not** count towards the qualifying period for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR). Only the time spent on a visa that leads directly to settlement, such as a Skilled Worker visa, will count towards the 5-year residency requirement needed for ILR.

For example, if you spent 2 years on a Tier 4 visa and then switched to a Skilled Worker visa, you would need to spend an additional 5 years on the Skilled Worker visa to qualify for ILR.
