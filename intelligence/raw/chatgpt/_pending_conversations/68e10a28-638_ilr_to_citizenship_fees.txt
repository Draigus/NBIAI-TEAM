If we have ILR in the UK, indefinite leave to remain, do we have to pay money to become citizens?

[ASSISTANT]
Short answer: yes. ILR does not convert to citizenship automatically. You have to apply for naturalisation and pay a fee.

- **Adult naturalisation fee:** £1,735 total, which includes the £130 citizenship ceremony fee. There is no extra charge for biometrics. citeturn0search1turn0search4  
- **Children (if applying to register them):** £1,214 per child. Some children can get a fee waiver if the family cannot afford the fee. citeturn0search3  
- **Fee waivers for adults:** Generally not available for naturalisation. The formal fee-waiver route is aimed at certain child registrations only. citeturn1search5turn0search13  
- **Possible extra costs:** If you still need them, the Life in the UK Test is £50 and an approved English test is usually around £150. If you already did these for ILR, you can normally reuse them. citeturn1search2
