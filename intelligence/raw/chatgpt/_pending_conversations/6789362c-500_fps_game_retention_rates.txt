The **average retention rate** for an **FPS (First-Person Shooter) video game** varies based on factors like platform, multiplayer vs. single-player focus, and monetization model. However, here are some industry benchmarks:

### **General Retention Rates for FPS Games**
Retention is usually measured as **D1 (Day 1), D7 (Day 7), and D30 (Day 30)**, representing the percentage of players who return after those days.

- **D1 Retention:** **35-50%** (Casual FPS) | **50-60%** (Competitive FPS)
- **D7 Retention:** **15-30%** (Casual FPS) | **30-40%** (Competitive FPS)
- **D30 Retention:** **5-15%** (Casual FPS) | **15-25%** (Competitive FPS)
- **D90 Retention:** **1-5%** (Casual FPS) | **5-15%** (Competitive FPS)

### **Key Factors That Affect Retention**
1. **Multiplayer vs. Single-player**
   - **Multiplayer FPS (e.g., Call of Duty, Valorant, Apex Legends)** tends to have higher retention due to social engagement and progression systems.
   - **Single-player FPS (e.g., Doom, Far Cry)** has lower long-term retention but can have high engagement during the initial weeks.

2. **Live Service & Content Updates**
   - **Games-as-a-Service (GaaS) FPS** titles with **battle passes, regular updates, and events** (e.g., Warzone, Rainbow Six Siege) retain players longer.
   - **Lack of new content** quickly reduces retention.

3. **Skill Progression & Matchmaking**
   - Well-balanced **ranked systems, skill-based matchmaking (SBMM), and rewarding progression** help keep players engaged.

4. **Game Monetization Model**
   - **Free-to-play FPS (e.g., Fortnite, Warzone)** typically has **higher retention** due to a low entry barrier.
   - **Premium FPS (e.g., Battlefield, Doom)** sees a **sharp decline after 1-2 months** unless there’s post-launch content.

5. **Community & Competitive Scene**
   - A **strong esports presence** (CS:GO, Valorant) leads to **higher long-term retention**.
   - A **thriving player-driven community** (modding, custom servers) extends longevity.

### **Comparisons to Other Genres**
- **Casual mobile games**: D30 retention is **<10%**.
- **MMORPGs**: Higher D30 retention (~20-30%) due to long-term progression.
- **Strategy & MOBAs**: Similar to FPS, but high-skill games like LoL/Dota 2 often have **better long-term retention**.
