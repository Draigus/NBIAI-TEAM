# Video Game Metrics and Key Performance Indicators

## Gameplay and performance metrics  
- **DPS (Damage per Second):** The average damage dealt by a weapon or character per second【22†L177-L178】. DPS is used across many action games (MMOs, shooters, MOBAs) to compare offensive power. (Applicable on all platforms.)  
- **DPM (Damage per Minute):** Similar to DPS, this measures average damage dealt per minute【44†L237-L244】. It provides a longer-term view of damage output (used in RPGs, shooters, etc.). (All platforms.)  
- **K/D Ratio (Kill/Death Ratio):** The number of enemy kills divided by the number of player deaths【49†L1971-L1974】. Higher K/D indicates better combat performance, commonly tracked in PvP shooters. (All platforms.)  
- **KDA (Kill/Death/Assist Ratio):** A variation of K/D that also counts assists【49†L1976-L1979】. Calculated as (kills+assists)/deaths, it reflects overall contribution in team games (especially MOBAs and shooters). (All platforms.)  
- **Win Rate (Winning Percentage):** The percentage of matches a player or team wins【56†L299-L302】. It shows how often games are won out of total games played, serving as a basic success metric in competitive games. (All platforms.)  
- **Time to Kill (TTK):** The average time (usually in seconds) required to eliminate an opponent【50†L73-L77】. TTK is used in game design/balancing to gauge weapon effectiveness and gameplay pace. (All platforms.)  

## Business and monetization metrics  
- **DAU (Daily Active Users):** The number of unique players who play the game in a day【55†L199-L203】. DAU measures daily engagement and is crucial for tracking active userbase growth. (All platforms.)  
- **MAU (Monthly Active Users):** The number of unique players who play at least once in a 30-day period【55†L214-L218】. Used together with DAU to compute retention and growth. (All platforms.)  
- **ARPDAU (Average Revenue Per Daily Active User):** The average revenue earned per active user each day. (Mobile-centric term, used in F2P analytics.)  
- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User):** Total revenue divided by total users over a period【40†L370-L374】. It measures how much each user is worth on average (across all users, including non-payers). (All platforms.)  
- **ARPPU (Average Revenue Per Paying User):** Total revenue from purchases divided by the number of paying users【1†L210-L214】. Indicates how much paying players spend on average. (All platforms.)  
- **LTV (Lifetime Value):** The total expected revenue from a player over their entire lifespan with the game【40†L372-L374】. LTV is projected from retention and spending patterns and guides marketing spend. (All platforms.)  
- **Retention Rate:** The percentage of players who return to play after install (often measured at day-1, day-7, day-30, etc.)【55†L294-L301】. For example, Day-7 retention is the percent of new players who open the game on day 7 after install. (All platforms.)  
- **Churn Rate:** The rate at which players stop playing (i.e. 1 – retention). It measures player drop-off over time【40†L376-L379】. (All platforms.)  
- **Conversion Rate:** The percentage of players who take a desired action (e.g. make a purchase)【40†L376-L379】. In F2P games this often means percentage of users who buy something. (All platforms.)  
- **Session Length:** The average duration of a single play session【55†L259-L268】. Indicates engagement intensity (longer sessions often mean more engaged players). (All platforms.)  
- **User Acquisition Cost (CPI – Cost per Install):** The marketing spend required to acquire one new user【55†L168-L174】. Primarily a mobile metric (“Cost per Install”), useful for budgeting UA campaigns. (Primarily mobile.)  

## Backend and development metrics  
- **FPS (Frames per Second / Frame Rate):** The number of screen frames rendered per second【38†L345-L353】. A higher FPS yields smoother gameplay; low or inconsistent FPS causes choppy experience. (Relevant to PC/console/VR performance.)  
- **Load Time:** Time taken for the game or level to load【40†L337-L343】. Shorter load times improve user experience; measured from launch or level start to when play begins. (All platforms.)  
- **Crash Rate:** Frequency of game crashes (often crashes per session or per play hours)【40†L337-L343】. High crash rate signals instability. (All platforms.)  
- **Error Rate:** Frequency of client-side or server-side errors encountered during play【40†L339-L341】. (All platforms.)  
- **Server Uptime:** Percentage of time the game servers are operational【40†L353-L356】. A key reliability metric for online games. (All platforms with server components.)  
- **Latency (Ping):** The network delay between a player’s action and the server response【40†L353-L356】. Low latency is critical for real-time games; high latency causes lag. (Online games on all platforms, especially PC/console/multiplayer.)  
- **Packet Loss:** The percentage of data packets lost in network transmission【53†L430-L434】. High packet loss leads to lag, rubber-banding, or dropped actions. (Online gaming on all platforms.)  
- **Bandwidth Utilization:** The amount of network bandwidth used by the game【53†L430-L434】. Especially important for streaming/cloud gaming to ensure smooth video. (Cloud gaming, online console/PC.)  
- **CPU Utilization:** Percentage of CPU capacity used by the game【53†L379-L384】. High sustained CPU usage can indicate performance issues. (All platforms.)  
- **GPU Utilization:** Percentage of GPU capacity used【53†L379-L384】. Reflects graphics load; maxed-out GPU can cause lower FPS. (All platforms with GPU.)  
- **Memory Usage:** Amount of system RAM the game uses【53†L394-L399】. Memory leaks or excessive usage can cause stutters or crashes. (All platforms.)  
- **Power/Heat Metrics (Mobile/VR):** Power consumption and thermal throttling data【53†L411-L419】. In mobile/VR, monitoring battery drain and overheating is important for user experience. (Mobile/VR.)  

## Streaming and viewership metrics  
- **Average CCV (Concurrent Viewers):** The average number of viewers watching a live stream at one time【41†L46-L53】. (Streamers on platforms like Twitch/YouTube; not a game metric per se, but a streaming metric.)  
- **Peak CCV:** The highest number of concurrent viewers during a stream【41†L61-L68】.  
- **Hours Watched:** Total viewing time (sum of all viewers’ watch durations)【41†L81-L87】. Often reported as “total watch time” or “hours watched.”  
- **Average Watch Time per Viewer:** Average time each viewer spends watching (e.g. “average time watched – live” from streaming analytics【41†L100-L112】).  
- **Subscriber/Follower Count:** Number of followers or subscribers on a streaming platform, and its growth rate【41†L146-L154】. Indicates channel popularity (Twitch, YouTube, etc.).  
- **Video Views:** The count of how many times a stream or video was viewed【41†L181-L184】. Useful for on-demand replays or highlights.  
- **Viewer Retention:** The viewership over the course of a stream (a retention graph showing how viewers join/leave)【41†L125-L134】. Aids in spotting which segments keep or lose audience. (Streaming platforms only.)  

Each metric above is widely used in the game industry and may apply across platforms (PC, console, mobile, VR, cloud) unless noted otherwise. Where applicable, we have flagged metrics that are essentially mobile-specific (e.g. CPI) or streaming-specific.

- eCPM (Effective CPM): revenue per thousand ad impressions.

- Fill-Rate: served ads ÷ ad requests.  
- Impression-Share: actual impressions ÷ potential impressions.  
- Ad-IMP/DAU: ads shown per active user daily.  
- RV-Views (Rewarded-Video Views): completed rewarded videos [mobile only].  
- RV-Completion%: completed rewarded videos ÷ starts.  
- Offerwall-IMP: offerwall impressions [mobile only].

## 3. Backend, technical quality and operations

- FPS (Frames per Second): frames rendered each second.  
- Avg-FPS: mean FPS over a session.  
- Med-FPS: median FPS.  
- 95-FPS: 95th-percentile FPS (tail performance).  
- Frame-Time (FT): milliseconds per frame.  
- FT-Var: frame-time variance (smoothness).  
- FD-PM (Frame Drops per Minute): dropped frames each minute.  
- Stutter-Rate: noticeable hitches per minute.  
- Boot-Time: seconds from launch to title screen.  
- Scene-Load-T: seconds to load a level.  
- Asset-Load-T: time to stream assets.  
- Time-to-First-Input: time until player can control character.  
- Crash-Rate: crashes per session.  
- Crash-Free-%: percent of sessions with zero crashes.  
- ANR-Rate (Application Not Responding): freeze incidents per session [Android mobile].  
- Exception-Rate: logged exceptions per session.  
- Error-Rate: client or server errors per session.  
- Mem-Use: average RAM used.  
- Peak-Mem: maximum RAM used.  
- Mem-Leak-Inc: sessions showing rising memory trend.  
- CPU-Util%: average CPU utilisation.  
- GPU-Util%: average GPU utilisation.  
- GPU-Pacing-Var: variance between GPU and CPU frame pace.  
- Battery-Drain/Min: percent battery lost per minute [mobile / VR only].  
- Thermal-Events: thermal-throttle occurrences [mobile / VR only].  
- Disk-IO: megabytes read-write per second.  
- Patch-Size: megabytes of update payload.  
- Update-Adopt-%: users on latest build.  
- CCU (Concurrent Connected Users): players online at one moment.  
- PCCU (Peak CCU): highest CCU in a period.  
- Server-Uptime%: time servers are available.  
- Latency-RTT: average round-trip network time.  
- Jitter: variation in latency.  
- Packet-Loss%: lost packets / total packets.  
- Bandwidth-Use: kilobits per second per client.  
- Queue-Time: matchmaking wait in seconds.  
- Disconnect-Rate: unexpected disconnects per session.  
- Reconnect-Success%: successful reconnects / disconnects.  
- API-Err-Rate: failed API calls per minute.  
- DB-Query-Latency: ms per database query.  
- Build-Freq: deployments per month.  
- MTTR (Mean Time to Recovery): average downtime after failure.  
- MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures): average uptime between failures.
