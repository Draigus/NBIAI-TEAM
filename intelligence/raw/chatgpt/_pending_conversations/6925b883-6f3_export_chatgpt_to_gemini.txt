You can export almost everything from ChatGPT, but you cannot do a true “one-click migration” into Gemini that perfectly recreates your separate chat threads, memory, and tool context. The practical way to “not lose everything” is: (1) make an official ChatGPT export as your master archive, then (2) create a curated set of documents that Gemini can reliably use as reference.

## 1) Export everything from ChatGPT (official)
You have two official routes:

**A. From ChatGPT settings**
1. Open ChatGPT (web).
2. Profile icon → **Settings** → **Data Controls** → **Export data** → confirm.
3. You’ll get an email link (it expires after 24 hours). citeturn1view0

**B. From OpenAI’s Privacy Portal**
You can also request “Download my data” via the Privacy Portal flow. citeturn1view0

What you get is a ZIP that includes a browser view (`chat.html`) plus other account data. citeturn1view0  
OpenAI also references a `conversations.json` in exports (used for chat migration between ChatGPT accounts). citeturn1view1

## 2) Understand what “move to Gemini” can mean (pick the right target)
There are two realistic goals:

### Goal 1: “I just want everything saved somewhere safe”
Keep the ZIP and its contents as your canonical backup. This is the only way to be confident you did not lose historical material.

### Goal 2: “I want Gemini to use my ChatGPT history as working context”
Gemini does not offer an official “import ChatGPT history” feature. Instead, you give Gemini files to read.

Gemini Apps lets you upload files to a chat (device or Google Drive). Limits are typically **up to 10 files per prompt**, with **100 MB per file for most file types** (video differs), and ZIPs have their own limits. citeturn3view0

## 3) The no-drama migration workflow (recommended)
### Step A: Make a “Gemini Knowledge Pack” folder
From your ChatGPT export (and any key chat threads), create a small set of documents that capture the value you actually reuse, for example:
- “Personal preferences and rules”
- “Active projects summary”
- “Key decisions and numbers”
- “Glossary, links, source list”
- “Best prompts / system prompts you rely on”

Save these as PDF or plain text, then upload them into Gemini.

### Step B: Upload into Gemini in batches
In Gemini (web): click **Add files**, then upload. citeturn3view0  
If your export is huge, split it into multiple smaller files because Gemini has file-count and size limits per prompt. citeturn3view0

### Step C: Build an index inside Gemini
In a new Gemini chat, upload your “Knowledge Pack” and ask Gemini to:
- create an index of what’s in the files
- list “where to find X” pointers
- summarise into a single master brief you can re-upload later

## 4) Optional: convert `conversations.json` into readable chunks
If you want one-file-per-chat in Markdown/PDF, you can use third-party exporters that ingest `conversations.json` and output nicer formats (example: an open-source “chatgpt-exporter” project). citeturn4search18  
If you go this route: treat it like installing any third-party tool. Only use ones you trust.

## 5) Privacy gotchas to avoid
- In Gemini, “sharing a chat” creates a public link that anyone with the link can read and reshare, so do not use that for sensitive archives. citeturn1view2
- Uploading your archive into Gemini means Google processes those files; if you have sensitive content, keep the master archive local and only upload curated, non-sensitive extracts.

### Capability first (hard truth)
There is **no single official “import everything from ChatGPT into Gemini and recreate it as native Gemini chats with all attachments”** feature. The closest you can get is: export everything from ChatGPT into an archive, then feed that archive to Gemini as reference files. ChatGPT’s export gives you a ZIP that includes `chat.html`, and OpenAI also documents exports that include `conversations.json` among other files. citeturn1view0turn4view0

Gemini can only ingest what you upload or what it can access from Drive, and uploads have limits (notably 10 files per prompt, 100 MB per non-video file, and ZIPs can contain at most 10 files and cannot include audio/video). citeturn1view1

### Best single option (the practical “make Gemini primary” path)
**Build a Google Drive “Knowledge Base” from your ChatGPT export, then use Gemini by attaching the right project pack(s) on demand.** This is the only approach that (a) preserves everything, and (b) makes Gemini usable day-to-day without pretending it can truly “import” your entire ChatGPT account.

## Brief plan
1) Export everything from ChatGPT once (your master backup).  
2) Convert that export into a Drive folder structured by project, plus a small number of “project packs” (files Gemini can actually read within limits).  
3) In Gemini, work from those project packs, attaching the relevant ones when you need them.

## Deliverable: exact steps
1) **Export from ChatGPT**
- ChatGPT settings → Data Controls → Export, download the ZIP (link expires after 24 hours). citeturn1view0
- Expect at least `chat.html`, and per OpenAI’s migration note, exports can include `conversations.json` too. citeturn1view0turn4view0

2) **Unzip and create Drive structure**
- Make a Drive folder: `ChatGPT Archive (raw export)` and upload the ZIP unchanged (this is your “never lose anything” vault).
- Make a second folder: `Gemini Knowledge Base`
  - Subfolders per project: `Sarge`, `Hule`, `Foundry`, `Salary Atlas`, etc.
  - In each project folder, create a small set of files Gemini can consume reliably:
    - `00_Project_Index.md` (what exists, where it is)
    - `01_Decisions_and_Numbers.md`
    - `02_Best_Prompts.md`
    - `03_Key_Deliverables/` (PDFs, docs, spreadsheets you care about)

3) **Respect Gemini ingestion limits while loading**
- Upload to Gemini in chunks: max **10 files per prompt**, **100 MB per non-video file**. citeturn1view1
- Do not rely on ZIP as a loophole: ZIPs are still max **10 files** and **100 MB**, and cannot include audio/video. citeturn1view1
- Result: you will not “upload your entire ChatGPT export” in one shot. You will upload per-project packs, or store everything in Drive and attach what matters.

4) **Make Gemini primary (operational habit)**
- For each project, keep one “current pack” file under 100 MB, and attach it whenever you start work in Gemini.
- If you want Gemini to reference uploaded files across more chats, Google notes higher limits may apply with paid plans. citeturn1view1

## Key risk (files)
OpenAI’s export article does not clearly promise that every file you ever uploaded is present in a convenient, re-importable way, it just says the ZIP includes `chat.html` and “other data on file.” citeturn1view0  
So you should verify what’s inside your ZIP. If some historical attachments are missing, there is no magic bulk “re-download every attachment ever” button inside Gemini to fix that. Your reliable preservation is the ChatGPT export plus any manual recovery you do.
