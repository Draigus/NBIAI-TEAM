## 2 | Metric Quality & Consistency

| KPI | Typical Issue in the Sheet | Why It Matters |
|-----|---------------------------|----------------|
| **Retention (D1/D7/D30)** | Different vendors define an “active user” differently (e.g., open vs session start).  Sheet mixes sources without harmonising. | A two-point swing on D1 retention can shift CPI targets by 15–20 %. |
| **ARPU / ARPPU / LTV** | ARPU windows (Day-90, Day-365, lifetime) are mixed.  Some values D90, others full LTV estimates. | Teams need matching time horizons to model payback curves. |
| **ROAS** | Uses blended cohorts (hybrid IAA/IAP vs IAP-only) without flagging. | Hidden ad-rev in denominator or numerator causes apples-to-oranges UA budgeting errors. |
| **CPI** | Pulls global CPI but later references NA as “higher” without giving actual NA/EU/ROW splits. | UA managers need CPI *by geo* and *by platform* to set bid caps. |
| **Session Length / Play Time** | Averages, not medians.  Mobile session distribution is power-law; median is a truer central value. | Designers tuning FTUE pacing might mis-time tutorial gates. |

## 5 | Actionability & Storytelling

*What can a studio actually change* based on this sheet?

- **No statistical confidence**: without confidence intervals or quartiles, product teams can’t tell if they’re actually *beating* the median or sitting inside noise.
- **No trendlines**: CFOs care about direction; a static median hides that D1 retention for puzzle is down ~3 pp post-ATT while hyper-casual CPI is up >40 % YoY.
- **No cross-metric relationships**: ROAS depends on CPI, retention, and payer conversion; table treats them siloed.  A modelling tab (Excel calc or Looker Studio) is needed.

---

## Quick Recommendations

1. **Disclose metadata:** sample size (n ), date range, data source per metric.  
2. **Swap max/min “outlier” to 90th/10th percentile** (or at least mark “single-title extreme”).  
3. **Add PC/console/Steam benchmarks:** pull CCU, median daily peak, and attach MAU ratios.  
4. **Break out telegram/web separately** with caveats (smaller sample, ad-only rev).  
5. **Add a trends row:** YoY change 2020→2024 so decision-makers see velocity, not just snapshot.  
6. **Bundle metrics into a simple LTV = f(retention, ARPPU) calculator** so UA and finance can scenario-plan.  
7. **Flag ATT / GDPR policy shocks** (iOS 14.5, Google Privacy Sandbox) as step-changes; past medians before/after are not interchangeable.  
8. **Provide clear “good / great” bands** per metric to help designers gauge success without drowning in numbers.

---

### Bottom Line

The draft provides a **solid first-pass yard-stick for mobile F2P**, but to serve as a *master* KPI cheat sheet for every platform and genre it needs:

- deeper PC/console figures,  
- genre-specific granularity,  
- transparent methodology, and  
- confidence bands & trendlines.  

Addressing those points will elevate the sheet from “handy reference” to a decision-grade tool executives can trust for green-lighting features, forecasting UA budgets, and sizing live-ops targets.
