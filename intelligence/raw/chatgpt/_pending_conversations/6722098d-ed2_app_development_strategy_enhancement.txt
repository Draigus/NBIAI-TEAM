The time it takes to change a production line in a manufacturing plant can vary significantly depending on the complexity of the line, the level of automation, and the type of product being manufactured. Here are some common factors and general timelines:

### 1. **Quick Changeover (SMED) Techniques**
   - **Single-Minute Exchange of Dies (SMED)**: Many manufacturing plants implement SMED to reduce changeover time, often getting it down to less than 10 minutes per line. This method involves streamlining setup tasks and often uses specialized tooling to make the switch more efficient. SMED techniques are especially common in industries with high product variability, such as automotive and electronics【47†source】.

### 2. **Simple vs. Complex Lines**
   - **Simple Assembly Lines**: For straightforward production lines with minimal customization (like basic packaging or labeling lines), changeovers can take anywhere from **a few minutes to an hour**. These lines typically involve simple adjustments to machinery or tooling.
   - **Complex Automated Lines**: In highly automated or intricate manufacturing setups, such as those used in automotive or electronics manufacturing, changeovers can take longer, ranging from **a few hours to an entire shift (8+ hours)**. These require adjustments to automated systems, robotic configurations, and quality control processes.

### 3. **Product Type and Customization Requirements**
   - **Flexible Manufacturing Systems (FMS)**: In plants equipped with FMS, changeovers can be faster because the equipment is designed to handle multiple product types with minimal reconfiguration. Changeovers can range from **minutes to a few hours** depending on the specific product and line design.
   - **Highly Specialized Production Lines**: For complex or customized products (e.g., pharmaceuticals or aerospace components), changeovers can be lengthy, often taking **several hours to a full day** due to stringent regulatory requirements and thorough cleaning protocols.

### 4. **Industry Examples**
   - **Automotive Manufacturing**: Many automotive plants aim for changeovers under an hour, but for more complex reconfigurations, such as shifting from one vehicle model to another, changeovers might extend to several hours or even overnight.
   - **Food and Beverage**: In food manufacturing, where sanitation is critical, changeovers can take from **2 to 8 hours** depending on the line’s complexity and the need for thorough cleaning to prevent cross-contamination.

### **Conclusion**
