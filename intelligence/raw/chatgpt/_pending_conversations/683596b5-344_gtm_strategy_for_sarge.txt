Fortunately, the Telegram viral mechanics (referrals, etc.) can substitute for heavy ad spend if executed smartly (as Hamster Kombat did with minimal marketing budget by leveraging viral referrals【7†L136-L143】). Second, SARGE should emphasize its **sustainable tokenomics and player retention** when communicating with potential partners or investors, as the era of “build it and they will come (for the token)” is over. DappRadar’s 2024 report noted that after the P2E hype subsided, investors now favor games with *“sustainable tokenomics and player-centric design”*【33†L37-L40】. We will highlight SARGE’s balanced economy (see Tokenomics section) and rich gameplay as selling points to differentiate from the failed P2E models.

Importantly, **market conditions have started improving in Q2 2025**. As the overall crypto market rebounded (Bitcoin reaching new highs above $65k in May 2025)【55†L163-L171】【55†L167-L174】, gaming tokens saw renewed interest. In mid-May 2025, industry observers declared *“crypto gaming is so back,”* as tokens like Axie Infinity’s AXS jumped ~12% in a week and Sandbox’s SAND ~9%【55†L155-L163】. Trading volumes for top gaming tokens spiked 25–35% concurrently【55†L159-L167】. On-chain metrics showed **daily active users in blockchain games up 40%** since the start of May【55†L169-L177】. This suggests a seasonal uptick: historically, crypto often rallies in Q2/Q4, and it appears gaming projects are getting a second look from the community as the macro sentiment turns more positive. For SARGE, launching in mid-2025 could be advantageous, aligning with this renewed enthusiasm. Our strategy will capitalize on the *“risk-on”* sentiment returning to crypto – meaning retail players might be more willing to try new Web3 games and investors more open to strategic partnerships.

That said, **lessons from 2022–2024 remain**. The collapse of Axie Infinity’s economy is a cautionary tale known to all: Axie peaked at 2.7 million DAU fueled by play-to-earn rewards, but was minting billions of tokens with insufficient sinks, leading to a 99% crash in token price【66†L31-L39】. Many crypto gamers and investors are now skeptical of unsustainable high-APR reward models. They look instead for games that can attract *organic engagement*. Notably, even some games that survived the bear market did so by focusing on gameplay – for example, **Splinterlands** (a blockchain card game) implemented better token burn mechanics, yet still saw its asset market cap fall by ~50% over 2023-24 because the game experience didn’t significantly grow the player base【66†L40-L48】. The takeaway for macro strategy: **hype cycles are shorter**, and players will churn if there’s no genuine fun. SARGE’s GTM cannot solely rely on “earn $$” messaging; it must highlight competitive gameplay, community and *intrinsic* rewards (like status, progression, and social enjoyment).

Investor behavior also varies by region and market cycle. A study by Addressable (a Web3 marketing firm) found that acquiring users via crypto gaming is currently **the most expensive among Web3 verticals**, with a median **cost per wallet user (CPW) of $8.74** for gaming campaigns【41†L104-L113】【41†L108-L116】. (In comparison, DeFi user acquisition has a median CPW around $2.79【41†L141-L149】.) This high cost is partly because in a bear market, fewer new users are willingly jumping into crypto games – raising the cost to convert the holdouts. Interestingly, the same data showed this trend was *global*: *“the amount of money it costs to onboard someone into web3 increases during bear markets and decreases during bulls”*, across almost every region except Southeast Asia【41†L127-L136】. For example, in 2024 the U.S. and Western Europe saw CPWs surge 4x to 27x higher as interest waned【41†L151-L159】. In contrast, emerging markets remained somewhat more cost-effective, and Southeast Asia stayed relatively stable in interest. This informs SARGE’s GTM focus: we should allocate marketing spend where we get more bang for buck (e.g. Southeast Asia, Eastern Europe, LATAM) especially if Western audiences are expensive to acquire in the current climate. We will also emphasize **community-driven growth** (referral programs, guild partnerships, etc.) to keep paid CPAs low.

In summary, the macro outlook for Q1–Q2 2025 is cautiously optimistic. While funding is down from last year, user interest is coming back as crypto markets recover. Investors are smarter and demand sustainable designs, and user acquisition requires savvy strategies to control costs. The SARGE Universe will approach launch with these realities in mind – combining a lean marketing budget with viral mechanics, targeting high-affinity regions, and messaging that resonates in a post-hype era (i.e. emphasizing fun and community as much as potential earnings).

## Telegram Wallet Integration & Onboarding Best Practices

A smooth and secure wallet integration is **critical** for a Web3 Telegram game. The SARGE Universe will leverage Telegram’s native TON wallet and strive to make blockchain aspects invisible but reliable for users. As noted, Telegram’s built-in wallet reached **50 million users by May 2025**, indicating a large portion of Telegram’s audience is already set up for crypto transactions【28†L17-L24】. This integration is mature: after being fully embedded in late 2024, the TON wallet allows instant transfers by @username and supports TON, USDT, and other tokens in chat. For SARGE, adopting TON (The Open Network) as the underlying blockchain is logical given Telegram’s endorsement – TON is fast, low-cost, and deeply integrated into the app’s mini-app framework【13†L93-L100】. We will follow standards set by previous games: using TON for the game’s token and NFTs means users can receive tokens in their Telegram wallet without any browser extension or external app.

**Onboarding flow:** When a new user starts SARGE via the Telegram bot link, the game will use Telegram’s Seamless WebApp login – instantly authenticating the user with their Telegram ID (no separate account needed). Upon first earning or needing a wallet action, we will invoke the TON Wallet API to either connect an existing wallet or auto-create a new TON wallet for that user (if they haven’t enabled it yet). In most cases, Telegram already silently created a TON wallet for the user when they set up payments or played other games【7†L113-L119】. Our goal is that the user **never has to copy/paste an address or manage private keys** at the start. They will have a custodied key via Telegram’s wallet, which they can later back up if they choose – but for day-one, they simply see “you earned 100 SARGE tokens!” and those tokens are already associated with their Telegram account.

We will implement **TON Sign-In** for any web-based components (if any external site for marketplace or account settings is used) so that users can authenticate by signing a message with their Telegram TON wallet – again avoiding any email/password friction. This best practice is widely adopted in TON’s ecosystem and ensures a unified identity between the game and Telegram.

**Security and fraud prevention:** Given the ease of creating Telegram accounts, botting and multi-accounting are risks. We will integrate **Telegram’s Verified Login** (if available) or phone-number verification gates for certain high-reward actions to prevent abuse. Additionally, using **TON’s features like smart contract wallets** could help – e.g., maybe binding certain in-game assets to a wallet that has a minimal TON balance or is older than X days, as a proxy for a “real” user. We’ll also tap into community tools: TON’s ecosystem provides analytics to detect unusual wallet activity (TON Stat etc.) which can help flag bots【27†L9-L17】. This is crucial for a fair Play-to-Earn economy.

From a **user experience standpoint**, Telegram has shown that abstracting blockchain yields higher adoption. Features like **username-based transfers and no-fee microtransactions** mean users treat tokens almost like in-game points【28†L25-L33】. SARGE’s UI will mimic this: when a player earns SARGE tokens or NFTs, it will appear as a simple balance or item in their inventory. Under the hood, it’s recorded on TON, but the player only needs to use familiar game menus. If they choose to trade or withdraw assets, we’ll provide an easy interface to do so via the TON Wallet bot (which can handle swaps or transfers out to exchanges). 

One emerging standard is enabling **direct fiat purchase** of tokens or NFTs via Telegram’s payment providers (which include Apple Pay, Google Pay, Visa, etc.)【29†L169-L177】【29†L170-L178】. Telegram mini-apps support third-party payment integration, meaning SARGE could allow a user to buy an NFT or in-game currency pack with a credit card, and behind the scenes it converts to crypto. We’ll explore this for monetization (for example, selling a starter pack of NFT units for a fiat price, where the user just confirms via Apple Pay inside Telegram – a flow that is now possible). This provides a Web2-like onboarding for those willing to pay but not deal with crypto exchanges. The key is making sure these purchases are seamlessly tied to the user’s TON wallet (most payment providers on Telegram handle the conversion and deposit of tokens via smart contract to the user’s wallet address).

**TON integration maturity:** TON blockchain itself has scaled well in 2024–25. At year-end 2024, TON had **12.4 million monthly active on-chain wallets (ATH)** and over 100M addresses created【27†L19-L27】【27†L37-L42】 – a testament to growth from Telegram. It’s a highly performant chain (sharded, fast finality) which so far has handled the likes of Hamster’s 300M account activity without major incident. By May 2025, some metrics show daily active TON wallets around 130k (down from a peak in late 2024)【27†L9-L13】, but this likely reflects a normalization after the airdrop rush. For SARGE, TON’s throughput and negligible fees mean even high-frequency in-game transactions (like battles yielding token rewards, NFT equipment trades, etc.) can be done on-chain without user frustration. We will use TON for critical asset and currency transactions, but for game state that doesn’t need to be on-chain (e.g., match outcomes that only matter for leaderboards), we’ll use off-chain databases to keep things responsive. This hybrid approach is standard to ensure **real-time gameplay isn’t bottlenecked by blockchain**.

To summarize, our wallet integration strategy is: **make blockchain invisible but beneficial**. New users play SARGE like any Telegram game; no extensions or seed phrases. But as they engage, they seamlessly accrue true ownership of assets (stored in their Telegram TON wallet). By following Telegram’s best practices and the successful patterns of earlier games, we ensure onboarding is frictionless, secure, and ready for Web3 scale. This foundation sets up SARGE to welcome both crypto veterans (who can dive right into token trading or NFT collecting if they want) and complete newcomers (who can play just for fun and gradually discover the crypto elements as an added bonus).

## Tokenomics Design & P2E Mechanisms

The SARGE Universe will implement a **sustainable, two-tier token economy** with a focus on long-term viability, learning from both successful and failed P2E models. Our goal is to avoid the **“inflationary death spiral”** that hit games like Axie Infinity, while still providing meaningful play-to-earn opportunities and a thriving marketplace for NFTs.

**Token Structure:** We propose a dual-token model:
- **$SARGE Token (fungible):** The primary in-game reward and utility token. Earned via gameplay (winning battles, completing quests, participating in events) and used for various in-game sinks (entry fees for tournaments, buying stamina or cosmetic upgrades, faction donations, etc.). **Supply:** Controlled inflation. Rather than unlimited minting, $SARGE emissions will be capped per day and adjust based on active users (to keep per-player earnings relatively stable). We will model this similar to Axie’s SLP but with dynamic rate limits. Also, **breeding or unit minting costs** in SARGE (if the game allows creating new NFT characters) will require burning $SARGE, creating a native demand for the token (Axie’s one saving grace was SLP had to be burned to breed Axies【40†L75-L83】). This introduces a feedback loop: growth in players drives demand for $SARGE (for new characters or upgrades), which stabilizes token value.
- **$WAR (governance/premium token):** A secondary token with a fixed supply (e.g., 1 billion) that is the governance token for SARGE’s universe and maybe used for high-level upgrades or staked for special rewards. This token would be distributed through an IDO or initial sale and also via community airdrops (similar to how Axie had AXS in addition to SLP). $WAR holders can vote on game direction (new factions, economic changes) and possibly receive a portion of marketplace fees. This token’s supply is largely predetermined, avoiding continuous inflation.

By separating the tokens, we ensure that the **utility token ($SARGE)** can inflate to reward players but is constantly needed/spent in the game, while the **governance token ($WAR)** carries long-term value and reflects the game’s overall success. Many newer P2E games, like *Illuvium* or *Guild of Guardians*, use a dual-token model for this reason – one token for gameplay economy and one for governance/value accrual – to mitigate the pressure that purely single-token systems face.

**NFT Integration:** NFTs in SARGE Universe will include collectible commanders, units, or item cards. These NFTs are core to gameplay but we will avoid upfront paywalls (players can get a starter set of basic units without purchase). NFTs primarily come into play as *enhancements or rare content*. For example, top players in tournaments might win NFT generals; seasonal battle passes could mint exclusive cosmetic NFTs; or players can merge/burn tokens to create higher-rarity NFT equipment. An **NFT marketplace** will be built into the Telegram mini-app (leveraging TON’s NFT standards). Importantly, **NFTs will have utility** – they aren’t just pictures, but confer in-game stats or access. This keeps demand organic (players want them to win, not just to speculate). We will, however, be cautious to not make it pay-to-win; any stat advantages from NFTs will be minor or balanced by matchmaking. NFTs also provide a monetization avenue: a **5% marketplace fee** on NFT trades (with a portion possibly going to $WAR stakers or back to a play-to-earn reward pool).

**Earning Mechanisms:** SARGE’s play-to-earn will incorporate **active and passive earning**:
- Active: Winning PvP battles yields $SARGE tokens (within daily limits). Completing daily missions or a campaign chapter yields tokens. Special events (e.g., a faction war or clan contest) have prize pools in $SARGE or even $WAR.
- Passive: We might allow players to stake certain NFTs or stake $SARGE to earn a yield (but capped, and source of yield is e.g. a portion of marketplace fees or a reward pool). However, pure yield farming will be minimal to avoid mercenary capital – focus is on *playing* to earn, not idle staking.

**Sustainability Measures:** A key lesson from earlier P2E is to *align incentives for players to keep tokens in the ecosystem*. We will implement:
  - **Sinks for $SARGE:** Every meaningful action should have a token sink. E.g., entering a weekly championship might require a small $SARGE fee (which is burned or put into prize pool). Crafting new NFT items could burn tokens. Upgrading a faction tech tree might require tokens. These sinks ensure that as players earn tokens, they also have reasons to spend them back, preventing oversupply. For instance, Axie Infinity’s economy initially worked because players burned SLP to breed Axies at a higher rate than minting【40†L79-L87】; we aim for a similar healthy burn/mint ratio.
  - **Limited Airdrop and Vesting:** If we do an airdrop (common to bootstrap community), it will be structured to avoid instant dump. We can take inspiration from PixelTap’s model – perhaps a small % of the airdropped $WAR tokens are liquid, and the rest unlock as the user achieves milestones in-game【16†L71-L77】. This encourages continued engagement for recipients. For $SARGE, we may not airdrop much at all (since it’s earned in-game primarily). 
  - **Anti-Bot Measures:** As mentioned, we’ll combat multi-account farming by requiring progressively more effort to extract value. For example, maybe withdrawals of $SARGE out of the game require account age > X or a certain campaign progress, etc. This way, bots can’t just farm and dump easily. (This is in line with how some games require KYC or have withdrawal taxes to deter bot farmers.)

**Case Studies Referenced:** We have studied what went wrong with **Axie Infinity** – unsustainable growth tied to constant new player influx, which dried up, causing a collapse【34†L125-L133】【66†L31-L39】. Axie taught that tokenomics alone can’t save a game if *engaging gameplay and new user sources* aren’t there【34†L107-L115】【66†L31-L39】. We incorporate that by making SARGE a genuinely fun strategy RPG (so players stay for the game, not just earnings) and by planning for *viral user acquisition loops* (referral bonuses, community-driven content) so there’s always fresh blood entering to balance those cashing out – without needing Ponzi-like growth. We also look at **Star Atlas and others** that over-promised rewards years before gameplay; SARGE will avoid lengthy reward speculation with no product by ensuring the game is playable and enjoyable at launch, and tokens only come into play alongside functional gameplay loops.

On the positive side, examples of *more sustainable economies* are emerging. For instance, **STEPN (move-to-earn)** adjusted its token emissions and added sinks (repair fees for shoes, achievement badges) to try stabilizing its economy, although late adjustments only partially succeeded. **Splinterlands** introduced a limited-print run for reward cards and a DEC token that targets a stable peg via an algorithmic approach. SARGE can borrow the idea of **dynamic emission control** – if $SARGE price falls too far (indicating oversupply), the game could automatically reduce token rewards and increase NFT or off-chain rewards temporarily, and vice versa. This kind of adaptive economy is something we’ll monitor and potentially implement with governance input.

Furthermore, **community governance** via the $WAR token will let the most invested players help shape the economy. For example, if inflation becomes an issue, token holders could vote to increase sink requirements or to allocate more revenue to buy back tokens, etc. Transparency will be key: we will publish dashboards of token supply, sinks, and other metrics so the community knows the health of the game economy (many games failed by hiding issues until too late).

**Effective vs. Failed** – to encapsulate:
- *Failed model:* Axie – hyperinflation of SLP, no sufficient sinks, relied on continuous new players paying old ones【66†L31-L39】.
- *Effective elements:* Axie’s use of SLP for breeding was a good design (native demand)【40†L75-L83】; we adopt similar. PixelTap’s selective airdrop to real players prevented massive instant dumping【16†L63-L71】; we adopt vesting. Catizen’s approach of building an interconnected game ecosystem to increase utility of assets is interesting【67†L55-L63】; we consider future expansions where SARGE NFTs might be usable in multiple mini-games, increasing their value and reducing one-dimensional speculation.

Finally, **player experience first:** As various analysts have pointed out, *“You can’t fix a bad game with tokenomics. Great gameplay is the foundation. Without it, even the best tokenomics will crumble.”*【66†L49-L57】 SARGE Universe’s tokenomics are built to support the game – not the other way around. The economy will enhance fun (through meaningful progression, trade, and competition), not overshadow it. If we execute this balanced approach, SARGE can offer players real value for their time without falling into the boom-bust trap that characterized the first generation of P2E games【34†L125-L133】【34†L129-L137】.

## Marketing Funnel Metrics & User Acquisition Forecast

To achieve a successful launch, we will drive users through a carefully designed marketing funnel – from initial awareness to long-term retention and monetization. Each stage of the funnel will have key performance indicators (KPIs) and targets, informed by industry benchmarks and data from comparable campaigns.

**1. Awareness → Click-Through (Advertising & Influencers):**  
We will raise awareness via **influencer promotions, Telegram Ads, and community referrals**. Our content will appear on platforms like Twitter, YouTube (game trailers, influencer reviews), and within Telegram’s own ad network.

- *Reach (Impressions):* For example, we plan to work with a handful of crypto gaming influencers with a total reach of ~5 million followers. Additionally, Telegram’s ad platform can provide up to **100 million daily impressions** across mini-app placements【44†L87-L95】 – we’ll purchase a slice of this inventory around launch. We estimate a combined **impression count of 20 million** in the first month (across all channels).

- *Engagement (CTR):* Telegram Ads are known for **high click-through rates, often 10–30%**【44†L113-L121】 due to their native appearance in the app. We conservatively assume a 5% CTR on Telegram in-app ads for SARGE (if we show e.g. 10M impressions of a promo message, that yields 500k clicks). Influencer content typically has slightly lower CTR (the audience isn’t all on Telegram ready to click). Crypto influencer campaigns average ~6.5% conversion of their audience【48†L368-L372】. So if an influencer video reaches 100k viewers, perhaps 6.5k will click through to try the game. Taken together, we anticipate **500k–1,000k clicks** to our Telegram game link from initial marketing.

- *Awareness KPI:* Cost per click (CPC) and click-through rate (CTR). We target a blended **CPC of ~$0.05**. Telegram Ads can be as cheap as $0.06 per click on average【44†L117-L120】, and influencer promotions often come out to ~$0.10 or less per click if done via performance deals. For CTR, we target >5% on Telegram Ads (given case studies show ~9–10% easily in some markets【43†L19-L27】).

**2. Click → Sign-Up (Onboarding Conversion):**  
In Telegram’s context, a “sign-up” is simply the user clicking Start on the SARGE bot and entering the tutorial. This is a low-friction step, so we anticipate a **high conversion rate from click to actual player**. If 1,000,000 people click our link, possibly 70–80% will fully enter the game (some drop-off occurs if network is slow or if they change mind, but since no forms to fill, it’s high). We set **CVR (click-to-start conversion) = 70%** as a goal. Thus, 500k clicks would yield 350k new players starting the game.

- *Onboarding KPI:* Conversion rate (%) and cost per acquisition (CPA). If CPC is $0.05 and CVR 70%, then **CPA = $0.07** per player. This is extraordinarily low vs typical mobile games (which often pay $1–$5+ per install). Even if our assumptions are optimistic, we’d like to keep CPA <$0.50. Industry data (Addressable) indicated median cost per *crypto wallet user* via gaming of $8.74【41†L108-L116】, but that’s a very qualified metric. We think leveraging Telegram’s virality will dramatically cut our cost per player relative to traditional channels.

**3. Activation → Retention (New User to Active User):**  
Activation means a new player actually plays and returns. We define an “Activated user” as one who finishes the tutorial battle and returns the next day (Day-1 retention). We aim for **Day-1 retention ~40%**, Day-7 retention ~20%. These are slightly above average for mobile games (which might see 30% D1, 10% D7), but we believe the community/social aspect of Telegram can boost it. For example, players who join via referral often come with friends and are more likely to stick. Also, the promise of earning might incentivize at least short-term retention (as seen in early P2E games where D1 could be very high – lots of people come back to not miss daily rewards).

- *MAU Projections:* Based on our earlier funnel: if 350k users start in launch month, and we retain say 20% into ongoing usage, that’s **~70k monthly active users (MAU)** after the initial wave. However, this does not yet count *viral referral growth.* We expect each satisfied player will refer others via our incentivized referral program (each player gets a unique invite link; both they and their friend get a small token bonus or rare item when the friend reaches a certain level). If our virality factor is, say, 1 new player per 5 existing players (i.e., each 5 players bring 1 more through referrals), that could add another ~70k players, bringing Month 2 MAU to ~140k. If we continue marketing and events, we could realistically target **200k MAU by 3 months post-launch**.

- *Retention KPI:* Day-1, Day-7, Day-30 retention rates; also **Monthly Active Users (MAU)** and **Average Session Length** (we expect engaged players to play ~20 minutes per day in short sessions). Another key metric is **referral rate (viral coefficient)**: we will track what percentage of new users come from referral links. A viral coefficient >1 (each user brings >1 user) would mean exponential organic growth (Hamster Kombat likely had >1 initially【7†L136-L143】). We aren’t assuming that, but even 0.2–0.5 range can significantly amplify growth for free.

**4. Conversion to Paying Users (Monetization):**  
Monetization in SARGE comes from optional NFT sales, token purchases, or premium passes. We will funnel a portion of engaged users into paying users:
- We expect maybe **5% of players will make a purchase** (this might seem low, but many will purely try to earn for free; however, 5% is a reasonable conversion to paying in F2P games, and crypto games sometimes see lower due to availability of earning). 
- *Average Revenue Per Paying User (ARPPU):* Could be high – if someone buys an NFT pack for $50 or trades a lot, ARPPU might be $50–$100. But early on, we price items accessibly ($10–$20) to encourage first-time purchases. Say ARPPU $20.
- That gives an **average revenue per user (ARPU)** of 5% * $20 = $1.0 per user. If we get 200k MAU, potentially $200k/month revenue. However, this doesn’t count the value of tokens being traded externally (which isn’t direct revenue to us, but is an indicator of economic activity).

We’ll also measure **player LTV (Lifetime Value)**. If a monthly ARPU is $1 and average user might stick 4 months (just as an example), LTV would be ~$4. But these numbers can drastically increase if the game catches on with whales or NFT collectors. Some crypto games have a small number of big spenders who drive a lot of revenue (e.g., a guild might buy up $10,000 of NFTs). We will keep an eye on distribution – **Gini coefficient of spend** – to ensure a few whales don’t completely imbalance the game.

- *Monetization KPI:* Conversion rate to paying, ARPU, ARPPU, LTV, and also *Economic Retention* (the percent of token holders who keep their tokens vs sell – a unique metric in crypto). We’d like to see a healthy portion of $SARGE tokens earned being used in-game or staked, not immediately dumped. For instance, if out of all tokens distributed in month 1, over 50% are spent or held, that’s good. If 90% get sent to exchanges, that’s a red flag of extractive behavior.

**5. Referral & Viral Channel Impact:**  
We will implement a referral leaderboard and perhaps a guild system where inviting members gives your guild points. These social funnels are key on Telegram. In Hamster Kombat, referrals and viral tasks were the primary growth driver【7†L136-L143】. For SARGE, we forecast that referrals could account for **30% of new players** post-launch, influencer/ads for 50%, and organic (Telegram bot discovery, app store, etc.) for 20%. We’ll watch **viral coefficient (K-factor)** as mentioned. Our goal is to reach at least 0.5 (each user brings half a user on average) in the first launch phase – that alone can halve our effective CPA.

**Channel Projections:** To clarify by channel:
- **Influencers:** Suppose we pay for 10 mid-tier crypto gaming YouTubers to cover SARGE. Combined reach ~2M. If 2% of viewers sign up (40k players)【45†L29-L36】 and we spent, say, $20k total on this (in fees or sponsorships), that’s **$0.50 CPA**. Influencer quality can vary, but we aim for credible ones to hit that ~6.5% conversion of audience to some action【48†L368-L372】 (maybe not all become active players, but at least click or join our Telegram group).
- **Telegram Ads:** We allocate $10k to Telegram sponsored messages. With CPC ~$0.06【44†L117-L120】, that buys ~166k clicks. If 70% convert, ~116k players. **CPA ~$0.09**. Very cost-effective.
- **Community Referrals:** Cost here is mostly the referral rewards (we might give, for example, 50 $SARGE tokens per invite, which might be equivalent to say $0.50 value). If a user invites 2 friends, they get $1 worth – still cheaper than paid ads in theory. We will cap referral rewards to avoid abuse. Assuming we “spend” $50k worth of tokens on referral bonuses and that yields 100k new users, that’s **$0.50 CPA** (in opportunity cost, not actual cash).
- **Other**: We might do Telegram channel cross-promotions (trading shout-outs with other bots/games), which could bring some thousands of users at virtually no cost.

Combining these, our blended **CPA might be around $0.20–$0.30**. With an LTV per user potentially around $4 (if our assumptions on spend hold), the **ROI on user acquisition is very high** – possibly on the order of **15x ($4 return for $0.25 spent)**. Even factoring only the direct revenue and not future token value, this is promising. In the broader market, influencer marketing has shown up to *11x higher conversion rates than traditional ads* and an average ROI of $6.5 per $1 spent【48†L375-L383】. Our strategy heavily leverages influencers and community, aligning with those high-ROI channels.

It’s worth noting the **retention-driven growth**: if we retain users, they keep inviting others and maybe eventually spend. Losing users quickly would tank our LTV and reduce organic growth. So a core part of the funnel is ensuring **engagement (ELTV)** – the *Engaged Lifetime Value* not just monetary but in terms of referrals and content creation. We will monitor metrics like **DAU/MAU (stickiness)** – aiming for DAU/MAU > 20% (meaning on average a user logs in 1 in 5 days or more). A high stickiness implies the game is part of users’ routine, which boosts lifetime and referrals.

To wrap up the projections: after 3 months, we target:
- **Total Sign-ups:** ~500k 
- **Monthly Active Users:** ~200k 
- **Daily Active Users:** ~50–60k (assuming 25–30% of MAU log in daily)
- ** Paying Users:** ~10k (5% of 200k) 
- **Monthly Revenue:** ~$200k (in-game purchases; not counting external token trading)
- **Cost Spent on Marketing:** ~$50k (most upfront in first month).
