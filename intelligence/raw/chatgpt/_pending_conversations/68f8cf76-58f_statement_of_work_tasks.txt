# Workstream F: Executive coaching for Senior Leader
## Outcomes
- Senior leader demonstrates consistent executive behaviours that improve decision quality, team trust, and delivery.
- Measurable improvements in conflict handling, remit focus, humility, and perspective-taking, evidenced by 360 feedback and operating outcomes.

## Capability focus
- Constructive conflict: turn disagreement into decisions and commitments without escalation.
- Learning stance over certainty bias: replace “answer-first” habits with inquiry, testing, and evidence.
- Ownership and humility: acknowledge missteps promptly, commit corrective actions, and close the loop.
- Operate within remit: respect peer decision rights and escalate via agreed governance rather than side-stepping.
- Patience and perspective: slow down to understand constraints, trade-offs, and other disciplines’ priorities before acting.

## Key tasks
1) Baseline and goals  
- Confidential intake with the CEO and the senior leader.  
- 360 inputs from direct reports, peers, and senior stakeholders.  
- Define 90-day behaviour goals tied to operating metrics.

- Micro-modules: conflict models, inquiry techniques, decision framing, apology and repair, and remit discipline.

3) Operating guardrails and tools  
- Personal Decision Rights Map aligned to the org RACI.  
- Conflict Playbook with step-by-step patterns for tense situations.  
- “Slow the decision” checklist to enforce patience and perspective-taking when stakes are high.

4) Evidence and reinforcement  
- Behavioural scorecard reviewed at 30, 60, and 90 days.  
- Two observed live interventions per month with written feedback.

## 8. Workstream F: Executive coaching for a senior leader
### What you get
- A leader who handles conflict well, stays in their lane, owns mistakes, and pauses to see other views.

### Focus areas
- Turn disagreements into clear decisions without drama.  
- Ask before telling. Test ideas and look for evidence.  
- Admit errors fast, state the fix, and close the loop.  
- Respect peer decision areas. Use the agreed path to raise issues.  
- Slow down on high-stakes calls. Check others’ constraints before acting.

### How we do it
1) Intake and goals  
Private intake with the CEO and the leader. 360 inputs from reports and peers. Set 90-day behaviour goals tied to delivery.  
2) Coaching rhythm  
Weekly one-to-ones and fortnightly check-ins. Field practice in live meetings, quick debriefs. Short modules on conflict patterns, inquiry, framing decisions, apology and repair, staying in lane.  
3) Tools  
Decision Map for the leader’s remit, a Conflict Playbook, and a “slow the decision” checklist.  
4) Evidence  
Behavioural scorecard at 30, 60, 90 days. Two observed interventions per month with written feedback. Short written reflections after key meetings. Coaching notes are private; only 30/60/90 summaries go to the CEO.

### Acceptance tests
- Resolve at least three high-stakes disagreements into clear decisions with commitments and no later flare-ups in the next cycle.  
- Write two short post-mortems naming a personal error and the fix; confirm closure with the people affected.  
- No instances of bypassing peer decision areas for eight weeks, confirmed by peers.  
- Use the “slow the decision” checklist on two high-stakes calls per month and log outcomes.

### Timeframe
Twelve weeks, with checks at days 30, 60, and 90.
