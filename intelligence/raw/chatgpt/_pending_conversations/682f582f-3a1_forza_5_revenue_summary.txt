Ubisoft does not break out “live-service” revenue on a per-game basis, so there’s no public figure for The Crew alone. Instead, all ongoing game revenues—including DLC, season passes and microtransactions—are lumped into two aggregates in their financial reports:

- **Player Recurring Investment (PRI) net bookings** (i.e. digital items, season passes, subscriptions, advertising):  
  - €456.5 million in the first nine months of FY 2024-25 citeturn1view0turn2search2  
  - €806.1 million for the full FY 2024-25 citeturn2search0  

- **Back-catalog net bookings** (older titles—many of which are live-service games—including The Crew Motorfest):  
  - €762.3 million in the first nine months of FY 2024-25 citeturn1view0  
  - €1 296.3 million for the full FY 2024-25 citeturn2search0  

Within these numbers, **The Crew Motorfest** (the latest entry in the franchise) is noted to have outperformed its predecessor in retention and monetization—session days grew 38 percent year-on-year in Q3—but its specific revenue contribution isn’t specified citeturn1view0. In short, you can look at Ubisoft’s PRI and back-catalog nets for a sense of scale, but The Crew’s standalone live-service revenue is not publicly disclosed.

what can you sell in a car game live service

[ASSISTANT]
Here are common live-service offerings in a car-focused game:

**1. New Vehicles**  
- Premium car packs (rare/exotic brands)  
- Limited-edition models tied to events or collaborations  

**2. Cosmetics & Customization**  
- Paints, finishes and wrap designs  
- Decals, vinyls and badge sets  
- Wheel and rim styles  
- Interior trims and dashboard accessories  

**3. Performance Boosts**  
- Temporary XP or credit multipliers  
- “Turbo” items that speed up upgrade times  
- Engine, transmission or tire upgrade kits sold outright  

**4. Seasonal/Progression Passes**  
- Battle or season passes with tiered reward tracks  
- Exclusive cosmetic unlocks for pass holders  
- “Premium” tiers that grant bonus currency  

**5. In-Game Currency Packs**  
- Bundles of credits or tokens at scaled price points  
- “Starter” bundles aimed at new players  

**6. Time-Savers & Convenience**  
- Instant unlocks for cars or parts  
- Skip-ahead vouchers for research trees or garages  
- Auto-repair or pit-stop tokens  

**7. Event & Challenge Tickets**  
- Entry tokens for special tournaments or time-limited races  
- “VIP” spectator passes with extra spectator modes or rewards  

**8. Vanity & Social Items**  
- Custom horns, emotes and driver avatars  
- Garage themes and background music packs  

Mixing purely cosmetic items with optional performance or progression aids lets you cater both to players who want to support you and to those focused solely on gameplay balance.
