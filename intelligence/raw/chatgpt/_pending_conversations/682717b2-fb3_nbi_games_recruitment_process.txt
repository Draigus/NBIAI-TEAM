Here’s a structured, end-to-end process for recruiting playtest participants for NBI Games’ UX and game-mechanics studies. This can slot seamlessly into your overall playtesting workflow (Define → Design → **Recruitment** → … → Debrief) citeturn0file1.

---

## 1. Establish Participant Profiles
- **Map to Personas:** Translate game-specific personas (e.g. “hardcore ARPG vets,” “casual mobile players”) into clear demographic, psychographic, and behavioral criteria.  
- **Segment by Role:** e.g. solo vs. co-op players, F2P vs. premium purchasers, platform (PC/console/mobile).  
- **Quantify Targets:** Aim for 5–8 participants per segment to balance depth and diversity.

## 2. Develop a Screener Survey
- **Core Eligibility Questions:** Age, platform ownership, playtime in genre, purchasing history.  
- **Advanced Filters:** Experience with key mechanics (e.g. crafting, PvP), prior involvement in playtests.  
- **Format & Delivery:** Use an online survey tool (e.g. Typeform, Qualtrics), designed for mobile and desktop.

## 3. Identify & Activate Recruitment Channels
- **Existing Panels & Databases:** Leverage NBI’s network of past participants and gaming-industry panels.  
- **Community Outreach:** Partner Discord servers, subreddit communities, genre-specific forums.  
- **Paid Ads & Social Media:** Targeted campaigns on Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok, using look-alike audiences based on screener data.  
- **Third-Party Vendors:** Engage specialist UX/research recruiters if niche profiles are required citeturn0file8.

## 4. Screen, Shortlist & Comply
- **Screening:** Review survey responses, flag qualifiers, and verify via brief follow-up call or email.  
- **NDAs & Consent:** Send digital NDA and consent forms tailored to in-person or remote sessions.  
- **Data Protection:** Store personal data in a secure CRM; ensure GDPR/CCPA compliance.

## 5. Participant Onboarding & Scheduling
- **Personalized Invites:** Send calendar links (e.g. Calendly) with clear session details—date, time, platform, prep instructions.  
- **Reminders & Confirmations:** Automate reminders at 48 hrs and 2 hrs before the session; confirm any technical requirements.  
- **Backup Slots:** Over-recruit by ~20% to hedge no-shows; maintain a “standby” list.

## 6. Incentive Management
- **Tiered Incentives:** E-gifts or honoraria aligned with time commitment (e.g. £50 for 60 min).  
- **Delivery Mechanism:** Digital vouchers or bank transfer; clarify post-session payout timeline.  
- **Tracking & Reconciliation:** Use a simple spreadsheet or your CRM to log completions and payments.

## 7. Continuous Recruitment Optimization
- **KPI Tracking:** Monitor conversion rate from screener to show-up, dropout rates, demographic mix.  
- **Channel Performance:** Shift budget and effort toward highest-yield sources.  
- **Participant Feedback:** After each session, ask testers for feedback on the recruitment/onboarding experience to refine process.

---

### How This Fits Into NBI’s Playtest Framework
By following this recruitment blueprint, you ensure you’re bringing in the _right_ players—those who match your defined profiles—and doing so efficiently, securely, and at scale. ### 2.1 Employment Categories

**Full-Time Employee (FTE)**  
• Scheduled to work ≥ 30 hours per week.

**Part-Time Employee**  
• Scheduled to work < 30 hours per week.

**Contractor / Consultant**  
• Engaged under a written **Consulting Services Agreement**.  
• Responsible for own tax, NI or self-employment contributions.  
• Not eligible for Employee-only benefits or statutory leave provisions.

• Supplied by a third-party agency under its engagement agreement.

### 2.2 U.S. “At-Will” Employment

> **Legal Basis:** Under U.S. federal and state law, all employment relationships at NBI Consulting are “at-will.” This means that either the Company or the Employee may terminate employment at any time, with or without cause or notice, subject to applicable federal, state, or local statutes prohibiting unlawful discrimination or retaliation.

**Key Points:**  
1. **No Guarantee of Tenure:** No contract—written or implied—limits the right to terminate employment at will.  
2. **Exceptions:** Only a written agreement signed by the CEO can modify at-will status.  
3. **Disclaimers:** Policies in this Handbook, including progressive discipline, are guidelines and do not alter at-will status.  

---

### 2.3 U.K. Written Statement of Particulars

> **Legal Requirement:** Under the Employment Rights Act 1996, all “workers” in the U.K. must receive a written Statement of Employment within two months of their start date.

**Mandatory Contents of the Statement (include, at minimum):**  
- Employer and employee names and addresses  
- Job title or a brief description of duties  
- Place of work and (if mobile) the mobility clause  
- Start date and, where applicable, end date of fixed-term contracts  
- Working hours, including normal days and times of work  
- Salary or pay rate and pay intervals  
- Holiday entitlement (statutory minimum: 28 days inclusive of Bank Holidays)  
- Notice periods for termination (minimum: 1 week after 1 month’s service; thereafter 1 week per year of service, up to 12 weeks)  
- Pension scheme arrangements (auto-enrolment details)  
- Reference to any collective agreements affecting terms and conditions

### 2.4 Probationary Period

All new hires—Employee or Contractor—serve a **three-month probation** to confirm mutual fit. During probation:

- **Review Meetings:**  
  - **30-Day Check-In:** Performance, training needs, culture fit.

- **Termination Notice During Probation:**

- **Extension of Probation:**
