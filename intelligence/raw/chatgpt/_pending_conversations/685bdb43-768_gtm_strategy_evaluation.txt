### 3  |  Comparing two proven **pre-launch** UA tactics

| Tactic | How it works | Strengths | Watch-outs | Source |
|--------|--------------|-----------|------------|--------|
| **Pre-registration campaign** | Collects e-mail / store opt-ins weeks before launch; players receive an exclusive reward at Day 1. | *• Creates a ready-made install spike ⟶ algorithm boost*  <br>*• Builds owned channel for re-engagement*  <br>*• Lets you A/B test creatives & store assets early* | *• Needs polished rewards & firm launch date*  <br>*• Risk of attrition if wait is too long* | fileciteturn8file117 |
| **Influencer-led seeding / early-access** | Mid/high-tier creators get hands-on beta, produce gameplay, host AMA streams and share invite codes. | *• Highly trusted social proof; reaches niche sub-genres*  <br>*• Generates UGC you can recycle in paid ads*  <br>*• Brings feedback that sharpens final build* | *• Must fit creator’s audience; wrong match = wasted spend*  <br>*• Need clear guidelines to avoid leaks & spoiler fatigue* | fileciteturn8file117 |

**Which to choose?**

*If you need a guaranteed day-one spike to climb the charts*, pre-registration is irreplaceable.  
*If your game’s hook requires explanation (e.g., new mechanic, Web3 layer) or you’re aiming at a tight community*, influencer seeding delivers deeper education and trust. Most top-grossing titles run **both in parallel**—pre-reg for scale and influencers for credibility—then amplify with paid UA once CPI models are proven.
