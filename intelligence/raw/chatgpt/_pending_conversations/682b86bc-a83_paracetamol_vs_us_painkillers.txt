Is paracetamol stronger than us pain killers?

[ASSISTANT]
Paracetamol (also known as acetaminophen in the US) is **not generally stronger** than most common US painkillers. Here's a breakdown:

### **1. Paracetamol (Acetaminophen):**
- **Strength:** Mild to moderate pain relief
- **Common Use:** Headaches, minor aches, fever
- **Available:** Over-the-counter
- **Mechanism:** Blocks pain signals in the brain, not inflammation

### **2. Common US Painkillers and Their Strength (compared to paracetamol):**

| Medication                   | Type                | Relative Strength       | Notes                                   |
|-----------------------------|---------------------|-------------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| **Ibuprofen** (Advil)       | NSAID               | Stronger (for inflammation-based pain) | Reduces inflammation and pain           |
| **Naproxen** (Aleve)        | NSAID               | Stronger                | Longer-lasting pain relief              |
| **Aspirin**                 | NSAID               | Comparable              | More side effects than paracetamol      |
| **Codeine** (often combined with paracetamol) | Opioid              | Stronger                | Prescription-only in the US             |
| **Hydrocodone**, **Oxycodone** | Strong opioids     | Much stronger           | For severe pain; tightly controlled     |

### **Summary:**
Paracetamol is a **mild analgesic**. Most US prescription painkillers, especially opioids and NSAIDs, are **stronger or more effective** depending on the type of pain (e.g. inflammation, nerve, or injury).
