## Who buys in a studio
**Economic buyer**  
- Studio Head or GM at midsize and above. At smaller teams, Founder/CEO. Controls budget and signs.  
**Primary champion**  
- Executive Producer or Head of Product. Sometimes Head of Live Ops. Owns outcomes and will drive adoption.  
**Technical buyer / gatekeeper**  
- Head of Analytics or Data Lead. Sometimes IT/Sec. Validates data sources, SSO, privacy, and export parity.  
**Functional co-buyers**  
- Head of Talent for staffing curves and salary bands.  
- Finance lead for budget, P and L, and headcount planning.  
**Everyday users**  
- Producers, PMs, Analysts, Design and Economy leads, Community.  
**Influencers**  
- Publishing partner producer, external investor, or platform partner if a launch is near.

**Buying triggers**  
- Greenlit project scaling up.  
- Pre-launch window risk and release crowding.  
- Live game softness after a content cycle.  
- Headcount plan scrutiny during a fundraise or publisher milestone.  
- Salary compression or churn risk in key roles.

---

## How to market Playsage to a studio
### Category and promise
- **Category:** Studio Decision System.  
- **Promise:** Faster, better calls across staffing, production, and product, with evidence you can show to your board, team, and investors.

### Message spine
1) **Outcome first:** Less drift, fewer missed windows, sharper spend.  
2) **System, not tool:** A shared data layer, operating patterns, and The Sage advisor.  
3) **One weave:** Plans, gates, forecasts, and alerts all reference the same facts.  
4) **Export parity:** What you see is what you ship to slides, docs, and CSV.  
5) **Proof visible:** Backtests, drivers, and sources on every critical chart.

### Entry-point offers by buyer
- **Executive Producer / Head of Product:** Release Radar and Collision Alerts audit for the next 90 days, plus an action plan.  
- **Head of Talent:** Headcount Planner and Salary Band Check for the next two quarters, tied to game phase and genre.  
- **Live Ops:** What Changed and Foresight review on your last two updates, with recommended tests and expected lift ranges.  
- **Finance:** One-page budget and P and L alignment from your staffing plan, with export to XLSX.

### Demo storyline that converts
1) Open on a real launch window: Release Radar shows a crowded month and an advisory window.  
2) Jump to Competitive Compare: show What Changed for the top two competitors after their last updates.  
3) Flip to Foresight: simulate cadence and pricing toggles, save two scenarios, show backtest error.  
4) Tie to Run: gate dashboard shows what is truly ready and what must slip.  
5) Close with Build: staffing curve and offer checker show the cost and timing to hit the safer window.  
6) Export: one click to a board-ready PDF and an XLSX headcount rollup.

### Land-and-expand motion
- **Land** with one paid pilot tied to a date certain: upcoming launch decision, headcount plan sign-off, or Live Ops recovery.  
- **Expand** in 30 to 60 days: unlock the full system after the pilot’s win, roll to additional teams, then add investor outputs and economy helpers.

### Pricing posture
- Single product with tiers. No module-brand sprawl. Entry tier includes Release Radar, Compare, MarketWatch, basic Alerts, and one staffing plan. Higher tiers add Foresight, economy helpers, and investor outputs.

## Does the Build–Run–Win framing dilute product identity
No. It strengthens it if you present it as a **journey through one system**, not three products. Keep these guardrails:
- **Name first, pillars second:** Lead with Playsage as the Studio Decision System. Use Build the Studio, Run the Studio, Win the Market as wayfinding, not brands.  
- **One advisor:** The Sage appears across all screens so guidance feels continuous.  
- **One visual language:** Common cards, tiles, and provenance tags across all areas.  
- **One contract:** One SKU with tiers. Avoid separate SKUs named after pillars.  
- **One story in sales:** Always connect an outcome in one area to an action in the next. Salary bands inform staffing. Staffing anchors gates. Gates set priorities that feed forecasts. Forecasts trigger alerts and decisions.
