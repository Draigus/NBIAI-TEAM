### 5) Source hierarchy (what counts as evidence)
Rank sources by reliability. Prefer top tiers.

Tier 1 (highest):
- Reputable compensation surveys and salary reports specific to games/tech (publisher-known, method disclosed)
- Major recruiting firm salary guides with clear methodology
- Government labour statistics where role mapping is defensible (used carefully, with mapping notes)

Tier 2:
- Aggregated market datasets from reputable platforms with strong sample sizes and filtering
- Job posting comp disclosures where legal and common, only when role, level, and location are explicit

Tier 3 (last resort, support only):
- Crowdsourced self-reports (only if sample size is reasonable and cross-checked)

Rules:
- Each row must have **at least two independent sources**, OR one Tier 1 source plus explicit justification.
- If only Tier 3 exists and cannot be triangulated, mark Evidence strength = Low and flag as provisional.

3) Triangulate:

   - If deltas are unexplained, downgrade Evidence strength and flag.

Recordkeeping:
- Every row includes verification date and links.
- Maintain a “dispute log” for rows with conflicting evidence, showing what was chosen and why.

Stop rule:
- If you cannot reach the evidence bar, you must output the row as “insufficient evidence” rather than inventing a number.
