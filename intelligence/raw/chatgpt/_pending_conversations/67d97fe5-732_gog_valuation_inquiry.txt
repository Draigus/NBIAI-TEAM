A “global mobility visa” generally refers to a type of immigration or work visa designed to facilitate the international movement of highly skilled professionals, executives, or entrepreneurs. Such visas are tailored to ease the process for individuals who work for multinational companies or in industries where global travel is common. Key points include:

- **Purpose:** They’re meant to simplify entry, work, and sometimes residency across borders, helping companies move talent quickly.
- **Target Audience:** Often aimed at highly skilled workers, innovators, and those in leadership positions.
- **Benefits:** May offer streamlined application processes, extended residency, and sometimes pathways to permanent residency or citizenship.
- **Country Variations:** Different countries offer programs that serve a similar purpose—examples include the UK's Global Talent Visa and various digital nomad or remote work visas worldwide.

Before applying, it’s important to review the specific criteria and benefits of the program offered by the destination country, as “global mobility visa” isn’t a single, uniform category but rather a term used to describe similar initiatives around the world.
