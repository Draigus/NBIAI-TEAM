Playsage is a cross-store games analytics and forecasting platform that helps studios and publishers decide what to build, when to launch, how to operate, and where risks sit. It joins market dashboards (Market Overview), competitor head-to-head (Competitive Compare), review and social insights (Sentiment), a forecast simulator (Foresight), a launch and update calendar with conflict detection (Release Calendar), alerts and notifications (Alerts), a recommendation layer (The Sage), and data export plus programmatic access (API) into one consistent system with normalised definitions and clear source notes.

## Problem
Studios answer high-stakes questions with scattered tools. KPI definitions drift between teams. Forecasts lack backtests and driver visibility. Release timing is planned without quantified collisions. Executives and investors get slow, stitched answers that are hard to trust.

## Solution
Provide one system of truth that connects market context, competitive gaps, sentiment, forward scenarios, release risk, alerts, and action recommendations. Keep every metric defined once, show provenance on hover, and make all exports match on-screen filters. Allow teams to simulate decisions, compare scenarios, and create alerts on breaches.

## Who it is for
- Budget owners and executives who need fast, defensible decisions.  
- Product managers, analysts, and live-ops leaders who plan roadmaps and run experiments.  
- Investors who need clear market position, forward risk, and clean exports.

It brings together market dashboards (Market Overview), competitor head-to-head (Competitive Compare), review and social insights (Sentiment), a forecast simulator (Foresight), a launch and update calendar with conflict detection (Release Calendar), alerts and notifications (Alerts), a recommendation layer (The Sage), and export plus API access (API). Everything is presented with consistent definitions, visible provenance, and export parity.

# 2. Problem
Studios and publishers rely on scattered tools and stitched spreadsheets to answer high-stakes questions. KPI definitions drift between teams. Forecasts rarely show backtests or the driver logic behind the numbers. Release timing is planned without quantified crowding and overlap. Executives and investors get slow answers that are hard to trust.

# 3. Solution overview
Provide one system of truth that connects market context, competitive gaps, sentiment, forward scenarios, release risk, alerts, and recommended actions. Keep every metric defined once. Show provenance on hover. Make all exports match on-screen filters. Allow teams to create, save, compare, and alert on scenarios with confidence bands and drivers visible.

# 4. Who it is for
- Budget owners and executives who need fast, defensible decisions  
- Product managers, analysts, and live-ops leaders who plan roadmaps and run experiments  
- Investors who need clear market position, forward risk, and clean exports

# 5. Product capabilities (common terms with brand names)
1) Market intelligence dashboard (Market Overview)  
Cross-store views by genre, platform, region, and time window. Saved views for briefings and export.

2) Competitor comparison (Competitive Compare)  
Side-by-side KPI gaps, feature diff matrix, and a What Changed view to explain movements over time.

3) Review and social analysis (Sentiment)  
Topic trends and verbatims tied to product areas, with impact indicators where possible.

4) Forecast simulator (Foresight)  
Editable baselines and drivers, backtests, confidence bands, top contributors, scenario save and compare. Outputs include peak and average DAU and revenue proxies with CSV, XLSX, PDF, PNG, and PPT.

5) Launch and update calendar with collision risk (Release Calendar)  
Crowding scores by genre, region, and platform, plus advisory windows for launches and content drops.

6) KPI alerts and notifications (Alerts)  
Threshold and slope alerts from any KPI. In-app feed with optional email, Slack, Teams, and SMS.

7) Recommendation engine (The Sage)  
Evidence-linked actions with expected lift ranges and links back to the tiles and charts that support the advice.

8) Data export and API access (API)  
One-click CSV, XLSX, PDF, PNG, PPT, and an API that returns the same numbers shown in charts and tables.

- Are we gaining or losing category share versus our closest peers
