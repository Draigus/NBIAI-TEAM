as a uk immigration lawyer answer the following questions 
I graduated on a Tier 4 visa and got a Skilled Worker Visa a couple months later. This visa is now ending. Does the 2 year grace period for graduating with a degree still apply to me, in which case I'd have a year left?
How long do I have, once a Skilled Worker visa ends, to get a new Visa?
How much does a Skilled Worker visa cost?
Can someone on a dependant visa live in a separate location so long as the provider is paying for said location?
How much can a dependant work?

[ASSISTANT]
Here are the answers to your questions based on UK immigration law:

1. **Two-Year Grace Period After Graduation**:
   - The two-year grace period for graduates applies to those on a Tier 4 (now Student Visa) who are applying for the Graduate Route Visa, not those on a Skilled Worker Visa. Since you moved from a Tier 4 to a Skilled Worker Visa, the Graduate Route is no longer applicable to you. The two-year period you are referring to does not apply once you’ve switched to a Skilled Worker Visa.

2. **Time to Get a New Visa After Skilled Worker Visa Ends**:
   - Once your Skilled Worker Visa ends, you do not automatically have a grace period to remain in the UK. You must either apply to extend your current visa, switch to a different visa category, or leave the UK before your visa expires. Overstaying your visa can lead to severe consequences, including difficulties with future immigration applications.

3. **Cost of a Skilled Worker Visa**:
   - The cost of a Skilled Worker Visa depends on the duration of the visa and whether you’re applying from inside or outside the UK:
     - Up to 3 years: £625 (outside UK) or £719 (inside UK)
     - More than 3 years: £1,235 (outside UK) or £1,423 (inside UK)
   - There’s also an Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS), which is £624 per year of the visa.

4. **Dependant Visa and Living Separately**:
   - A dependant on a Skilled Worker Visa is expected to live with the main visa holder in the UK. Living separately may be questioned by the Home Office and could affect the dependant's visa status, especially if the separation appears to be permanent or indefinite. The main visa holder must support their dependants financially, but they are generally expected to share the same residence.

5. **Work Rights for Dependants**:
   - A dependant on a Skilled Worker Visa can work in most jobs in the UK without any restriction on the number of hours they can work. However, they cannot work as a professional sportsperson (including as a sports coach). 

These answers are based on general immigration rules, and specific circumstances may affect the outcome. Always consult directly with an immigration lawyer for personal legal advice.
