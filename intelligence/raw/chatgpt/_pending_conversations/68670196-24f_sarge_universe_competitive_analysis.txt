**Why user acquisition (UA) sits at the heart of a mobile-game’s P&L**

* *Growth engine:*  the vast majority of new installs come from intentional UA rather than “organic” discovery.  Without a pipeline of fresh players a free-to-play title quickly stalls— there are no ad impressions to sell, no IAP whales to surface, and no data to optimise against.  A disciplined UA programme therefore “lays the foundation for a game’s growth and overall profitability”fileciteturn7file61.  
* *Revenue driver:* monetisation models (IAP, ads, battle-passes) are built on large, engaged cohorts.  Effective UA widens the top of the funnel and shortens payback time on marketing spend, converting marketing dollars into lifetime value (LTV).  
* *Retention catalyst:* new players feed social loops (guilds, PvP ladders, referral bonuses) that keep existing users engaged.  When acquisition slows, even the best-designed retention features decay.
