[ASSISTANT]
**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** is the average amount of money you spend to acquire one new paying (or active) user.

### Simple formula
\[
\text{CAC} \;=\; \frac{\text{Total marketing & user-acquisition spend in a period}}{\text{Number of new users acquired in the same period}}
\]

### What counts as “spend”?
* Paid ads (e.g., Telegram Ads, Facebook, TikTok)
* Influencer fees
* Agency commissions and attribution fees
* Giveaway or prize-pool costs that are explicitly aimed at attracting new users
* Creative-production costs tied to UA campaigns

### Why CAC matters
* **Budget guard-rail** – If your forecast lifetime value (LTV) per user is £1.25, you must keep CAC safely below that (e.g., ≤ £0.50) to stay profitable.
* **Channel comparison** – Knowing CAC per channel lets you shift spend toward the most efficient sources.
* **Board & investor metric** – CAC/LTV ratio is a quick health check of the whole go-to-market model.

In short, CAC tells you how efficiently your marketing money turns into new players; keeping it lower than LTV is non-negotiable for sustainable growth.

waht does target K>12 mean

[ASSISTANT]
**K-factor** (often written just **K**) is the viral-growth multiplier—the average number of *additional* new users that each existing user brings in.

### How it’s calculated
\[
K = \text{average invites sent per user} \; \times \; \text{conversion rate of those invites}
\]

*Example*  
- Each player sends **5** referral links on average.  
- **24 %** of invitees install the game.  

\[
K = 5 \times 0.24 = 1.2
\]

### Why “K ≥ 1.2” matters  
- **K = 1** Every new user replaces only herself ⇒ flat growth.  
- **K < 1** Each user brings in < 1 new user ⇒ viral loop dies.  
- **K = 1.2** Each 10 000 users generate 12 000 more (20 % net growth) without extra ad spend.

So when the GTM timeline says **“target K ≥ 1.2”** it means *aim for each player to pull in at least 1.2 newcomers via the referral bot and other sharing mechanics*. A typo of “12” would be unrealistic; the intent is **1.2**.

### “Creative Factory” — what it means in the Sarge Universe GTM

Think of the **Creative Factory** as a mini-studio dedicated solely to producing, approving and shipping every marketing asset the launch will require—at the cadence and quality that Telegram’s fast-moving ad channels demand.

| Component | Purpose | Typical staffing & tools | Outputs for Sarge Universe |
|-----------|---------|--------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Content project manager** | Owns the asset calendar, routes briefs, enforces deadlines. | 1 FTE • Monday.com or Trello for kanban • Slack + Notion for briefs. | Keeps the 60-asset pre-launch backlog on track; calls daily stand-ups with art & UA. |
| **Motion designer** | Creates short 6–15 s gameplay clips, meme GIFs, end-cards. | 1 FTE • After Effects, Premiere, Lottie, Kapwing for quick cut-downs. | 30 motion assets: Sponsored-Message MP4s, influencer clip templates, GIFs for channel takeovers. |
| **Static/graphic designer** | Banners, thumbnails, iconography, localisation of text layers. | 1 FTE • Figma, Photoshop; Smart-Objects for fast language swaps. | 20 static ads in 4 aspect ratios + 5 key art variations for press & Steam page. |
| **Copywriter / localisation loop** | Writes ad copy variants, push-card text, channel post hooks; coordinates translators. | Copy role can be 0.5 FTE internal + freelance translators via Smartling. | 3 language packs (EN, ES, PT-BR) ready by W-6; expansion RU at M 2. |
| **Brand & UA review gate** | Ensures every asset meets specs, brand voice, and is tagged with UTM parameters. | Check-boxes in the kanban; final sign-off by UA buyer & CPO. | Avoids costly re-jections in Telegram Ads Manager or mismatch in AB tests. |

#### Why lock it **six weeks before launch?**
* Telegram’s Ads Manager and channel takeovers require *fresh* creatives every ~5 days to avoid ad fatigue—having 60 iterations pre-built lets you rotate without last-minute crunch.  
* Influencers need ready-made overlays and end-cards so their turn-around is <24 h.  
* Localised assets must be QA-checked before the paid “booster week,” otherwise non-English CPMs spike due to relevance penalties.

#### Deliverables timeline snapshot
| Week | Factory milestone |
|------|-------------------|
| **-8** | Finalise ad concepts & storyboard list (UA + brand sign-off). |
| **-7** | First batch of 10 motion cuts delivered; static master PSD approved. |
| **-6** | Localisation files sent to translators; Figma components locked. |
| **-4** | All 60 assets (30 motion, 20 static, 10 influencer templates) complete; start drip-feeding to UA buyer. |
| **-1** | “Booster” set of high-contrast variants created using top-performing copy/visuals from AB test data. |

In short, the **Creative Factory** is *not* a metaphorical idea—it’s a tightly-run content-production pipeline that guarantees every ad, banner, or influencer overlay is ready well before the launch spike, keeping your paid CAC low and your brand coherent.
