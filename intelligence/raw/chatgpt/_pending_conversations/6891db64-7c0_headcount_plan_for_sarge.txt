### 1  Why user acquisition (UA) is the lifeblood of a mobile game  

* **Kick–starts the growth loop.** A steady inflow of new players fuels daily-active-user (DAU) numbers, which in turn improves matchmaking, social virality and store-front rankings. Without it, even a great game “struggles to reach its target audience and achieve long-term success”. fileciteturn0file14  
* **Monetisation depends on a big funnel.** Only a fraction of players ever convert to payers, so revenue from IAPs, ads or battle-passes scales with the size and quality of the acquired cohort. fileciteturn0file14  
* **Algorithmic visibility.** App-store algorithms reward titles that generate installs and engagement quickly; aggressive UA boosts keyword positions and “Top Charts”, creating a self-reinforcing organic flywheel. fileciteturn0file14  
* **Investor confidence & runway.** Early UA metrics (CPI, D1/D7 retention, pay-rate) are the north-star signals VCs and publishers use to price IP value and green-light additional funding. fileciteturn0file14  

---

### 2  Three stubborn challenges teams face  

| Challenge | Why it hurts | Practical mitigation |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------|
| **Escalating acquisition costs** | Meta, Google and Apple Search Ads each captured ~30 % / 10 % of UA spend in 2022; CPMs and CPIs continue to rise, squeezing margin. fileciteturn0file14 | Hybrid bidding (CPI→CPA), aggressive creative testing, and cheaper alternative channels (TikTok, Unity Ads). |
| **Fierce competition & ad fatigue** | Dozens of launches every week crowd user attention; repeated creatives quickly lose click-through. fileciteturn0file14 | Refresh creatives every 7-10 days, rotate ad concepts, lean on playable ads to reset novelty. |
| **Retention after install** | High churn makes UA money vanish—getting the download is only half the battle. fileciteturn0file14 | Integrate onboarding tutorials, early meta-game hooks, live-ops events and community channels (Discord/Telegram) from day 0. |

---

### 3  Two proven **pre-launch** UA tactics—similar goals, different strengths  

|  | **Pre-registration campaign** | **Influencer seeding & wait-list** |
|--|------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **What it is** | List the game on Google Play / App Store (or Telegram mini-app bot) months early; users tap “Pre-register” and receive a notification (and a reward) on launch day. | Give alpha access or exclusive content to mid-tier creators on YouTube, TikTok, Twitch; drive viewers into a Discord/Telegram wait-list bot that captures IDs and referrals. |
| **Why it works** | Locks in intent while CPI is zero; store algorithms see a “launch-day spike” that boosts featuring and organic installs. fileciteturn0file14 | Leverages creator trust and FOMO; the wait-list provides a qualified, community-ready cohort and viral K-factor through referral links. fileciteturn0file14 |
| **Cost & risk profile** | Low media cost; requires attractive reward assets and back-end to fulfil them. Risk: numbers look big but may under-convert to payers. | Higher upfront spend (creator fees, builds, community mods); delivers warmer leads and valuable UGC, but success hinges on picking creators whose audience truly matches the game. |
| **Best for** | Casual or mid-core titles that rely on store discovery and broad reach. | Niche, mid-core / hardcore or Web3 titles where community and social proof are paramount. |

**Take-away:** Use **pre-registration** to guarantee a day-one install surge and algorithmic lift; layer **influencer-driven wait-lists** to seed an engaged core that fuels reviews, UGC and early monetisation. Combining both tactics—in sequence—often yields the healthiest launch curve.

write out the number format in excel for showing £124K rather than £124,000.00

[ASSISTANT]
Use this custom number format in Excel:

```
£0,"K";£-0,"K";£-?   ;@
```

**Why it works**

* The comma (`,`) tells Excel to divide the underlying value by 1 000 when displaying it, so **124 000 → 124**.  
* The literal **"K"** (enclosed in quotes) is appended after that scaled number.  
* The three semicolon-separated sections format positive ; negative ; zero ; text respectively.

### Variations

| Desired look | Custom format |
|--------------|---------------|
| No decimals → “£124K” | `£0,"K";£-0,"K";£0,"K";@` |
| One decimal → “£124.3K” | `£0.0,"K";£-0.0,"K";£0,"K";@` |
| Round to nearest 10 K → “£120K” | `£0,"0K";£-0,"0K";£0,"0K";@` |

Apply the format via **Home › Number › Custom** and your salary figures will render as compact “£###K” while retaining the full numeric value for calculations.

**Why you’re seeing “£0.00K”**

Excel divides the displayed number once for **every comma** in a custom format:

- `£0,"K"` → divides by 1 000 (shows thousands)  
- `£0,,"K"` → divides by 1 000 000 (shows millions)

If you accidentally used **two commas** or if your worksheet already stores the values in thousands (e.g. “124” not “124000”), the result after the extra division is ≈0 → Excel rounds that to 0 K.

---

### Pick the format that matches the actual numbers in your sheet

| Your cell contains | Use this custom format | Example display |
|--------------------|------------------------|-----------------|
| **124000** (full amount) | `£0,"K";-£0,"K";£0,"K";@` | £124K |
| **124000** with 1 decimal place | `£0.0,"K";-£0.0,"K";£0,"K";@` | £124.0K |
| **124** (already “thousands”) | `£0"K";-£0"K";£0"K";@` | £124K |

*(The four sections handle **positive ; negative ; zero ; text.**)*

---

### How to apply it

1. Select the salary cells.  
2. **Ctrl + 1** → *Format Cells* → **Number › Custom**.  
3. Paste the format string that matches your data.  
4. Press **OK**.

If your sheet mixes both kinds of numbers, first convert everything to full values (e.g. multiply 124 → 124 000) or to “thousands” before applying the format—otherwise the display will be inconsistent.
