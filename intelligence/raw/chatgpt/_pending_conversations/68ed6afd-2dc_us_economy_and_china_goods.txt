No. A blanket refusal of all Chinese goods would not be a good idea.

Why not, in brief  
• Immediate shortages in electronics, machinery parts, pharmaceuticals inputs, solar and battery components.  
• Sharp inflation from supply shocks and costly substitutions.  
• Production hits in autos, defence, energy and consumer tech because many inputs are China-centric even when final assembly is elsewhere.  
• Evasion and leakage risk through third countries without strict rules of origin, which adds cost but not real autonomy.  
• Likely retaliation and allied friction, which compound the economic drag.

What could be good policy instead  
• Targeted controls on truly strategic tech and components, not a blanket ban.  
• Phased diversification to Mexico, Vietnam, India and domestic capacity, with enforceable rules of origin.  
• Stockpiles and surge programs for rare earths, batteries, PCBs and APIs.  
• Time-boxed subsidies and procurement to stand up critical supply chains, paired with competition safeguards.  
• Clear inflation management plan and support for affected small manufacturers and low-income households.
