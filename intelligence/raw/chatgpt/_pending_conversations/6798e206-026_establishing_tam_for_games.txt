### **How a Video Game Can Establish Its Total Addressable Market (TAM)**  

Establishing the **Total Addressable Market (TAM)** for a video game is crucial for understanding potential revenue, investor pitches, marketing strategy, and long-term viability. Below are **detailed steps** to determine the TAM for a video game.  

---

### **Step 1: Define the Core Game Concept and Genre**
- Identify the genre (e.g., FPS, RPG, MOBA, strategy, survival horror).
- Determine whether itŌĆÖs single-player, multiplayer, live-service, etc.
- Define the business model (e.g., premium, free-to-play, subscription-based).
- Establish the gameŌĆÖs key differentiators from competitors.

**Why?**  
- The genre and gameplay type impact player demographics, engagement levels, and pricing models.  
- Investors and stakeholders need a clear understanding of the gameŌĆÖs positioning.  

---

### **Step 2: Identify the Market Segments**
- Segment the gaming audience based on **demographics** (age, gender, income).  
- Consider **platform segmentation** (PC, console, mobile, VR).  
- Look at **regional segmentation** (North America, Europe, Asia-Pacific, etc.).
- Understand **player behaviors** (casual vs. hardcore gamers, live-service engagement).

**Why?**  
- Helps determine which platforms, regions, and player types have the highest engagement potential.  
- Defines targeted marketing and monetization strategies.  

---

### **Step 3: Analyze Market Size and Growth Trends**
- Gather data from industry reports (e.g., **Newzoo, Statista, PwC, ESA, Sensor Tower**).
- Examine the **growth rate** of the genre (e.g., battle royale, roguelike, MMORPG).
- Compare market trends in **similar games** (e.g., Steam stats, Twitch viewership, Metacritic ratings).
- Research the **installed base** of gaming hardware (e.g., PlayStation user base, PC configurations, mobile penetration).

**Why?**  
- Ensures the game is targeting a growing, not shrinking, market.  
- Identifies genre-specific opportunities and risks.  

---

### **Step 4: Competitive Analysis**
- Identify **direct competitors** (games in the same genre with similar mechanics).
- Identify **indirect competitors** (alternative games competing for player time).
- Analyze sales data of top-performing competitors.
- Evaluate **monetization models** (premium, microtransactions, season passes, etc.).
- Research **player retention** statistics in competing titles.

**Why?**  
- Helps understand market saturation, pricing strategies, and potential user acquisition costs.  
- Allows developers to define their unique selling proposition (USP).  

---

### **Step 5: Estimate Total Addressable Market (TAM)**
- Use market research to estimate **the total number of potential players** globally.
- Multiply by **average revenue per user (ARPU)** for the game type:
  - Free-to-play games ŌåÆ ARPU based on in-game purchases (e.g., Fortnite: ~$85 per paying user annually).
  - Premium games ŌåÆ Full-price sales (e.g., $60 per copy for AAA).
  - Subscription models ŌåÆ Recurring revenue estimates (e.g., Game Pass, PlayStation Plus).
- Factor in **market penetration assumptions** (what % of the total market will actually play the game?).

If the **FPS market size** is **500 million** players and an FPS title expects to capture **2%** of that market at an ARPU of **$40**, the TAM would be:  

\[
500M \times 0.02 \times 40 = 400M \text{ (Total Revenue Potential)}
\]

**Why?**  
- Provides a realistic **revenue forecast** for budgeting and development decisions.  
- Critical for investor funding, determining marketing budget, and feature prioritization.  

---

### **Step 6: Adjust for Serviceable Available Market (SAM)**
- The **Serviceable Available Market (SAM)** is a subset of the TAM that aligns with **platform choice, age rating, and localization**.
- Example: A PlayStation-exclusive game excludes Xbox, PC, and Switch users, reducing its SAM.
- Consider **monetization constraints** (some regions have lower spending habits on microtransactions).
- Refine assumptions based on **competitor benchmarks and industry averages**.

**Why?**  
- Helps **realistically assess** what portion of the TAM can be effectively reached.  
- Ensures that projections are **grounded in real-world constraints**.  

---

### **Step 7: Define Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)**
- The **Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)** represents **realistic acquisition potential**.
- Calculate expected **user conversion rates** based on:
  - **Marketing effectiveness** (how much advertising spend converts into users).
  - **Retention rates** (do players stay engaged?).
  - **Competitive differentiation** (how unique is the game?).
- Conduct **pre-launch surveys, beta testing, and early access metrics** to refine numbers.

**Why?**  
- Determines **realistic** sales and revenue expectations.  
- Helps plan **scalable** production, marketing, and post-launch content strategies.
