if you move from a tier 2 visa to a global talent visa in the uk does that reset your time

[ASSISTANT]
Yes, moving from a Tier 2 visa to a Global Talent visa in the UK resets the time counted towards your indefinite leave to remain (ILR). This means that the five-year qualifying period for ILR will start again from the date you switch to the Global Talent visa.

how can you loose your global talent visa

[ASSISTANT]
You can lose your Global Talent visa in the UK under several circumstances:

1. **Breach of Visa Conditions**: If you do not comply with the conditions of your visa, such as not working in your field of expertise or not maintaining your endorsement requirements, your visa may be revoked.

2. **Criminal Activity**: Engaging in criminal activities can lead to the revocation of your visa and possible deportation.

3. **False Information**: Providing false or misleading information on your visa application can result in the cancellation of your visa.

4. **Change in Endorsement**: If the endorsing body withdraws their endorsement, your visa can be cancelled.

5. **Extended Absence**: If you spend more than 180 days outside the UK in any 12-month period during the qualifying period for ILR, it may affect your eligibility for ILR and could lead to the loss of your visa status.

6. **Failure to Renew**: Not applying for an extension or renewal of your visa before it expires can result in the loss of your visa status.

It is important to adhere to all visa conditions and maintain good standing to avoid losing your Global Talent visa.
