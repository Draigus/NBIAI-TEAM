[ASSISTANT]
Yes, it is acceptable under the UK immigration rules for your son, who is a dependent on your Tier 2 (now Skilled Worker) visa, to attend university while you, his parents, pay for his tuition and living expenses, including rent for an apartment. This arrangement complies with the rules governing dependent visas, provided certain conditions are met.

**Key Points to Consider:**

1. **Age of the Dependent:**
   - **Under 18 Years Old:** If your son is under 18, he is generally allowed to be in the UK as your dependent without additional scrutiny regarding independence.
   - **Over 18 Years Old:** If your son is over 18 but was last granted permission to stay in the UK as your dependent child, he can extend his stay as a dependent as long as he is not leading an independent life.

2. **Not Leading an Independent Life:**
   - The UK immigration rules specify that a dependent child over 16 must not be leading an independent life. This means they should not be:
     - Married or in a civil partnership.
     - Living away from the family home (except for full-time education).
     - Financially independent.
   - **Full-Time Education Exception:** Living away from home for the purpose of full-time study at a university is acceptable. Since you are funding his education and accommodation, he is considered financially dependent on you.

3. **Financial Dependency:**
   - You must continue to provide financial support to your son. Evidence of this support may be required when extending his visa or if questioned by immigration authorities.
   - Keep records of financial transactions, such as tuition payments and rent, to demonstrate ongoing support.

4. **Documentation:**
   - For visa applications or extensions, your son may need to provide:
     - Proof of enrollment in a full-time educational course.
     - Evidence of financial dependency, such as bank statements showing your payments for his expenses.
     - Documents confirming his residential address.

5. **Compliance with Visa Conditions:**
   - Ensure that both you and your son comply with all the conditions of your respective visas.
   - Any change in circumstances, such as marriage or employment, should be reported to the Home Office, as it may affect his dependent status.

**Conclusion:**

Your son's attendance at a university while you cover his tuition and living expenses aligns with the UK's immigration rules for dependents of Tier 2 (Skilled Worker) visa holders. As long as he remains financially dependent on you and does not lead an independent life, this arrangement is acceptable. It is advisable to maintain thorough documentation of your financial support and his educational enrollment to facilitate any future visa applications or extensions.
