If you move from a salaried position of **£80,000** to an **IR35 contract**, you need to account for taxes, benefits, and time off. Here’s a breakdown of what you should ask for:

### **1. Salary to Contractor Rate Calculation**
A salary of **£80,000** typically translates to a **daily rate** of:
- **£80,000 ÷ 260 working days ≈ £307 per day** (before tax and deductions)

However, contractors **do not** get:
- **Paid holidays (approx. 28 days including bank holidays)**
- **Sick pay**
- **Employer pension contributions (usually 5-10%)**
- **Bonuses**
- **Job security**

### **2. Adjusting for Additional Costs**
To compensate, you should increase your rate to cover:
- **Holidays (around 20 working days)**
- **Pension contributions (5-10%)**
- **Additional taxes and National Insurance (as an IR35 contractor, you pay PAYE taxes)**
- **Lack of employment benefits**

A rough multiplier for IR35 contractors is **1.25x to 1.5x your equivalent salaried rate**.

### **3. Recommended Contract Rate**
To match **£80,000 salary**, you should **at minimum** ask for:
- **£400 - £450 per day** inside IR35  
  (This ensures you cover taxes, lack of benefits, and holidays)

However, if your industry pays premium contract rates, you may be able to push for **£500 per day or more**.

3. **Paid Time Off:** Approximately 28 days (including bank holidays)  
4. **Pension Contributions:** Usually 5-10%  
5. **Additional Costs:** Accounting for lack of sick pay, health insurance, etc.

### **Step 1: Calculate Equivalent Daily Rate**
Assuming 260 working days per year (52 weeks x 5 days):
- **Daily Rate Equivalent:** £75,000 ÷ 260 ≈ **£288 per day**

### **Step 2: Adjust for IR35 Costs**
For IR35, contractors pay:
- **Employee National Insurance (NI)**
- **Employer NI** (currently 13.8%)
- **Income Tax (PAYE)**

Contractors generally use a **1.25x to 1.5x multiplier** to adjust for the lack of benefits, taxes, and other costs.

### **Recommended Daily Rate**
To maintain the same standard of living:
- **Minimum:** £288 x 1.25 ≈ **£360 per day**  
- **Optimal:** £288 x 1.5 ≈ **£432 per day**  

### **Annual Equivalent Earnings**
- **£360 per day x 220 working days (after holidays)** ≈ **£79,200**  
- **£432 per day x 220 working days** ≈ **£95,040**  

### **Key Points:**
- To **match a £75,000 salary**, you should target around **£80,000 - £95,000** annually as an IR35 contractor.
