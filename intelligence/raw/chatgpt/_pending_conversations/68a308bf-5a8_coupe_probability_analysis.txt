Short answer, assuming you mean the United Kingdom as of 18 August 2025:

- Next 6 months: ~0.01% to 0.05%
- Next 12 months: ~0.02% to 0.1%
- Next 24 months: ~0.05% to 0.2%

Why so low
1) Base rates. In consolidated Western European democracies, successful coups in the modern era are essentially absent. Spain’s 1981 attempt failed, and Portugal’s 1974 coup removed an authoritarian regime. The UK has had no modern coup, with the oft-cited 1968 Cecil King plot considered unserious. citeturn5search1turn5news22turn5search8

3) Stability indicators. World Bank “Political Stability and Absence of Violence” for the UK sits around 0.51 on a −2.5 to 2.5 scale and about the 62nd percentile in 2023. That is lower than early-2000s peaks, yet still consistent with low coup risk. citeturn1search6turn1search8  
4) Recent “warning factors.” The UK faces civil-unrest and security concerns that affect business continuity, and the government has warned of elevated external security risks. What would move the probability up
- Open elite splits that include senior military or police leadership, or overt military involvement in politics.  
- Suspension of elections or extra-constitutional transitions.  
- Large, sustained protests combined with security-force defections.
