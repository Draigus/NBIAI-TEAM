what does New entrant threshold: £33,400/year mean

[ASSISTANT]
The **New Entrant threshold** of **£33,400/year** is a **reduced salary requirement** under the UK Skilled Worker visa route. It applies to specific applicants who are considered *early in their careers*. Here's what it means and who qualifies:

---

### ✅ **What It Means**
- If you qualify as a “new entrant,” your **employer can sponsor you at a lower salary** than the standard £41,700.
- You must still meet all other visa requirements (e.g. job eligibility, SOC code, RQF level).

---

### ✅ **Who Qualifies as a “New Entrant”**

You may be eligible if **at least one** of the following applies:

1. **You’re under 26 years old** at the time of visa application or extension.
2. **You’re switching from a UK student visa (Tier 4 or Graduate route)** to a Skilled Worker visa.
3. **You’re working toward a recognised professional qualification**.
4. **You’re moving into a postdoctoral role** in science or academia.

---

### ✅ **How Long You Can Be a New Entrant**
- You can only be treated as a new entrant **for up to 4 years total**, across all Skilled Worker visas.
- After that, you must meet the **full salary threshold** (now £41,700) for extensions, job changes, or settlement.

---

### ⚠️ **Implication**
If you're earning **£33,400 or more** and you qualify as a new entrant, your visa application **can still be valid**—even though you're below the £41,700 general threshold.
