Whether it's cheaper to hire a **Full-Time Employee (FTE)**, **contractor**, or **consultant** in the UK depends on several factors, but generally the cost tiers look like this:

---

### **1. Full-Time Employee (FTE)**
#### **Pros:**
- Lower **daily rate**
- More **long-term loyalty** and cultural integration
- Employer has **control over time and task priorities**

#### **Costs:**
- Salary + Employer National Insurance (13.8%) + Pension Contributions (3% min)
- Paid holidays (28 days incl. bank holidays)
- Sick leave, maternity/paternity leave
- Equipment, office space, training

> **Rough cost multiplier:** ~1.25x to 1.35x base salary for total employer cost  
> (e.g., £60K salary → £75K to £81K true annual cost)

---

### **2. Contractor (via Ltd company or umbrella)**
#### **Pros:**
- Flexibility, no long-term commitment
- No holiday/sick pay, pension, or equipment costs
- Only pay for work delivered

#### **Costs:**
- Higher **day rate** (e.g., £400–£800/day for experienced roles)
- May fall under **IR35**, meaning employer pays **NI & PAYE** like an FTE
- Less control over scheduling and workload prioritisation

> **Typical annualised cost:** £100K–£180K depending on rate and role  
> (e.g., £500/day * 220 days = £110K/year)

---

### **3. Consultant (via firm or specialist)**
#### **Pros:**
- Brings **strategic value**, speed, and experience
- Often delivers **fixed-scope** or **high-impact** outcomes
- No admin, onboarding, or tax concerns

#### **Costs:**
- Highest **rate per hour/day/project**
- Cost covers not just labour, but IP, brand, overheads, and risk
- Typically used short-term or for specialist tasks

> **Typical cost:** £800–£2,000+/day or fixed project fees  
> (e.g., £20K–£100K+ for multi-week engagements)

---

### **General Rule of Thumb (Cost per Value vs. Control):**

| Role Type    | £ Cost | Value Delivery | Control | Commitment |
|--------------|--------|----------------|---------|------------|
| FTE          | Low    | Medium (Long-term) | High     | High       |
| Contractor   | Medium | Medium-High     | Medium   | Medium     |
| Consultant   | High   | High (Specialist/Fast) | Low      | Low        |

---

### **When to choose which:**
- **FTE** → Best for **core operations**, long-term projects, team culture
- **Contractor** → Best for **scaling quickly**, niche skills, project bursts
- **Consultant** → Best for **strategic input**, audits, or solving high-cost problems fast

If you're cost-focused for long-term needs, **FTEs are cheapest overall**, but contractors offer **lower risk** and **faster ramp-up** despite higher per-day costs. Consultants are rarely cheaper in raw cost terms but may be cheaper in **speed to value** or **avoiding errors**.
