## 3) Target Operating Model

### Two-Lane Workflow (Plain Language)

- Lane A (Regulated, CUI boundary):
  - Work occurs only in approved tools with evidence packs and explicit US-residency gating.  
  - Drafts, intermediate artefacts, and finals live only in Lane A repositories.  
  - No copy/paste into unapproved web tools, personal accounts, or unmanaged plugins.

- Lane B (Non-CUI research):
  - Used for open-source research, market scans, general writing acceleration, and prototyping.  
  - Still constrained by provenance rules (Assumption A3).  
  - Anything destined for Lane A must pass the controlled merge.

### Work Classification Guide (Decision Tree, Text Form)

- Step 1: Is any input CUI, CDI, client-sensitive operational detail, or contract-restricted information?
  - Yes or Uncertain: Route to Lane A.
  - No: Continue.

- Step 2: Is the output intended for a Lane A deliverable, client contract performance, or will it be combined with Lane A material later?
  - Yes: Treat as Lane B-to-A candidate and apply merge controls from the start.
  - No: Continue.

- Step 3: Are you using external sources that must be defensible to a sceptical reviewer?
  - Yes: Use the Extreme Citation Standard (Source Map + Claim Ledger).
  - No: Document minimal sources and assumptions anyway.

Default rule when uncertain: Lane A until classified.

Borderline examples
- “Generic policy memo template” with no client references: Lane B, merge required before use in Lane A.  
- “Slide deck skeleton for a CUI programme” with no CUI data: Lane B, merge required.  
- “Diagram of a client network” even if redacted: Lane A (structure itself may be sensitive).  
- “Ontology schema for programme reporting” intended for Lane A: Lane B allowed for initial design, but merge required before adoption.

### Merge Controls And Artefacts

- Sanitisation checklist completed  
- Human reviewer sign-off  
- Source Map completed with locators  
- Claim Ledger completed with claim-to-source mapping  
- Storage rule enforced: final regulated artefacts only in Lane A repositories

## 6) Extreme Citation And Validation Standard (Hard To Game)

### Source Tiers

- Tier 1 (Primary Authoritative): DoD issuances, NIST publications, acquisition.gov clause text, official agency sites. citeturn0search0turn0search1turn0search6  
- Tier 2 (Secondary Credible): GAO, CRS, major standards bodies, top-tier academic sources.  
- Tier 3 (Contextual): vendor documentation, reputable legal or advisory summaries, trade press.

### Source Map Table (Required For All External Sources)

Title | Publisher | Publication Date | Access Date | URL | Locator (section/page/paragraph) | Claim Supported | Confidence | Notes

### Anti-Gaming Rules

- No orphan claims: every substantive claim must map to at least one source with a locator.  
- High-impact claims require corroboration from at least two independent sources, with one in Tier 1 or Tier 2.  
- Quote vs paraphrase rules:
  - Quotes must be short, exact, and attributed with locators.  
  - Paraphrases must still point to locators.

### Hallucination Detection Checklist (Required)

- For each section: list claims that would fail if wrong.  
- Verify each against Source Map entries and locators.  
- If not supported: mark Unverified, create verification task, and block merge.
