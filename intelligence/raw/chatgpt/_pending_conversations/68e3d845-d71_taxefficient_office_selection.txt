### Core tax principles
1. **Corporate income tax** is generally owed where the *work is performed* and where the *entity providing the service* is registered.  
2. **Value-added tax (VAT)** in the EU (including Sweden) applies to *cross-border services*, but business-to-business (B2B) services are **reverse-charged** to the client—so you don’t collect Swedish VAT.  
3. **Withholding tax** only applies if Sweden treats the payment as income sourced there (rare for services performed abroad).

---

### Office choice comparison

| Factor | **UK Entity** | **US Entity** |
|---------|----------------|---------------|
| **Corporate tax rate (2025)** | 25% | 21% federal (+ 0–10% state) |
| **Treaty with Sweden** | Yes (UK–Sweden tax treaty) | Yes (US–Sweden tax treaty) |
| **VAT handling** | B2B export of services → 0% VAT (reverse charge in Sweden) | Outside EU VAT system entirely |
| **Administrative friction** | Simple for EU/EEA work; UK is non-EU but familiar with EU VAT rules | US entity may trigger Swedish “permanent establishment” review if work or personnel enter Sweden |
| **Currency and banking** | GBP vs SEK | USD vs SEK |
| **Perceived proximity for client** | European timezone and legal familiarity | Seen as offshore / non-EEA |

---

### Practical implications

- **If the work is done entirely from the UK office** (no staff in Sweden):  
  - No Swedish corporate tax.  
  - Swedish client reverse-charges VAT.  
  - You pay UK corporation tax on profits.  
  - Simpler compliance and European commercial alignment.

- **If the work is billed from the US entity**:  
  - No Swedish VAT at all.  
  - Profits taxed in the US (21% + state).  
  - Possible *Controlled Foreign Company* or *transfer-pricing* adjustments if NBI UK contributes materially.  
  - Currency and treaty paperwork increase administrative cost.

- **If personnel travel or work in Sweden** (for >183 days or with on-site presence), Sweden may assert a **permanent establishment**, leading to Swedish corporate tax on local profits regardless of which entity invoices.

---

### Typical efficient setup
For EU or EEA clients, **most consulting firms choose the UK entity** to bill and deliver work.  
Reasons:
- Clear VAT treatment (export of services, zero-rated).  
- No Swedish withholding or local tax if no PE.  
- Lower compliance risk than a US invoice into the EU.  
- Still competitively taxed if profits are later distributed via group transfer pricing.

---

### Summary Recommendation
> **Perform and invoice the work from the UK office** unless the delivery requires on-site work in Sweden.  
> This minimises total tax exposure and administrative friction while maintaining EU-aligned VAT treatment and treaty protection.

## Option analysis

### 1) Bill and perform from the UK entity
- **Corporate income tax:** 25 percent on UK profits.  
- **VAT:** B2B supply of services to an EU customer is reverse charged. You invoice without UK VAT and state “Reverse charge. Customer to account for VAT in Sweden.”  
- **Sweden exposure:** No Swedish corporate tax if there is no permanent establishment. Short trips, no fixed place of business, and no dependent agent keeps PE risk low.  
- **Admin and cash:** Simple, European time zone, clients are used to reverse-charge mechanics.  
- **When to choose:** Remote consulting, light travel, no Swedish payroll, no client requirement for a local entity.

### 2) Bill and perform from the US entity
- **Corporate income tax:** 21 percent federal plus state (often 3 to 8 percent) on US profits.  
- **VAT:** Outside EU VAT. Swedish client will still self-account for VAT under reverse charge for electronically supplied or general B2B services, but some procurement teams prefer an EU or UK supplier.  
- **Risk and admin:** Higher transfer-pricing complexity if UK staff help. PE risk in Sweden if US personnel spend real time on-site. USD-SEK FX adds friction.  
- **When to choose:** You want profits in the US for NOL use or credits, cash must sit in USD, or the work is truly delivered by US staff with minimal EU travel.

### 3) Reopen a Sweden entity (AB or branch)
- **Corporate income tax:** 20.6 percent on Swedish-sourced profits.  
- **Employer social costs:** c. 31 to 33 percent on Swedish payroll. This can outweigh the lower corporation tax if you staff locally.  
- **VAT:** Swedish VAT registration. You will charge 25 percent VAT to Swedish B2B customers, then they deduct it. Input VAT is recoverable.  
- **Admin:** Full local bookkeeping, returns, statutory requirements, payroll if you hire, potential audit thresholds.  
- **When to choose:** You will have recurring on-site work that risks PE anyway, plan to hire Swedish staff, clients insist on a Swedish supplier, or annual Sweden revenue is large enough to justify the overhead.

1) **On-site presence becomes continuous or frequent** enough to risk a fixed place of business or dependent agent in Sweden.
