[ASSISTANT]
Transferring someone from **IR35 (contractor status) to Full-Time Employment (FTE)** in the UK requires several steps, both legal and operational. Here's a breakdown of what needs to be done:

---

## **1. Offer & Employment Contract**
- Issue a **formal offer letter** outlining the new terms of employment.
- Provide a **contract of employment** that includes:
  - Salary
  - Benefits (pension, holidays, sick pay, etc.)
  - Working hours and location
  - Notice periods
  - Any restrictive covenants (if needed)

---

## **2. IR35 and Tax Implications**
- The individual will move from **self-employed (Limited Company or Umbrella)** to **PAYE employee**.
- If previously working inside IR35, their tax situation may not change much, but they will now receive:
  - Employer's **National Insurance Contributions (NICs)** paid by the company.
  - Deducted **PAYE income tax** and **employee NICs** via payroll.

---

## **3. Payroll Setup**
- Register the employee on **PAYE (Pay As You Earn)** payroll.
- Ensure compliance with **HMRC Real-Time Information (RTI) reporting**.
- Set up **pension auto-enrollment** (if applicable).

---

## **4. Benefits & HR Compliance**
- Enroll in company benefits such as:
  - Pension scheme (e.g., **Workplace Pension via Nest, Aviva, etc.**)
  - Private health insurance (if offered)
  - Annual leave and sick pay policies

- Conduct **Right to Work** checks (e.g., Passport, Visa if applicable).
- Verify **DBS checks** if required for the role.

---

## **5. Transition from Ltd/Umbrella to FTE**
If the individual previously operated through a **Limited Company**, they may need to:
- **Close their Ltd company** or make it dormant (if no longer needed).
- **Cease VAT registration** if applicable.
- **Submit final accounts** and tax returns for the Ltd.

If working via an **umbrella company**, they will:
- **Terminate their contract** with the umbrella provider.
- Ensure any pending payments are processed.

---

## **6. Probation & Onboarding**
- Define a **probation period** (usually **3-6 months**).
- Provide **employee handbooks and policies**.
- Assign any required **training or onboarding tasks**.

---

### **Key Considerations**
✅ **Contractor Take-Home Pay vs. Salary** – Ensure they understand the impact of employer tax & benefits.  
✅ **Statutory Employee Rights** – They now receive protections like unfair dismissal rights, redundancy pay, and maternity/paternity leave.  
✅ **Pension Contributions** – The employer must contribute at least **3%** to a qualifying pension scheme.
