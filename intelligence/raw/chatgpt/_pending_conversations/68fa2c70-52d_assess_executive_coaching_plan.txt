Guardrailing means setting clear boundaries so work and behaviour stay on track without you steering every move. You define what is allowed, what is not, when to stop, who decides, and what happens if a boundary is crossed. It reduces rework and risk while leaving room for people to choose how they execute.

How it differs from micromanaging: micromanaging tells people exactly how to do the task. Guardrails state the limits and outcomes, then let the team choose the route.

1) Simple purpose in one line.
