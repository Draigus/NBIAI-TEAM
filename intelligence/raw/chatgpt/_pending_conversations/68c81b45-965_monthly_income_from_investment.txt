---

### Assumptions
1. **Lottery prize**: €34,000,000  
2. **Convert to GBP**: Assume €1 = £0.84 (mid-2025 average rate).  
   → €34m ≈ **£28.56m**  
3. **House purchase**: Spend £5m outright, leaving:  
   → £28.56m – £5m = **£23.56m investable capital**  
4. **Sheltered strategy**: Assume a conservative diversified portfolio in a tax-efficient UK wrapper (e.g., offshore bond, SIPP, ISA, trust).  
   - **Net safe withdrawal rate**: 3.5% annually (reasonable for long-term wealth preservation in GBP).  
   - If you prefer “interest only” without touching principal, a **3% conservative net yield** is feasible today on mixed assets.  
   I’ll show both.  

---

### Scenario A – Safe Withdrawal (3.5%)
- Annual income: £23.56m × 3.5% = **£824,600**  
- Monthly: £824,600 ÷ 12 ≈ **£68,700**  

---

### Scenario B – Interest Only (3%)
- Annual income: £23.56m × 3% = **£706,800**  
- Monthly: £706,800 ÷ 12 ≈ **£58,900**  

---

### Scenario C – Slightly More Aggressive (4%)
- Annual income: £23.56m × 4% = **£942,400**  
- Monthly: £942,400 ÷ 12 ≈ **£78,500**  

---

### Answer
With £23.56m invested in a sheltered strategy:  
- **Conservative (interest only)**: ~£59k per month  
- **Standard safe withdrawal**: ~£69k per month  
- **Aggressive**: up to ~£79k per month  

This is **after the house purchase** and assuming UK tax-sheltering structures are used properly.
