## Assumptions
- You’re on Foundry v13 and the DnD5e system.
- By “scroll of teleportation” you mean a spell scroll that casts **Teleport** (a 7th level spell).

## Plan
Fastest reliable method is: create a Teleport scroll from the spell, then delete (or ignore) the generic “Spell Scroll (7th Level)”.

## Do this on the character sheet (recommended)
1) Open the character sheet.  
2) Go to **Inventory**.  
3) Find the **Teleport** spell (Compendium Browser or Spells directory).  
4) Drag **Teleport** onto the character’s **Inventory** tab.  
5) In the popup, choose **Create Scroll** and set the level to **7** (or leave default if it is already 7). citeturn5view0  
6) You now have a scroll for Teleport in inventory. Delete the old “Spell Scroll (7th Level)” if you do not need it.

This is an official DnD5e workflow: right click “Create Scroll”, or drag a spell onto an Actor’s Inventory to create a scroll. citeturn5view0  

## If you insist on converting the existing generic scroll
This only works if your “Spell Scroll (7th Level)” item sheet has a field that lets you pick the embedded spell.
1) Right click the scroll in inventory, **Duplicate** it (safety copy).
2) Open the scroll item.
3) Look for a “Spell” selector or similar, set it to **Teleport**, then rename it to something like “Spell Scroll (Teleport)”.
If there is no spell selector, that generic item cannot be “bound” cleanly. Use the recommended method above.

## Acceptance tests
- The scroll appears under **Inventory** as a scroll/consumable.
- Clicking “Use” (or equivalent) triggers Teleport and consumes the scroll (usually 1 use, destroy on empty).
