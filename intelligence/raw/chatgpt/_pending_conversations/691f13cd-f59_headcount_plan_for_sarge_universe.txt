## 8. `DAU_MAU` sheet

Approximate DAU and MAU for hosting, UX sense check and KPI charts.

Columns:

A. `Month`  
B. `NewInstalls`  
C. `Approx_MAU`  
D. `Approx_DAU`

Rows:

- A2: 1, fill down to 60.  
- B2: `=Cohorts!B2` copy down.

MAU approximation: simple weighted 3 month model:

- For month 1 (row 2): `C2 = B2`  
- For month 2 (row 3): `C3 = B3 + 0.6*B2`  
- For month 3 and later (row r ≥ 4):  
  `C_r = B_r + 0.6*B_{r-1} + 0.3*B_{r-2}`  

DAU:

- D2: `=C2/3` copy down.

This is not perfect, but it uses a decay shape and is easy to read. Document the simplification in `ReadMe` as a known modelling compromise.

## 14. Expert red team and rating

### CEO / CPO view

Score: **9 / 10**

Strengths

- Clear separation between strategic inputs (`Scenario_Controls`, `Drivers_Main`) and engine sheets.  
- Cohort view of installs, cost, CPI and LTV so you can argue payback by cohort and by channel.  
- Explicit multi stream monetisation split (IAP, pass, cosmetics, tournaments).

Residual risks

- Tournament economics are folded into top level LTV shares rather than a separate structural model of entry fees and rake per tournament.

Mitigation

- Use this for fundraise and board-level planning now, then layer more detailed tournament and 180 day revenue curves once you have real data.

## 15. Open risks and questions called out explicitly

Without asking you to pause and answer, there are three modelling risks that remain and should be written into the `ReadMe` and data room notes:

1. **Retention fidelity**  
   - Risk: LTV per install is a scalar, and revenue is recognised in one lump at month+6. This ignores detailed day-by-day retention and monetisation patterns.  
   - Impact: Early revenue ramp shape is stylised; payback timing within the first six months is not precise.

2. **DAU/MAU realism**  
   - Risk: DAU and MAU are approximated using a three month weighted rule, not constructed from daily retention.  
   - Impact: Hosting and “engagement scale” discussions are approximate until you revisit with real data.

3. **Tournament specific economics**  
   - Risk: Tournament entry fees and rake are not modelled separately; they are a slice of LTV.  
   - Impact: Harder to answer investor questions specifically about creator incentives, rake sensitivity and tournament-led UA.

If you are happy with this v2 structure, the next step would be for you or a finance-savvy teammate to transpose this into Excel and we can then iterate by auditing concrete formulas in your actual file, cell by cell, rather than fighting tooling limitations here.
