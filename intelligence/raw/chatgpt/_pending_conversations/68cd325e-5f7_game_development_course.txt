# A) Course Playbook — Build and Launch a Game

## 0. First principles

- Make something specific, not general. Tie mechanics to a clear core loop and a few key emotions you want to evoke. The MDA framework is a useful lens: mechanics create dynamics that evoke aesthetics, which are the player feelings you want to target. citeturn0search12  
- Bake in the five sticky system families as you design: progression, social, skill, emotional, and reward systems. Your images reflect these and they map cleanly to MDA. citeturn0search12

Output for this step  
A one-pager that states: target players, core loop, 3 emotions to evoke, and 3 supporting systems.

Pitfalls  
Too many features, no emotional target, unclear player promise.

## 1) How to have an idea for a game

**Do**  
- Start from constraints: player fantasy, platform, and session length.  
- Survey nearest neighbours and contrasts. Size demand with public league tables and high-level market figures so you know where the audience lives. Newzoo’s 2025 outlook is a solid anchor for scale and region. citeturn1search2turn1search6

**Learn**  
- MDA basics to align feelings to systems. citeturn0search12

**Produce**  
- Concept One-Pager template (below).  
- 10 comparable titles and what you will out-do or avoid.

**Pitfalls**  
“Novelty only” thinking, ignoring session fit for the audience, and chasing clones in a crowded sub-genre.

## 2) Turning an idea into a design

**Do**  
- Write a mini-GDD that is short, living, and visual. Long, rigid tomes slow teams. Use a modern, lightweight GDD structure. citeturn0search5turn0search9  
- Prototype to validate the core verb. Then build a vertical slice to prove quality and integration. Prototypes answer “should we build this,” vertical slices answer “can we build it at target quality.” citeturn0search10turn0search13

**Learn**  
- Core loop and sub-loops.  
- Reward schedules and pacing.  
- How progression, social, skill, emotional, and reward systems interact.

**Produce**  
- Mini-GDD, Prototype Plan, Vertical Slice Brief.

**Pitfalls**  
Over-documenting, skipping playable tests, calling a prototype a vertical slice.

## 3) What earns investor interest in 2025

**Reality check**  
- 2025 investors and publishers prioritise identity and maturity. Identity is a crisp product vision; maturity is proof you understand budgets, models, platforms, and go-to-market. citeturn0search7  
- Use publisher guidance for pitch artefacts. Raw Fury’s public checklist is representative of what many publishers expect. citeturn1search0turn1search16  
- Wishlist momentum and community signals matter for PC. Steam practice emphasises building wishlists and timing calls to action. citeturn1search1turn1search5

**Artefacts to prepare**  
- Trailer or 60-second gameplay cut.  
- Deck: hook, market, comps, roadmap, budget, team, risks, asks.  
- Build: prototype or vertical slice.  
- Cap table and hiring plan.  
- GTM outline and wishlist or community proof. Guidance on “deck + build” is widely echoed by publisher-facing articles. citeturn1search12

**Pitfalls**  
Pitching an idea without a build, overpromising TAM, vague budgets.

## 4) Define your game and validate market fit

**Do**  
- Validate there is space in the sub-genre and platform you target using fresh market snapshots and by triangulating against top countries by revenue and players. citeturn1search2turn1search6  
- Run fast playtests to confirm the intended emotions are actually felt.  
- For PC, track wishlists and conversion cohorts through time as leading signals. citeturn1search1

**Produce**  
- Market Fit Brief: target segment, why you win, early signals, next proof.

**Pitfalls**  
Confusing audience excitement with team excitement, over-indexing on aesthetics over feel.

## 5) Structuring your production

**Key structure**  
- Gate on outcomes: Prototype, Vertical Slice, Alpha, Beta, 1.0.  
- Cross-functional squads aligned to features.  
- A producer is a servant leader and a communicator who kills ambiguity, protects focus, and anticipates risks. They run the cadence, surface trade-offs, and make the plan legible.

**Common pain points**  
- Unstable scope, unclear owners, late cross-discipline integration, missing telemetry, no realistic content pipeline for LiveOps.

**Produce**  
- RACI by craft, cadence calendar, risk register, and a thin telemetry spec for every milestone so you learn earlier, not later. For live games, building data architecture early shortens rework and trains the team. fileciteturn0file7L73-L112

## 6) GTM, roadmapping, milestones — how to make the soup

**Do**  
- Map a GTM arc by milestone: teaser, page live, demo or playtest, beats to launch, and post-launch seasonal plan.  
- LiveOps is the discipline of running evolving content, events, offers, and community rhythms that improve retention and revenue. Treat it as customer-centric and data-informed. citeturn1search3turn1search7

**Produce**  
- Milestone Map with success metrics, event calendar for the first 90 days, and a content pipeline that you can sustain.

**Pitfalls**  
No owner for LiveOps, bolt-on events that do not connect to progression or the economy, or GTM without a playable moment.

# B) Upgraded course structure with outcomes and acceptance tests

Each module lists Objectives, You Produce, Acceptance Test, Pitfalls.

## Module 0. Definition of Ready
- **Objectives**: Align on audience, platform, session length, three target emotions.  
- **You Produce**: Concept one-pager, neighbour and contrast list, success thesis.  
- **Acceptance Test**: A stranger can pitch the game in one sentence and name the feelings it should evoke.  
- **Pitfalls**: Vague fantasy, unclear platform, conflicting emotions.

## Module 1. Idea to Emotional Design
- **Objectives**: Tie mechanics to feelings and session goals.  
- **You Produce**: Pillars, core loop sketch, early feel targets, top three comparable titles with differentiation.  
- **Acceptance Test**: A 30-second paper or greybox loop demonstrates the core verb.  
- **Pitfalls**: Feature sprawl, aesthetics over feel.

## Module 2. Market and Player Reality
- **Objectives**: Size the reachable niche and verify platform fit.  
- **You Produce**: Niche definition, player jobs-to-be-done, platform choice rationale, early community plan.  
- **Acceptance Test**: One page proving where these players already spend time and how you will reach them.  
- **Pitfalls**: Total addressable market slides with no reach plan.

## Module 3. Mini-GDD and Prototype Plan
- **Objectives**: Capture the design in a living, lightweight spec and plan the first proof.  
- **You Produce**: Mini-GDD, prototype hypotheses, 2-week build scope, playtest script.  
- **Acceptance Test**: Prototype answers three yes-or-no questions about fun and feel.  
- **Pitfalls**: Treating wireframes and docs as progress.

## Module 4. Vertical Slice
- **Objectives**: Prove quality and integration, not just possibility.  
- **You Produce**: Slice brief, content sample, performance targets, feel checklist, review plan.  
- **Acceptance Test**: External testers describe the intended feelings, performance targets met, crash rate under target, and a short cut trailer can be cut from the slice.  
- **Pitfalls**: Calling a polished prototype a slice.

## Module 5. Funding and Publishing Readiness
- **Objectives**: Show identity and maturity.  
- **You Produce**: Pitch deck, 60-second gameplay cut, budget and burn, team matrix, risks with mitigations, use of funds, cap table summary, demand proof ladder.  
- **Acceptance Test**: Funding-readiness score 70 or higher on the scorecard below.  
- **Pitfalls**: No build, vague numbers, no clear ask.

## Module 6. Production Operating System
- **Objectives**: Build a cadence that ships.  
- **You Produce**: Outcome-based roadmap, capacity plan by sprint, RACI, decision log, risk register with triggers, vendor plan.  
- **Acceptance Test**: Every roadmap item has an owner, estimate, dependency, and a testable outcome.  
- **Pitfalls**: Calendar theatre, late cross-discipline integration.

## Module 7. GTM and Community
- **Objectives**: Turn interest into conversion.  
- **You Produce**: Store and page plan, creator outreach plan, beats timeline, playable moments, demo or playtest plan, pre-launch funnel.  
- **Acceptance Test**: A public beat generates measurable followership and playtest sign-ups.  
- **Pitfalls**: Marketing without playtime, trailer with no call to action.

## Module 8. Telemetry, KPI canon, and Experimentation
- **Objectives**: Measure learning, not just outcomes.  
- **You Produce**: Minimal event schema, dashboards by phase, KPI canon with definitions, experiment backlog.  
- **Acceptance Test**: For each milestone, three KPIs have a target, owner, and decision rule.  
- **Pitfalls**: Collecting data with no decision hooks.

**KPI canon by phase (examples)**  
- Prototype: first-session fun rating, time to first smile, input error rate.  
- Slice: frame time, crash rate, encounter success curve, repeat-play intent.  
- Beta: day 1, day 7 retention, conversion to first purchase or equivalent value action, tutorial drop-off step.  
- Launch and Live: DAU, WAU, MAU, session count and length, payer rate, ARPPU, attach to events or passes, sentiment index, defect density.

## Module 9. Economy and Progression
- **Objectives**: Build pacing that sustains.  
- **You Produce**: Sinks and sources table, reward schedule, difficulty ramp, content budget per month, anti-exploit rules.  
- **Acceptance Test**: A 30-day paper sim shows no hard stalls and avoids runaway inflation.  
- **Pitfalls**: Over-tight currencies, stingy rewards, or trivial content loops.

## Module 10. Post-launch Operating Model
- **Objectives**: Sustain and grow.  
- **You Produce**: 90-day event calendar, content pipeline capacity, incident response plan, roadmap for quality of life fixes, creator and community care plan.  
- **Acceptance Test**: Two events shipped on time in test, with learnings applied to the third.  
- **Pitfalls**: Event spam without meaning, economy shocks, chasing short-term revenue at the cost of trust.

## 4) Production RACI and cadence

- Fortnightly: integrated build, outside playtest, roadmap replan vs capacity.

## 5) Economy sinks and sources table (columns)

## 6) Vertical slice QA grid

## 7) Hiring wave plan

# New Module 4C. Playtesting Operating System

**Purpose**  
Make reliable playtesting a habit from paper to post-launch so decisions are grounded in evidence, not taste.

**Objectives**  
- Define test types by phase with sample sizes, profiles, and questions.  
- Standardise recruitment, scripts, telemetry, and analysis.  
- Tie tests to gate decisions and GTM beats.

**Test types by phase**  
- Paper and greybox (Modules 1 and 3): 5 to 8 participants, 2 sessions each.  
- Vertical slice (Module 4): 10 to 15 participants, 2 rounds, external only.  
- Alpha and Beta (Module 6): 50 to 500 players, NDA closed tests, cohort analysis.  
- Demo and festival (Module 4B and 9): open tests, conversion tracking.

**Cadence**  
- Fortnightly usability for development builds.  
- Monthly external test from slice onwards.  
- Post-beat review within 72 hours of any public playable.

**Recruitment profiles**  
- Primary audience segment, platform owners, input device split, language needs, accessibility needs. Maintain a panel of opt-in testers with consent and privacy controls.

**What to measure**  
- First five minutes: comprehension, input errors, time to first success.  
- Core loop: repeat engagement, mistake recovery, perceived fairness.  
- FTUE: step drop-offs by step_id.  
- Emotion targets: free-response quotes mapped to the three chosen feelings.  
- Performance: 95th percentile frame time, hitch count, crash rate per hour.  
- Conversion where relevant: wishlist, install, purchase intent.

**Scripts and instruments**  
- Think-aloud with a fixed task list.  
- SUS or a short 7-item usability survey, plus two questions on emotion and one open response.  
- Post-session interview guide.  
- Telemetry hooks aligned to Module 7 schema.

**Sample sizes and rules of thumb**  
- 5 users find the majority of first-order usability issues.  
- For comparative A/B, target 25 to 50 per arm for directional reads.  
- For open demos, segment by source, session length, and first-session cohorts.

**Decision rules**  
- Prototype gate: at least 70 percent complete the main task within 10 minutes.  
- Slice gate: 80 percent sentiment alignment on your three emotions, crash rate under 1 percent per hour, 95th percentile frame time on target.  
- Beta gate: day 1 and day 7 retention within target bands for closed cohort, tutorial completion above 70 percent.

**Do this now**  
1) Publish a 12-week test calendar tied to the milestone map.  
2) Draft three test scripts: Prototype, Slice, Demo.  
3) Implement FTUE, crash, performance, and CTA telemetry.  
4) Create a test report template with summary, issues, severity, and actions.

**You produce**  
Test calendar, recruitment panel brief, three scripts, telemetry checklist, report template, and a decision log entry after each test.

**Acceptance test**  
Two consecutive tests result in at least three prioritised actions and a gate decision recorded within 72 hours.

**Pitfalls**  
Unclear hypotheses, testers too close to the team, reports filed but not acted on, missing telemetry, ethics or consent not captured.

from pptx import Presentation
from pptx.util import Inches, Pt
from pptx.enum.text import PP_ALIGN
from pptx.dml.color import RGBColor

prs = Presentation()
# Widescreen 16:9 is default

def add_title_slide(title, subtitle=""):
    slide = prs.slides.add_slide(prs.slide_layouts[0])
    slide.shapes.title.text = title
    slide.placeholders[1].text = subtitle
    return slide

def add_bullets_slide(title, bullets, footer=None):
    slide = prs.slides.add_slide(prs.slide_layouts[1])  # Title and Content
    slide.shapes.title.text = title
    tf = slide.shapes.placeholders[1].text_frame
    tf.clear()
    for i, b in enumerate(bullets):
        if i == 0:
            p = tf.paragraphs[0]
        else:
            p = tf.add_paragraph()
        p.text = b
        p.level = 0
    if footer:
        left = Inches(0.5)
        top = Inches(6.8)
        width = Inches(12)
        height = Inches(0.5)
        tx = slide.shapes.add_textbox(left, top, width, height).text_frame
        p = tx.paragraphs[0]
        p.text = footer
        p.font.size = Pt(10)
        p.alignment = PP_ALIGN.LEFT
        p.font.color.rgb = RGBColor(100,100,100)
    return slide
