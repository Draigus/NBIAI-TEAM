## Why a White (or Near-White) Canvas Works

## Ways to Keep White from Feeling “Flat”

1. **Use Warm White**  
   Instead of pure `#FFFFFF`, try a micro-tint (`#FAFAF8` or `#FDFCF9`). It still reads as white but feels softer against charcoal text.

2. **Alternate Sand Bands**  
   Drop full-width sand (`#F1E9DE`) or very light grey (`#F5F7F9`) behind key sections. That rhythm breaks monotony and anchors the user’s scroll.

3. **Add Subtle Depth**  
   Give cards a 2 px charcoal shadow at 5 % opacity and 4 px radius. It’s barely visible, yet it lifts components and prevents a “print-flat” look.

4. **Leverage Micro-Animation**  
   Fade-in cards at 10–20 px translate-Y. Even on white, motion adds a layer of sophistication that keeps the interface lively.

5. **Typography Hierarchy**  
   Oversize slate H1s (58–64 px) contrasted with roomy body copy (18 px, 1.5 line-height) and generous margins create visual richness that colour alone can’t provide.

| Scenario | Recommended Base |
|----------|------------------|
| Night-mode option or accessibility toggle | Charcoal background, sand text, gold CTAs |
| Portfolio site for a creative studio (lots of imagery) | Very dark slate to make images luminous |
| Event landing page meant to feel theatrical | Brand gradient or rich colour vignette |
