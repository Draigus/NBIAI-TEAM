Below is a pragmatic “menu” of automation paths, ordered from most compliant / enterprise-grade to quickest DIY.  Pick the tier that matches your budget, volume, risk tolerance and existing tech stack.

---

### 1  |  Native ATS integrations (the *clean* way)

| What you do | What happens automatically | Typical cost |
|-------------|---------------------------|--------------|
| ● Hold a LinkedIn Recruiter or Job Slots licence <br>● Use a supported ATS (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, SmartRecruiters, iCIMS, etc.) | Enable **Apply Connect / Recruiter System Connect (RSC)** inside LinkedIn *and* your ATS.  LinkedIn pushes each “Easy Apply” application—resume file, profile metadata, screening-question answers—into the ATS in real time.  The CV lands on the candidate record; status updates sync both ways. | No extra fee beyond your existing Recruiter & ATS licences. |

Why choose it  

* Zero scraping; you stay inside LinkedIn’s Terms & GDPR.  
* One source of truth—no forwarding rules, no scripts to maintain.  
* Rich data (profile links, skills, LinkedIn tags) flows in with the PDF/Doc resume.  

Resources  
LinkedIn’s help centre lists every certified ATS and shows how to switch the integration on citeturn7view0.

---

### 2  |  LinkedIn → email → parsing service (the *fast* way)

1. **Create the job post with “Easy Apply”.**  
   In the *Receive applicants* field, enter a dedicated address such as `linkedin@jobs.yourcompany.com`.

2. **Point that mailbox at a parser.**  
   *Options:*  
   * Low-/no-code SaaS (Skima AI, Zapier + CandidateZip, Make/Integromat, etc.)  
   * Your own Gmail/Outlook rule that forwards every incoming “New applicant for …” message to the parser address.

3. **Parser saves the attachments** (PDF/Doc/RTF) and extracts name, email, phone, skills → pushes into Google Sheet, Airtable or your ATS via API.

Skima’s guide shows three variants—straight email forwarding, Easy Apply with custom email, or redirecting applicants to your career site citeturn5view0.

Pros & cons  

| ✅ Pros | ❌ Cons |
|--------|--------|
| Live in <30 minutes; no LinkedIn partner approval. | You still manage a mailbox (spam, storage). |
| Works even on the free Job Post tier. | If LinkedIn changes its email template, your parser needs tweaking. |
| Parser can enrich data (skills, seniority, sentiment). | Candidate experience ≈ OK, but slower than in-ATS flow (no auto-status updates). |

---

### 3  |  Mail-API or IMAP + small Python job (the *hackable* way)

If you prefer full control and have dev resources, run a lightweight service that:

```text
• Polls the inbox via Gmail API / Microsoft Graph every X minutes  
• Reads messages where subject LIKE "New applicant for"  
• Downloads attachments (resume + cover letter)  
• Writes metadata to a database or S3 bucket  
• Marks the email archived / moves to “Processed”
```

Open-source starters:  

* GitHub repo **Linkedin-Auto-Resume-Downloader** (Python + Gmail API) citeturn0search3  
* Blog with a cleaner refactored script & cron setup citeturn6view0  

Add a resume-parsing library (e.g., `python-resume-parser` or `Rchilli`) if you need structure.  Keep OAuth secrets in a secrets-manager; encrypt files at rest (GDPR).

---

### 4  |  LinkedIn Talent Solutions APIs (the *heavy* way – partner only)

LinkedIn exposes *Job Posting, Apply Connect and Candidate Data* endpoints.  A certified partner (often your ATS vendor) can:

* Post the job programmatically.  
* Collect full applicant payload (profile URN + resume download URL + answers) via webhook.  

⚠️  Access is restricted—you must pass LinkedIn’s partner vetting, sign data-use addenda, and respect “one customer, one app” rules citeturn1view0.  Unless you’re building a commercial HR product, it’s overkill.

### Governance & compliance checklist (don’t skip)

* **Privacy notice:** Update your careers page to tell candidates their data is stored in XYZ system.  
* **Retention rules:** Purge or anonymise CVs after statutory period (UK: 6 months for unsuccessful applicants unless consent).  
* **Security:** Encrypt in transit & at rest; restrict S3/G-Drive buckets to HR.  
* **LinkedIn ToS:** Scraping the UI with Selenium violates section 8.2—stick to email/API routes.

5. **Review data-processing agreements** and add LinkedIn + parser vendors as sub-processors.

---

## A. 100 % free with Google Apps Script (no-code once installed)

1. **Point LinkedIn “Easy Apply” to a Gmail address**  
   *When you create each job post, choose “Email address to receive applicants” and enter something like `li-apps@yourcompany.com`.*  

2. **Create a Gmail label called `LI-Applicants`**  
   *Filter: `from:(@linkedin.com) subject:"New applicant for"` → Apply label + Skip Inbox.*

3. **Copy-paste this Apps Script:**

```javascript
function saveLinkedInResumes() {
  const label = GmailApp.getUserLabelByName('LI-Applicants');
  const threads = label.getThreads();

  const folder = DriveApp.getFolderById('YOUR_DRIVE_FOLDER_ID');      // destination
  const sheet  = SpreadsheetApp.openById('YOUR_SHEET_ID')
                               .getSheetByName('Applicants');         // log sheet

  threads.forEach(t => t.getMessages().forEach(m => {
    m.getAttachments({includeInlineImages:false}).forEach(att => {
      if (/\.(pdf|docx?)$/i.test(att.getName())) {
        const file = folder.createFile(att.copyBlob())
                           .setName(att.getName());
        sheet.appendRow([new Date(), m.getSubject(), m.getFrom(), file.getUrl()]);
      }
    });
  }));
  // stop duplicates next run
  threads.forEach(t => t.removeLabel(label));
}
```

4. **Add a time-driven trigger** in the Script Editor → Runs every 10 min.  

Result: every resume file lands in your Drive folder and is logged to Google Sheets; nothing to pay. The Gmail/Drive quotas are in the tens of thousands per day—plenty for hiring needs. (Classes used: `GmailAttachment`, `GmailApp`, etc. citeturn1search2)

## B. Zapier “Free” plan recipe (small-volume teams)

> **Caveat:** the free tier allows only **one action per Zap** and **100 tasks a month**; multi-step or “Formatter” steps require a paid plan citeturn0search0turn0search2.  
> Workaround: create **two separate single-step Zaps** that fire off the same email trigger.

**Zap #1 – store the file**

| Step | App | What to configure |
|------|-----|-------------------|
| Trigger | **Gmail – New Attachment** | Search string `from:@linkedin.com subject:"New applicant for"` & “Has attachment = yes” citeturn2search0 |
| Action  | **Google Drive – Upload File** | Folder: `LinkedIn Resumes` |

**Zap #2 – log the meta-data**

| Step | App | What to configure |
|------|-----|-------------------|
| Trigger | *same as above* | |
| Action | **Google Sheets – Create Spreadsheet Row** | Columns: Date-received, Gmail “From Name”, Subject, `Attachments` (this holds the file-URL) citeturn2search8 |

You now use 2 tasks per applicant, so the **free 100-task cap ≈ 50 candidates/month**. If your volume grows, upgrade Zapier (£20/mo) or switch to the always-free Apps Script in Option A.

---

## Why the resumes really are in the email

When a candidate hits **“Easy Apply”** and uploads a CV, LinkedIn emails the poster:

*Subject:* “New applicant for *[Job Title]*”  
Attachments: the PDF/Doc resume (plus any cover letter).  
No scraping needed; you’re simply harvesting the file from your own inbox, which keeps you inside LinkedIn’s Terms of Service and GDPR.
