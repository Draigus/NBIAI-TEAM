While we sort access, here is exactly how to read any “Strategic Investor Road-show Support” proposal and answer your three questions fast.

# 1) What are they actually offering
- **Scope of work**: Narrative refinement, investor deck edits, model sanity checks, talking-points, pitch and Q&A coaching, target-list build, outreach, calendar management, and meeting prep/follow-ups.  
- **Output guarantees vs best-efforts**: Do they **guarantee** a number of qualified investor meetings, or is it “introductions where possible.”  
- **Targeting**: Which investor types, stages, geographies, fund sizes, and named firms.  
- **Deliverables**: Final deck, teaser, one-pager, data-room checklist, outreach copy, and a post-roadshow summary.  
- **Team**: Who actually does the work, seniority, hours committed, references.  
- **Timeline**: Start date, prep window, booking window, road-show window, and wrap.

# 2) What do they want
- **Fees**: Up-front retainer, monthly retainer, success fee on funds raised, expenses, and payment triggers.  
- **Terms**: Exclusivity on fundraising, minimum term, renewal, cancellation, claw-backs or refunds if targets are missed.  
- **Access**: Permission to use your brand, deck, data room access, management time for rehearsals and meetings.  
- **Control**: Approval rights over messaging, who approves investor lists, who can speak for Sarge.

# 3) What Sarge would actually get
Translate the above into hard numbers and artefacts:
- **Meetings**: Count of qualified investor meetings they will book, by tier, with an expected hit-rate to second meetings.  
- **Materials**: A deck that is investor-ready, a 1-page teaser, a cleaned model tab set, a diligence checklist, and a standard follow-up pack.  
- **Calendar**: A scheduled road-show block with confirmed dates and cities, plus a weekly pipeline report.  
- **Coaching**: A fixed number of rehearsal sessions and a live Q&A drill.  
- **Post-mortem**: A report listing investor feedback, objections, and next steps per account.

## Red flags to check in their doc
- Vague “introductions” with no meeting targets.  
- Success fee on **commitments** you sourced yourself.  
- Broad exclusivity that blocks you from talking to investors you already know.  
- No right to approve the target list.  
- No KPI or refund language if they miss booking targets.  
- Junior team doing the work while seniors sell it.
