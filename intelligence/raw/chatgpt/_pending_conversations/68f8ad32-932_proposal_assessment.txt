## **Areas for Improvement**

### 1. **Strategic Framing / Narrative Hook**
The opening is factual but could add a **contextual lead-in** that positions this RFP as part of Lighthouse’s larger *data-driven launch strategy* (as established in *Overview of Service and Approach for Lighthouse Games*【19†Overview of Service and Approach for Lighthouse Games.pdf】).  
**Suggestion:** Begin with one short paragraph framing *why* this model is critical to Lighthouse’s transition into live service profitability — connecting to internal analytics maturity, the hybrid/F2P strategy exploration, and the intent to unify financial and design decisions.

Example revision for the first paragraph:
> Lighthouse Games is commissioning a business modelling partner to quantify and stress-test the commercial strategy for Wonderland’s launch and early live-service years. The goal is to deliver a unified financial model that links acquisition, retention, monetisation, and live-service cadence to profitability, providing a decision engine for publishing and finance.

---

### 2. **Section Balance**
The **Objectives** section is comprehensive but lengthy. Consider breaking it into **sub-sections with short headers** (e.g., “Forecasting,” “AERM Modelling,” “Pricing and Elasticity,” “Scenario Testing”) so that evaluators can skim and reference requirements quickly.  

Also, move the **Output KPIs** under a single table or bulleted block labeled “Required Outputs” for brevity.

---

### 3. **Consistency with Prior Modelling Scope**
The prior “Hybrid vs Sequel” RFP (the *LH – live service modelling.docx* version)【22†LH - live service modelling.docx】and this new “Launch Strategy” RFP overlap but differ in purpose. The earlier RFP focused on **strategic comparison and cashflow mechanics**, while this one focuses on **launch-phase scenario planning**. To avoid confusion for recipients who have seen both, explicitly state this is *a separate engagement focused on launch modelling only*, not the hybrid comparison.

Suggested addition at the end of the Overview:
> This RFP supersedes prior hybrid/sequel financial model work and focuses solely on launch-phase scenario modelling for the premium and DLC strategy.

---

### 4. **Technical Clarity and Guardrails**
While it’s detailed, you might clarify:
- **Model Input Data**: specify if Lighthouse will provide player forecasts, baseline retention curves, or benchmark data (from *MScience*, *NPD*, etc.) to avoid scope ambiguity.  
- **Sensitivity Expectations**: you note that full toggling isn’t required — clarify that consultants should *structure for toggling later* but not *implement full automation*.  
- **Regional Modelling**: specify whether regional FX assumptions, taxation rates, and fees will be supplied or stubbed.  

---

### 5. **Tone and Flow**
The tone is clear but leans toward **dense corporate phrasing** in places. You could improve readability for non-financial readers (especially production or design stakeholders) by reducing compound sentences and splitting lists into tables or shorter bullets.  

For instance:
> “The model should estimate how player retention, reactivation, content cadence, and UGC influence revenue, LTV and annual EBITDA, including the ROI of live service and DLC.”  
→  
> “The model must estimate how retention, reactivation, and content cadence affect LTV and EBITDA, and calculate ROI for both live service cadence and DLC releases.”

---

### 6. **Timeline / Evaluation Criteria**
The timeline section is strong but one item is missing: **evaluation criteria** for selection. Consider adding a short paragraph such as:
> Proposals will be evaluated on technical capability, prior experience with game financial modelling, methodological clarity, and value for cost.

This anchors expectations for both parties.
