# Ghost Ship Games: Open Development and Live-Ops Production (Deep Rock Galactic)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Ghost Ship Games (Copenhagen, ~40-54 people) developed Deep Rock Galactic through 6+ years of crunch-free active development, from early access through full release into sustained live-service operations. The studio's production philosophy centres on "develop by doing rather than talking" -- getting things on screen as quickly as possible rather than extensive documentation and meetings.

Team coordination relies on free cross-department discussion rather than hierarchical approval processes. Daily stand-ups focus on sharing news rather than assigning tasks. The studio is primarily in-office with work-from-home Fridays and two additional WFH days per month.

Live-ops cadence: seasonal content updates every 4-6 months, adding enemy types, equipment, missions, and cosmetics. Deep Rock Galactic: Rogue Core entered Early Access May 2026 with an 18-month build window. The studio has grown to include a publishing arm (Ghost Ship Publishing) applying the same open-development philosophy to partner studios.

Community involvement is a production input, not just a marketing function -- player feedback directly drives product decisions throughout development.

## Decisions / Insights

- "Develop by doing, not talking" -- minimise meetings and documentation in favour of rapid iteration and visual output
- Stand-ups are for information sharing, not task assignment -- fundamentally different from Scrum's daily stand-up pattern
- In-office-first with limited WFH preserves the cross-department communication that drives their methodology
- 4-6 month seasonal cadence is sustainable for a ~50 person team doing live-ops without crunch
- Early Access with an explicit build window (18 months for Rogue Core) sets expectations for both team and community
- Community as production input: player feedback shapes priorities, not just validates finished features

## Context

Ghost Ship Games was founded 2016 by six industry veterans from larger studios who wanted to avoid common industry pitfalls. Deep Rock Galactic has sold 8M+ copies and maintains an active player base. The studio's success has enabled expansion into publishing. Sources: Creative Denmark case study, Ghost Ship Games website, TechTimes, Wikipedia, fandom wikis.

## Applicability

The 40-54 person team size sits squarely in NBI's client range. The "develop by doing" philosophy and information-sharing stand-ups are adoptable patterns for studios frustrated with Scrum overhead. The 4-6 month seasonal cadence provides a reference point for clients planning live-ops staffing. The in-office-first model with limited WFH is relevant for clients debating remote vs hybrid policies. Less applicable for fully remote studios, which need more structured async communication.
