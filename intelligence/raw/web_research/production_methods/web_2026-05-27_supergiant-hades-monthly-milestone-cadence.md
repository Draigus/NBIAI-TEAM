# Supergiant Games: Monthly Milestone Cadence and Anti-Crunch Production (Hades)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Supergiant Games (17 people, all original founders retained over 10+ years) evolved from loose 2.5-3 month milestones to a strict monthly milestone cadence for Hades. Each month follows a phased structure: early phase permits major code changes; mid phase locks code and shifts to data-driven changes (narrative events, voice-over, tuning); late phase is testing, bug fixes, and polish; completion involves playtesting before the cycle repeats.

Early Access converted internal milestones into publicly committed update schedules displayed on the game's main menu, creating external accountability. The studio's anti-crunch philosophy: "If your milestone schedule involves crunching every milestone, your whole production process is broken." Mandatory 20 vacation days per year (replaced unlimited PTO after discovering it created invisible pressure). No work emails after 5 PM Friday. Peer accountability for workload monitoring.

Creative-engineering coordination works because the team is small enough that individuals wear multiple hats (composer voices characters, art director handles multiple visual domains). Leadership distinguishes between self-contained extra effort (encouraged) and effort that creates ripple effects across departments (discouraged).

## Decisions / Insights

- Monthly milestones with internal phase gates (code-open, code-locked, polish, ship) create natural coordination rhythms without heavyweight process
- Public commitment (Early Access) forced production discipline that internal deadlines alone did not
- Unlimited PTO was counterproductive -- mandatory minimums work better for preventing burnout
- The "ripple effect" test for scope changes: does this extra work stay within one person's domain, or does it cascade across departments?
- Supergiant views their studio journey as a "mega-marathon" -- sustainability enabling future game creation is the metric, not shipping speed

## Context

Supergiant Games is one of the most consistently acclaimed indie studios (Bastion, Transistor, Pyre, Hades, Hades II). Their 17-person team size and 10+ year retention rate are exceptional. Sources: Game Developer interview with Greg Kasavin (creative director), Kotaku interview with Amir Rao (studio director) and Kasavin. Hades won 50+ GOTY awards and sold 4M+ copies in its first year.

## Applicability

The monthly milestone cadence with phase gates is directly transferable to studios of 15-40 people. The anti-crunch policies (mandatory PTO, Friday email cutoff, ripple effect test) are implementable immediately at any team size. The Early Access as production discipline tool is relevant for NBI clients considering early access strategies. However, the "everyone wears multiple hats" model breaks down above ~25 people, requiring more formal coordination structures.
