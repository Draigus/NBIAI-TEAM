# Critical Stage Analysis (CSA): Continuous Feedback Framework Replacing Postmortems

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Wolfgang Hamann (President/CEO of Koolhaus Games, formerly Radical Entertainment) developed Critical Stage Analysis as a replacement for traditional postmortems, presented at GDC. The core problem: postmortems occur after project completion, making feedback too late to impact the current project.

CSA implements monthly feedback loops at critical milestones using three questions: (1) Five things that went right during the period; (2) Five things that went wrong; (3) Five things that could be improved for the future. Respondents rate each answer on a 1-5 importance scale (1 = most important).

Implementation timeline: responses collected within 3 days post-milestone; compilation and team-lead meeting within 2 days of collection; full team presentation within 1 week of milestone. Total process time: 2-4 hours per cycle. Previous milestone issues receive status updates at subsequent meetings, creating accountability chains.

All compiled, unedited CSA documents are archived centrally for organisational learning. The framework acknowledges that larger teams (50+ people) require systematic feedback mechanisms because informal communication breaks down at scale.

## Decisions / Insights

- Traditional postmortems are structurally flawed: feedback arrives too late to help the current project and team composition has often changed
- Monthly cadence catches micro-level daily issues before they snowball into major problems during crunch
- The 3-question format with forced ranking (five items each, importance-rated) prevents vague feedback and forces prioritisation
- Archiving unedited documents preserves institutional knowledge across projects and team changes
- 2-4 hours per month is a negligible time investment for the information quality returned
- The accountability chain (status updates on previous issues at the next CSA) prevents "we identified this last month and did nothing"

## Context

Wolfgang Hamann developed CSA at Koolhaus Games (Vancouver) and Radical Entertainment. The framework was presented at GDC and published on Gamasutra/Game Developer. Koolhaus worked on licensed titles with publisher milestone requirements, so the framework was designed to integrate with external milestone structures. Radical Entertainment (True Crime, Prototype) operated at 200-400 person scale, but the CSA framework was designed to work from ~15 people upward.

## Applicability

Highly applicable for NBI's production consulting. CSA can be inserted into any existing production methodology without disrupting it -- it is a feedback overlay, not a replacement workflow. The 3-question format with forced ranking is simple enough to implement immediately at any client studio. Particularly valuable for studios transitioning from informal "we talk about it" feedback to structured continuous improvement. The monthly cadence aligns naturally with sprint reviews or milestone deliveries. NBI could standardise CSA as part of its production audit toolkit.
