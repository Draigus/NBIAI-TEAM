# Tim Cain's 9-Stage Game Production Framework (Including "Beautiful Corner")

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Tim Cain (Fallout creator, 30+ year industry veteran) defines nine sequential production stages: (1) Test Rooms -- isolated levels testing individual features; (2) Prototype -- playable mechanics without final art; (3) Beautiful Corner -- a small, highly polished area showcasing final visual quality, usually not playable; (4) Vertical Slice -- one complete section with nearly all features and finished art; (5) Horizontal Slice -- all game areas playable but unfinished, testing connectivity and playtime; (6) Alpha -- all areas and content present but unbalanced with bugs; (7) Beta -- complete content, focused on bug fixes, balancing, and optimisation; (8) Ship -- release; (9) Post-Launch/DLC.

The "Beautiful Corner" concept is the distinctive contribution: a small, non-playable area created to prove what the final game will look like at launch. It serves dual purposes -- stakeholder communication ("this is what we're building towards") and artistic feasibility testing ("can we actually achieve this quality?"). The Beautiful Corner sits between prototype and vertical slice, de-risking the art pipeline before committing full production resources.

## Decisions / Insights

- The Beautiful Corner is an underused tool: most frameworks jump from prototype to vertical slice, missing the intermediate step of proving visual quality is achievable
- Horizontal Slice (all areas playable but unfinished) tests structural integrity and total playtime -- a different validation than the Vertical Slice's quality validation
- The 9-stage sequence creates natural quality gates without requiring formal gate review meetings
- Test Rooms as the earliest stage emphasise feature isolation -- test combat, dialogue, and stealth separately before combining
- The framework is deliberately sequential: each stage builds on the previous one's validation

## Context

Tim Cain presented this framework via his YouTube channel in November 2023. Cain is the creator of Fallout, co-founder of Troika Games, and worked at Obsidian Entertainment on The Outer Worlds. His framework reflects experience across teams ranging from 10 to 150+ people and multiple decades of PC/console RPG development. The framework is genre-agnostic despite his RPG background.

## Applicability

The Beautiful Corner concept is immediately applicable for NBI's production consulting -- it provides a low-cost risk reduction step that studios can insert into their existing pipeline between prototype and vertical slice. The Horizontal Slice concept is valuable for open-world and non-linear games where connectivity testing is critical. The 9-stage framework is most useful as a communication tool between studios and publishers/investors, providing a shared understanding of "where are we?" that is more granular than the standard 4-phase model (pre-production, production, alpha/beta, launch).
