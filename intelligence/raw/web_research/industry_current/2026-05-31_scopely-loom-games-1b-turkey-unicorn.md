# Scopely Acquires Majority Stake in Loom Games (Pixel Flow) at $1B Valuation -- Turkey's Latest Gaming Unicorn

**Date:** 2026-05-31 (deal announced February 24, 2026; closing confirmed Q1 2026)
**Source:** pocketgamer.biz, gamedeveloper.com, hollywoodreporter.com, mobidictum.com, yenisafak.com
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** M&A, Mobile Gaming, Emerging Markets
**Relevance:** 8 | **Novelty:** 7 | **Actionability:** 8

## What happened

Scopely (subsidiary of Saudi Arabia's Savvy Games Group) acquired a majority stake in Istanbul-based Loom Games at a valuation exceeding $1 billion. The deal is structured as a multi-year, performance-based earn-out. Loom Games is a 20-person studio founded by CEO Kubra Gundogan and CTO Emre Celik, behind Pixel Flow -- a mobile puzzle game that launched late 2025 and became the only casual game released in the past 12 months to break into the monthly US top-20 grossing charts. Over 10 million players globally.

## Deal structure

- Multi-year performance-based structure valuing the business at $1B+
- Precise equity split and payment schedule undisclosed
- Founders retain creative independence and continue leading from Istanbul
- All 20 employees remain with the company
- Lead early investor: Arcadia Gaming Partners (Managing Director Akin Babayigit)

## Why it matters

1. **Turkey's gaming hub thesis confirmed at scale.** Loom Games ($1B, Istanbul) joins Grand Games ($70M Series B, Istanbul, cycle 2) and Dream Games ($2.75B valuation peak) as Turkish mobile gaming successes. Three unicorn-tier outcomes from one city is no longer coincidence -- it is a functioning ecosystem. Istanbul's combination of technical talent, lower labour costs, and proximity to European markets is producing mobile gaming outcomes comparable to Helsinki's golden era.

2. **Saudi capital continues reshaping M&A.** Scopely is owned by Savvy Games Group (Saudi PIF). Including Savvy's $6B Moonton acquisition, Saudi-backed entities accounted for $7B+ of the $7.7B total Q1 2026 M&A -- over 90% of all gaming deal value. This is not a trend; it is a structural shift in who controls gaming's M&A market.

3. **20 people, $1B valuation.** The $50M valuation per employee is extraordinary even by mobile gaming standards. It demonstrates that in casual/hybrid-casual mobile, a single breakout title with strong unit economics can generate valuations decoupled from team size. This is the inverse of AAA, where hundreds of employees produce uncertain returns.

4. **Earn-out structure as norm.** Performance-based earn-outs are now the default structure for mobile gaming acquisitions (also seen in Krafton/Subnautica 2 at $250M and Playstack/IMC at $151M). Buyers are pricing in execution risk rather than paying upfront for potential.
