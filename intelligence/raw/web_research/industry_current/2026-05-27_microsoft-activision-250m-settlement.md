# Microsoft Settles Activision Blizzard Shareholder Lawsuit for $250M (22 May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

On 22 May 2026, Microsoft and former Activision Blizzard executives agreed to pay $250M to settle a class-action lawsuit brought by Swedish pension fund Sjunde AP-Fonden (AP7) in Delaware Court of Chancery. The suit alleged former CEO Bobby Kotick rushed the $69B sale to avoid termination threats stemming from the 2021 California workplace misconduct lawsuit, thereby depriving shareholders of better value.

Microsoft funds 40% of the settlement directly; the remainder comes from Activision Blizzard's directors and officers liability insurance. Eligible shareholders (anyone holding ATVI stock between January 2022 announcement and October 2023 close) receive approximately $0.30 per share.

The settlement resolves all outstanding claims including Kotick's counter-suit against AP7. No party admits wrongdoing. Subject to judicial approval from the Delaware court, considered a formality.

## Decisions / Insights

- The $250M settlement on a $69B deal represents a 0.36% post-close legal cost -- a reference point for M&A advisory on litigation reserves
- D&O insurance covering 60% of the payout highlights the importance of adequate coverage in mega-deals
- AP7's success signals that pension fund activism in gaming M&A is viable and may encourage similar challenges

## Context

This is the final legal chapter of the largest gaming acquisition in history. Microsoft completed the $69B Activision Blizzard purchase in October 2023 after extended FTC and CMA reviews. The settlement closes nearly four years of shareholder litigation.
