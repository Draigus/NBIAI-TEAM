# Morgan Stanley: AI Could Unlock $22B in Annual Profits for Gaming Industry

## Key Content

Morgan Stanley Research (April 2026) projects AI tools could create a $22 billion annual profit opportunity for gaming companies by cutting development costs by "nearly half." Key numbers:

- Global consumer spending on video games: $275B (2026)
- Reinvestment in development and operations: $55B (~20% of total spending)
- AI cost reduction potential: ~44%, translating to ~$22B in freed-up margin

Named beneficiaries: Tencent, Sony, and Roblox (platform operators); Take-Two, EA, and Ubisoft (large publishers with scale to deploy AI across multiple titles). Named companies facing pressure: Playtika and Netmarble. Unity and Unreal Engine face a "binary outcome" -- AI could either strengthen their value proposition or render parts of their toolchain redundant.

AI applications driving the savings: environment creation, dialogue generation, software testing, and shortening production timelines. However, the report warns that lower costs also lower barriers to entry, "potentially heightening competition."

## Decisions / Insights

The $22B figure is the first major investment bank to put a specific number on AI's profit impact in gaming. The "nearly half" cost reduction is aggressive but directionally consistent with what studios are reporting anecdotally. The key insight for NBI: AI benefits accrue disproportionately to large publishers with scale, while simultaneously lowering barriers for new entrants. This creates a strategic squeeze on mid-tier studios -- too large to be nimble, too small to deploy AI at scale.

The warning about increased competition from lower barriers is critical for advisory work. Clients need to hear both sides: yes, AI reduces your costs; but it also reduces your competitors' costs and creates new competitors.

## Context

Released alongside the GDC 2026 narrative that "AI took over GDC." The report supports NBI's EAD (Evaluate, Adopt, Deploy) framework positioning -- studios need structured approaches to AI adoption, not ad hoc experimentation.
