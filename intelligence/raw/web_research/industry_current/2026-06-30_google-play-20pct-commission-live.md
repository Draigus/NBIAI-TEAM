# Google Play Commission Cut Goes Live June 30 -- 20% Standard Fee Replacing 30% for EEA, UK, and US

**Date:** 2026-06-30
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** Platform Policy
**Relevance:** 8 | **Novelty:** 6 | **Actionability:** 9

## Summary

The revised Google Play Store commission structure agreed as part of Google's Epic Games settlement enters force on 30 June 2026 for the EEA, United Kingdom, and United States. The standard in-app purchase commission drops from 30% to 20%, with subscriptions at 10%. Developers in Google's Apps Experience Programme or Play Games Level Up programme receive a further reduction to 15% on new-install transactions. The rollout reaches Australia by 30 September and South Korea and Japan by 31 December.

## Key Facts

- Effective 30 June 2026: EEA, UK, US markets
- New baseline: 20% service fee on in-app purchases (previously 30%); 10% on recurring subscriptions
- Additional 5% fee if using Google's own billing system (separate from the service fee)
- Google Apps Experience Programme / Play Games Level Up participants: 15% on new-install transactions, 20% on existing installs
- Background: changes result from Epic Games v Google settlement (announced March 2026); Fortnite returning to Google Play globally as part of settlement
- Further rollout: Australia 30 September 2026; South Korea and Japan 31 December 2026; remaining territories by 30 September 2027

## Strategic Significance

1. A 10-percentage-point reduction on the base rate (30% to 20%) is the single largest change to Google Play economics since the platform launched; for a studio generating $10M in annual Play Store revenue, this is a direct $1M annual uplift before any programme participation benefits.
2. The split fee structure (service fee + optional billing fee) creates a new optimisation variable: studios with sufficient volume to maintain their own billing infrastructure can avoid the additional 5%, meaning the effective rate for sophisticated operators could reach 15%.
3. This accelerates competitive pressure on Apple's App Store, which has resisted equivalent reductions outside the EU; studios and advisors should expect Apple to face further regulatory or competitive pressure on its 30% rate through H2 2026 and into 2027.

## NBI Advisory Relevance

- Immediately actionable for any NBI client with significant Google Play revenue: the June 30 date triggers a commercial review of platform economics and potentially pricing strategy
- For clients in early commercial discussions with publishers: Google Play unit economics have materially improved, which changes revenue share negotiation baselines
- NBI should flag this to clients in mobile who may not have updated financial models since the March announcement -- many studios will not have reflected the new rates in planning
