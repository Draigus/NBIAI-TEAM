# Bungie Plans Significant Layoffs as Destiny 2 Ends Development (21 May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

On 21 May 2026, Bloomberg reported that Sony's Bungie unit is planning a "significant" third round of layoffs following the decision to end Destiny 2 development. The final content update, Monument of Triumph, drops 9 June 2026 as a free download. No Destiny 3 is greenlit.

This is Bungie's third wave since Sony's $3.6B acquisition in 2022. Previous rounds: ~100 employees (8%, October 2023) after Lightfall revenue ran 45% below target; 220 employees (17%, July 2024) plus 155 transferred to SIE. Surviving staff funnel toward Marathon, the extraction shooter that launched 5 March 2026 to ~1.2M copies sold and $55M gross in three weeks, but whose Steam concurrent players fell 59% from an 88,337 launch peak to ~31,000 by mid-April.

Sony has taken $766M in total asset write-downs on Bungie: $201M in Q2 FY2025 (Destiny 2 decline) and $565M in Q4 FY2025 (post-Marathon launch).

## Decisions / Insights

- $3.6B acquisition yielding $766M in write-downs and three layoff rounds is a cautionary case for live-service studio valuations
- Live-service games that enter sunset mode leave hundreds of specialised staff without a follow-up project -- the transition cost is structural, not just financial
- Marathon's 59% concurrent player decline in six weeks signals the extraction shooter genre may be saturated (joining The Finals, ARC Raiders pattern)

## Context

Bungie had ~850 employees in 2024. After losing ~375 staff across two rounds plus the planned third wave, the studio is substantially smaller than when Sony acquired it. Destiny 2 has been active for nearly a decade (franchise since 2014).
