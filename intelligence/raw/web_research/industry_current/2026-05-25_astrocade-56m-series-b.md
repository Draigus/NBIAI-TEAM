# Astrocade Raises $56M (Series A + B) for AI-Powered Game Creation Platform

## Key Content (max 200 words)

On 5 May 2026, Los Altos-based Astrocade announced $56M in combined Series A (led by Sea) and Series B (led by Sequoia Capital) funding. Other investors include Google's AI Futures Fund, NVIDIA, LG Technology Ventures, Dentsu Ventures, Conviction Embed, Chaac Ventures, and Rogue VC.

The platform lets users create, play, and share games using natural language prompts with no coding required. In eight months since launch: 20M+ engaged users, ~5M MAU, ~140M monthly game plays, 75,000+ games built by creators from 80 countries.

Leadership: Amir Sadeghian (CEO), Ali Sadeghian (CTO), Fei-Fei Li (CSO - Stanford AI professor).

Funds earmarked for: engineering/product team growth, AI model performance improvements, and a $10M creator fund to incentivise top content builders.

Broader context: $1.8B has been invested in AI x Gaming over the past five years, with in-game content generation comprising 65% of deal value.

## Decisions / Insights

- Sequoia + NVIDIA + Google AI Futures Fund backing signals institutional conviction that AI-native game creation is a real category, not a novelty
- 5M MAU and 140M monthly plays in 8 months is genuine traction, not vapourware
- The $10M creator fund mirrors Roblox/Epic's playbook for bootstrapping a creator ecosystem
- AI gaming investment thesis is now dominant: AI companies are where the funding flows, not traditional studios

## Context

Gaming startup funding hit a decade low in 2025 even as the broader industry boomed. The money that does flow is overwhelmingly going to AI-native companies. Traditional studios are largely shut out of VC unless they have an AI angle.
