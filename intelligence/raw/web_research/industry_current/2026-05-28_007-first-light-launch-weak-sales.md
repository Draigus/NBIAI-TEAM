# IO Interactive's 007 First Light Launches to Strong Reviews but Weak Sales (27 May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

IO Interactive's 007 First Light launched on 27 May 2026 for PS5, Xbox Series X/S, and PC, with a Nintendo Switch 2 version delayed to Q3 2026. The game received strong critical reception, with reviews calling it "the best James Bond game ever made" and praising its cinematic storytelling and stealth mechanics.

However, analyst tracking indicates opening sales are not performing well. Pre-order numbers ran below expectations, with interest tracking more in line with Bethesda's Indiana Jones and the Great Circle (solid but not blockbuster) rather than the Uncharted franchise (which IO Interactive reportedly benchmarked against). IO Interactive responded to weak pre-order data by shifting its marketing approach in the final stretch before launch.

IO Interactive CEO Hakan Abrak confirmed 007 First Light is the studio's most expensive project ever, with development costs exceeding $100M. The cast includes Patrick Gibson (young Bond), Gemma Chan, and Lenny Kravitz, with a David Arnold score and Lana Del Rey theme song.

## Decisions / Insights

- The $100M+ budget against Indiana Jones-tier sales (estimated 5-8M units) creates significant financial pressure. Indiana Jones reportedly sold ~5M copies in its first quarter -- a good result for most games but marginal for a $100M+ production
- Licensed IP does not guarantee AAA sales velocity: both 007 First Light and Indiana Jones demonstrate that recognisable brands do not automatically match the installed audience of gaming-native franchises (GTA, Zelda, God of War)
- IO Interactive's pivot from Hitman (owned IP, World of Assassination trilogy with 75M+ players) to a licensed Bond game represents a strategic bet on franchise diversification that may not have paid off commercially
- The weak opening could affect the future of the IO Interactive Bond deal, which was announced as a multi-game partnership

## Context

IO Interactive spent approximately 5 years developing 007 First Light after announcing the Bond partnership in November 2020. The studio opened a new Barcelona office specifically for the project. This is IO Interactive's first non-Hitman game since 2016's Hitman season one, representing a significant strategic pivot for the studio.

## Applicability

Reference case for NBI advisory on licensed IP economics: $100M+ budgets for licensed games require blockbuster-tier sales to break even. Studios evaluating licensed IP deals should model conservative opening scenarios (Indiana Jones tier, not Uncharted tier) and ensure the licensing terms allow profitability at those volumes. The IO Interactive case also illustrates the risk of betting the studio's most expensive project on an untested IP format (Bond as third-person action-adventure rather than the proven Hitman formula).
