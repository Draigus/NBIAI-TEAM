# Embracer Group Spins Off Fellowship Entertainment -- Major IP Restructuring (20 May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

On 20 May 2026, Embracer Group announced the spin-off of Fellowship Entertainment as a separate publicly listed company on Nasdaq Stockholm, planned for calendar year 2027. Fellowship will be an IP-led entertainment company housing Embracer's premium game development assets.

Fellowship Entertainment takes 10 studios: 4A Games, Crystal Dynamics, Dambuster Studios, Dark Horse Media, Eidos-Montreal, Fishlabs, Flying Wild Hog, Gunfire Games, Middle-earth Enterprises, Redoctane Games, and Warhorse Studios. Major IPs include Tomb Raider, The Lord of the Rings, The Hobbit, Metro, Kingdom Come Deliverance, Dead Island, Darksiders, and Remnant. Dormant IPs under evaluation: Deus Ex, Saints Row, Legacy of Kain, Thief, TimeSplitters.

Fellowship had net sales of SEK 4,393M (~$420M) in FY 2025/26 and 2,169 headcount as of 31 March 2026. CEO Phil Rogers and COO Lee Guinchard transition from Embracer to lead Fellowship.

The remaining Embracer entity retains mobile games, PC/console publishing, physical distribution (Plaion), collectibles, and film distribution (Plaion Pictures). A new CEO recruitment is underway.

## Decisions / Insights

- This is Embracer's third major restructuring since the 2023 Saudi Arabia deal collapse, following the THQ Nordic/Plaion/Coffee Stain trifurcation and subsequent consolidation
- The IP-led model (licensing across games, film, consumer products) mirrors the Hasbro/Mattel playbook -- monetise franchises across media, not just through game sales
- Fellowship's dormant IP portfolio (Deus Ex, Saints Row, Thief, TimeSplitters) represents potential licensing or co-development opportunities for mid-tier studios
- SEK 4.4B revenue across 2,169 staff implies ~$194K revenue per employee -- below the industry average for premium studios, suggesting room for operational improvement

## Context

Embracer grew from a Nordic mid-cap to the world's largest gaming company by studio count through 78 acquisitions between 2019-2023, funded by a $2B Saudi Arabia deal that collapsed in May 2023. The subsequent restructuring saw 4,500+ layoffs, 15+ studio closures, and the sale of Gearbox to Take-Two for $460M. This spin-off is the culmination of that multi-year unwinding.
