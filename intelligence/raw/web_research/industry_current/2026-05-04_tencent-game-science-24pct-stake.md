# Tencent Raises Stake in Black Myth: Wukong Developer Game Science to 24% — Becomes Sole External Shareholder

**Date:** 2026-05-04
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** M&A / Investment
**Relevance:** 7 | **Novelty:** 7 | **Actionability:** 6

## Summary

In early May 2026, Tencent increased its registered stake in Game Science, developer of Black Myth: Wukong, from 5% to 24% by acquiring the 19% share previously held by Hero Games, the studio's original external backer. Tencent's initial 5% stake was taken in 2021 before Wukong's release. Following Hero Games' exit, Tencent is now the sole external shareholder. Game Science is in active development on Black Myth: Zhong Kui, their next title.

## Key Facts

- Tencent purchased Hero Games' 19% stake in Game Science (approximately 1 May 2026)
- Combined with Tencent's existing 5% stake, total holding is now 24% — sole external shareholder
- Hero Games was the studio's original external backer and has fully exited at this point
- Game Science's next title: Black Myth: Zhong Kui (sequel/companion project in the same IP family)
- Tencent originally took its 5% stake in 2021, before Black Myth: Wukong shipped
- Black Myth: Wukong is the highest-selling Chinese-developed game in history (~20M copies sold)
- Game Science founders retain majority; the 24% is a significant minority but not controlling
- Source: Daniel Ahmad (ZhugeEX) via verified industry analysis, corroborated by Game World Observer

## Strategic Significance

1. Tencent's pattern of deepening minority stakes after commercial validation is consistent across its global portfolio. The move from 5% to 24% following Wukong's commercial success mirrors its escalating positions in Riot Games and Epic Games — minority stake at inception, control or majority following demonstrated IP value. Game Science should be modelled as a likely full acquisition target once Zhong Kui ships.

2. Hero Games' clean exit signals a successful liquidity event for an early-stage gaming backer — one of the cleaner Chinese gaming VC exit stories of recent years. The timing (post-commercial success, pre-sequel) is textbook: exit at peak valuation without disrupting the development roadmap.

3. Tencent's sole external shareholder status, even at 24%, gives it preferential information rights and a structural inside position for future consolidation. There is no competing strategic investor who can offer an alternative to a Tencent bid.
