# Nacon Files for Insolvency, Cuts 90 Jobs, Closes Big Bad Wolf and Kylotonn Studios (May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

French publisher Nacon filed for insolvency on 25 February 2026 after parent company BigBen's banking pool refused to release funds earmarked for a partial repayment of a EUR 43M bond loan. By May 2026, the restructuring eliminated 90 positions (~15% of workforce) across multiple subsidiaries.

Key studio impacts: Big Bad Wolf (The Council, Vampire: The Masquerade - Swansong) closed entirely. Kylotonn (Test Drive Unlimited, WRC series) suffered near-total layoffs announced 13 May after a postponed 6 May meeting. French union STJV issued a public statement blaming Nacon's mismanagement for the closures.

The collapse stems from declining physical game sales and a failed transition to digital distribution. Nacon's portfolio leaned heavily on licensed AA titles (WRC, Tour de France, rugby) with narrow audiences, leaving insufficient margin to absorb the digital transition costs.

## Decisions / Insights

- AA licensed-game publishers are the most vulnerable segment in the current market -- narrow audiences, declining physical retail, and no live-service revenue buffer
- The EUR 43M bond default triggered by a banking pool decision shows how quickly publisher liquidity can evaporate without diversified financing
- 18-year-old studios (Spiders/GreedFall also closed under Nacon) destroyed by parent company financial mismanagement, not product failure

## Context

Nacon joins the growing list of European mid-tier publishers under severe stress (Embracer Group restructuring, Focus Entertainment rebranding, Koch Media/Plaion downsizing). The AA publishing segment is being squeezed between indie self-publishing and AAA consolidation.

## Applicability

Cautionary case for NBI's advisory on publisher partnerships and studio dependency risk. Studios considering publishing deals with mid-tier European publishers should evaluate the publisher's balance sheet and financing structure, not just their royalty terms.
