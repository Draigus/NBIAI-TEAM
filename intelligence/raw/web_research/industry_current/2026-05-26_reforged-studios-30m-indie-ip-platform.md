# Reforged Studios Secures $30M to Scale Indie Game IP Platform (April 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Malta-based Reforged Studios secured $30M in growth investment from MEP Capital Management, a New York-based private investment firm specialising in media and entertainment. The funding accelerates Reforged's strategy of building a consolidation platform for independent game IP.

The investment follows Reforged's acquisition of German indie publisher Headup. Portfolio now includes Bridge Constructor, Pumpkin Jack, and Super Meat Boy 3D, with further expansion planned across content and capability.

CEO Peter van der Watt: "Our focus is not just on acquisition, but on creating a platform that expands the reach of great IP." The model targets proven indie IP to extend game lifecycles through operational and technical expertise -- essentially a roll-up strategy for the independent games mid-market.

The $30M positions Reforged as a mid-market consolidator in the indie space, distinct from the mega-deals (Savvy/Moonton $6B, Scopely/Loom $1B) happening at the top of the market.

## Decisions / Insights

- A new category of acquirer is emerging: mid-market IP platforms that buy proven indie games and extend their lifecycles, rather than buying studios for their teams
- $30M from a media PE fund (not gaming VC) signals that non-endemic capital sees gaming IP as undervalued media assets
- The "platform for IP" model parallels what Embracer attempted at scale but collapsed on -- Reforged's smaller, focused approach may prove more sustainable

## Context

The indie mid-market has been squeezed between rising development costs and a VC funding freeze. Studios that cannot self-fund their next title face either closure (Spiders/Nacon, Midsummer) or acquisition. Reforged represents a third option: IP sale with continued lifecycle management.
