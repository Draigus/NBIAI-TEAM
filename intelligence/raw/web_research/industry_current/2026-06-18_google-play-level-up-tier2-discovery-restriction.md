# Google Play Level Up: Tier 2 Membership Now Required for Pre-Registration and Discovery — Rolling Out 30 June 2026

**Date:** 2026-06-18
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** Platform Policy
**Relevance:** 8 | **Novelty:** 7 | **Actionability:** 8

## Summary

Google Play's Level Up tiered fee and benefits structure goes live from 30 June 2026 in the EEA, UK, and US. The programme creates two tiers: Tier 1 retains the 20% commission from the Epic v Google settlement; Tier 2 reduces it to 15% but requires integration of Google AI features, Google Play Games achievements, and cloud saves. Critically, Tier 2 membership becomes a gate for access to editorial recommendations, algorithmic discovery surfaces, and pre-registration campaigns. The App Experience programme (full discoverability enforcement) rolls out by 30 September 2026. Developers not adopting Google's technology stack face both a higher commission rate and reduced organic discoverability — a compounding disadvantage at launch.

## Key Facts

- Rollout dates: fees from 30 June 2026 in EEA, UK, US; App Experience (discoverability) from 30 September 2026 in EEA, UK, US, Australia
- Tier 1: 20% commission (current Epic settlement rate), no additional technology requirements
- Tier 2: 15% commission, conditional on integrating: Google Play Games achievements, cloud saves, and Google AI (strongly implied to be Gemini)
- Tier 2 gates: editorial recommendations, algorithmic discovery surfaces, broader search results, and pre-registration campaigns
- Developers can change an app's tier per country every 6 months
- Google Play Games Level Up and App Experience programmes are the enforcement mechanism for Tier 2 discoverability
- Google will also shift chargeback costs to Android developers separately in 2026

## Strategic Significance

1. Discovery on Google Play is being restructured from quality/keyword/install-velocity signals to compliance-tier signals. Apps in Tier 1 are algorithmically deprioritised on editorial and recommendation surfaces regardless of review quality or install momentum. This is a structural change in how organic growth on Android works.

2. The 5% commission difference (15% vs 20%) is presented as an incentive, but the real mechanism is the discovery penalty for non-participation. For a mobile title where organic UA from featured placement materially reduces paid acquisition cost, the discoverability disadvantage may exceed the commission saving in practice. The PocketGamer.biz cost analysis ("The true cost of Google's Level Up") explicitly models this compounding effect.

3. Requiring Google AI integration as a Tier 2 condition creates algorithmic lock-in. Developers building Android-first games now have a financial and discoverability incentive to use Google's AI stack rather than competitors. This is not exclusive platform lock-in — it is a soft preference enforced through commission and recommendation economics.

## NBI Advisory Relevance

- Mobile clients in production need to make a tier decision before 30 June 2026. The choice is not purely financial — it is a UA strategy decision. A title expecting significant organic discovery at launch must be in Tier 2 or pay to replace that lost organic traffic with paid UA. NBI should flag this to any client within 6 months of their Android launch.
- Clients already live on Google Play should audit their tier status and model: (a) cost of integrating Play Games, cloud saves, and Google AI; versus (b) incremental revenue from the 5% commission saving on existing GMR; versus (c) estimated organic traffic uplift from Tier 2 discovery eligibility.
- The chargeback cost shift is a separate financial risk factor — review with clients who have dispute-heavy player bases or operate in jurisdictions with high chargeback rates.
- Tier 2 requires Google AI integration: this should be in any tech stack planning discussion for new Android projects. The decision is not just about Google Play policy — it implies a product feature set (achievements, cloud saves) that affects design and QA scope.
