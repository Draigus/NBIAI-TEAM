# Paramount Skydance Launches Dedicated Games Division With Three AAA Titles in Pipeline

**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** Industry Structure
**Relevance:** 7 | **Novelty:** 9 | **Actionability:** 7

## Summary

At Summer Game Fest on 5 June 2026, Paramount Pictures (now merged with Skydance Media) announced the formal launch of Paramount Games Studio, consolidating Skydance Interactive, Skydance New Media, and Paramount's IP licensing activity into a single games division. Paramount's President Tony Driscoll described games as "a core pillar of our content strategy alongside film, television, and streaming" -- marking the end of a passive IP-licensing model and a structural shift to first-party game development against one of the richest Hollywood IP libraries.

## Key Facts

- Announced 5 June 2026, Summer Game Fest (Dolby Theatre, Los Angeles)
- Leadership: Tony Driscoll (President), Dan Prigg (EVP Head of Games, formerly Skydance Interactive head), Amy Hennig (Studio Creative Director, previously Skydance New Media co-president), Shawn Kittelsen (SVP Creative & Production)
- Julian Beak (co-president, Skydance New Media) departed at launch
- Titles confirmed or in development:
  - TMNT: The Last Ronin -- collaboration with Platinum Games (AAA action, based on IDW graphic novel)
  - Star Trek: Shadow Frontier -- newly revealed at SGF
  - Avatar Legends: The Fighting Game -- newly revealed at SGF
  - Marvel 1943: Rise of Hydra (Skydance New Media, with Marvel/Lucasfilm Games)
  - Untitled Star Wars game (Skydance New Media, with Lucasfilm)
- IP library available includes TMNT, Star Trek, Mission: Impossible, Avatar, Transformers, Top Gun
- No investment figure disclosed; structure is internal division rather than externally funded studio

## Strategic Significance

1. Hollywood's largest remaining independent studio is now a games publisher in earnest -- this is qualitatively different from prior Hollywood licensing arrangements because Paramount retains creative control and owns the output, rather than licensing IP to third parties for a fee.
2. The Platinum Games partnership on TMNT: The Last Ronin is particularly notable -- Platinum is one of the few studios globally capable of executing high-quality action games at scale; aligning a Hollywood IP with a specialist developer indicates Paramount is pursuing quality rather than quick tie-ins.
3. The combined Paramount/Skydance IP library (TMNT, Star Trek, Mission: Impossible, Avatar, potential Transformers) gives this division an unusually deep bench compared with any previous Hollywood entrant to games.

## NBI Advisory Relevance

- Relevant for any NBI client pitching to or co-developing with major IP holders: Paramount's model (internal publisher + external development partnerships like Platinum) may become a template other studios expect
- For clients in the action/AAA space: the Platinum Games collaboration sets a quality benchmark; being in the orbit of deals like this requires demonstrated technical excellence
- The structural move -- from IP licensor to IP-owner/publisher -- is worth flagging to clients considering how to position themselves relative to Hollywood money entering games
