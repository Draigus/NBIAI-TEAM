# UK ASA Begins Active Loot Box Advertising Enforcement -- 26 May 2026

## Key Content

The UK Advertising Standards Authority (ASA) commenced active monitoring of loot box advertising on 26 May 2026, with targeted enforcement to follow. The rules require that consumers know before purchasing or downloading a game whether it contains purchasable loot boxes. Disclosures must be "immediately visible without requiring users to expand additional text" on App Store and Google Play listings. Generic labels like "offers in-app purchases" are explicitly insufficient.

Coverage: mobile game listings on Apple App Store and Google Play that feature loot boxes purchasable with real money or with virtual currency obtainable only through real-money purchases.

This follows the UK games industry's 2023 self-regulatory principles, which are widely criticised as failed -- non-binding with no enforcement mechanism. The ASA enforcement notice represents the first formal UK regulatory requirement with active monitoring.

Separately, the EU's Digital Fairness Act (expected Q4 2026) may ban loot boxes outright or require parental consent for minors. While a total ban is not expected, consumer protection-focused regulation targeting minors is likely.
