# Q1 2026 Gaming M&A Hits $7.7B Across 52 Deals -- Console Overtakes Mobile

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Q1 2026 gaming M&A reached $7.7B across 52 deals (Aream & Co report), more than triple Q1 2025's $2.4B and up from Q4 2025's $400M. Drake Star's parallel report counted 51 deals at $100B+ disclosed value (inflated by Paramount's $110B Warner Bros. Discovery acquisition including WB Games).

Top deals: Savvy Games Group/Moonton ($6B, Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, 1.5B downloads, 110M MAU), Scopely/Loom Games ($1B, 20-person Istanbul studio behind Pixel Flow), NCSoft/JustPlay ($202M, 70% stake), Mattel/Mattel163 ($159M, full buyout from NetEase JV), Nazara/Bluetile ($100M, 50% controlling stake).

Private financings: 106 deals, $785M disclosed. Top three all AR/XR hardware: RayNeo ($143M), Xreal ($100M), Viture ($100M). VC-led early-stage gaming deals dropped to post-pandemic low.

Revenue benchmarks for the quarter: Steam hit an all-time high of $5.6B. Console revenue reached a record $21.7B, surpassing mobile IAP ($20.5B) for the first time, driven by Switch 2's unprecedented launch. Mobile downloads declined year-over-year despite seventh consecutive quarter above $20B.

Public market: Pearl Abyss led with 149% gain on Crimson Desert success. Discord and PlaySimple preparing potential IPOs.

## Decisions / Insights

- Console overtaking mobile IAP for the first time is a structural shift worth tracking -- Switch 2 drove it, but the trend may persist as mobile growth stalls
- The VC early-stage freeze in gaming continues even as overall tech VC hit $300B in Q1 2026 (AI-driven). Gaming startups are being starved while incumbents acquire
- Saudi capital (Savvy + Scopely) accounted for $7B+ of the $7.7B total -- a single sovereign fund is reshaping the M&A landscape
- Turkey's emergence as a mobile gaming hub is confirmed: Loom Games ($1B, Istanbul) joins Grand Games ($70M Series B, Istanbul) as major outcomes

## Context

The $7.7B figure is the highest quarterly M&A total since the Microsoft/Activision Blizzard close. Excluding mega-deals, Q1 2026 had the highest quarterly deal value since end of COVID. The console > mobile reversal is historically significant.
