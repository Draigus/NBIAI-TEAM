# Valve Steam Machine Hardware Launch Imminent -- Four SKUs, Summer Game Fest Reveal Expected (May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Valve is preparing a multi-tier hardware launch with four Steam Machine configurations identified in Steam client metadata. Confirmed storage tiers: 512GB and 2TB SSD, with two additional SKUs likely bundling each with the new Steam Controller. All models share unified internals: semi-custom AMD CPU, 28-CU RDNA 3 GPU with 8GB GDDR6 VRAM, 16GB DDR5 RAM. Performance target: 4K/60fps via AMD FSR upscaling with ray tracing support.

Entry-level pricing projected at ~$499, though Czech retailers listed 512GB at ~$950 and 2TB at ~$1,070. US logistics tracking confirmed 50 tons of hardware (~20,000 units estimated) arrived as "Ready to Ship" inventory. Valve is reportedly preparing a queue-based reservation system prioritising verified Steam accounts over scalper bots.

Summer Game Fest (5 June) is the expected reveal venue. Geoff Keighley's 4K/60fps broadcast announcement and a steam-themed GIF response fuelled speculation. Valve has not officially confirmed main-stage participation. A companion "Steam Frame" standalone VR headset is also rumoured for certain bundle configurations.

## Decisions / Insights

- A new console entrant at $499 directly competes with PS5 and Xbox Series X on price while offering access to Steam's 50,000+ game library
- SteamOS (Linux-based) reaching console form factor could accelerate Linux gaming adoption and reduce Windows dependency for studios
- The 28-CU RDNA 3 GPU sits mid-range -- studios targeting Steam Machine should optimise for FSR upscaling rather than native 4K

## Context

Valve's last Steam Machine attempt (2015) failed due to fragmented hardware partnerships and immature SteamOS. Steam Deck's success (2022-present) proved the SteamOS concept. This is Valve's second attempt at a living room console, now with a unified hardware specification they control directly.
