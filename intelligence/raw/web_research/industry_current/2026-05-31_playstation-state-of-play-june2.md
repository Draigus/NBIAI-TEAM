# PlayStation State of Play June 2 -- 60-Minute Showcase With Major First-Party Reveals

## What happened

Sony announced a State of Play for June 2, 2026 at 2pm PT -- 60+ minutes of updates, announcements, and gameplay reveals. Alamo Drafthouse is hosting free in-cinema watch parties across the US, signalling Sony views this as a major consumer event. This is the largest pre-SGF showcase announced, running before Summer Game Fest proper (June 5).

## Confirmed content

- **Marvel's Wolverine** (Insomniac Games) -- kicks off the show with extended gameplay. Release confirmed September 15, 2026. Third-person action-adventure.
- Sony filed a new "Break In" trademark in the US and Europe, labelled as "video game software" -- potentially a rebrand of Fairgame$ (the live-service heist game).

## Leaked / high-confidence rumours

- **God of War spinoff starring Faye** -- multiple reliable leakers (AlexandreNGamR and others) claim this is the event's marquee reveal. Faye and Tyr as protagonists, Kratos no longer central. Exploration of Japanese and Chinese mythologies beyond Norse. Release targeted H1 2027.
- **Infamous** reboot or new entry -- less confident but multiple sources.
- **Resident Evil: Code Veronica remake** -- Capcom, reliable leaker-backed.

## Why it matters

1. **Sony's first-party pipeline visibility.** If God of War Faye and a new Infamous both appear, Sony is showing 3+ first-party AAA titles in development simultaneously (Wolverine, GoW, Infamous) plus the Break In live-service entry. This counters the narrative that PlayStation's first-party output has slowed post-Bungie acquisition fallout.

2. **Live-service strategy signal.** The Break In trademark (if it is Fairgame$ rebranded) suggests Sony is quietly repositioning its live-service bets rather than abandoning them. After Destruction AllStars shutdown and Bungie write-downs, any new live-service commitment is strategically significant.

3. **Pre-SGF positioning.** By running State of Play on June 2 (three days before SGF main show on June 5), Sony ensures its reveals dominate the news cycle before third-party announcements dilute attention. Xbox follows on June 7. The sequencing is deliberate competitive positioning.

4. **God of War franchise direction.** Moving away from Kratos as protagonist and into non-Norse mythologies is a major franchise pivot. If confirmed, it signals Sony Santa Monica is building GoW into a multi-protagonist, multi-mythology platform -- a long-term IP expansion strategy similar to how Assassin's Creed evolved.
