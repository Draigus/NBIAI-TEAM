# FTC Secures $20M Genshin Impact Loot Box Settlement; Global Regulatory Convergence Accelerating

## Key Content (max 200 words)

The FTC secured a $20M settlement with Cognosphere/Hoyoverse (Genshin Impact) over deceptive gacha loot box practices. The settlement bans selling loot boxes to under-16s without parental consent, requires odds disclosure, and mandates age verification. The FTC referred the complaint to the DOJ, signalling escalation beyond administrative action.

Simultaneously, regulatory convergence is accelerating globally:
- **Brazil**: new law bans loot box sales to minors (under 18) from March 2026
- **EU**: from 19 June 2026, distance contracts (including apps/games) require a visible cancellation button with immediate enforcement
- **Germany**: Bundesrat pushing for gambling classification of loot boxes (potential 18+ rating)
- **Australia**: chance-based purchases now rated minimum 15+
- **Belgium/Netherlands**: paid loot boxes already classified as gambling and effectively banned

The FTC's staff perspective paper stops short of blanket regulation but confirms ongoing monitoring and willingness to act against deceptive practices case by case.

## Decisions / Insights

- The Genshin settlement sets a baseline: odds disclosure, age gating, and parental consent are now the minimum standard in the US
- Studios with gacha/loot box mechanics should audit monetisation UX against dark-pattern risks immediately
- The EU cancellation button requirement (June 2026) affects every game with in-app subscriptions or recurring purchases
- Regulatory direction is one-way: tighter, not looser. Studios should design for the strictest jurisdiction they ship to

## Context

2026 is the year governments, courts, and market forces are converging to treat in-game currency as real money with real consequences. This is no longer a "maybe someday" regulatory risk -- it is active enforcement across multiple jurisdictions.
