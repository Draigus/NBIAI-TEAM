# EA / PIF $55B Acquisition: EU July 22 Deadline Confirmed; CFIUS Outside Date September 28

**Date:** 2026-06-19
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** Industry Structure
**Relevance:** 8 | **Novelty:** 7 | **Actionability:** 7

## Summary

The Saudi PIF-led consortium (with Silver Lake and Affinity Partners) pursuing the $55B acquisition of Electronic Arts has formally filed with the European Commission, which has set a July 22, 2026 deadline to either clear the deal or open a full formal investigation. The deal also faces separate scrutiny under the EU's Foreign Subsidies Regulation, which applies because PIF's stake triggers the non-EU government contribution threshold. CFIUS clearance is outstanding with a contractual outside date of September 28, 2026. No formal CFIUS objection has been reported as of June 19. The deal's original Q1 FY27 close target (by June 30) is not expected to be met.

## Key Facts

- European Commission phase 1 decision deadline: July 22, 2026 -- clear, clear with remedies, or open formal investigation
- Deal also subject to EU Foreign Subsidies Regulation review; PIF's sovereign fund status triggers the non-EU government contribution threshold (above EUR 50M)
- CFIUS review ongoing; contractual outside date September 28, 2026; no public formal objection filed as of June 19
- HSR (US antitrust) clearance already obtained
- EA shareholders approved the transaction in December 2025 at $210 per share, approximately $55B enterprise value
- Consortium: PIF (Saudi sovereign wealth fund), Silver Lake (private equity), Affinity Partners (Jared Kushner)
- EA stock trading approximately $9 below the $210 buyout price as of mid-June, indicating market pricing in regulatory risk
- June 30 close target slipping; deal completion now expected later in calendar 2026

## Strategic Significance

1. The July 22 EC deadline is the next hard date in this deal -- a phase 2 investigation would extend the timeline by months and likely force structural remedies (IP licensing, studio divestiture, or interoperability commitments), materially altering acquisition economics and potentially triggering Material Adverse Change clauses.
2. The FSR dimension is novel for a gaming deal of this scale. A PIF-owned EA would face ongoing EU scrutiny of subsequent M&A or market practices the Commission viewed as subsidy-distorted, setting a precedent for all future sovereign fund gaming acquisitions.
3. EA stock trading $9 below offer price signals meaningful market doubt about clean regulatory clearance. If the deal collapses, EA reverts to public-company constraints under continued pressure to monetise its sports franchises -- which may accelerate appetite for third-party studio partnerships.
