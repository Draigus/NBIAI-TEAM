# India Enacts Comprehensive Online Gaming Regulation -- PROG Rules 2026 (Effective 1 May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

India's Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) notified the Promotion and Regulation of Online Gaming (PROG) Rules 2026 on 22 April 2026, effective 1 May 2026. The rules establish the Online Gaming Authority of India (OGAI) with representatives from six ministries (MeitY, Home Affairs, Finance, I&B, Youth Affairs, Law & Justice).

Key provisions: complete ban on "online money games" (where users stake money with expectation of monetary gains), with banks and financial institutions barred from processing payments for such platforms. Esports receives formal legal recognition as a competitive sport for the first time. Registered esports platforms can charge entry fees and distribute performance-based prize money.

User protection measures include mandatory age verification, age-gating, parental controls, and counselling support. Grievance redressal and appellate mechanisms give users formal complaint routes. All gaming platforms must register with OGAI.

The rules apply to all platforms accessible in India, including foreign operators, creating extraterritorial compliance requirements.

## Decisions / Insights

- India's 500M+ gamer market now has a formal regulatory framework -- any studio or publisher targeting India must assess PROG compliance
- The real-money gaming ban eliminates a $3B+ Indian market segment overnight, while legitimising esports and social gaming
- Extraterritorial application means non-Indian studios with Indian users face registration and compliance obligations

## Context

India's gaming market is projected at $8-10B by 2028, driven by smartphone penetration and young demographics. Previous regulatory attempts were fragmented at state level (Karnataka, Tamil Nadu bans struck down by courts). PROG 2026 is the first national-level framework. The rules follow a pattern of emerging market gaming regulation (Brazil's betting regulation 2024, Turkey's gaming tax 2025).
