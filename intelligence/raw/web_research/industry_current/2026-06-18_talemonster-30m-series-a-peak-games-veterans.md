# TaleMonster Games Raises $30M Series A — Peak Games Veterans, a16z + Arcadia, Match Valley Reports 100-Minute Daily Playtime

**Date:** 2026-01-15
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** Funding
**Relevance:** 8 | **Novelty:** 8 | **Actionability:** 7

## Summary

Istanbul-based TaleMonster Games closed a $30 million Series A on 15 January 2026, led by Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) and Arcadia Gaming Partners, with participation from Point72 Ventures and General Catalyst. The studio was founded by former Peak Games developers who built Toon Blast and Toy Blast (the titles that made Peak Games worth $1.8 billion to Zynga). Their first game, Match Valley, combines traditional match-3 puzzle mechanics with hero collection and tower defence elements, reporting an average daily playtime of 100 minutes — more than double the benchmark for top casual mobile titles. The round follows a $7 million seed 8 months prior, bringing total funding to $37 million. Funds will scale Match Valley globally and develop a second title.

## Key Facts

- Round: $30M Series A (closed January 15, 2026)
- Lead investors: Arcadia Gaming Partners and a16z (Andreessen Horowitz)
- Participating investors: Point72 Ventures, General Catalyst
- Total funding: $37M ($7M seed, raised approximately May 2025; Series A January 2026)
- Time from seed to Series A: approximately 8 months
- Studio: TaleMonster Games, Istanbul; founders are former Peak Games engineers and designers
- Founder heritage: Toon Blast and Toy Blast development (Peak Games, acquired by Zynga for $1.8B in 2020)
- CEO: İrem Sümer — civil engineer by training, ex-Pfizer, ex-Google, ex-Peak Games
- Title: Match Valley — puzzle matching layered with hero progression and tower defence mechanics (hybrid casual)
- Reported average daily playtime: 100 minutes (industry casual benchmark is 30-60 minutes; top titles approach 60-80)
- Valuation: approximately sixfold increase from seed implied by PocketGamer.biz reporting

## Strategic Significance

1. The Peak Games lineage carries specific investment credibility. Toon Blast and Toy Blast generated hundreds of millions of dollars in annual revenue for Zynga after the acquisition — they are proven at-scale retention loop machines. When their designers build a new studio and a16z leads the round, the investment thesis is team track record more than product. TaleMonster enters its growth phase with the same VC confidence signal that propelled Voodoo and Homa.

2. 100 minutes of average daily playtime for a mobile casual title is exceptional — if the figure is sustained at scale. Standard casual games target 30-40 minutes; top performers reach 60-80. 100 minutes suggests the hero and tower defence layer meaningfully extends sessions beyond the base puzzle loop. This is the hybrid casual design thesis (pioneered by companies like ScopelyGames and Hyperhuman) demonstrated in a Turkish studio context.

3. The Istanbul mobile gaming cluster now has three notable data points: TaleMonster ($37M total), Grand Games ($103M total across Series A + B), and the original Peak Games ($1.8B Zynga acquisition). The pattern is consistent enough to name as a geographic hub in any mobile gaming market landscape.
