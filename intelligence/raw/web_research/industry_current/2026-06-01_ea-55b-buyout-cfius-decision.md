# EA $55B Saudi-Led Buyout -- CFIUS Decision Imminent

**Date:** 2026-06-01
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** M&A / Regulation
**Relevance:** 9 | **Novelty:** 7 | **Actionability:** 8

## Summary

Electronic Arts' $55 billion take-private deal -- the largest leveraged buyout in gaming history -- is approaching its regulatory decision window. The consortium (Saudi Arabia's PIF 93.4%, Silver Lake, Affinity Partners/Jared Kushner) expects closure by June 30, 2026. CFIUS review is the final hurdle after HSR antitrust clearance was obtained and 99% shareholder approval passed in December 2025.

## Key Facts

- **Deal value:** $55B ($210/share). $36B equity + $20B debt financing.
- **PIF ownership post-close:** 93.4% of EA.
- **Shareholder vote:** 99% approved (December 22, 2025). 1.9M shares voted against.
- **Expected close:** June 30, 2026 (start of EA FY2027). Contractual outside date: September 28, 2026.
- **HSR antitrust:** Cleared.
- **CFIUS:** Under review. Final regulatory hurdle.
- **Reverse break fee:** $1B if deal terminates due to regulatory failure.
- **Debt load:** $20B in debt financing will burden EA post-close.

## Regulatory Opposition

- **40+ House Democrats** wrote to FTC chair Andrew Ferguson requesting comprehensive review (May 2026).
- **CWA (Communications Workers of America)** urged both FTC and CFIUS scrutiny. Concerns: market consolidation, potential layoffs, loss of creative control, US consumer data exposed to foreign sovereign entity.
- **CWA Canada** president Carmel Smyth wrote to Minister of Industry Melanie Joly (May 5, 2026) citing "serious concerns" about national security implications.
- **US Rep. Maxwell Frost** publicly protested the Saudi ownership of a major American media company.

## Strategic Significance

1. **Largest gaming M&A ever** -- eclipses Microsoft/Activision ($69B) in strategic significance due to foreign sovereign ownership and LBO structure.
2. **Saudi gaming consolidation accelerates.** PIF already owns Savvy Games Group (Moonton $6B acquisition in March 2026). EA would give Saudi Arabia controlling ownership of FIFA/EA Sports FC, Battlefield, Apex Legends, The Sims, and all EA mobile properties.
3. **Sets precedent for CFIUS treatment of gaming.** If approved, signals that gaming companies are not treated as critical infrastructure. If blocked or conditioned, establishes gaming as a national security concern.
4. **LBO structure creates execution risk.** $20B debt on a company with ~$7.5B annual revenue creates pressure for cost-cutting and monetisation maximisation. Likely impacts headcount and creative risk-taking.
5. **Creative control assurances.** EA says it will retain creative control, Wilson remains CEO, HQ stays in Redwood City. But 93.4% ownership provides ultimate governance authority.

## NBI Advisory Relevance

- **M&A advisory:** Largest-ever gaming deal as reference case for valuation multiples, LBO structure, and CFIUS process timelines.
- **Studio advisory:** Studios owned by or partnering with EA should model scenarios for both approval and rejection. Cost-cutting pressure likely post-close.
- **Regulatory intelligence:** CFIUS outcome will define regulatory treatment of all future gaming M&A involving foreign sovereign capital.
- **Market impact:** If closed June 30, EA delists from NASDAQ. No more public financial reporting. Reduces market transparency.
