# Grand Games Raises $70M Series B for Hybridcasual Expansion

## Key Content

Istanbul-based Grand Games closed a $70M Series B led by Balderton Capital, with participation from Bek Ventures, Laton Ventures, and angel investor Mert Gür. Total funding: $103M. Valuation increased nearly sixfold since their previous round just over a year prior.

Founded in 2024, the studio has ~75 employees organised into five autonomous internal game studios. Their puzzle games Magic Sort and Block Out recently held the top two spots simultaneously on the US iOS download charts, with over 50M total downloads. CEO Bekir Batuhan Celebi emphasised a talent-centric operating model: "the people building the games are at the centre of everything we do."

Funds will go to accelerating marketing, scaling existing titles, and supporting a pipeline of new releases.

## Decisions / Insights

Grand Games is a counter-narrative to the "funding winter" thesis. The hybridcasual segment continues attracting capital at aggressive valuations when studios can demonstrate chart performance. Five autonomous studios within a 75-person company is a structural model worth noting -- it distributes creative risk while centralising UA and analytics. The nearly 6x valuation jump in ~12 months signals that proven mobile hit-makers can still command premium terms.

## Context

Sits alongside the Astrocade raise (captured in cycle 1) as evidence that mobile gaming funding has bifurcated: proven performers with retention data raise at premium; unproven studios face the $500K-$3M dead zone identified in earlier research. Grand Games' Istanbul base also reflects the continuing shift of mobile development talent to Turkey, following Dream Games ($2.75B valuation) and Peak Games (acquired by Zynga for $1.8B).
