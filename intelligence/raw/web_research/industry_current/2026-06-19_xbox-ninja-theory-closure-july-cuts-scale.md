# Ninja Theory Confirmed Closed; Xbox July Layoffs Expected in Thousands as Division Revenue Falls $500M

**Date:** 2026-06-19
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** Industry Structure
**Relevance:** 9 | **Novelty:** 7 | **Actionability:** 8

## Summary

Microsoft confirmed the closure of Ninja Theory on June 16, 2026 -- nine days after the studio appeared at the Xbox Games Showcase to reveal Senua (Hellblade 3). Double Fine and Compulsion Games remain in active spin-off negotiations with no buyer confirmed as of June 19. Xbox's broader July layoff wave is expected to number in the thousands, following a division revenue decline of nearly $500M over five years and a fiscal 2026 operating margin of approximately 3%. CEO Asha Sharma's 100-day "reset" is the framing for the restructuring.

## Key Facts

- Ninja Theory (Cambridge, UK) confirmed closed June 16, 2026; team seeking outside buyer but closure is confirmed
- Double Fine (San Francisco) and Compulsion Games (Montreal) in active negotiations for management buyouts or third-party acquisition; no buyer named as of June 19
- "Several other studios" also reportedly in flux beyond the named three (Bloomberg / Jason Schreier)
- Xbox division revenue down approximately $500M over five years; FY26 operating margin approximately 3%
- July 2026 layoffs expected to run into the thousands across studios, hardware, marketing, and corporate roles
- Xbox fiscal year closes June 30; layoff announcements expected immediately after
- CEO Asha Sharma replaced Phil Spencer in February 2026 and is leading the restructuring
- Even successful spin-offs expected to involve significant headcount reductions at the freed studios

## Strategic Significance

1. The Ninja Theory closure pattern -- announcing a game at showcase then shuttering the studio nine days later -- signals that Microsoft's reset is executing on a compressed timeline with no regard for optics or morale, which will accelerate developer distrust of Xbox as a publisher and partner at precisely the moment Microsoft needs studios to want to work with it.
2. The Double Fine and Compulsion spin-off model, if it closes, validates an emerging template for studio survival: MBO or third-party acquisition as an alternative to closure, similar to Toys For Bob's exit from Activision. This is an M&A opportunity signal for mid-tier publishers and PE-backed acquirers watching the market.
3. Xbox gaming at approximately 3% operating margin on a $500M revenue decline creates continued pressure to exit from first-party publishing commitments, which reshapes the platform deal landscape for independent studios over the next 12-18 months.
