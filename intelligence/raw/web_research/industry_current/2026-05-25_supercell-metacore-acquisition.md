# Supercell Fully Acquires Metacore (Merge Mansion) Amid 160-Job Restructuring

## Key Content (max 200 words)

On 5 May 2026, Supercell announced full acquisition of Metacore, the Helsinki-based studio behind Merge Mansion (2020). Supercell was already the largest shareholder after investing EUR 180M across multiple rounds starting with $5.9M in 2018. Deal terms undisclosed.

Simultaneously, Metacore initiated change negotiations in Finland that could cut up to 160 of its 280+ roles (57% of workforce). Operations in Germany and Sweden also under review.

The rationale: Merge Mansion's growth has plateaued since launch and Metacore's attempts to launch follow-up titles globally have not succeeded. Supercell plans to run Merge Mansion as a live game alongside Hay Day.

The Tencent angle: as Supercell's majority owner, this effectively extends Tencent's footprint in casual gaming further.

## Decisions / Insights

- Mobile studios built on a single hit face existential risk when growth plateaus and second games fail to land
- Supercell's playbook: invest early, acquire fully when the parent company stumbles, fold the IP into live ops
- EUR 180M invested over multiple rounds before full acquisition suggests the total cost of ownership for a single mobile IP

## Context

Metacore is the latest mid-tier mobile studio to be absorbed by a larger parent after failing to diversify beyond a single title. This pattern (single-hit studios plateauing then being acquired or shut) has repeated across mobile gaming since 2023.
