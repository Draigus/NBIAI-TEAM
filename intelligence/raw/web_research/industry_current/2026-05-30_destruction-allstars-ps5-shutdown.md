# Destruction AllStars Delisted and Shut Down — Sony's Live-Service Reckoning Continues

**Date:** 2026-05-26
**Source:** pushsquare.com, kotaku.com, playstationlifestyle.net, thesixthaxis.com
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** Platform / Live Service
**Relevance:** 7 | **Novelty:** 6 | **Actionability:** 6

## What happened

Sony delisted Destruction AllStars from the PlayStation Store and shut down multiplayer servers effective May 26, 2026 at 14:00 UTC, citing "ongoing technical issues." The game is no longer available for purchase. Single-player arcade mode remains accessible until November 25, 2026, when all remaining server support will be terminated.

## Context

Destruction AllStars launched as a PS5 exclusive in February 2021 — one of the earliest first-party live-service titles for the console generation. It was added to PlayStation Plus at launch to build a player base, but never achieved meaningful retention. The game struggled with a thin content pipeline, repetitive gameplay, and a small concurrent player base throughout its life.

## Why it matters

1. **Sony's GaaS retreat is now a pattern, not an incident.** The timeline: Concord failed at launch (August 2024, shut down in weeks). Firewalk Studios closed (October 2024). Bluepoint's live-service God of War cancelled (January 2025). Bluepoint itself shuttered (February 2026). Now Destruction AllStars is quietly killed. Sony has systematically unwound its PS5-era live-service strategy.

2. **Quiet shutdown signals industry shame.** The lack of advance notice — the game was simply delisted without warning — suggests Sony wanted minimal press coverage. This contrasts with planned shutdowns that give communities time to organise farewell events. Studios and publishers taking this approach risk community backlash and eroding trust.

3. **Digital ownership debate.** Another live-service game where paying customers lose access to content they purchased. This feeds directly into the regulatory push (EU cancellation button, digital ownership legislation) already being tracked. Every shutdown like this gives regulators more ammunition.

4. **Five-year lifecycle.** Despite being a first-party title with PS Plus distribution, the game survived only five years — and was functionally dead long before the formal shutdown. This is a data point for live-service viability modelling: even with platform-holder backing and free distribution, a weak core loop means death.
