# Playstack Acquired by IMC (Fandom/GameSpot Parent) for $151M

**Date:** 2026-05-28 (announced 2026-05-21, closing expected June 10)
**Source:** gamedeveloper.com, pcgamer.com, kotaku.com, shacknews.com
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** M&A
**Relevance:** 8 | **Novelty:** 8 | **Actionability:** 7

## What happened

TruFin agreed to sell its 84.5% stake in UK publisher Playstack to VantageCo (a subsidiary of Integrated Media Company / IMC, itself owned by private equity firm TPG) for approximately GBP 112.4M ($151M). The deal values Playstack at roughly GBP 125M ($169M) in total. Subject to shareholder vote; expected to close June 10, 2026.

## Who is involved

- **Playstack** — UK-based publisher behind Balatro (2024 breakout hit, 10M+ copies), Abiotic Factor, VOID/BREAKER. Combined catalogue has grossed over $100M on Steam and generated 20M+ downloads in the last fiscal year.
- **IMC / TPG** — Private equity-backed media conglomerate that owns Fandom, GameSpot, Screen Junkies, and Fanatical. Acquiring through subsidiary VantageCo.
- **TruFin** — London-listed fintech holding company selling its majority stake. Will receive GBP 112.4M in net cash proceeds.

## Why it matters

1. **PE consolidation of gaming value chain.** TPG/IMC already owns gaming media (Fandom, GameSpot) and a game key retailer (Fanatical). Adding a publisher with a proven hit catalogue creates a vertically integrated gaming media + publishing entity. This is the content-meets-distribution thesis playing out.

2. **Balatro's commercial success drives the valuation.** The $169M total valuation is anchored almost entirely on one breakout title. This is a pattern: single-hit publishers commanding premium multiples when the IP has proven legs (Balatro still sells well 2+ years post-launch).

3. **UK indie publisher exit.** Playstack was one of the few mid-tier UK publishers operating independently. Its acquisition by a PE-backed media group reduces the number of independent publishing options for UK indie developers — relevant context for NBI's client advisory on publishing strategy.

4. **Revenue-based valuation benchmark.** $100M+ Steam gross revenue backing a $169M acquisition price gives a rough 1.5-1.7x revenue multiple for a publisher with a proven hit but no guaranteed pipeline. Useful comp for NBI's game investment analysis work.

Direct relevance to game investment analysis (/gi skill) and client advisory. The deal provides a current comp for publisher valuations.
