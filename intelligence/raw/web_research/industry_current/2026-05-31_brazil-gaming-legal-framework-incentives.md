# Brazil Gaming Legal Framework (Law 14,852) -- Tax Incentives and Government-Backed Accelerator

**Date:** 2026-05-31 (law enacted May 2024; accelerator and implementation ongoing through 2026)
**Source:** gamedeveloper.com, unctad.org, kokkugames.com, mayerbrown.com, pocketgamer.biz

## What happened

Brazil's Legal Framework for the Electronic Games Industry (Law No. 14,852), enacted May 2024, is now in active implementation. Game Developer ran a detailed feature in late May 2026 examining the framework's impact. Simultaneously, Abragames (Brazil's games trade body) launched the Brazil Games Accelerator -- a 9-month programme to internationalise Brazilian studios with mentoring, consulting, workshops, and investor access.

## Key provisions

- **70% tax reduction** on remittances abroad related to licensing or exploitation of games, provided funds are reinvested in independent Brazilian game projects
- **Income tax rebates** of up to 16% of annual income tax reinvested in cultural endeavours (video games now officially classified as cultural products since 2024)
- **Simplified SDK imports** and reduced taxation on development equipment and tools
- **New CNAE classification** -- games companies are now classified separately from generic software, unlocking sector-specific tax treatment
- **Proposed public fund mechanism** (not yet enacted) to pool tax payments and institutional investment for game development funding

## Brazil Games Accelerator

- Run by Abragames (trade body)
- 9-month programme targeting international market readiness
- Services: international mentoring, expert consulting, workshops, investor/publisher access
- 30+ Brazilian studios brought to GDC 2026 to showcase playable titles
- Estimated 13,225 developers operating in Brazil as of latest survey (3.2% YoY studio growth)

## Why it matters

1. **Government-backed gaming ecosystem model.** Brazil is implementing the most comprehensive government support framework for games in Latin America. The combination of tax incentives, cultural classification, and trade body accelerator is a template that other emerging markets will study. India's PROG rules (captured cycle 4) take a different regulatory approach -- Brazil's is incentive-led rather than compliance-led.

2. **Work-for-hire cost advantage.** The tax reductions on development tools and the separate CNAE classification make Brazilian studios more cost-competitive for outsourcing and co-development. Studios like Kokku (established work-for-hire) will benefit directly. For any NBI client considering outsourcing arrangements, Brazil has become meaningfully more attractive.

3. **Reinvestment requirement is smart policy.** The 70% remittance tax reduction is conditional on reinvesting in Brazilian indie projects. This creates a flywheel: international revenue comes in, tax savings are mandated back into local development, building the next generation of Brazilian studios. It is the most targeted version of cultural reinvestment policy in gaming globally.

4. **Scale is meaningful.** 13,000+ developers and a formal government framework put Brazil alongside South Korea, Finland, and Canada as countries with dedicated gaming industry support. The LATAM market ($8.1B, 290M players) has lacked a production hub -- Brazil is positioning to fill that gap.
