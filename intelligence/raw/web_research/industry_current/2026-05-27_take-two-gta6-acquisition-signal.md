# Take-Two CEO Signals Studio Acquisitions Funded by GTA 6 Revenue -- $8B+ Projected (May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

During Take-Two's latest earnings call (May 2026), CEO Strauss Zelnick outlined three uses for the capital windfall expected from Grand Theft Auto 6 (confirmed launching 19 November 2026): organic growth (new games across labels), inorganic growth (acquiring studios), and returning capital to shareholders.

Take-Two projects net bookings exceeding $8B for the next fiscal year, driven primarily by GTA 6. Zelnick stated acquisitions remain a key investment channel, responding directly to a Baird analyst question about whether GTA 6 revenue would primarily return to shareholders.

This extends the earlier Take-Two signal (iIcon conference) where Zelnick discussed the three-pillar capital allocation. The earnings call language was more explicit: acquisitions are not hypothetical but planned. The company has a track record -- Zynga ($12.7B, 2022), Socialpoint, Playdots -- and the mobile segment is now generating $3.3B in bookings (+15% YoY) with Match Factory growing 33%.

## Decisions / Insights

- Take-Two with $8B+ in GTA 6 revenue becomes the most active potential acquirer in gaming alongside Saudi-backed entities
- Mobile and mid-core studios are the likely targets given Zynga integration success and mobile bookings growth trajectory
- The explicit "inorganic growth" language in an earnings call is a signal to the M&A market -- intermediaries and studios seeking exits should note
