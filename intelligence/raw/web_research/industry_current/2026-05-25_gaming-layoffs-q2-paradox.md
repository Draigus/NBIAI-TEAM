# Gaming Industry Layoffs Continue Into Q2 2026: The Profit-Layoff Paradox Deepens

## Key Content (max 200 words)

May 2026 layoffs so far: Metacore (up to 160 roles), MercurySteam (undisclosed count, 12 May), 31st Union/2K (8 May), Iron Galaxy Studios (1 May), Night Street Games (13 May).

Cumulative since 2022: over 44,000 video game development jobs cut globally. 28% of developers worldwide lost their jobs over the past two years; 33% in the US specifically.

The paradox: the global video game industry generated $195.6B in content revenue in 2025, its highest annual total on record. Yet layoffs accelerate. Example: EA cut staff at all four Battlefield studios in March 2026, less than five months after Battlefield 6 sold seven million copies in three days.

The industry is projected at $205B revenue in 2026 (up 4.6%). Mobile leads at ~$107B (52% share). Asia-Pacific leads regionally at $87.6B (46% share). Global player base: 3.6 billion.

## Decisions / Insights

- Record revenue coexisting with mass layoffs indicates structural workforce reshaping, not cyclical downturn
- Studios are optimising for profit margin, not headcount -- fewer people, same or higher revenue
- Mid-tier studios (100-300 people) face the highest restructuring risk
- The "post-project layoff" pattern (MercurySteam after Blades of Fire underperformed) remains the dominant trigger

## Context

This is year four of continuous industry contraction in headcount despite revenue growth. The pattern is now structural: studios hire for production peaks, shed after launch, and increasingly use AI/outsourcing to avoid rehiring. This creates a permanent reduction in full-time roles industry-wide.
