# Playstack/VantageCo (IMC) Acquisition Closes -- UK Indie Publisher Now Under Fandom/GameSpot Parent

**Date:** 2026-06-10
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** M&A
**Relevance:** 7 | **Novelty:** 7 | **Actionability:** 7

## Summary

The acquisition of Balatro publisher Playstack by VantageCo Limited -- a subsidiary of Integrated Media Company (IMC), the New York group that owns GameSpot, Fandom, Fanatical, and Screen Junkies -- completed on or around 10 June 2026, following TruFin shareholder approval on 8 June. The enterprise value was £125 million ($169M), with TruFin receiving approximately £112.4 million in net cash. The deal places a profitable, IP-owning UK games publisher inside a US media and games-adjacent conglomerate.

## Key Facts

- TruFin shareholder vote held 8 June 2026: 68,270,955 votes in favour (72.56% of issued share capital), 83,813 against
- Enterprise value: £125 million / approximately $169 million (TruFin proceeds: approximately £112.4 million)
- TruFin held an 84.5% stake in Playstack; VantageCo acquired the majority stake
- IMC already owns GameSpot, Fandom, Fanatical (games retail), Screen Junkies, and related media assets
- Playstack's portfolio includes Balatro (solitaire roguelike, significant viral success) plus a wider indie publishing slate
- Playstack CEO Harvey Elliott stated "business as usual" under new ownership
- IMC's strategic rationale: combining game publishing with its media and retail footprint (Fanatical is a key digital storefront) creates a vertically adjacent games media and publishing stack

## Strategic Significance

1. IMC is building a vertically integrated games stack: publisher (Playstack) + storefront (Fanatical) + editorial/review (GameSpot) + fandom/community (Fandom) -- a model that gives it influence at multiple points in the discovery and purchase funnel simultaneously.
2. This is a rare example of a US media conglomerate acquiring a profitable UK indie publisher rather than a studio; it signals that IP-owning publishers with proven hits are valued as media assets, not just development studios.
3. Balatro's outsized cultural impact (viral roguelike crossover) almost certainly inflated the acquisition premium beyond Playstack's back-catalogue alone.

## NBI Advisory Relevance

- Directly relevant to any NBI client considering fundraising or exit options: the Playstack deal shows that UK indie publishers with proven IP can attract US media money at meaningful multiples
- For the Playstack model specifically: a single break-out hit (Balatro) changed the exit trajectory of the entire business -- relevant when advising studios on portfolio strategy and when to pursue external investment
- IMC's vertical model (publisher + storefront + editorial) is worth tracking as a template; if it proves effective, other media groups may replicate it
