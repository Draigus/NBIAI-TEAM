# Ubisoft Closes Winnipeg and Belgrade Studios -- Sixth Layoff Wave of 2026 Claims 380 Roles

**Date:** 2026-06-10
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** Industry Structure
**Relevance:** 9 | **Novelty:** 8 | **Actionability:** 8

## Summary

Ubisoft announced the closure of its Winnipeg (Canada) and Belgrade (Serbia) studios on 10 June 2026, with up to 380 further roles at risk across Barcelona and its global publishing operation. The closures mark the sixth distinct redundancy wave Ubisoft has executed in 2026 alone, with an estimated 680 jobs eliminated in the year to date. The Winnipeg studio was the custodian of Ubisoft's proprietary Anvil and Snowdrop engine families.

## Key Facts

- Ubisoft Winnipeg and Ubisoft Belgrade confirmed closed, 10 June 2026
- Up to 380 roles at risk pending consultation; 51 further cuts proposed at Ubisoft Barcelona
- Winnipeg was the primary team behind the Anvil (Rainbow Six Siege, Far Cry) and Snowdrop (Immortals Fenyx Rising, The Division) engine families
- Belgrade (founded 2016) supported The Crew 2, Ghost Recon Breakpoint/Wildlands, Skull & Bones
- Ubisoft Barcelona being restructured around Rainbow Six Siege only; 12% of Siege's existing team being reassigned
- Total 2026 Ubisoft job losses estimated at 680 before mid-year
- Closures follow Ubisoft's restructuring into five creative houses managing key franchises
- Context: Tencent invested approximately £1.25 billion into Ubisoft earlier in 2026; company introduced a mandatory five-day return-to-office policy

## Strategic Significance

1. The closure of the Winnipeg engine team is structurally significant: Ubisoft is concentrating proprietary engine expertise rather than distributing it, which signals a tighter centralisation of its technical infrastructure around fewer, more powerful franchises.
2. The sixth redundancy wave in six months indicates Ubisoft has not reached the end of its restructuring; the Tencent investment has not stabilised headcount, suggesting the capital injection is being used for strategic repositioning rather than operational continuity.
3. Barcelona being reduced to a single-franchise studio (Siege) represents a broader culling of Ubisoft's mid-tier production capacity -- the studio was previously a multi-project site.

## NBI Advisory Relevance

- Relevant when advising studios on workforce planning and multi-site production risk: Ubisoft's pattern shows that engine/tooling teams at satellite offices are disproportionately vulnerable during publisher restructuring
- For clients evaluating Ubisoft as a publisher or co-development partner: the ongoing instability is a genuine counterparty risk flag
- Useful context for any client benchmarking redundancy consultation processes -- Ubisoft's rolling consultation model (pending status rather than immediate cuts) is a pattern worth understanding for UK studios subject to collective redundancy rules
