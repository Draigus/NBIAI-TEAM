## Summary

Grand Theft Auto VI has been delayed from May 26 to November 19, 2026 -- a nearly 6-month slip. Key developments:

- **Stock impact:** Take-Two stock fell ~$30/share in after-hours on the delay announcement; subsequently rebounded partially on pre-order rumours (adding ~$2B market cap); then sold off again after the May 21 fiscal report, opening May 27 at 11.5% below its May 14 peak
- **CEO confirmation:** Strauss Zelnick has publicly stated the November 19 date is locked, with no further delays
- **Pre-orders:** Expected to open months before launch; Trailer 3 anticipated by late June (possibly at Summer Game Fest)
- **Industry ripple effect:** Multiple publishers have reportedly adjusted their own release schedules to avoid the November window

## NBI Relevance

The biggest single-title event in gaming history will reshape Q4 2026 for every publisher. Studios and publishers NBI advises need to plan releases around the November window. The stock volatility pattern (delay drop, rumour spike, earnings sell-off) is a useful case study for investment advisory. The delay also validates NBI's general advice against rigid release date commitments. The industry-wide schedule reshuffling creates both risks and opportunities for smaller studios.
