# Google Settles with Epic Games -- Play Store Commissions Drop to 20%

## Key Content

Google and Epic Games resolved their years-long legal dispute. Google Play's default commission drops from 30% to 20% on in-app purchases, with an additional 5% only if developers use Google's billing system. Recurring subscription fees drop from 15% to 10%. Under the new Apps Experience Program, developers pay 20% on existing installs and just 15% on new installs.

Rollout timeline: EEA, UK, and US by 30 June 2026; Australia by 30 September 2026; Korea and Japan by 31 December 2026; global by 30 September 2027.

Google also launched the Registered App Stores programme, reducing security warnings that previously deterred users from installing third-party stores. Fortnite returns to Google Play globally. Tim Sweeney stated the settlement means "Google is opening up Android all the way with robust support for competing stores, competing payments, and a better deal for all developers."

For a developer generating $1M in annual revenue, the shift from 30% to 20% means an extra $100,000 staying in-house -- a 33% reduction in platform costs.

## Decisions / Insights

The 10-point commission drop is the single largest structural shift in mobile platform economics since the App Store launched. Every mobile gaming client NBI advises should model the margin impact. Studios with own billing infrastructure benefit most (20% vs 25% with Google billing). The phased rollout creates a regional arbitrage window through 2027.

## Context

The settlement follows Epic's 2020 lawsuit after Fortnite was removed from both App Store and Play Store. Apple's parallel fight continues with Fortnite's global App Store return (May 2026) framed as the "final battle." The combined Google and Apple commission reductions represent a fundamental repricing of mobile distribution.
