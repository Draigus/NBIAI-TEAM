# CD Projekt Red Announces Witcher 3 "Songs of the Past" Expansion -- 12 Years After Launch (28 May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

CD Projekt Red officially announced The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt - Songs of the Past, a new expansion DLC co-developed with external studio Fool's Theory, planned for release in 2027 on PC, PS5, and Xbox Series X/S. The announcement was forced ahead of schedule after the expansion was accidentally leaked on the RED Launcher platform on 27 May, ahead of a planned Blood and Wine anniversary stream on 28 May.

CD Projekt had filed a trademark for "Songs of the Past" (EUIPO trademark number 019371013) covering game software, merchandise, and online services. The DLC will reportedly bridge the narrative gap between The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt and the upcoming Witcher 4 (Project Polaris), currently in full production at CDPR. Further details are expected in late summer 2026.

This represents the third major expansion for The Witcher 3, following Hearts of Stone (2015) and Blood and Wine (2016), arriving 12 years after the base game's original May 2015 launch.

## Decisions / Insights

- CDPR is demonstrating a "legacy IP monetisation" model: rather than building a new live-service game, they are returning to a completed single-player title with proven commercial demand. The Witcher 3 has sold 50M+ copies lifetime
- Co-development with Fool's Theory (a Polish studio founded by ex-CDPR developers working on Witcher Remake) allows CDPR to keep its main team focused on Witcher 4 while extracting additional revenue from the existing installed base
- The "bridge DLC" approach serves dual purpose: generates revenue from existing IP and builds narrative momentum for Witcher 4's launch
- This model is replicable for any studio with a large installed-base single-player title: new DLC years after launch can be commercially viable if the franchise has sufficient scale

## Context

CDPR's recovery since Cyberpunk 2077's troubled 2020 launch has been remarkable. Cyberpunk's Phantom Liberty DLC (2023) was critically acclaimed, and the company's stock has recovered. The Witcher 4 is now CDPR's primary focus, with a second Cyberpunk game also in early development at a new Boston studio.

## Applicability

The legacy DLC model is worth flagging for NBI clients with large installed-base single-player games: returning to a completed title with new expansion content (especially via co-development to manage cost) can generate meaningful revenue without the live-service infrastructure investment. The bridge-to-sequel narrative strategy is particularly clever as a marketing tool for an upcoming franchise entry.
