# CI Games Terminates Epic Games Store Exclusivity Deal for Lords of the Fallen 2 (May 2026)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

CI Games announced that its publishing agreement with Epic Games granting exclusive worldwide PC distribution rights for Lords of the Fallen II on the Epic Games Store has been terminated. The Separation Agreement was executed on 14 April 2026 but disclosed publicly on 18 May 2026; CI Games stated delayed disclosure was necessary to avoid prejudicing the company's "legitimate interests."

The original exclusivity deal was signed 14 June 2024. Under the termination, Lords of the Fallen II will now launch on Steam alongside other PC storefronts when it releases in 2026. The agreement explicitly preserves CI Games' use of Unreal Engine 5 and Epic Online Services infrastructure.

This follows the first Lords of the Fallen (2023 reboot), which sold 3M+ copies across platforms. The sequel's multi-platform PC release significantly changes its commercial prospects given Steam's dominant PC market share.

## Decisions / Insights

- The EGS exclusivity model appears to be weakening: studios are choosing to forgo guaranteed minimum guarantees in favour of Steam's larger audience. This follows broader EGS strategy shifts as Epic focuses on the Google settlement and mobile
- The delayed disclosure (April termination, May announcement) suggests complex negotiation -- likely involving return of exclusivity fees or offset arrangements
- CI Games' explicit preservation of UE5 and Epic Online Services access demonstrates that engine licensing and store exclusivity are now fully decoupled -- studios can reject EGS exclusivity without losing engine access
- For NBI clients evaluating platform exclusivity deals: the calculus has shifted. Steam's ~75% PC market share makes exclusivity deals increasingly difficult to justify unless the guaranteed minimum exceeds projected Steam revenue at 70% rev share

## Context

Epic launched its exclusivity programme in 2018-2019, paying guaranteed minimums to secure timed PC exclusivity. High-profile deals included Borderlands 3, Metro Exodus, and Control. However, player backlash, lower-than-expected EGS sales, and Steam's persistent market dominance have made the programme less attractive. The Google/Epic settlement (commission drop to 20%) may signal Epic's strategic pivot toward mobile platform economics rather than PC storefront competition.

## Applicability

Direct advisory relevance for any NBI client negotiating PC distribution deals. The key decision framework: EGS exclusivity is only advisable when the guaranteed minimum exceeds the estimated incremental revenue from simultaneous Steam launch (typically 2-4x the EGS-only projection). CI Games' decision to walk away from the deal -- even at the cost of delayed disclosure and likely financial settlement -- suggests their own analysis showed Steam access was worth more than the EGS guarantee.
