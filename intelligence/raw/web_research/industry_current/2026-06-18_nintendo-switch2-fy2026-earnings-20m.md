# Nintendo Switch 2 Sells 19.86M Units in FY2026 — Net Sales Up 99%, Shares -40% on Price Hike Announcement

**Date:** 2026-06-18
**Domain:** industry_current
**Category:** Market Data
**Relevance:** 8 | **Novelty:** 8 | **Actionability:** 7

## Summary

Nintendo's FY2026 annual report (year ended 31 March 2026) records Switch 2 unit sales of 19.86 million — outpacing the original Switch's equivalent first-year performance. Net sales grew 99% year-on-year, with Mario Kart World alone selling 14.7 million units (74% attach rate). Despite the hardware performance, Nintendo shares fell approximately 40% from peak after the company announced a price hike, attributed by Nintendo's president to tariff effects from the war on Iran and an AI-driven DRAM memory crisis. FY27 guidance forecasts 16.5 million additional Switch 2 units, implying expected year-on-year decline. The original Switch reached 155.9 million lifetime units.

## Key Facts

- FY2026 Switch 2 sales: 19.86 million units (fiscal year ended 31 March 2026; console launched June 2025)
- Outpaced original Switch over equivalent launch period (original Switch sold approximately 14.86M in its first full fiscal year)
- FY2026 Switch 2 software: 48.7 million units sold across the platform
- Mario Kart World: 14.7 million units — 74% attach rate to hardware; flagship launch title
- Net sales: up 99% year-on-year (FY2026 vs FY2025)
- Revenue geography: more than two-thirds of sales revenue from outside Japan
- Share price: approximately -40% decline from peak following price hike announcement (reported May 2026)
- Nintendo president cited war on Iran (component supply disruption) and AI-driven DRAM demand as price hike causes
- FY27 forecast: 16.5 million Switch 2 units (year-on-year decline expected as launch cohort exhausts)
- Original Switch: 3.8M units sold in FY2026; 155.9 million lifetime total

## Strategic Significance

1. A console at 19.86M units in its first fiscal year with 99% revenue growth confirms Switch 2 as a tier-1 platform. Combined with the original Switch's 155.9M installed base still generating 3.8M annual unit sales, Nintendo's total addressable hardware audience now approaches 175M+ devices. Any multi-platform strategy that excludes Nintendo is leaving a substantial segment on the table.

2. Mario Kart World's 74% attach rate is exceptional even by first-party Nintendo standards. It confirms that first-party IP drives Nintendo hardware adoption at a structural level — clients building games for Switch 2 need to understand that they are competing for attention against Nintendo's own output, which has a proven attach rate ceiling to push against.

3. The -40% share decline despite excellent operating results is a useful case study in how investor sentiment in gaming can decouple from operational performance. The price hike narrative (external causes: tariffs, DRAM) was enough to override strong hardware data. This is relevant context when discussing gaming company valuations with clients considering investment or acquisition.
