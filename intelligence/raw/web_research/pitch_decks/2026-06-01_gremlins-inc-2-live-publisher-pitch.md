# Gremlins Inc. 2 Publisher Pitch -- Live Deal Negotiation Case Study

**Domain:** pitch_decks

## What happened

Charlie Oscar (led by Sergei Klimov) documented their live, in-progress publisher pitch for Gremlins Inc. 2 -- a PC strategy sequel to a game with 500K Steam users and 5,100+ positive reviews. This is rare: a studio showing its hand during negotiations, not retrospectively.

## The pitch deck

**35 slides** covering:
- Statistics on original game performance (500K users, 5,100+ reviews)
- Gremlins Inc. 2 concept and feature details
- Comparative G1/G2 feature table (concrete "what's new" evidence)
- Development roadmap
- Separate "studio/term sheet" deck alongside the product deck (smart -- separates creative pitch from business negotiation)

## Budget breakdown (actual numbers)

- Early Access launch: EUR 400,000
- Year one of Early Access: EUR 400,000
- First 6 months live ops: EUR 200,000
- **Total ask:** ~EUR 1,000,000
- **Projected return:** EUR 3M+ from the EUR 1M investment

When publishers suggested "bigger everything" at EUR 2-5M budgets, the studio pushed back: "We don't play any 'little games'" about inflated budgets. They kept the ask tight and defensible.

## Pitch campaign results

- ~15 publishers approached
- 4 active discussions progressing at different speeds
- 1 possible studio acquisition discussion
- 3 product-focused deals
- EU funding also being considered

## Three deal structures encountered

1. "You keep IP, we build success together" (developer-friendly)
2. "We only work with studios that hand over IP" (publisher-controlled)
3. "Right to buy IP written into contract" (hybrid/escalation clause)

## Regional audience data used in pitch

Detailed Steam audience breakdown: 11% Japan, 8% Russia, 30% China. This geographic data gave publishers a concrete market story beyond "strategy game fans."

## Key learnings documented

**What worked:**
- Community validation (Steam announcement got 1,100+ likes -- social proof before any publisher commitment)
- Prior developer references for publisher vetting (they researched publishers as hard as publishers research them)
- Separate studio deck + product deck
- Tight, defensible budget with clear ROI projection

**What failed:**
- Publishers too busy with existing title priorities (2022-2023 backlog)
- Insufficient contact maintenance -- needed touchpoints every 6 months, not just at pitch time
- Cold emails without specific value proposition got ignored

## Critical insight

"Smart publishers look beyond your product to the audience it will add to their portfolio." This reframes the pitch from "here's our game" to "here's the audience segment we bring you."

## Why this matters for NBI

This is the only publicly documented in-progress publisher negotiation with real budget numbers, pitch structure, and deal terms encountered. Most pitch advice is retrospective and sanitised. This captures the messy reality: multiple conversations, different deal structures, publisher pushback on budget. The EUR 1M ask with EUR 3M projected return is a concrete example of the ROI framing publishers respond to. The "audience portfolio" insight is directly usable in NBI client pitch coaching. The separate studio/term sheet deck approach is a tactical best practice worth recommending.
