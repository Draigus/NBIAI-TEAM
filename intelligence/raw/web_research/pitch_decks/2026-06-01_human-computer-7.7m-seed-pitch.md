## What happened

Human Computer, founded by Alex Schleifer (former Airbnb Chief Design Officer) and Eliot Garcia Weisberg, raised $7.7M total for PC/console game development in March 2025. $5.7M seed round led by Makers Fund, with a16z and former Airbnb COO Belinda Johnson also investing. Tyler Matheson (Makers Fund) joined the board. Sam Rosenthal (The Game Band founder) as first advisor.

## The 19-slide pitch deck

Described as "designed to be a conversation starter -- visual, concise, and with gameplay and world-building front and center." Key sections covered:

1. Shared universe concept (building an interconnected world, not isolated games)
2. Viable model for new IP creation leveraging technological shifts
3. Specific audience segment targeting (audience hungry for innovation)
4. Modern production methods -- smaller teams, modern tools, faster iteration
5. Capital allocation breakdown
6. Production principles
7. Competitive positioning
8. Anti-addiction design philosophy -- games with clear endpoints, no dark patterns
9. Community-first development strategy (Discord, etc.)
10. Tangible demonstrations -- game demo, trailer, production concepts

Not a template slide deck. The pitch was built around the studio's philosophy and differentiation, not standard categories.

## What investors responded to

- "Having stuff to show" was critical. Quote: "Seeing momentum helps people get a sense of your vision."
- The debut game concept "particularly excited" investors (title unannounced, story-driven, Q4 2025 planned launch)
- Studio's anti-addiction positioning: "meaningful engagement rather than maximizing hours played"
- Founders' pedigree (senior Airbnb leadership = operational credibility)

## Why this matters for NBI

This is a rare documented case of a PC/console indie studio publicly disclosing both the raise amount AND the pitch deck structure with investor rationale. Most studios that raise in this range stay silent. The "19 slides, conversation starter" framing is directly actionable for client pitch coaching. The founder pedigree angle (tech executive pivoting to games) is a pattern NBI sees with advisory clients. The anti-addiction/ethical positioning is a strategic differentiator that worked with top-tier VCs (a16z, Makers Fund) -- worth noting for clients with similar values-based pitches.
