# Little Polygon: 50+ Publisher Pitches, Zero Term Sheets (2024 Post-Mortem)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Little Polygon published a raw post-mortem of pitching their game Nightshift Galaxy (mecha combat + arcade flight, Unreal Engine 4, PC/console) across 2024. Key data: submitted to 50+ publishers, gave the pitch ~24 times at DICE and GDC, 15 publishers never responded, 35 responded with positive feedback on the game but uniformly cited poor financial outlooks. Original ask: $3M for a 6-person team over 2.5 years; revised to ~$2M after market feedback. The 30-slide deck covered: elevator pitch, team credentials, unique features, playtime, engine, target market, comps with commercial performance, release date, platforms, funding ask. Development of the deck took 2+ months with industry feedback. Total out-of-pocket: ~$10,000 (demo, legal, conferences, travel). Outcome: no publishing deals materialised. Publishers reported that places previously funding $5M deals now capped at $500K-$1.5M. The core finding: "Nobody is investing in mid-range indie games" -- only very small or very large projects receive backing in the current climate.

## Decisions / Insights

- The "funding gap" in indie games is real and documented: $500K-$3M range is a dead zone in the current market. Studios asking for $1M-$3M face the hardest fundraising environment
- 30 slides is too many -- most successful decks referenced in this research cycle are 10-15 slides. The deck took 2+ months to build, suggesting over-engineering
- Positive game feedback + zero funding = the game quality is no longer the bottleneck. Business model, market timing, and genre selection matter more than creative excellence
- Conference pitching (DICE, GDC) still generates the most meetings, but conversion rates are extremely low
- $10K out-of-pocket cost for pitching (before any development funding) is a real barrier for indie studios
- "Mid-range" indie games (~$2-5M budget) are the hardest category to fund; the market has bifurcated into micro-budget and large-budget

## Context

Published in 2024 on Little Polygon's dev blog. The studio is small (6 full-time + 8 part-time at peak). Nightshift Galaxy is a PC/console game, not mobile/F2P, but the fundraising climate observations apply broadly. The developer continues solo development with plans for Steam Early Access. The post is unusually honest about failure, which makes it more valuable than success stories for understanding current market conditions.

## Applicability

- **For NBI advisory**: Critical reality check for any client planning to fundraise in the $1-3M range. NBI should counsel clients on the funding gap and help them position either below $500K or above $5M
- **Market intelligence**: The bifurcation of the indie funding market is a key trend NBI should communicate to clients entering fundraising
- **Limitation**: This is a PC/console game, not mobile/F2P. Mobile games with proven retention metrics may fare better, but the macro funding climate affects all segments
