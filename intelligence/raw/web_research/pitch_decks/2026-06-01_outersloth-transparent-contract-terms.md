# Outersloth Open Contract -- $19.2M Across 24 Games with Published Deal Terms

**Source:** https://www.gamedeveloper.com/production/outersloth-shares-contract-terms-and-game-funding-lessons-for-sickos + https://www.innersloth.com/the-outersloth-contract/

## What happened

Outersloth (Innersloth's indie funding arm, founded 2022) published its full standard contract publicly following a GDC 2026 presentation. This is unprecedented -- no other game funder has released their actual contract terms online. Victoria Tran (Community Director) and Forest Willard (CEO) presented.

## The numbers

- **Total invested:** $19,161,040 across 24 games
- **Budget:** $25 million over five years (quarterly disbursement)
- **Per-game range:** $50,000 to $2 million
- **Signing rate:** ~1.4% of pitches received (slightly above industry average)

## Contract terms (published verbatim)

- **Pre-recoup split:** 50/50 (Outersloth and developer split revenue equally until investment recoups)
- **Post-recoup split:** 15% Outersloth / 85% developer
- **Contract term:** Fixed seven years post-release, regardless of success or failure
- **IP ownership:** Developer retains IP
- **Platform rights:** Outersloth funds select platforms but retains revenue share on additional platforms developer releases independently
- **No publishing services:** Pure funding, no marketing/PR/QA/localisation provided
- **Monthly reporting:** Required from funded studios

Quote from Willard: "We don't offer services, so it doesn't really make sense for us to tack on margin."

## What they look for in pitches

- Production timeline viability (can the team actually finish this?)
- Budget slides are "make or break" -- must show understanding of true costs (QA, localisation, etc.)
- "Vibe check" through calls/meetings to ensure mutual understanding
- Focus on getting games to market and achieving sustainability, not chasing growth

## Named funded games

Flock Around, Dosa Divas, Mars First Logistics, One Btn Bosses, Battle Suit Aces (10+ more in development). Mars First Logistics (Shape Shop, Ian MacLarty) has "more than recouped its funding since release." Dosa Divas (Outerloop Games, Chandana Ekanayake): "The game wouldn't exist without funding."

## Comparison to traditional publisher deals

Industry standard from Voyer Law analysis of 30 deals:
- Average advance: $318K ($460K when advance exists; 18% of deals have no advance)
- Standard split: 60% developer / 40% publisher
- 42% of deals require full recoup before developer sees revenue
- Outersloth's 85/15 post-recoup is significantly more developer-friendly than the 55/45 industry average for advance-bearing deals

## Why this matters for NBI

This is the single most transparent funding deal structure publicly available in the indie games space. The published contract is a benchmark document for any studio negotiating terms. The 50/50 pre-recoup then 15/85 post-recoup structure creates a concrete reference point for NBI advisory work on fundraising terms. The $19.2M total across 24 games gives a real average (~$800K per game) that calibrates what indie PC/console projects can expect from non-traditional funders.
