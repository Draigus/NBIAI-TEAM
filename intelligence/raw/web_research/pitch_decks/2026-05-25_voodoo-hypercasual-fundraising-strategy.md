# Voodoo: Hypercasual Mobile Gaming Unicorn Fundraising Deep Dive

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Comprehensive Substack analysis of Voodoo, the French company that created the hypercasual mobile gaming category. Fundraising history: EUR280K equity crowdfunding (2014), $200M from Goldman Sachs Growth Fund (2018, all European VCs passed), minority stake (20-25%) from Tencent (2020, $1.4B unicorn valuation). At 2019 peak: EUR360M revenue, 300M MAU, 3.7B total downloads, 100+ games, 250 employees, profitable. Revenue split: ~67% publishing third-party games, ~33% internal development. Core pitch to investors: (1) Category creator with defensible ecosystem, (2) Systematised hit-generation process (weekly prototypes, data-driven kill/scale decisions), (3) Flywheel -- more hits attract more developers attract more data attract more hits, (4) Clear success metrics: D1 retention >55%, D7 >22%, CPI <$0.25. Key strategic lesson: process over single hit. Investors funded the machine, not the games. Challenges emerged post-2020: low barriers to entry, iOS 14 IDFA tracking restrictions, revenue share compression (20% to 10%), zero consumer brand recognition. Voodoo responded with genre diversification (hybrid-casual), acquisitions, and Tencent partnership for Asian expansion.

## Decisions / Insights

- "Process over single hit" is the dominant investor thesis for mobile gaming -- Voodoo, Homa, and Play Ventures all converge on this principle
- Voodoo's success metrics (D1 >55%, D7 >22%, CPI <$0.25) provide concrete benchmarks for hypercasual pitches. These are aggressive -- most studios won't hit them, but knowing the bar matters
- Goldman Sachs investing $200M when all European VCs passed is a cautionary tale about VC herd mentality and the opportunity for contrarian investors
- The 67/33 publishing/internal revenue split shows that successful mobile publishers derive most value from ecosystem management, not own-IP development
- Post-2020 challenges (IDFA, revenue share compression, zero brand equity) are precisely the headwinds any mobile studio pitch must address today
- Voodoo's pivot to hybrid-casual and Tencent partnership for Asia distribution is the playbook for scaling beyond hypercasual
