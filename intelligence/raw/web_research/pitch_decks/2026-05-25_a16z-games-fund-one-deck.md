# a16z Games Fund One LP Pitch Deck ($660M Raise)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Andrew Chen (GP, a16z) publicly shared the fundraising deck for a16z's $660M Games Fund One in October 2023. While an LP-facing deck (VC pitching to its own investors), the structure provides a masterclass in gaming investment narrative. Key structural elements: (1) Industry framing -- games positioned as experiencing a "Marvel Moment" with cultural crossover (Last of Us TV, Super Mario Movie, Hogwarts Legacy), (2) Historical thesis -- "games are often the killer app for new technologies" (3D, GPUs, freemium, social), (3) Market evolution -- shift from cartridge purchases to "decade-plus long-running games" as next-gen social networks, (4) Operating team differentiation -- hires from Supercell (Doug McCracken, Marketing), YouTube (Lester Chen, Creators), Riot (Jordan Mazer, Talent). By year one: 25 investments, 10 team hires. 80% of 2023 investments featured major AI components. The deck contained numerous redactions of specific returns and company names. Portfolio at time of writing: 25 companies across game studios, infrastructure, and (pivoted away from) Web3.

## Decisions / Insights

- LP-facing decks work differently from studio-facing decks, but the narrative architecture transfers: frame the industry moment, show team unfair advantage, prove thesis with early portfolio signals
- a16z's pivot: entered with game studios + Web3 + infrastructure thesis, within one year 80% of investments were AI x Games -- shows how fast VC thesis evolves
- Operating team strategy (named hires from tier-1 studios) is a differentiator NBI clients should note: VCs that bring operational support beyond capital are increasingly the norm
- Cultural crossover argument ("Marvel Moment") is a powerful framing device for any game pitch targeting generalist investors

## Context

Andrew Chen is a prominent tech essayist and General Partner at a16z. His decision to share the actual LP deck publicly is unusual -- most VCs keep these confidential. The deck was published approximately one year after the fund closed. a16z Games focuses on early-stage investments across studios and infrastructure.

## Applicability

- **For NBI advisory**: When helping clients build pitch decks, the industry-framing approach (cultural moment + historical precedent) is directly transferable from LP-level to studio-level pitches
- **For NBI consulting**: Understanding how top-tier gaming VCs position the market helps NBI advise clients on what language and framing resonates with institutional capital
- **Limitation**: This is a VC-to-LP deck, not a studio-to-VC deck -- the metrics and structure differ significantly. The team slide approach (named operators from elite studios) does transfer
