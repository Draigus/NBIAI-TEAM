# Homa Games: 13-Slide Deck That Raised $50M Series A (Mobile Hypercasual)

## Key Content (max 200 words)

Homa Games, a Paris-based mobile gaming platform, raised $50M in Series A (2021, led by Northzone) using a 13-slide deck, then $100M in Series B (2022) using a 19-slide deck (exclusive breakdown in Business Insider, paywalled). The company positions itself as a "game technology lab" combining creativity, data analytics, and no-code tools for hypercasual and casual game development. At pitch time: 1 billion+ downloads across portfolio. The deck's effectiveness came from: (1) Proven track record of top-tier gaming apps demonstrating execution, (2) Strategic positioning that "successful game building transcends mere creation" -- selling the platform/process, not individual games, (3) Market expertise with advanced analytical tools, (4) Long-term vision with reliable execution. Homa's model: market analysis, no-code solutions, data analytics, and creative testing -- essentially a technology-enabled publishing platform for indie mobile developers. The company later raised additional rounds, with total funding exceeding $150M. Investors: Northzone (lead), with earlier seed from Idinvest Partners and E.ventures ($15M seed).

## Decisions / Insights

- 13 slides for a $50M raise confirms the "less is more" principle -- no padding, every slide carries weight
- Homa pitched the platform/process, not individual games -- this is the Play Ventures/Katkoff principle ("sell the company, not the game") executed at scale
- Technology-driven game development (data analytics, no-code, creative testing) is what differentiated Homa from traditional publishers in investor eyes
- The progression from 13-slide ($50M) to 19-slide ($100M) Series B suggests decks expand as traction data accumulates -- more slides means more evidence, not more fluff
- For indie-to-mid-tier studios: if you're building a platform or toolkit alongside games, you may attract higher valuations than pure game studios

## Context

Homa Games operates in the hypercasual and casual mobile gaming space, which was experiencing peak investor interest in 2021-2022. The market has since cooled significantly (iOS 14 IDFA changes, ad revenue compression), but the deck structure remains a strong reference for how to pitch a data-driven mobile gaming business. The 19-slide Series B deck was exclusively obtained by Business Insider and is paywalled. Only the 13-slide Series A deck structure is accessible via derstartupcfo.com.
