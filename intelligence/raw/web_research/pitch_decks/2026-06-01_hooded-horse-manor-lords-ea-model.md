## The Hooded Horse model

12-person publisher founded 2019. ~20 titles published. Laser-focused on strategy, simulation, and RPGs for PC.

### Deal terms (public)

- **Revenue split:** 65% developer / 35% publisher for the entire game lifetime
- **No recoup provisions against developer share** -- developer earns from day one
- **Minimum $100K marketing spend per game** (guaranteed, not discretionary)
- **No IP transfer required**

CEO Tim Bender on why: "That model [recoup-first] has become way too accepted within the industry." Traditional recoup shifts risk to the less-equipped party -- publishers have portfolios, developers have one game.

### Performance benchmarks

2022 releases: 90,000-350,000 units each (6-month median). Successfully attracted self-published games with established audiences: Xenonauts 2, Manor Lords, Old World.

## Manor Lords -- the early access pitch that became the biggest indie of 2024

**Developer:** Slavic Magic (Greg Styczen, solo developer, Poland)
**Publisher:** Hooded Horse
**Genre:** Medieval city-builder / RTS

### The pitch trajectory

- Early development funded by Patreon donations
- Received Epic MegaGrants funding (2021)
- Signed with Hooded Horse (timing not public)
- Built to #1 most wishlisted game on Steam with 3.2 million wishlists pre-launch
- Shot to #6 on Steam's global wishlist chart after Steam Next Fest demo

### Sales results (Early Access)

- 1 million copies in 24 hours (EA launch 26 April 2024)
- 2 million copies within 3 weeks
- 3 million copies by February 2025
- Peak: 173,178 concurrent players (highest ever for a City Builder tag game on Steam)

Tim Bender: reaching two million copies "exceeded even those [optimistic] hopes."

## What Hooded Horse looks for in pitches

- Deep, systems-heavy design for long-form play
- Strong vertical slice or 10-15 minutes of polished, bug-free gameplay footage
- Clear business case alongside creative vision
- Unique selling point -- "something that players can fall in love with"
- Approach publishers about one year before planned launch
- Genre fit matters: strategy, simulation, RPGs only

## How early access changes the pitch

Manor Lords demonstrates the "traction flywheel" for EA pitches:
1. Build a compelling demo (Steam Next Fest)
2. Accumulate wishlists as social proof (3.2M)
3. Use wishlist velocity as the core pitch metric
4. EA launch creates immediate revenue, de-risking the publisher's position
5. Post-EA roadmap becomes the ongoing value story

Key benchmarks publishers care about:
- 30,000+ wishlists = credible launch cushion
- Wishlist momentum (month-over-month increases) matters as much as raw total
- EA wishlist-to-sales conversion is ~8% lower than full releases
- Median first-month sales: ~27% of total launch wishlists

## Why this matters for NBI

Hooded Horse's transparent terms (65/35, no recoup) represent the most developer-friendly deal structure from a traditional publisher. Manor Lords is the ultimate early access pitch case study -- solo dev, wishlist-driven social proof, traction metrics that made the pitch self-evident. The progression from Patreon to Epic MegaGrants to publisher deal is a replicable funding ladder for indie clients. The "approach one year before launch" timing guidance is concrete and actionable. For NBI advisory clients building strategy/sim games, Hooded Horse should be on the shortlist.
