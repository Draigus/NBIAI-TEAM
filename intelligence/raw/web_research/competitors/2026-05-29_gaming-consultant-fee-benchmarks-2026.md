# Gaming Consultant Fee Benchmarks 2026: Hourly, Project, and Retainer Rates

## Key Content

Comprehensive fee data for gaming consultants by specialisation, experience level, and engagement model (May 2026):

**Hourly rates by experience:**
- Early-career (0-3 years): $65-$100/hr
- Mid-level (4-8 years): $100-$175/hr
- Senior specialist (9+ years): $150-$275/hr
- Principal/boutique owner: $200-$350/hr

**Hourly rates by specialisation (premium commands):**
- Monetisation strategy: $150-$300/hr
- Live ops design: $150-$275/hr
- Game economy design: $150-$275/hr
- Esports/competitive design: $125-$250/hr
- Marketing/UA consulting: $125-$250/hr
- Production/pipeline consulting: $100-$200/hr

**Project-based fees:**
- Monetisation audit: $8,000-$30,000
- Live ops strategy: $10,000-$35,000
- Game economy design: $10,000-$30,000
- Technical/production: $10,000-$40,000
- Game design review: $6,000-$25,000

**Retainer models:**
- On-call advisory: $2,000-$5,000/month
- Strategic advisory: $4,000-$10,000/month
- Live ops/monetisation advisory: $6,000-$15,000/month
- Embedded studio partner: $10,000-$25,000/month

**Day rates:**
- Mid-level: $800-$1,400/day
- Senior specialist: $1,200-$2,200/day
- Principal/boutique owner: $1,600-$2,800/day

## Decisions / Insights

These benchmarks provide the first structured market rate data for gaming consulting in 2026. Key takeaways for NBI:
- NBI's senior advisory work (Glen-level) should sit at $200-$350/hr or equivalent day rates of $1,600-$2,800.
- Production and operations consulting commands lower premiums ($100-$200/hr) vs monetisation and economy design ($150-$300/hr). NBI's production consulting should be priced at the high end given the scarcity of experienced production consultants.
- The embedded studio partner retainer ($10,000-$25,000/month) maps well to NBI's longer-term client engagements.
- Value-based pricing is increasingly preferred over hourly -- "hours are not the unit of value being delivered, studio outcomes are."

## Context

Source is consultfees.com, a benchmarking platform. Data appears to be aggregated from industry surveys and publicly available rate cards. US-centric pricing -- UK/European rates may be 10-20% lower. These are independent consultant rates, not Big Four or MBB (McKinsey/Bain/BCG) rates which run 3-5x higher.
