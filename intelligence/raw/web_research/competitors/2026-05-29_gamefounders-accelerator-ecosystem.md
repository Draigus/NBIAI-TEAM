# GameFounders: Gaming Accelerator Pivots to Ecosystem Building

## Key Content

GameFounders (founded 2012, Estonia) has operated gaming accelerator programmes across three regions:
- **Europe (Estonia):** 28 teams (2012-2014)
- **Southeast Asia (Malaysia):** 39 accelerator teams + 40 LITE participants (2015-2017)
- **Middle East (Saudi Arabia, Riyadh):** 21 accelerator teams + 100 incubator participants (2022-2023), in partnership with Saudi Ministry of Communications and IT

Total: 100+ game studios supported. Programme types include full accelerator cycles, "Accelerator LITE," incubator programmes, university partnerships, and ecosystem events (game jams, monthly meetups, conferences).

Mentor network includes gaming veterans from King, Rovio, Nintendo, Google, Microsoft, and Sony.

No pricing information published. Accelerator model likely involves equity stakes (standard for accelerators: 5-10% for seed funding + programme access).

## Decisions / Insights

GameFounders is not a direct competitor to NBI in the advisory market:
- **Accelerator model is fundamentally different** from paid consulting. GameFounders takes equity; NBI charges fees.
- **Their pivot to ecosystem building** (university partnerships, K-12 education, government partnerships) moves them further from NBI's space.
- **The Saudi Arabia programme** is relevant to NBI's MENA interests -- it indicates Saudi government willingness to fund gaming ecosystem development, which could create pipeline for NBI advisory work with Saudi-incubated studios.
- **Execution Labs (Montreal, founded 2012)** has effectively wound down active operations -- they are no longer accepting new studios and focus on supporting their existing portfolio. Not a current competitive threat.

## Context

GameFounders represents the accelerator/incubator tier of gaming support -- earlier stage and more hands-on than NBI's advisory model. Their government partnership model (Saudi MCIT) is a potential template for NBI to explore in other markets.

## Applicability

Low competitive threat. Monitor for pipeline opportunities -- studios graduating from GameFounders' programmes may need the kind of strategic advisory NBI provides. The Saudi partnership model is worth noting for NBI's MENA business development.
