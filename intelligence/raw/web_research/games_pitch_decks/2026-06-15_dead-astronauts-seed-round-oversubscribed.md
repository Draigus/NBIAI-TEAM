# Dead Astronauts — €4M Oversubscribed Seed, Massive Entertainment Veterans, Pre-Prototype (February 2025)

## Key Content

Dead Astronauts raised an oversubscribed €4M seed round in February 2025 led by Behold Ventures (managing partner: Karl Magnus Troedsson, former DICE/Battlefield studio head). Co-investors: Lifelike Capital, 1Up Ventures, Acequia Capital, plus angels including Kristian Segerstrale (SuperEvil MegaCorp), Matt Bilbey (former EA executive), and Klaas Kersting (Kolibri Games/Miniclip). No prototype or vertical slice existed at funding.

**Founding team:** Four founders from Massive Entertainment (Ubisoft), the studio behind Avatar: Frontiers of Pandora. Credits include senior development roles across AAA console titles.

**Pitch framing (reconstructed from press materials):**
1. Team slide — Massive Entertainment / Ubisoft veterans with verifiable AAA credits on a recognisable recent title
2. IP vision — VEIL: open-world Nordic action-adventure built in Unreal Engine 5
3. Technical differentiation — procedural content generation and custom tooling (claimed, no prototype to demonstrate)
4. Market positioning — Nordic wilderness as an underutilised cultural IP category

**Investor rationale on record:** Troedsson (Behold Ventures): "we are passionate about supporting visionary founders who are shaping the future of interactive entertainment. Investing in a new game studio is more than just funding — it's about empowering creative talent to push boundaries."

The oversubscription is attributable to a two-stage approach: angel investors (from personal professional networks) validated the team's quality first, then that validation was used to open institutional VC conversations.

## Decisions / Insights

The most important structural insight here is the role of **operator-investors as validation signals**. Segerstrale and Bilbey are not generalist VCs — they are practitioners who have worked at or alongside studios comparable to Massive Entertainment. Their participation signals to institutional VCs that independent domain experts have already validated team quality. The angel round preceded the institutional round.

The lead investor (Behold Ventures) being run by an ex-DICE studio head is not incidental — Troedsson can personally evaluate Massive Entertainment credits in a way a generalist VC cannot. The pitch was not made to the VC market at large; it was made to a specific investor who was uniquely qualified to assess the claim.

The oversubscription outcome suggests the pitch was not a single cold-outreach to VCs — it was built through a prior professional network that enabled a competitive round.

## Context

This is one of the few 2025 European studio-formation cases with a documented oversubscription. The raise is notable because it inverts the typical framing: rather than the founders demonstrating to the market that they are credible, they found an investor whose personal background made the founders' credentials immediately legible.

The "Nordic wilderness as underutilised IP" positioning is interesting as a market gap thesis — similar in structure to Midsummer's "life-sim successor vacuum" framing. Both successful pre-prototype pitches included a verifiable market positioning thesis, not just team credentials.

## Applicability

**For NBI:** This is the strongest European comparator for a studio-formation seed pitch. Key lessons:
1. Domain-specialist investors are more likely to fund on team credentials than generalist VCs — pitch selection matters as much as deck quality
2. Angel validation from operator-investors (practitioners who can assess team quality independently) de-risks the institutional round
3. A market-gap thesis anchors the vision element; the team credentials alone are insufficient
