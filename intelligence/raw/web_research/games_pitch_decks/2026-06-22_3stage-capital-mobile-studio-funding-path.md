# 3-Stage Capital Framework for Mobile Game Studios: Seed → Grey Zone Debt → UA Scaling (Gamigion, 2026)

## Key Content

Gamigion's 3-Stage Capital framework proposes a structured funding path for mobile game studios from pre-launch to proven unit economics, specifically addressing the funding gap between proof-of-concept and scalable business.

**Stage 1: Seed Capital (Discovery Equity)**
- Fund size: $25-50M pool targeting 50-100 portfolio investments at $500K initial checks
- Investor profile: gaming domain experts accepting high failure rates (portfolio bet logic)
- What the pitch must demonstrate: team credentials, early concept, closed alpha/beta retention data
- Success threshold: soft launch completion + Day 30 retention of 8-10%
- Instrument: equity with meaningful ownership stakes

**Stage 2: Grey Zone Capital (Build-Out Convertible Debt)**
- This addresses the $500K-$3M dead zone identified in prior research cycles (Little Polygon post-mortem)
- Fund structure: $1-3M facility deployed in tranches, 12-15% PIK coupon (compounding), senior liquidation preference, 5% warrants or conversion optionality
- Qualification threshold: Day 30 retention of 8-10% AND $50K/month early revenue AND functioning core loop
- What the pitch must demonstrate: retention curves, content burn rate, specific use-case allocation (content development vs infrastructure), repayment timeline tied to scaled revenue
- Intended providers: regional development bodies, strategic studios building acquisition pipelines, credit-focused investors, family offices — NOT traditional gaming VCs
- What it is NOT: this is not equity. It is structured debt for studios that have proven concept but cannot raise a conventional round at an attractive valuation

**Stage 3: UA Scaling Debt (Proven Economics)**
- Instrument: cohort financing or accounts-receivable factoring based on breakeven period length
- Qualification threshold: LTV > CAC proven, predictable unit economics, repeatable UA channels, multi-month cohort data
- What the pitch must demonstrate: cohort analysis by acquisition channel, CAC/LTV ratios, retention curves by channel, scaled revenue projection with debt repayment schedule

**Typical growth timeline:**
- Year 0-1: $500K seed → soft launch → $50K/month revenue
- Year 1-2: $1-3M grey zone → content build-out → $300K/month revenue
- Year 2-3: Cohort debt → UA scale → multi-million revenue

## Decisions / Insights

The most important element of this framework is **Stage 2: the grey zone convertible debt instrument**. Prior research in this bank (Little Polygon post-mortem, 2026-05-25 cycle) documented the $500K-$3M dead zone as a structural funding gap where studios with proven concept cannot raise conventional equity at acceptable dilution. The grey zone instrument is a specific answer to that gap:

- **Why debt, not equity:** at Day 30 retention of 8-10% and $50K/month revenue, a studio has de-risked concept but not proven scalability. Equity at this stage forces a valuation negotiation with high uncertainty, leading either to excessive dilution or failure to close. Debt bypasses the valuation problem.
- **Why PIK coupon (12-15%):** Payment-in-Kind means the coupon compounds into principal rather than requiring cash outflows. This preserves studio cash for content development during the build-out period.
- **Why senior liquidation preference:** Investors who are not gaming VCs (regional bodies, family offices) require downside protection that equity does not provide in a distressed liquidation. Senior preference with 5% warrants gives them equity upside without requiring them to take equity risk during the most uncertain phase.

The pitch for Stage 2 is structurally different from equity pitches: it is a **repayment thesis**, not a growth thesis. The deck must demonstrate that $50K/month current revenue can grow to a repayment-covering level within the debt term.

## Context

This framework is a proposal, not a documented industry standard. No named studios have publicly disclosed using a Stage 2 grey zone instrument. However, the mechanics (PIK coupon, senior preference, warrant kicker) are standard structured finance terms applied to a gaming-specific context.

The framework's practical value is in the pitch preparation it implies: a mobile studio in the Stage 1-2 transition zone should be building towards the Stage 2 qualification metrics (D30 8-10%, $50K/month) and framing their ask around debt repayment capacity rather than equity growth projections. This is a materially different narrative.

Regional development bodies (UK Screen Industries Growth Fund, Sweden Game Arena, various EU digital economy funds) are named as potential Stage 2 providers. These are real sources of non-dilutive or low-dilution capital that NBI clients rarely include in their funding strategy.

## Applicability

**For NBI:** This framework is useful in two ways:
1. As a diagnostic tool for clients who are stuck in the $500K-$3M dead zone — the Stage 2 description gives a specific instrument type, term structure, and pitch narrative for studios in this position
2. As a roadmap for pitch preparation — clients can work backwards from Stage 3 (UA scaling) requirements to identify what they need to demonstrate at each stage before approaching investors

Key practical lesson: studios pitching at the Stage 1-2 transition should NOT be pitching VCs. They should be pitching structured debt providers (regional bodies, family offices) and building their deck around repayment capacity, not growth multiples.

### 3-Stage Mobile Studio Funding Framework — Seed → Grey Zone Debt → UA Scaling
**Structure:** Stage 1: $500K equity (soft launch + D30 8-10%); Stage 2: $1-3M convertible debt with PIK coupon 12-15%, senior preference, 5% warrants (D30 8-10% + $50K/month revenue); Stage 3: cohort debt/AR factoring (LTV > CAC proven)
**What works:** Stages 2 and 3 use non-equity instruments that bypass valuation negotiations during high-uncertainty phases. Stage 2 specifically addresses the $500K-$3M dead zone documented in prior NBI research (Little Polygon post-mortem).
**Key differentiator:** Stage 2 (grey zone) changes the pitch narrative from growth thesis to repayment thesis — different audience (regional bodies, family offices), different deck structure (repayment timeline vs growth multiples), different instrument type (debt, not equity).
**Applicable when:** Mobile studio with soft launch data but unable to raise Series A equity at acceptable dilution. The Stage 2 instrument is most useful for studios sitting on D30 8-10% retention with early revenue but no institutional VC backing.
