# Grand Games — $103M in 2 Years: Hybrid Casual Pitch Built on Creator Ownership + Chart Dominance (Istanbul, May 2026)

## Key Content

Grand Games raised $70M Series B in May 2026 led by Balderton Capital's Growth Fund, bringing total funding to $103M across three rounds in under two years. Previous rounds: $3M pre-seed (2024), $30M Series A (January 2025, also Balderton Early Stage Fund). Valuation increased approximately 6x from Series A to Series B with single-digit dilution. The company is approaching unicorn status.

**Studio facts:** ~75 people, Istanbul. Six live hybrid casual puzzle games. 50M+ cumulative downloads, 200+ countries. Two titles (Magic Sort! and Block Out) simultaneously ranked #1 and #2 on US iOS most-downloaded chart at time of fundraise. Year-over-year revenue growth: 5x.

**The five-autonomous-studios model:** Grand does not operate as a single centralised studio. It runs five autonomous internal studios with decision-making authority delegated to individual game development teams. The founding team (Batuhan Celebi, Mehmet Calim, Mustafa Firtina) functions as strategic support, not product dictators. This structure was a primary pitch differentiator — the founders argued it produces faster, higher-quality execution than centralised studios because the people building the games own the decisions.

**Investor rationale on record:** Rana Yared, Balderton Capital partner: "Having invested in the mobile gaming space for many years, we know what exceptional teams look like in this category, and Grand is unquestionably one."

**Pitch structure (reconstructed from press materials and Balderton rationale):**
1. Team slide — founders' belief in creator ownership over command-and-control structures
2. Organisational model — five autonomous studios as a structural speed advantage
3. Proof of execution — #1 and #2 US iOS simultaneously; 50M downloads; 5x YoY revenue
4. Market positioning — hybrid casual as fastest-growing mobile sub-category (IAP 100% YoY growth in Q2 2025)
5. Capital allocation — marketing acceleration for live titles, pipeline support for upcoming releases

## Decisions / Insights

The pitch was built on **proof over promise** at every level. Rather than projecting future revenue, Grand entered their Series B with simultaneous chart leadership in the world's largest revenue market (US iOS) and audited 5x revenue growth. The deck argument was: "we have already done it; here is how much more we can do with more capital." This mirrors Homa's Series A positioning ($50M, also platform-over-games thesis) but with stronger market proof at a later stage.

The **creator-ownership operating model** served a dual purpose in the pitch: it differentiated Grand from the typical centralised mobile studio AND explained why the team could continue scaling (five studio leaders making independent decisions is more resilient than a single executive bottleneck). The pitch essentially argued that the organisational model was itself the competitive moat.

**Balderton doubling down from Series A to Series B** is the strongest possible signal. The fund had full information and internal performance data when they decided to lead the growth round. Their partner Rana Yared's category knowledge ("we know what exceptional teams look like") was leveraged as investor credibility — the pitch did not need to explain what good looks like; it needed to demonstrate it was one of the examples.

## Context

Grand Games represents the strongest 2026 example of a mobile studio raising on proven metrics rather than vision. Founded in 2024, they reached simultaneous US iOS chart dominance and a $103M total raise by mid-2026. The Turkish talent pool (Istanbul) as a pitch geography signal: Gram Games, Rollic, Peak, Masomo — all Istanbul-based exits. Grand positioned itself as the next in that sequence.

The hybrid casual sub-category context strengthens the case: hybrid casual IAP grew 100% YoY in Q2 2025 and 67% in Q1, with top-10 projects generating $126M in a single quarter. A deck pitched against this market backdrop could demonstrate category tailwinds without needing to educate investors on why hybrid casual is worth backing.

## Applicability

**For NBI:** This is the clearest 2026 template for a mobile studio Series B pitch. Key lessons:
1. Enter a funding round with chart proof, not projections — simultaneous #1 and #2 positions are unambiguous
2. Organisational model differentiation (creator ownership, autonomous studios) is as important as product quality — it answers "why will you keep winning?"
3. Repeat-investor lead (Balderton backed both Series A and B) removes the credibility question entirely; NBI clients should cultivate a lead investor who can grow with them across rounds
4. Revenue multiple-based pitch ($103M raised on 5x YoY growth) sets a benchmark NBI should use when coaching clients preparing Series A/B materials
