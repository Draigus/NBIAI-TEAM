# Midsummer Studios — $6M Seed on Team Pedigree + Genre Thesis, No Prototype (May 2024)

## Key Content

Jake Solomon (XCOM creative director, 23 years at Firaxis) and Will Miller (Civilisation: Beyond Earth lead designer) raised $6M from Transcend Fund (lead), Tirta Ventures, Betaworks Ventures, 1Up Ventures, F4 Fund, Krafton, and Day Zero Productions in May 2024. No prototype or vertical slice existed at funding — the entire pitch was built on (a) cumulative team credentials and (b) a genre thesis.

**Pitch structure (reconstructed from press materials):**
1. Team narrative — "category leadership pedigree" framing (60+ combined years across Civilization, XCOM, The Sims at Firaxis/Maxis), presented not as a CV list but as a homecoming story: industry veterans reuniting to reinvent a genre they were uniquely positioned to own
2. Genre thesis — "player-driven narratives" and the underserved life-sim gap; positioned as a documented successor vacuum (The Sims, dormant at EA, has no credible spiritual successor)
3. Studio design philosophy — community-generated storytelling as the core differentiator, not a specific game pitch

**Investor rationale on record:** Shanti Bergel (Transcend Fund) specifically cited "rare experience and design artistry" in the life-sim genre and his personal familiarity with the genre's potential as the investment rationale.

Studio had 11 developers at announcement.

**Critical context:** Midsummer Studios closed in early 2026 after failing to secure a Series A. The seed pitch succeeded; the studio did not survive the seed-to-Series-A transition.

## Decisions / Insights

The pitch worked because the genre thesis was independently verifiable — the Sims successor vacuum is a real, documented market gap, not a claim. VCs who follow the life-sim genre could validate the thesis without needing a playable demo.

The team narrative was structured as a "homecoming story" rather than a credentials list: not "we have impressive CVs" but "we are the team the market has been waiting for." This framing positions the founders as inevitable rather than merely experienced.

The multi-investor backing (5 VCs + strategic investors including Krafton and Day Zero) suggests the pitch was made simultaneously to a broad group — likely an oversubscribed seed where the team's visibility in the industry created inbound interest alongside outbound pitching.

## Context

This is the clearest 2024 case study of a pure pre-prototype team pitch succeeding with institutional VCs in gaming. However, the failure mode is equally important: the team + genre thesis was sufficient to clear the seed bar but not the Series A bar, where execution evidence (retention data, demo performance, player testing) is required. The seed pitch approach is not a complete fundraising strategy — it is a first-round strategy only.

The 11-person team at announcement was deliberately lean. Overheads were kept low to extend seed runway while awaiting Series A.

## Applicability

**For NBI:** Use as a positive case study for seed pitch coaching with two caveats clearly stated: (1) this level of pedigree (Firaxis/Civilization/XCOM) is genuinely rare — the approach is more replicable than the specific credits; (2) pair with the closure context — the seed strategy and the survival strategy are different questions.
