## What This Bank Knows

- **Common failure modes in 40-100 person founder-led studios** -- documentation gap hidden by enthusiasm, single producer failure, chain-of-command bypass, scope invisibility, garden leave eligibility gaps, and five patterns of "what clients hide" that NBI diagnoses on entry

- **Pre-production and VS patterns** -- live service vs box game mindset gap (leadership commercially misaligned, hard cutoff date fix), VS real game anxiety (scope anxiety and scope creep are the same phenomenon; explicit mandate plus confirmed-only scope enforcement), poisoned phrase problem (terminology from failed initiatives blocking communication; proactive reframing before others trigger the label)

- **Delivery and advisory methodology** -- milestone as advisory lever for funded studios with no urgency, decision owner model for meeting bloat (named owner per pipeline stage, attendees restricted to decision makers only), executive RAG meeting format (action-item-first, triage and accountability only), CTO assessment criteria for live service studios (live service credits as threshold requirement, not preference; pushed back on bad ideas as positive signal)

- **HR and employment** -- three-tier garden leave fix, staged staff replacement methodology, UK probation exit documentation grounds, hiring pipeline governance; junior hire support requirements for remote studios (training/mentoring/buddy/director check-ins mandatory; ~3 veterans, 2 mids, 1 junior target shape; higher junior density makes the veteran a bottleneck)

- **Engagement delivery** -- written decision records as mandatory deliverable from day one, red-teaming all NBI outputs (multi-role adversarial challenge + evidence tables), SoW structure with measurable acceptance criteria (most-resisted section: clients want discretion on sign-off), scope fear containment protocol, S-curve change management, blind affinity estimation, three-day offsite pattern

## Most Recent Additions (June 2026, 8 new extracts -- second batch)

- **Live service vs box game mindset gap** -- head of development optimising for box product while commercial model requires live service architecture from day one; hard base game cutoff date as fix; must be identified and raised in pre-production
- **VS real game anxiety** -- hedged effort and scope creep are the same phenomenon; uncertainty about what is real drives both; explicit leadership mandate plus confirmed-only scope enforcement
- **Poisoned phrase problem** -- founder/CEO reacts to label not substance; proactive reframing before others use old language; must identify what the stakeholder actually wants vs what they objected to
- **CTO assessment criteria for live service studios** -- live service/MMO credits required at lead or principal level; vacancy-filled vs earned-upward distinction matters; pushback on bad ideas in interview is positive signal; large-AAA background correlates with unworkable salary expectations at Series A
- **Meeting bloat and the decision owner fix** -- named decision owner per pipeline stage; attendees = decision makers for that stage only; extends via executive RAG format (action items first, RAG + plan + closure date, no problem-solving, persistent item titles)
- **Milestone as advisory lever** -- hard internal milestone with no conference attached forces production discipline; investor readiness follows from discipline, not the other way around
- **Junior hire support requirements** -- structured support mandatory for remote juniors: dedicated training hours + 1:1 mentoring + senior buddy + director check-ins every 3 weeks; maximum ~1 junior per 5-person subteam
- **Executive RAG meeting format** -- integrated with decision owner model; action items visible with names, 5-7 minutes per area owner, no problem-solving in the meeting, persistent item titles week-to-week for tracking against fixed reference
