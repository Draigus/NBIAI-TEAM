## What This Bank Knows

- **Production maturity gap is universal and predictable.** Studios in the 40-100 person range systematically present as further along than they are. Working prototype code without GDDs, impressive art against undocumented systems, feature scope 2-3x larger than assumed at first formal estimate. This is the entry diagnostic for every engagement.

- **Scope is the root cause of almost every other failure.** Features added informally, approval chains bypassed, founders injecting ideas mid-sprint. The studio has no formal mechanism to say no until one is installed. Every NBI engagement involving production structure has hit this pattern.

- **The single producer configuration is a critical risk above ~40 people.** Works to ~25, fails non-linearly above ~40. Remediation: EP hub overseeing four discipline tracks, each with an embedded producer.

- **Employment contract gaps are consistent across founder-led studios.** Garden leave, IP assignment, and contractor/employee boundary clauses are routinely incomplete. Three-tier contract fix for garden leave (NEW): probation = one week / company termination = full PILON + garden leave / employee resignation = garden leave equivalent to notice period, activated at company discretion. Confirm with specialist employment counsel.

- **Staged replacement converts disruption into an incremental process (NEW).** Pattern: open role → recruit candidate → 2-3 week overlap → exit underperformer. Wave sizing: 3-5-7-8. Brief COO and EP before CEO. Some underperformers self-select out when new hires arrive.

- **Studio seniority distribution advisory (NEW).** Target: ~60% seniors, ~30% mids, ~10% juniors (only with real mentorship infrastructure). Remote juniors without senior mentors develop bad habits or stall. Frame as capability elevation, not cost reduction.

- **Employee survey timing: don't launch until you can act (NEW).** A survey creates a commitment backlog. Unmet visible expectations damage trust more than the survey helps. Interim alternative: all-studio 1:1 rotation at senior leadership level. Target timing: after Jira, build pipelines, and VS are stable.

- **Founder midnight ideas are a recurring production failure mode (NEW).** Mechanism: shared idea log; weekly review rhythm; volume as natural self-regulator; in-meeting coaching in direct 1:1, never in front of the team.

- **Producer as cross-department defect translator is the delivery methodology (NEW).** Check ask quality before work starts; check output against delivery criteria before sign-off; track patterns in retros; feed rework into the producer backlog. Escalation: one defect = human; repeated pattern = escalate to director; persistent = escalate to COO.

- **Quad assessment for production readiness is the entry point for high-stakes phases (NEW).** Core question: "Can this person deliver product-level quality at speed, right now?" Tiers: red triangle (managed exit) / saves with director accountability / J (juniors, separate track) / unmarked (lower urgency exits). Lead cap ("you get three picks") forces honest assessment.

- **AI-native hiring advisory for analytics clients (NEW).** Sega 2026 benchmark: AI component required in analyst interviews. Advisory position: AI fluency outweighs raw technical skill over a 3-year horizon. Headcount arithmetic changes: one instead of three.

- **Audit-first conversion is NBI's highest-ROI entry point.** A 3-4 week production audit surfaces gaps the founders cannot see. Direct conversion to fractional engagement is the expected outcome.

- **Written decision records are non-negotiable.** Most common single failure mode in NBI-supported engagements: decisions made in meetings not written down. Decisions log is a mandatory deliverable from day one.

- **Red-teaming deliverables is structural quality control.** Multi-role adversarial challenge before delivery. Evidence tables: every non-obvious claim mapped to source, date, confidence, and gap.
