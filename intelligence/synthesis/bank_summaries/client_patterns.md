## What This Bank Knows

- **Audit-first conversion is NBI's highest-ROI entry point.** A 3-4 week production audit at a 55-person studio surfaced an early-vs-mid-production gap the founders could not see. The founder's response was "Can you help us fix this?" -- converting directly to a GBP 30k/month fractional CPO engagement.

- **Proposals win by enabling partial buy-in and anchoring to client urgency.** Separating workstreams (pricing benchmarking vs live service consulting) allowed a launch-stage F2P studio to take what they could afford ($10K SOW) without rejecting the whole engagement. Urgency framing (launch window, platform certification deadline) beats capability framing.

- **Red team validation is structural quality control, not optional polish.** NBI's own research scored 53/100 before red teaming and 93/100 after three passes. Claim accuracy is weighted highest (30%) because wrong claims in deliverables destroy credibility. Original research had ~47% quality issues before validation.

- **Hiring at growing studios consistently exposes three failure modes:** salary expectation misalignment between interviews and offer letters, solo HR overload when one person manages 7+ concurrent pipelines, and GC over-prescription of contracts that embeds milestones and fees incompatible with iterative creative work.

- **The fractional CPO model combines strategic authority with operational depth.** Glen operates as genuine internal authority -- running 1:1s, making hiring decisions, setting production governance -- not as an external adviser handing over reports. WorkSage as a client-facing tool independently generated a $5k/month engagement when a partner observed it in use.
