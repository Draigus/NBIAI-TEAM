# Research Brief: Competitor Watch

## Objective
Monitor gaming consultancies and advisory firms. Track their services, pricing signals, case studies, positioning, and gaps NBI can exploit.

## Focus This Cycle
Full scan each cycle — all categories:
- Services offered by gaming consultancies
- Published pricing or pricing signals
- Case studies published (who they worked with, what outcomes they claim)
- New service launches or capability announcements
- Gaps they don't cover that NBI does
- Their positioning and messaging (how they describe what they do)

## Quality Criteria

### What to capture
- Specific service offerings with descriptions
- Pricing data points (even indirect: "starting at $X", team sizes, engagement lengths)
- Named client case studies with claimed outcomes
- New capability announcements
- Positioning language and differentiators

### What to EXCLUDE
- Generic consulting firm listings without gaming focus
- Job postings (unless they reveal new capability areas)
- Social media posts without substance
- Firms operating at completely different scale (McKinsey gaming practice = different market)

## Competitors to Monitor
- Lightspeed (advisory)
- Execution Labs
- GameFounders
- Game economy consultancies (specific firms TBD as discovered)
- Any new entrants in gaming advisory / studio services

## Where to Search
- Competitor websites (services pages, case studies, blog posts)
- LinkedIn company pages and employee posts
- Conference speaker lists (GDC, PGC, Pocket Gamer Connects)
- Industry awards and recognition lists
- Client testimonials and references on their sites

## Purpose
Keep NBI's competitive positioning current. Inform brain/services_ai_operations.md and pitch materials.
