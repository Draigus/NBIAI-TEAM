## Executive Summary

Cross-client learnings from NBI engagements across Couch Heroes (GBP 30k/month), Goals/PlayGOALS (100K SEK SOW), Lighthouse Games, and prospect work (Sarge Universe, NSI). Key patterns: proposals that separate workstreams win partial buy-in, the three-artifact delivery pattern (Excel + Word + WorkSage) ensures alignment, red team validation is mandatory for research deliverables, and contract flexibility beats over-prescription for early-stage studios. New additions include hiring scorecard methodology, contract flexibility as advisory pattern, and art escalation handling.

## Engagement Approaches That Worked

**Fractional C-suite model:** Glen as fractional CPO at Couch Heroes (GBP 30k/month) combines strategic authority with operational depth. Key: Glen sets up recurring 1:1s with all direct reports and participates in executive meetings, not just advisory calls [source: gmail_composite_hiring_wave].

**Audit-first engagement:** Glen's 3-4 week audit at CH revealed the studio was in early production, not mid-production. Vardis responded with "Can you help us fix this?" -- the audit built trust that converted to ongoing engagement [source: granola_50612dd7].

**Pre-decision framing:** Laying binding strategic decisions before offsites prevents relitigating. Burden of proof shifts onto anyone disagreeing. Six binding decisions set before the CH offsite covered monetisation, distribution, beta strategy, naming, platform, and 12-month goals [source: ch_offsite_pre_decisions_2026-04-27].

**Three-artifact delivery:** Excel (planning) + Word (execution guide) + WorkSage (live tracking) ensures alignment. Tested on Goals engagement: 21 tasks, 3 output formats, 3-way alignment verified [source: handoff_2026-04-21].

**Hiring scorecard methodology:** Glen produces detailed structured scorecards for interview candidates (e.g. Stephen Bain rated 8/10) shared with client leadership. This adds tangible value beyond general hiring advice [source: slack_aris-dm_2026-05-25_hiring].

## Proposals That Won

**Separate workstreams for partial buy-in:** Goals was offered two workstreams; they took pricing only ($10K) not live service ($45K). The ability to buy a piece worked [source: goals_sow_proposal_2026-03-31].

**Frame around client urgency, not NBI capability:** Goals proposal anchored to 6-week pre-launch window and Sony platform submission deadline [source: goals_sow_proposal_2026-03-31].

**Evidence Table appendix:** Every non-obvious claim mapped to source, date, confidence, and gap. Multi-role red teaming embedded in the process [source: chatgpt_6907ec33].

**"Embed and Align" positioning:** Differentiates from report-only consultancies by showing NBI works inside client teams [source: goals_sow_proposal_2026-03-31].

## Delivery Patterns

**Red team validation (mandatory):** Research deliverables must pass red team before client delivery. Five scoring dimensions with claim accuracy weighted highest (30%). Original research scored 53/100, remediated to 93/100 over three passes [source: goals_red_team_report_2026-04-21].

**Contract flexibility over prescription:** Dino (CH General Counsel) created contract templates that were too prescriptive (milestones, deadlines, fees per milestone). Glen rejected them -- contracts need to be "more general and flexible" for early-stage hiring [source: slack_lorenza-dm_2026-05-25_contracts].

**Client-branded design systems:** NBI creates bespoke design systems for client dashboards (Lighthouse: magenta/blue/pink palette, dark-first, cinematic automotive theme) rather than using generic templates [source: lighthouse_design_system].

**EAD Framework:** NBI's proprietary process optimisation methodology applied in strict order: Eliminate (does this need to exist?), Automate (can a system do it?), Delegate (match to capability tier including AI agents). The key anti-pattern: "automating before eliminating" -- building sophistication around waste [source: nbi_ead_framework_2026-05-15].

**AI adoption for regulated environments:** Two-lane model separates regulated from non-regulated AI use with explicit merge controls, citation-first approach with Source Maps and Claim Ledgers [source: chatgpt_6972eb8d].

## Common Client Challenges

**Single-producer bottleneck:** At ~50 people, one producer cannot coordinate game content, platform work, backend services, build pipelines, playtests, partners, and vendors simultaneously [source: chatgpt_69034e5d].

**Production state misperception:** Teams commonly believe they are further along than they are. CH thought mid-production when actually in early production. Art being ahead of systems creates the "illusion of a full game" [source: granola_50612dd7].

**Chain-of-command bypass:** Art leads bypassing production chain to complain directly to CEO undermines production authority. Must be addressed directly by meeting with the bypassing parties [source: slack_production-council_2026-05-25_escalation].

**Salary expectation gaps:** Candidates told one thing in interviews, then see a different number in the offer letter (Charlie Todd: "it really was just a bit of a shock") [source: gmail_19dfdf4dd46d86f7].

**Solo HR overload:** Lorenza handling all hiring, onboarding, and contracts as solo HR person for a 55-person studio with 7+ concurrent pipelines [source: gmail_19e3b1c434f765ba].

**MTX strategy gap:** "No one on the client team had done MTX on console before" -- common gap NBI can address [source: chatgpt_68e3cfc8].

## What To Avoid

**Auto-calculated health states:** Glen refuses auto-fill of health state. Manual control over probability estimates and health assessments [source: handoff_2026-04-06a].

**Over-prescriptive contracts:** Lock in general terms, not specific milestones and fees per milestone at contract stage [source: slack_lorenza-dm_2026-05-25_contracts].

**Citing unverified metrics in proposals:** Goals SOW cited 50% D1 retention on PlayStation; actual data showed 34-36% [source: goals_sow_proposal_2026-03-31].

**Report-only consulting:** "Embed and Align" positioning works; pure report delivery does not differentiate [source: goals_sow_proposal_2026-03-31].

**Website UX failures:** First-5-seconds clarity test: "within 5 seconds, is it clear what the firm does, for whom, and why it is credible?" [source: chatgpt_6972eb79].
