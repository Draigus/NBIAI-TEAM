# Salary Benchmarks

## Executive Summary

This bank consolidates gaming industry compensation data from NBI client engagements, headcount planning exercises, and published benchmark sources. It covers C-level bands (UK and Greece), game development roles (UK/US/EU), contractor day rates, consultant fee benchmarks, employer-of-record cost multipliers, and NBI's salary audit methodology. The data is strongest for UK roles at mid-to-senior level in the 40-100 person studio bracket. US data is less granular; EU data is limited to Greece and general Western European benchmarks. All salary figures are 2025-2026 unless otherwise noted. Client identifiers anonymised.

## Fully Loaded Cost Multipliers

### UK Employer On-Costs

The total employer cost of a UK employee significantly exceeds base salary:

**Components:**
- Employer National Insurance: 15% of earnings above GBP 5,000 threshold (from April 2025)
- Minimum auto-enrolment pension: employer pays at least 3%, many schemes at 4-6%
- Benefits (healthcare, life, perks): 3-7% of salary as flat estimate

**Standard multipliers:**
- Conservative (NI + minimum pension): 1.25x-1.30x base salary
- Mid-range (NI + good pension + benefits): 1.4x-1.5x base salary
- Full loaded (NI + pension + benefits + overhead/equipment/software/HR): 1.5x-1.75x base salary
- NBI planning default: 1.6x for fully loaded cost

**Worked example (GBP 40,000 base):**
- Employer NI: GBP 5,250 (15% of GBP 35,000 above threshold)
- Employer pension (5%): GBP 2,000
- Employer benefits (5%): GBP 2,000
- Total per employee: GBP 49,250/year (1.23x base before overhead)

**Three-person data pod example:** Senior Game Data Analyst (GBP 65k) + Senior Data Engineer (GBP 80k) + Data Engineer (GBP 60k) = GBP 205k base, GBP 328k fully loaded at 1.6x. [source: chatgpt_691ef1eb, chatgpt_69160453]

## Production Cost Scaling

### Labour Cost Inflation (Global)

US game developer fully-loaded compensation trend:
- 2022: ~$95,000/year
- 2026: ~$115,000/year
- CAGR: ~5%/year

Senior engineers and technical artists saw steepest increases (~8%/year), driven by competition from big tech and remote work premium.

### Overall Cost CAGR

Game development costs compound at ~7.8% per year (2022-2026), driven by labour cost inflation AND team size growth (multiplicative). Global spend: ~$37B (2022) to ~$50B (2026). A project budgeted today for 2028 delivery should inflate costs by ~25% over a 3-year development cycle.

### Quick Estimation Formula

**Cost = (Hourly_Rate x 8) x (Team_Size x 20) x Months**

Worked example: ($40/hr x 8) x (10 people x 20 days) x 6 months = $384,000. [source: web_2026-06-02_production_cost_scaling_trends]

## Salary Audit Methodology

### Two-Track Approach

NBI's salary audit methodology operates on two parallel tracks:

**Track 1 -- Market Benchmarking:**
- Source salary data from minimum 3 reputable sources per role (Glassdoor, Morgan McKinley, Robert Half, Skillsearch, ITJobsWatch, PayScale)
- Break by seniority (junior/mid/senior/lead/director/VP/C-level)
- Break by geography (UK by region, US by timezone, EU by country)
- Four-quartile bands (Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4) for each role-seniority-geography combination
- Citation column per data point

**Track 2 -- Internal Equity Analysis:**
- Map current compensation against market bands
- Identify outliers (overpaid vs underpaid relative to market and internal peers)
- Flag compression (seniors paid close to mids, leads paid close to seniors)
- Assess title inflation (job title vs actual seniority/output)

**Deliverable:** Structured spreadsheet with one row per role, per seniority, per region, with source citations. Overview document covering methodology, key findings, and recommendations. [source: chatgpt_69205f6a, chatgpt_69206092]

### UK Gross-to-Net Reference

For planning purposes, UK employees see approximately:
- GBP 30,000 gross: ~GBP 24,500 net (18% effective deduction)
- GBP 50,000 gross: ~GBP 38,000 net (24% effective deduction)
- GBP 80,000 gross: ~GBP 57,000 net (29% effective deduction)
- GBP 120,000 gross: ~GBP 80,000 net (33% effective deduction)

These are approximations for tax year 2025-26 and vary with pension contributions, student loan status, and Scottish tax rates. [source: chatgpt_691ef1eb]
