## Executive Summary

The gaming industry generated $195.6B in 2025 (record high, projected $205B in 2026) while shedding 44,000+ jobs since 2022 -- the profit-layoff paradox deepens. Platform economics are being restructured: Google Play commissions dropped to 20% (from 30%), Fortnite returned to App Store globally, and Apple's 30% rate is increasingly isolated. Regulatory convergence is accelerating across US (FTC), UK (ASA), and EU (Digital Fairness Act). AI investment dominates: $1.8B into AI x Gaming over five years, with Morgan Stanley projecting $22B in annual cost savings. The funding market has bifurcated: AI-native companies and proven mobile performers attract capital; traditional studios face a dead zone.

## Platform Changes

### Google Play Commission Drop to 20%

Default commission dropped from 30% to 20% on IAP, with additional 5% only if using Google billing. Subscriptions dropped from 15% to 10%. New installs pay only 15% (vs 20% for existing). Rollout: EEA/UK/US by 30 June 2026, Australia by September 2026, Korea/Japan by December 2026, global by September 2027. For $1M revenue, $100K more stays in-house [source: web_2026-05-25b_google-epic-settlement-commission-drop].

### Fortnite Returns to App Store Globally

Epic returned Fortnite to Apple App Store in every country except Australia (where Epic won court case, awaits compliance order). Sweeney framed it as "the beginning of the end of the Apple Tax worldwide." Studios should model three scenarios: status quo (30%), negotiated (25%), regulatory-forced (20%) [source: web_2026-05-25b_fortnite-global-app-store-return].

### Steam AI Disclosure Policy

Two-tier system (January 2026): Tier 1 (development tools) -- no disclosure needed. Tier 2 (player-facing AI content) -- disclosure required, safeguards mandatory for runtime AI. 7,300+ games have disclosed AI content in ~14 months. Player reporting mechanism via Steam Overlay creates community enforcement [source: web_2026-05-25_steam-ai-disclosure-policy].

## Technology Shifts

### AI in Gaming

**Morgan Stanley (April 2026):** AI could create $22B annual profit opportunity by cutting development costs by "nearly half." Named beneficiaries: Tencent, Sony, Roblox, Take-Two, EA, Ubisoft. Warning: lower costs also lower barriers to entry, "potentially heightening competition." AI benefits accrue disproportionately to large publishers; mid-tier studios face strategic squeeze [source: web_2026-05-25b_morgan-stanley-ai-gaming-22b-report].

**AI applications driving savings:** Environment creation, dialogue generation, software testing, production timeline reduction [source: web_2026-05-25b_morgan-stanley-ai-gaming-22b-report].

**Astrocade model:** AI-native game creation (natural language prompts, no coding). $10M creator fund mirrors Roblox/Epic playbook. Advisory: Fei-Fei Li (Stanford) as CSO [source: web_2026-05-25_astrocade-56m-series-b].

## Regulatory

### US -- FTC Loot Box Enforcement

FTC secured $20M settlement with Cognosphere/Hoyoverse (Genshin Impact). Settlement bans selling loot boxes to under-16s without parental consent, requires odds disclosure, mandates age verification. FTC referred complaint to DOJ (escalation signal) [source: web_2026-05-25_ftc-genshin-lootbox-settlement].

### UK -- ASA Loot Box Advertising Enforcement

Active monitoring commenced 26 May 2026. Requires consumers to know before purchasing/downloading whether a game contains purchasable loot boxes. Disclosures must be "immediately visible without requiring users to expand additional text." Generic "offers in-app purchases" is explicitly insufficient [source: web_2026-05-25b_uk-asa-loot-box-enforcement].

### EU -- Digital Fairness Act

Expected Q4 2026. From 19 June 2026, distance contracts require a visible cancellation button. Loot box measures likely to include at minimum parental consent for minors. Total ban not expected but consumer protection focus is clear [source: web_2026-05-25_ftc-genshin-lootbox-settlement].

### Other Jurisdictions

- **Brazil:** New law bans loot box sales to minors (under 18) from March 2026
- **Germany:** Bundesrat pushing gambling classification (potential 18+ rating)
- **Australia:** Chance-based purchases now rated minimum 15+
- **Belgium/Netherlands:** Paid loot boxes classified as gambling, effectively banned

**Regulatory direction is one-way: tighter, not looser.** Studios should design for the strictest jurisdiction they ship to [source: web_2026-05-25_ftc-genshin-lootbox-settlement].

## Competitive Landscape

### Layoffs (Q2 2026)

May 2026: Metacore (up to 160), MercurySteam, 31st Union/2K, Iron Galaxy, Night Street Games. Cumulative since 2022: 44,000+ jobs globally. 28% of developers lost jobs over past two years; 33% in the US. Revenue-layoff paradox: EA cut Battlefield staff 5 months after BF6 sold 7M copies in 3 days [source: web_2026-05-25_gaming-layoffs-q2-paradox].

**Structural, not cyclical:** Studios optimise for profit margin, not headcount. Mid-tier studios (100-300 people) face highest restructuring risk. "Post-project layoff" remains dominant trigger [source: web_2026-05-25_gaming-layoffs-q2-paradox].

### Studio Models

**Five-studio model (Grand Games):** 75 employees in five autonomous internal studios. Distributes creative risk while centralising UA and analytics. Istanbul-based, following Dream Games ($2.75B) and Peak Games (Zynga $1.8B) [source: web_2026-05-25b_grand-games-70m-series-b].

### Community Sentiment Patterns

Anti-P2W sentiment is the community's hardest red line. PlayGOALS beta: NPS +24.6 (strong for beta), defensive AI lowest metric (2.54/5, 50.9% negative), 5v5 ranked demand overwhelming across all channels [source: goals_beta_community_sentiment].
