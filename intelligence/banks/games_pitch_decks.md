## Executive Summary

This bank captures NBI's methodology for building, reviewing, and auditing game studio pitch decks, data rooms, and investor materials. It contains a complete 16-section data room checklist applicable to Seed through Series C, a reusable investor connection framework with named funds and check sizes, a detailed pitch deck teaching manual for F2P mobile founders, an annotated Playsage seed deck, a seven-role data room audit methodology, and five new external exemplars including an LP-facing deck from a16z ($660M), Homa's 13-slide $50M raise, The Games Fund's published VC template, Voodoo's unicorn fundraising trajectory, and a brutally honest failure post-mortem from Little Polygon. The material is directly applicable to NBI's game investment advisory (/gi) service offering. Strongest assets: the data room checklist, audit methodology, and VC-published pitch template are production-ready client deliverables.

## What Makes a Game Pitch Deck Work

Across all extracts, successful game pitch decks share these structural elements:

1. **Evidence over assertion.** Every claim needs a source or data point. Data rooms must distinguish "verified-within-data-room", "internally-inconsistent", and "unverifiable" claims. Ambiguous KPI definitions (what counts as MAU, how retention is measured) are a common cross-document consistency failure. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

2. **Process over single hit.** Investors fund the machine, not the game. Voodoo, Homa, and Grand Games all raised by demonstrating systematised hit-generation: weekly prototyping, data-driven kill/scale decisions, autonomous studio structures. A single promising title without a repeatable process is underfundable at scale. [source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy, web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck]

3. **Scenario-based financials.** Single-case models are rejected. Investors expect Base/Upside/Downside scenarios with sensitivity grids on key assumptions (WACC vs terminal growth, multiple vs revenue). [source: chatgpt_690c8b4f, chatgpt_6908ac7d]

4. **Games-specific round definitions.** Generic startup stage definitions fail in gaming. Seed = team, thesis, prototype, prove fun. Series A = product-market fit, soft launch metrics trending. Series B = scale what works, predictable KPIs. Series C = institutional scaling, M&A readiness. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

5. **Platform-specific traction evidence.** Premium games show wishlists and demo conversion. F2P shows CPI, retention (D1/D7/D30), ARPDAU, LTV, and ROAS. These are separate data room sections, not combined. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

6. **Multi-role red-teaming.** Pitch materials benefit from review by multiple functional perspectives (VC partner, monetisation lead, UA lead, tech lead, finance lead, legal lead) before investor exposure. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

7. **Concise decks outperform.** Homa raised $50M with 13 slides. The Games Fund template is similarly tight. Little Polygon's 30-slide deck (which took 2+ months to build) failed across 50+ publisher pitches. Every successful exemplar in this bank is 10-15 slides. [source: web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck, web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem]

8. **Industry-moment framing for generalists.** a16z's LP deck frames gaming as experiencing a "Marvel Moment" (cultural crossover: Last of Us TV, Super Mario Movie, Hogwarts Legacy) and as "the killer app for new technologies." This narrative architecture transfers from LP to studio pitches when targeting non-gaming investors. [source: web_2026-05-25_a16z-games-fund-one-deck]

## By Platform

### Mobile (F2P, Hypercasual, Mid-core)

The NBI pitch deck teaching manual is specifically scoped to F2P mobile games with IAP monetisation and optional respectful ads. Explicitly excludes crypto, tokens, heavy loot box, and grey-area gambling patterns. Multi-role coaching panel (CEO, CTO, CPO, CFO, UA lead, generalist VC, specialist games fund) sanity-checks every section. Designed as a reusable client resource strong enough to use directly with founders as teaching material. [source: chatgpt_691f1230]

Key metrics investors expect for mobile F2P: CPI by channel, D1/D7/D30 retention, ARPDAU, LTV, ROAS, payer conversion rate. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

**Hypercasual benchmarks (Voodoo standard):** D1 retention >55%, D7 >22%, CPI <$0.25. These are aggressive thresholds -- Voodoo used them as kill/continue gates for weekly prototypes. Most studios won't hit them, but knowing the bar matters for investor conversations. [source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy]

**Platform positioning:** Homa and Voodoo both succeeded by pitching the platform/process, not individual games. Homa positioned as a "game technology lab" (data analytics + no-code + creative testing). Voodoo positioned as a systematised flywheel (more hits attract more developers attract more data attract more hits). Both sold the infrastructure for generating hits, not the hits themselves. [source: web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck, web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy]

### PC/Console Indie (Premium, Early Access)

Traction evidence for premium games centres on wishlist counts, demo conversion rates, and Steam Next Fest performance. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

**The indie funding gap is documented and real.** Little Polygon pitched Nightshift Galaxy (mecha combat, Unreal Engine 4, PC/console) to 50+ publishers across 2024. 15 never responded. 35 gave positive game feedback but cited poor financial outlook. Original ask: $3M for a 6-person team over 2.5 years, revised to ~$2M. Zero term sheets. Publishers reported budgets previously funding $5M deals now capped at $500K-$1.5M. The $500K-$3M range is a dead zone. [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem]

**Structural bifurcation:** The indie market has split into micro-budget (<$500K, often self-funded or crowdfunded) and large-budget (>$5M, publisher-backed with proven teams). Mid-range indie ($1-3M) faces the hardest fundraising environment. This is the range where NBI clients are most likely to need advisory. [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem]

### Live Service / GaaS (Any Platform)

For live service games, the GTM strategy becomes a critical deck component. The Sarge GTM plan provides a template: week-by-week timeline from Week -8 to Week +12, with specific budget allocations (trailer GBP5k, key art GBP3k, influencer programme GBP20k fees + GBP5k co-marketing, launch week performance marketing ~GBP100k). Target metrics: CPI under USD2, 100k+ first-week players, 1M+ impressions. [source: chatgpt_685bfb00]

## By Stage

### Pre-Seed / Concept

No specific pre-seed deck template exists yet. Data room at this stage is minimal: team, thesis, concept document, competitive landscape. [See Open Questions]

### Seed / Pre-Production

**Playsage example (GBP2.5M, 24-month runway):** 14-slide structure: Title, Problem (3 pain points), Opportunity/Timing, Solution (Evidence Layer concept), Product Modules (6 modules named), Differentiation, Data and Tech Moat, Market Context (global games ~USD188.8B, game analytics ~USD2.21B), Buyer and JTBD (4 personas), Competitive Landscape, Business Model (SaaS tiers + data packs), Traction/Roadmap (Alpha > Private Beta > Public Beta > GA), GTM (founder-led), Team, The Ask. Key differentiation rested on evidence layer (source attribution, confidence scoring) vs competitors' static reports. [source: chatgpt_68e8a242]

At seed, data rooms need: one-pager, cap table, team bios tied to shipped titles, genre thesis, prototype or playable, competitive landscape. Lightweight but professional. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

### Series A / Production

At Series A, the data room expands significantly. Required sections: full cap table with option pool, hiring plan, traction split by monetisation model, tech stack with CI/CD and code ownership, platform compliance status (TRC/XR), financial forecasts with UA payback periods, legal (IP chain, middleware licences, GDPR/COPPA). [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

**Deck expansion at Series A:** Homa's progression from 13-slide ($50M Series A) to 19-slide ($100M Series B) suggests decks expand as traction data accumulates. More slides at later stages means more evidence, not more fluff. [source: web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck]

### Late-Stage / Live Service

Series B requires predictable KPIs and evidence of scalability. Series C is institutional scaling and M&A readiness with full normalised financials and audit trails. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

## Exemplars

### a16z Games Fund One LP Deck ($660M Raise)

- **Type:** LP-facing (VC pitching to its own investors), not studio-to-VC
- **Structure:** Industry framing ("Marvel Moment" + "killer app for new tech" thesis) > historical precedent > market evolution > operating team differentiation > portfolio signals
- **What works:** Named operator hires from tier-1 studios (Supercell, YouTube, Riot) as differentiation; cultural crossover framing for generalist LPs; thesis-to-portfolio proof (80% of 2023 investments had major AI components)
- **Key insight:** a16z entered with game studios + Web3 + infrastructure thesis; within one year pivoted to 80% AI x Games -- shows how fast VC thesis evolves
- **Applicability:** Narrative architecture (industry moment + team unfair advantage + early signals) transfers directly from LP to studio pitches targeting generalist investors
[source: web_2026-05-25_a16z-games-fund-one-deck]

### Homa Games ($50M Series A, Mobile Hypercasual)

- **Structure:** 13 slides, no padding
- **What works:** Pitched the platform/process, not individual games ("game technology lab" combining creativity, data analytics, no-code tools); 1 billion+ downloads as execution proof
- **Key differentiator:** Technology-enabled publishing platform for indie developers, not a traditional studio
- **Progression:** 13-slide ($50M Series A) to 19-slide ($100M Series B, paywalled via Business Insider)
- **Investors:** Northzone (lead), Idinvest Partners, E.ventures ($15M seed)
- **Caveat:** Raised during 2021 mobile gaming peak; 2024-2026 conditions are materially different (lower valuations, higher proof bars)
- **Applicability:** Direct template for clients building mobile game studios or publishing platforms; parallels with Playsage positioning
[source: web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck]

### Voodoo (EUR280K to $1.4B Unicorn, Hypercasual)

- **Structure:** Category-creator narrative with systematic evidence
- **What works:** Proved process over single hit; flywheel thesis (more hits > more developers > more data > more hits); concrete kill/continue gates (D1 >55%, D7 >22%, CPI <$0.25)
- **Key differentiator:** Created the hypercasual category; at 2019 peak: EUR360M revenue, 300M MAU, 3.7B downloads, 100+ games, profitable
- **Revenue model:** 67% publishing third-party, 33% internal
- **Notable:** Goldman Sachs invested $200M when all European VCs passed -- cautionary tale about VC herd mentality
- **Post-2020 headwinds:** IDFA tracking restrictions, revenue share compression (20% to 10%), zero consumer brand equity
- **Applicability:** Template for how gaming VCs benchmark mobile studios; success metrics usable as pitch appendix benchmarks
[source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy]

### The Games Fund: VC-Published Pitch Template

- **Type:** Published by a VC (#1 early-stage gaming investor 2024, #4 all stages)
- **Access:** Free Google Slides template at https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1xoFUWgW9msRkaWZZZVThccnE_35Gt1ltkn2HfyuJRt8/edit
- **Companion tools:** P&L example spreadsheet + company budget calculator (team size, dev costs, hiring, fundraising)
- **What it signals:** Literally what one of the top gaming VCs wants to see
- **Recommended resources (reveals VC values):** Venture Deals (VC mechanics), Hitmakers (audience engagement), Creativity Inc. (creative process)
- **Fund positioning:** "Bridge between international companies and local developers" -- favours studios outside traditional hubs
- **Action:** Download and archive template, P&L, and budget calculator for NBI's consulting toolkit
[source: web_2026-05-25_games-fund-vc-pitch-template]

### Playsage Seed Deck (Data Platform, GBP2.5M)

- **Structure:** 14 slides, problem-solution-market-ask flow
- **What works:** Four distinct buyer personas with JTBD; evidence layer as core differentiator; specific market sizing (USD188.8B gaming, USD2.21B analytics)
- **Key differentiator:** "Operating system for game decisions" positioning vs point-tool competitors
- **Applicability:** Any B2B SaaS seed deck targeting game industry buyers
[source: chatgpt_68e8a242]

### Sarge Universe Data Room (Telegram F2P, GBP10M)

- **Structure:** Valuation workbook (6 tabs) + headcount plan + P&L + GTM strategy
- **What works:** Three-scenario valuation with sensitivity grids; phased headcount tied to development milestones; go/no-go gates anchored to retention metrics, not dates
- **Key differentiator:** Formula-driven workbook where all assumptions are explicit blue-cell inputs
- **Applicability:** Any F2P mobile game fundraising at Series A scale
[source: chatgpt_690c8b4f, chatgpt_690dcbec, chatgpt_691f13cd]

### Little Polygon: Failure Post-Mortem (50+ Pitches, Zero Deals)

- **Structure:** 30 slides covering elevator pitch, team, features, comps, ask (too many slides)
- **Game:** Nightshift Galaxy (mecha combat + arcade flight, UE4, PC/console)
- **Ask:** $3M revised to $2M for 6-person team
- **Result:** 50+ publisher submissions, ~24 in-person pitches at DICE/GDC, 15 non-responses, 35 positive-on-game/negative-on-market -- zero term sheets
- **Out-of-pocket cost:** ~$10K (demo, legal, conferences, travel)
- **Key finding:** "Nobody is investing in mid-range indie games." Positive game feedback + zero funding = game quality is no longer the bottleneck
- **Applicability:** Critical reality check for any NBI client planning $1-3M fundraise; counsel clients to position either <$500K or >$5M
[source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem]

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Valuing on gross receipts while collecting net.** Platform fees (Apple 30%, Google now 20%, Telegram varies) must be netted before revenue is stated. This is a common red flag that triggers instant investor distrust. Note: Google Play's drop from 30% to 20% (June 2026 settlement) changes the net calculation for Android. [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d]

2. **Cherry-picked comps.** Comparables must show range and quartile position, never a single favourable comparator. [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d]

3. **Ambiguous KPI definitions.** If "MAU" is defined differently in the deck vs the financial model vs the data room, investors will catch it. Single definitions enforced across all documents. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

4. **Missing IP ownership chain.** IP assignments from contractors, middleware licence terms, and open-source audit are frequently absent from game studio data rooms. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

5. **Forward revenue without showing drivers.** Revenue projections must trace to visible inputs (retention curves, CPI, ARPDAU) not be presented as freestanding numbers. [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d]

6. **Single-case financial models.** No scenario analysis = no investor confidence. Minimum three scenarios (Base/Upside/Downside) with percentage modifiers, not separate models. [source: chatgpt_690c8b4f]

7. **Oversized decks.** 30 slides is too many. Successful raises in this bank use 10-15 slides. Long decks signal either lack of focus or inability to prioritise. The 2+ months Little Polygon spent building their 30-slide deck would have been better spent on 13 tight slides. [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem, web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck]

8. **VC funding for non-VC businesses.** Midsummer Studios ($6M VC raise, closed without shipping) illustrates the structural problem: game studios rarely produce the 10x returns VCs require. Jake Solomon himself noted that exit events (acquisitions/IPOs) are rare for game studios. Most studios should seek publisher funding or revenue-based financing rather than equity VC. [source: web_2026-05-25_midsummer-studios-closure]

9. **Ignoring post-IDFA reality.** Any mobile deck that doesn't address iOS 14 IDFA restrictions, ad revenue compression, and the resulting shift to first-party data is dated. Voodoo's post-2020 challenges (revenue share compression from 20% to 10%, zero brand equity) are the headwinds every mobile pitch must now address. [source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy]

## Data Room Structure

The NBI standard data room has 16 sections [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]:

1. Company overview (one-pager, runway)
2. Cap table (option pool, prior financing)
3. Team (bios tied to shipped titles, hiring plan)
4. Market and strategy (genre thesis)
5. Portfolio and pipeline (gate criteria)
6. Game design docs, economy docs, art bibles
7. Tech (engine, CI/CD, code ownership, open-source audit)
8. Platform (dev kit status, TRC/XR compliance, store strategy)
9. Traction -- split: Premium (wishlists, demo) vs F2P (CPI, retention, ARPDAU, LTV, ROAS)
10. Monetisation and economy
11. Financials (forecasts, UA payback)
12. Legal (IP chain, middleware, GDPR/COPPA)
13. Contracts and vendors
14. Operations and policies
15. References
16. Data room hygiene (index, versioning)

### Data Room Audit Methodology

Seven-role investor committee simulation: Partner (Games VC), Mobile F2P Monetisation Lead, UA and Growth Lead, Product and Live Ops Lead, Tech and Platform Lead, Finance Lead, Legal and Compliance Lead. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

Step 0: Create Data Room Index (every file with type, date, purpose, missing artefacts). Step 1: Document-by-document line audit labelling each claim as Fact/Assumption/Projection/Opinion, recording every numeric claim with value/unit/timeframe/definition/source/reconciliation status. Step 2: Cross-Document Reconciliation Matrix checking metrics across files. Each document gets section-level scores on clarity/completeness, numeric integrity, assumption realism, evidence strength, and investor relevance plus top 5 risks, top 5 fixes, and "diligence traps" that trigger investor distrust. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

### Valuation Model Structure

12-tab workbook: Summary, Assumptions (named ranges with source/date), Historical Financials (LTM/TTM), Normalisations (add-backs), Forecast (driver-based 36-month), DCF (unlevered FCF, WACC, terminal value, sensitivity), Multiples (public + transaction comps), EV to Equity Bridge (net debt, preferred liquidation, TSM dilution), KPIs and Cohorts, Evidence/Sources, Checks (balance + audit block), Change Log. Display standards: inputs blue, outputs black, links green, checks red until passing. [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d]

### VC-Published Financial Tools

The Games Fund provides free companion tools alongside their pitch template: a P&L example spreadsheet for due diligence and a company budget calculator for estimating team size, development costs, hiring plans, and fundraising requirements. These should be archived and offered to NBI clients as starting points. [source: web_2026-05-25_games-fund-vc-pitch-template]

## Fundraising Climate (2024-2026)

The current fundraising environment is characterised by bifurcation and selectivity:

**What gets funded:** Studios with proven metrics (chart performance, retention data, revenue visibility) raise at aggressive valuations. Grand Games ($70M Series B, 6x valuation jump in 12 months) and Homa ($50M Series A on 1B+ downloads) demonstrate that execution evidence unlocks capital. Process-over-product narratives dominate: investors fund the hit-generation machine, not individual games. [source: web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck, web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy]

**What doesn't get funded:** Mid-range indie games ($500K-$3M) are in a dead zone. Studios with ambitious creative visions but no retention data, no systematic process, and no path to VC-scale returns face near-zero conversion rates. 50+ pitches yielding zero deals is documented reality. [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem]

**VC as wrong instrument:** VC funding structurally mismatches most game development. Exit events are rare, 10x returns are unlikely, and the pressure to build "transformational" products (not merely great games) distorts creative and business decisions. Most studios should consider publisher deals, revenue-based financing, or bootstrapping. [source: web_2026-05-25_midsummer-studios-closure]

**Conference pitching still works but converts poorly.** DICE and GDC generate the most meetings. Conversion rates are extremely low. Out-of-pocket pitching costs (~$10K before any development funding) are a real barrier for indie studios. [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem]

## Open Questions

- **Pre-seed deck template.** No structured template exists for very early stage (concept only, no prototype). What is the minimum viable deck at pre-seed?
- **Publisher pitch vs VC pitch.** All current material is VC-focused. How do publisher pitch requirements differ? Little Polygon's post-mortem mentions publisher pitches but doesn't detail structural differences.
- **Console premium pitch examples.** Current exemplars are F2P mobile, SaaS, and LP-facing. Need annotated examples of successful premium PC/console indie pitches.
- **Investor database freshness.** Fund data is from mid-2025. Investment preferences, fund sizes, and active partners may have changed. a16z's 80% pivot to AI x Games illustrates how fast thesis evolves.
- **Post-deck follow-up process.** How to structure the follow-up sequence after initial investor meetings. No methodology exists yet.
- **Apple commission trajectory.** Google dropped to 20%; Apple still at 30% but under global pressure. Revenue modelling in pitch decks should scenario-plan Apple commission reductions.
