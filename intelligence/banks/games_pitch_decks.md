## Executive Summary

This bank covers the full spectrum of game studio fundraising: exemplar deck analysis (a16z $660M LP deck, Homa $50M Series A, Voodoo's unicorn trajectory), reusable NBI frameworks (The Games Fund VC-published template, NBI's F2P mobile teaching guide, 16-section data room checklist, seven-role audit methodology), and active Telegram-native fundraising intelligence. The dominant investor thesis across all sources is "process over single hit" — investors fund the machine, not individual games. The funding market has bifurcated sharply: studios with proven metrics raise at premium; the $500K–$3M range is a documented dead zone. Telegram mini-app economics represent a genuine structural edge for early-stage pitches: CPI 7–12 cents vs $2+ on iOS/Android with no 30% platform overhead. Data room quality is as important as the deck itself — ambiguous KPIs and missing IP chain documentation are the most common investor disqualifiers.

## What Makes a Game Pitch Deck Work

**Process over single hit.** Voodoo, Homa, and Play Ventures all converge on this principle. Investors fund the systematised hit-generation machine, not individual games. Voodoo's pitch to Goldman Sachs was the process: weekly prototypes, data-driven kill/scale decisions, a flywheel of hits attracting developers attracting data [source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy]. Homa raised $50M by positioning as a "game technology lab" rather than a game studio [source: web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck].

**Industry framing + team unfair advantage.** a16z's $660M LP deck positioned games as experiencing a "Marvel Moment" (Last of Us TV, Super Mario Movie, Hogwarts Legacy), then grounded the thesis historically ("games are often the killer app for new technologies") before proving execution via named hires from Supercell, YouTube, and Riot [source: web_2026-05-25_a16z-games-fund-one-deck]. This three-part structure — moment, thesis, team — transfers directly from LP-facing to studio-facing decks.

**Less is more.** Homa raised $50M with 13 slides. Their progression to 19 slides for the $100M Series B shows decks expand as traction data accumulates, not as aspiration grows [source: web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck]. Little Polygon built a 30-slide deck over 2+ months and raised nothing — the over-engineering itself signals founder inexperience [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem].

**Concrete success metrics.** Voodoo's investor bar: D1 retention >55%, D7 >22%, CPI <$0.25. These are aggressive — most studios will not hit them — but knowing the bar matters when building the deck's traction section [source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy].

**Narrative arc is the structure.** NBI's F2P mobile teaching guide defines the arc: open with the problem the player has (not the game's features), establish the market opportunity, show the solution with proof, demonstrate the team can execute, close with the ask and use of funds. Every slide earns its place by advancing this story [source: chatgpt_691f1230].

**Start from the VC's own template.** The Games Fund (ranked #1 early-stage gaming VC in 2024) publishes a free Google Slides pitch deck template representing exactly what they want to see. Adapt from this rather than building from scratch. Complementary P&L and budget calculator tools are also publicly available [source: web_2026-05-25_games-fund-vc-pitch-template].

## By Platform

### Mobile (F2P, Hypercasual, Mid-core)

**Telegram-specific economics.** CPI 7–12 cents on Telegram vs $2+ on iOS/Android. No 30% platform store overhead — a material margin advantage that should be explicit in the deck's financial model. The competitive moat is timing: Telegram mini-apps are in their early-mover window. For the pitch narrative, this framing is more powerful than the game design itself [source: granola_beef5f26].

**Hypercasual benchmark: Voodoo.** At 2019 peak: EUR 360M revenue, 300M MAU, 3.7B total downloads, 100+ games, 250 employees, profitable. Revenue split: ~67% publishing third-party games, ~33% internal. Raised $200M from Goldman Sachs when all European VCs passed; subsequently sold ~20–25% stake to Tencent at $1.4B valuation. Post-2020 headwinds — iOS 14 IDFA tracking restrictions, revenue share compression (20% to 10%), zero consumer brand — must be addressed in any deck pitching a similar model today [source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy].

**Mobile platform deck (The Games Fund template).** The publicly accessible Google Slides template from The Games Fund covers all sections investors need. Supplemented by a P&L example and budget calculator for financial planning. Template URL: https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1xoFUWgW9msRkaWZZZVThccnE_35Gt1ltkn2HfyuJRt8/edit [source: web_2026-05-25_games-fund-vc-pitch-template].

**F2P Telegram fundraising assumptions (anonymised).** For a strategy game raising GBP 10M: 200M TAM on Telegram, USD 4 ARPU, USD 0.08 CPI, 200k starting MAU, 300k Month 1 installs, 5% monthly install growth, 25% monthly churn, 24% DAU/MAU ratio, 5.5% payer conversion, GBP 8 ARPPU, GBP 4.99 battle pass at 20% attach. Go/no-go gates should anchor hiring waves to D1/D7/D30 retention and payer conversion milestones. Financial model uses 1.3x loaded factor on UK salaries [source: chatgpt_690dcbec, chatgpt_690c8b4f].

**Hybrid-casual pivot.** Voodoo's response to post-2020 market correction: genre diversification to hybrid-casual, Tencent partnership for Asian distribution. Any deck for a hypercasual studio must address iOS 14 IDFA headwinds and show a hybrid-casual or hybrid-monetisation roadmap [source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy].

### PC/Console Indie (Premium, Early Access)

**The funding dead zone.** $500K–$3M is the hardest range to fund in the current market. Little Polygon submitted to 50+ publishers, gave the pitch ~24 times at DICE and GDC, 15 publishers never responded, 35 responded positively on the game but cited poor financial outlooks. Original ask: $3M; revised to ~$2M after market feedback. Zero term sheets. Publishers reported that places previously funding $5M deals now cap at $500K–$1.5M [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem].

**Deck size and cost.** Little Polygon's 30-slide deck took 2+ months to build and cost ~$10K out-of-pocket (demo, legal, conferences, travel). Most successful decks are 10–15 slides. Conference pitching (GDC, DICE) still generates meetings but has extremely low conversion rates [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem].

**Market bifurcation reality.** "Nobody is investing in mid-range indie games" (Little Polygon, 2024). The market has split into micro-budget ($0–$500K self-funded or grant-backed) and large-budget ($5M+, proven team with prior exits). Studios in the $1–3M range face the hardest fundraising environment. NBI should counsel clients to position either below $500K or above $5M [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem].

### Live Service / GaaS (Any Platform)

**GaaS deck requirements.** Live service pitches require metrics evidence at pitch time: retention curves (D1/D7/D30/D90), ARPDAU trend, payer conversion, LTV vs CPI, ROAS by channel. The deck's traction section must distinguish soft-launch metrics from full-launch projections — investors have seen too many decks conflate the two [source: chatgpt_68ee2760].

**AI-native investment signal.** a16z's Games Fund One shifted from Web3 to AI within one year: 80% of 2023 investments featured major AI components. Any GaaS studio pitching now should address AI in tools, content generation, or live ops automation — not as a feature, but as an operational cost and speed advantage [source: web_2026-05-25_a16z-games-fund-one-deck].

## By Stage

### Pre-Seed / Concept

Team, thesis, prototype, prove fun. The deck must answer three questions: who are you (unfair advantage), what is the market (why this problem matters), and what have you built (evidence you can execute). Financial model is a placeholder at this stage — investors are buying the team and the bet [source: chatgpt_68ee2760].

### Seed / Pre-Production

Prototype with early retention signal. Financial model should be three-scenario (base/upside/downside) with driver-based assumptions, not a single-case spreadsheet. Playsage seed deck structure (14 slides): Title, Problem, Opportunity/Timing, Solution, Product Modules, Differentiation, Data/Tech Moat, Market Context, Buyer/JTBD, Competitive Landscape, Business Model, Traction/Roadmap, GTM, Team, The Ask. Playsage seed ask: GBP 2.5M for 24-month runway [source: chatgpt_68e8a242].

For F2P Telegram games (anonymised): GBP 10M raise structured with staged headcount across three 6-month phases. Phase 1 focused on core engineering and leadership; Phase 2 brings full team online; Phase 3 scales community, support, and content. 26 internal roles total. Key UK salary benchmarks (with 1.3x loaded factor): CEO/Game Producer GBP 120k, CTO GBP 110k, COO GBP 95k, Head of Product GBP 90k, Lead Backend Engineer GBP 80k, DevOps GBP 75k, Economy Designer GBP 65k, QA Lead GBP 55k [source: chatgpt_691f13cd].

### Series A / Production

Proven traction data required. Homa raised $50M with 1 billion+ downloads as evidence. The deck evolves from 13 to ~19 slides as evidence accumulates. Round definition (games-specific): product-market fit demonstrated, soft-launch metrics trending positively. Metrics investors need: D1/D7/D30 retention, CPI, ARPDAU, LTV, ROAS [source: web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck, chatgpt_68ee2760].

### Late-Stage / Live Service

Institutional scaling — deck becomes a business performance document with auditable KPIs, M&A readiness signals, and platform diversification evidence. Round definitions shift from product to unit economics: predictable KPIs, scalable UA, sustainable ROAS [source: chatgpt_68ee2760].

## Exemplars

### a16z Games Fund One — $660M LP Raise (2022)

**Platform/Stage:** LP-facing (VC pitching to its own investors). **What made it work:** Three-layer structure — industry moment framing ("Marvel Moment"), historical thesis ("games as killer app for new tech"), named operators from tier-1 studios (Supercell, YouTube, Riot). **Key differentiator:** Publicly shared by Andrew Chen; unusual transparency makes it the best available window into how institutional capital thinks about gaming. **Applicability:** The industry-framing approach transfers from LP-level to studio-level pitches. The team slide approach (named operators from elite studios) is directly reusable [source: web_2026-05-25_a16z-games-fund-one-deck].

### Homa Games — $50M Series A (2021, Northzone Lead)

**Platform/Stage:** Mobile hypercasual, Series A. **Amount raised:** $50M Series A, $100M Series B, $150M+ total. **What made it work:** 13-slide deck; pitched the platform/process ("game technology lab"), not individual games. Technology-driven development (data analytics, no-code tools, creative testing) was the differentiator vs traditional publishers. **Key differentiator:** Process/platform positioning at scale; total downloads >1 billion at pitch time. **Applicability:** Template for mobile studio/publishing platform pitches. The "technology lab" positioning is proven with institutional capital. Caveat: raised during 2021 mobile peak; 2024–2026 conditions require higher proof bars [source: web_2026-05-25_homa-games-series-a-deck].

### Voodoo — $200M Goldman Sachs, $1.4B Tencent Valuation

**Platform/Stage:** Mobile hypercasual, growth. **What made it work:** Category creator with defensible ecosystem; systematised hit-generation process; flywheel. All European VCs passed — Goldman Sachs contrarian bet on the machine not the games. **Key differentiator:** EUR 360M revenue, 300M MAU, 67% publishing revenue (proving the ecosystem, not just internal games). **Anti-pattern signal:** Zero consumer brand recognition, post-2020 IDFA vulnerability, revenue share compression. Any pitch following the Voodoo model must address these headwinds explicitly [source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy].

### Playsage — GBP 2.5M Seed (Active, NBI)

**Platform/Stage:** B2B SaaS (game analytics/intelligence), Seed. **Positioning:** "The Operating System for Game Decisions." **Deck structure:** 14 slides. Four buyer personas: COO, Executive Producer, CFO, Investor — each with distinct jobs-to-be-done. Differentiation anchored on evidence layer (source attribution, confidence scoring) vs competitors' static reports. Competitors framed as point tools and static analysts vs integrated platform [source: chatgpt_68e8a242].

### Sarge Universe — USD 1–2M Target (Active, Telegram)

**Platform/Stage:** Telegram strategy game (Clash of Clans mechanics + 4X gameplay), pre-seed/seed. **What makes it work:** Timing thesis (first-mover on Telegram mini-apps), structural CPI advantage (7–12 cents), no platform overhead, multiple smaller investors preferred over single large cheque. Materials span full investment lifecycle: pitch decks, AERM forecast model, GDD, legal docs, VDR checklist, 1,350-entry investor list. GDC dinner investor contacts include SpyFox (David/Riley Ettery), RMT (Jackson), Behold (Binnie). Post-funding art partner: Room8 Studios (offices in Cyprus/Poland/Spain/Romania) [source: granola_beef5f26].

## Investor Expectations by Type

### Gaming-Specialist VCs

The Games Fund (ranked #1 early-stage, 2024), LVP (UK, GBP 500k–2M, warm introductions required), Play Ventures (Singapore, mobile F2P), BITKRAFT (US, seed-B, gaming/esports). Expect: game-specific metrics (D1/D7/D30 retention, CPI, ARPDAU, LTV, ROAS), games-specific round definitions, IP ownership chain, platform status (dev kit, TRC compliance), and demonstrated understanding of genre economics. NBI's F2P guide uses a six-role coaching panel (CEO, CTO, CPO, CFO, UA lead, generalist VC, specialist games fund) to sanity-check every section [source: chatgpt_68ee2760, chatgpt_691f1230, web_2026-05-25_games-fund-vc-pitch-template].

### Generalist VCs

a16z (US, growth), Accel (US), Goldman Sachs (growth equity). Need industry framing (why games, why now), cultural crossover argument, and team with named operators from elite studios. The "Marvel Moment" framing — games as entertainment's dominant cultural force — is the entry point for generalist capital. Once the category is established, metrics matter but team pedigree is the primary signal [source: web_2026-05-25_a16z-games-fund-one-deck, web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy].

## Data Room Essentials

A strong pitch deck without a clean data room fails at due diligence. The data room is the pitch deck's evidence base, and investors expect both to be consistent.

**16-section structure** [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]:
1. Company overview with one-pager and runway
2. Cap table with option pool and prior financing
3. Team bios tied to shipped titles plus hiring plan
4. Market and strategy with genre thesis
5. Portfolio and pipeline with gate criteria
6. Game design docs, economy docs, art bibles
7. Tech: engine choice, CI/CD, code ownership, open-source audit
8. Platform: dev kit status, TRC/XR compliance, store strategy
9. Traction — split by model: Premium (wishlists, demo metrics) vs F2P (CPI, retention D1/D7/D30, ARPDAU, LTV, ROAS)
10. Monetisation and economy
11. Financials: forecasts and UA payback period
12. Legal: IP chain, middleware licences, GDPR/COPPA
13. Contracts and vendors
14. Operations and policies
15. References
16. Data room hygiene: index, versioning, access log

**Valuation model structure (12-tab workbook):** Summary (base/downside/upside), Assumptions (named ranges, source, last-updated), Historical Financials, Normalisations, Forecast (driver-based 36-month), DCF (unlevered FCF, WACC, terminal value, sensitivity tables), Multiples (public and transaction comps), EV to Equity Bridge, KPIs/Cohorts, Evidence/Sources, Checks, Change Log. Display convention: inputs blue, outputs black, links green, checks red until passing [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d].

**Data room audit methodology.** Seven-role investor committee simulation (Games VC Partner, Mobile F2P Monetisation Lead, UA/Growth Lead, Product/Live Ops Lead, Tech/Platform Lead, Finance Lead, Legal/Compliance Lead). Step 0: create index listing every file with type, date, purpose, missing standard artifacts. Step 1: label every claim as Fact/Assumption/Projection/Opinion. Step 2: cross-document reconciliation matrix checking same metrics across files. Section scorecards: clarity/completeness, numeric integrity, assumption realism, evidence strength, investor relevance. "Diligence traps" (things that trigger fast investor distrust) called out separately from general issues [source: chatgpt_6964dcca].

**Common red flags that trigger investor distrust:**
- IP ownership chain absent or incomplete
- Middleware licences undocumented
- Contractor IP assignments missing
- Ambiguous KPI definitions (what counts as MAU, how retention is measured) — a common cross-document consistency failure
- Valuing on gross receipts while the business collects net
- Forward revenue without showing drivers
- Cherry-picked comp multiples (must show range and quartile position)
[source: chatgpt_68ee2760, chatgpt_6908ac7d, chatgpt_6964dcca]

## Anti-Patterns

**The funding dead zone.** $500K–$3M is extremely difficult to raise in 2024–2026. Little Polygon's documented experience: 50+ publisher pitches, 24 conference pitches, zero term sheets [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem].

**Over-engineering the deck.** 30 slides took 2+ months to build and raised nothing. Successful decks at the same stage are 10–15 slides. Deck complexity signals founder inability to prioritise [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem].

**Positive reception does not mean fundable.** Little Polygon received uniformly positive game feedback from 35 publishers while receiving zero offers. Game quality is no longer the bottleneck — business model, market timing, and genre selection matter more than creative excellence in the current climate [source: web_2026-05-25_little-polygon-pitching-postmortem].

**Single-hit studio risk.** Studios without a portfolio or systematised development process face existential risk if their lead title fails. Investors have seen too many single-hit dependency failures [source: web_2026-05-25_voodoo-hypercasual-fundraising-strategy].

**VC misalignment for most game studios.** VC demands "transformational" exit events, but game studio acquisition events (the primary exit route) are rare and often modest. The mismatch between VC return requirements and actual game studio exit multiples is a structural problem that founders should address directly in investor conversations [source: prior_compilation].

**Cherry-picked comps.** Valuation comps must show range and quartile position. Single comparators that flatter the ask are an immediate red flag in due diligence [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d].

**GTM launch budget under-estimate.** For a Telegram F2P game targeting 100k+ first-week players: GBP 100k performance marketing at launch, GBP 20k influencer fees plus GBP 5k co-marketing, GBP 5k trailer production, GBP 3k key art. Total pre-launch cash cost approximately GBP 130k+ before development funding. Studios pitching with no UA budget in the financial model lose credibility immediately [source: chatgpt_685bfb00].
