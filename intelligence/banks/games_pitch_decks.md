## Executive Summary

This bank captures NBI's methodology for building, reviewing, and auditing game studio pitch decks, data rooms, and investor materials. It contains a complete 16-section data room checklist applicable to Seed through Series C, a reusable investor connection framework with named funds and check sizes, a detailed pitch deck teaching manual for F2P mobile founders, an annotated Playsage seed deck, and a seven-role data room audit methodology. The material is directly applicable to NBI's game investment advisory (/gi) service offering. Strongest assets: the data room checklist and audit methodology are production-ready client deliverables.

## What Makes a Game Pitch Deck Work

Across all extracts, successful game pitch decks share these structural elements:

1. **Evidence over assertion.** Every claim needs a source or data point. Data rooms must distinguish "verified-within-data-room", "internally-inconsistent", and "unverifiable" claims. Ambiguous KPI definitions (what counts as MAU, how retention is measured) are a common cross-document consistency failure. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

2. **Scenario-based financials.** Single-case models are rejected. Investors expect Base/Upside/Downside scenarios with sensitivity grids on key assumptions (WACC vs terminal growth, multiple vs revenue). [source: chatgpt_690c8b4f, chatgpt_6908ac7d]

3. **Games-specific round definitions.** Generic startup stage definitions fail in gaming. Seed = team, thesis, prototype, prove fun. Series A = product-market fit, soft launch metrics trending. Series B = scale what works, predictable KPIs. Series C = institutional scaling, M&A readiness. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

4. **Platform-specific traction evidence.** Premium games show wishlists and demo conversion. F2P shows CPI, retention (D1/D7/D30), ARPDAU, LTV, and ROAS. These are separate data room sections, not combined. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

5. **Multi-role red-teaming.** Pitch materials benefit from review by multiple functional perspectives (VC partner, monetisation lead, UA lead, tech lead, finance lead, legal lead) before investor exposure. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

## By Platform

### Mobile (F2P, Hypercasual, Mid-core)

The NBI pitch deck teaching manual is specifically scoped to F2P mobile games with IAP monetisation and optional respectful ads. Explicitly excludes crypto, tokens, heavy loot box, and grey-area gambling patterns. Multi-role coaching panel (CEO, CTO, CPO, CFO, UA lead, generalist VC, specialist games fund) sanity-checks every section. Designed as a reusable client resource that is strong enough to use directly with founders as teaching material. [source: chatgpt_691f1230]

Key metrics investors expect for mobile F2P: CPI by channel, D1/D7/D30 retention, ARPDAU, LTV, ROAS, payer conversion rate. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

### PC/Console Indie (Premium, Early Access)

Traction evidence for premium games centres on wishlist counts, demo conversion rates, and Steam Next Fest performance. No detailed premium pitch deck template exists in the current knowledge. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

### Live Service / GaaS (Any Platform)

For live service games, the GTM strategy becomes a critical deck component. The Sarge GTM plan provides a template: week-by-week timeline from Week -8 to Week +12, with specific budget allocations (trailer GBP5k, key art GBP3k, influencer programme GBP20k fees + GBP5k co-marketing, launch week performance marketing ~GBP100k). Target metrics: CPI under USD2, 100k+ first-week players, 1M+ impressions. [source: chatgpt_685bfb00]

## By Stage

### Pre-Seed / Concept

No specific pre-seed deck template exists yet. Data room at this stage is minimal: team, thesis, concept document, competitive landscape. [See Open Questions]

### Seed / Pre-Production

**Playsage example (GBP2.5M, 24-month runway):** 14-slide structure: Title, Problem (3 pain points), Opportunity/Timing, Solution (Evidence Layer concept), Product Modules (6 modules named), Differentiation, Data and Tech Moat, Market Context (global games ~USD188.8B, game analytics ~USD2.21B), Buyer and JTBD (4 personas), Competitive Landscape, Business Model (SaaS tiers + data packs), Traction/Roadmap (Alpha > Private Beta > Public Beta > GA), GTM (founder-led), Team, The Ask. Key differentiation rested on evidence layer (source attribution, confidence scoring) vs competitors' static reports. [source: chatgpt_68e8a242]

At seed, data rooms need: one-pager, cap table, team bios tied to shipped titles, genre thesis, prototype or playable, competitive landscape. Lightweight but professional. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

### Series A / Production

At Series A, the data room expands significantly. Required sections: full cap table with option pool, hiring plan, traction split by monetisation model, tech stack with CI/CD and code ownership, platform compliance status (TRC/XR), financial forecasts with UA payback periods, legal (IP chain, middleware licences, GDPR/COPPA). [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

### Late-Stage / Live Service

Series B requires predictable KPIs and evidence of scalability. Series C is institutional scaling and M&A readiness with full normalised financials and audit trails. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

## Exemplars

### Playsage Seed Deck (Data Platform, GBP2.5M)

- **Structure:** 14 slides, problem-solution-market-ask flow
- **What works:** Four distinct buyer personas with JTBD; evidence layer as core differentiator; specific market sizing (USD188.8B gaming, USD2.21B analytics)
- **Key differentiator:** "Operating system for game decisions" positioning vs point-tool competitors
- **Applicability:** Any B2B SaaS seed deck targeting game industry buyers
[source: chatgpt_68e8a242]

### Sarge Universe Data Room (Telegram F2P, GBP10M)

- **Structure:** Valuation workbook (6 tabs) + headcount plan + P&L + GTM strategy
- **What works:** Three-scenario valuation with sensitivity grids; phased headcount tied to development milestones; go/no-go gates anchored to retention metrics, not dates
- **Key differentiator:** Formula-driven workbook where all assumptions are explicit blue-cell inputs
- **Applicability:** Any F2P mobile game fundraising at Series A scale
[source: chatgpt_690c8b4f, chatgpt_690dcbec, chatgpt_691f13cd]

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Valuing on gross receipts while collecting net.** Platform fees (Apple 30%, Google 30%, Telegram varies) must be netted before revenue is stated. This is a common red flag that triggers instant investor distrust. [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d]

2. **Cherry-picked comps.** Comparables must show range and quartile position, never a single favourable comparator. [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d]

3. **Ambiguous KPI definitions.** If "MAU" is defined differently in the deck vs the financial model vs the data room, investors will catch it. Single definitions enforced across all documents. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

4. **Missing IP ownership chain.** IP assignments from contractors, middleware licence terms, and open-source audit are frequently absent from game studio data rooms. [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]

5. **Forward revenue without showing drivers.** Revenue projections must trace to visible inputs (retention curves, CPI, ARPDAU) not be presented as freestanding numbers. [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d]

6. **Single-case financial models.** No scenario analysis = no investor confidence. Minimum three scenarios (Base/Upside/Downside) with percentage modifiers, not separate models. [source: chatgpt_690c8b4f]

## Data Room Structure

The NBI standard data room has 16 sections [source: chatgpt_68ee2760]:

1. Company overview (one-pager, runway)
2. Cap table (option pool, prior financing)
3. Team (bios tied to shipped titles, hiring plan)
4. Market and strategy (genre thesis)
5. Portfolio and pipeline (gate criteria)
6. Game design docs, economy docs, art bibles
7. Tech (engine, CI/CD, code ownership, open-source audit)
8. Platform (dev kit status, TRC/XR compliance, store strategy)
9. Traction -- split: Premium (wishlists, demo) vs F2P (CPI, retention, ARPDAU, LTV, ROAS)
10. Monetisation and economy
11. Financials (forecasts, UA payback)
12. Legal (IP chain, middleware, GDPR/COPPA)
13. Contracts and vendors
14. Operations and policies
15. References
16. Data room hygiene (index, versioning)

### Data Room Audit Methodology

Seven-role investor committee simulation: Partner (Games VC), Mobile F2P Monetisation Lead, UA and Growth Lead, Product and Live Ops Lead, Tech and Platform Lead, Finance Lead, Legal and Compliance Lead. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

Step 0: Create Data Room Index (every file with type, date, purpose, missing artefacts). Step 1: Document-by-document line audit labelling each claim as Fact/Assumption/Projection/Opinion, recording every numeric claim with value/unit/timeframe/definition/source/reconciliation status. Step 2: Cross-Document Reconciliation Matrix checking metrics across files. Each document gets section-level scores on clarity/completeness, numeric integrity, assumption realism, evidence strength, and investor relevance plus top 5 risks, top 5 fixes, and "diligence traps" that trigger investor distrust. [source: chatgpt_6964dcca]

### Valuation Model Structure

12-tab workbook: Summary, Assumptions (named ranges with source/date), Historical Financials (LTM/TTM), Normalisations (add-backs), Forecast (driver-based 36-month), DCF (unlevered FCF, WACC, terminal value, sensitivity), Multiples (public + transaction comps), EV to Equity Bridge (net debt, preferred liquidation, TSM dilution), KPIs and Cohorts, Evidence/Sources, Checks (balance + audit block), Change Log. Display standards: inputs blue, outputs black, links green, checks red until passing. [source: chatgpt_6908ac7d]
