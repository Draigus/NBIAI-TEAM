## Market State: Mid-2026

### Vertical Slice = Effective Series B Threshold

Game investment has dropped sharply. The market has effectively shifted the VS from a Series A proof point to a Series B prerequisite.

**Source:** Riley Graebner, Magna Capital partner, 2026-06-22. Direct investor market experience, not secondary research.

**Current state:**
- No investor is funding game development without a vertical slice as of mid-2026
- VS is now the de facto gate for gaming-specific VC (previously a Series A deliverable)
- Studios raising their first meaningful institutional round must have one

**Real funding sources for pre-VS studios:**
1. Grants (e.g. UK Games Fund up to GBP 100k)
2. Publisher advances or rev-share arrangements
3. Founder capital / family offices
- VC and gaming funds are NOT a realistic source at pre-VS stage

**Advisory implication:** Studios approaching investors pre-VS are 1-2 years early for the current market. The advice is to build the VS first, not pitch earlier. [source: 26f722af_vs_threshold]

### 2025 VC Consensus Shift

Multi-investor roundtable (LVP, Hiro Capital, 1Up Ventures, Kowloon Nights) confirmed: where studios once raised on decks alone, the current bar requires either a working prototype or exceptional pedigree. The 2024 market allowed exceptional-pedigree exceptions; the 2025 market has largely closed that exception. Scope discipline signals execution maturity -- first-game scope is interpreted as a signal of self-awareness. Distribution plan required alongside creative vision; audience-building strategy must be in the pitch, not a post-funding afterthought. [source: web_2026-06-15_2025-vc-seed-criteria-roundtable]

### AI and Market Convergence

Democratisation of AI tools is making game development cheaper but monetisation harder as models converge. This does not reduce the VS threshold -- it likely increases investor scrutiny. [source: 26f722af_vs_threshold]

## Investor Readiness by Round Type

Framework for what visual fidelity and system completeness is required at each investment stage:

**Early-round investors (family offices, non-gaming funds, warm intros):**
- Proxy kit + concept art booklet + roadmap is sufficient
- Non-gaming investors engage with the vision narrative; they do not need a playable build
- A concept art booklet alone enables a credible demo at early industry events

**Industry conferences (e.g. DICE, September timeline):**
- Needs a representative build on a PC in a demo room
- Must show the game, not a concept: avatars with purpose, combat, trading, partner art
- Large gaming funds ask "where's the fun?" -- systems must be visible and interactive
- Side-by-side proxy vs. mid-poly bake shows velocity

**Later rounds (large gaming funds, institutional):**
- Full system depth: animator counts, market segmentation, financial model
- CFO and finance lead needed in the room
- Players per zone targets, engagement metrics, monetisation model must be articulate

**Cross-round narrative device:** Show meaningful visual progress from round open to round close. Side-by-side proxy vs. mid-poly bake as proof-of-velocity. [source: 4e3e7bfd_investor_readiness]

## Investor Connection Framework (4-Part Reusable)

1. **Operator-investor validation first:** Angel investors from personal professional networks validate team quality before institutional round. Operator-investors (practitioners who can assess team quality independently) de-risk the institutional raise.

2. **Domain-specialist lead investor:** The lead investor should be uniquely qualified to assess the team's credentials. An ex-studio-head investor evaluating ex-studio founders does not need a demo to validate the team.

3. **Two-stage approach:** Angel round for social proof, then institutional round leveraging angel names as validation signals.

4. **Market-gap thesis anchor:** Team credentials alone are insufficient. A verifiable market positioning thesis (underserved genre, platform gap, audience vacuum) anchors the vision element.

[source: web_2026-06-15_dead-astronauts-seed-round-oversubscribed]

## Data Room Audit Methodology

Seven-lens evaluation for investor data room completeness:

1. **Financials:** P&L, cash flow, burn rate, runway, cap table
2. **Team:** Org chart, key bios, vesting schedules, key-person risk
3. **Product:** Build status, technical architecture, demo/VS access
4. **Market:** TAM/SAM/SOM, competitive positioning, audience research
5. **IP:** Ownership chain, any encumbrances, platform agreements
6. **Operations:** Production pipeline, tools, QA process, live ops readiness
7. **Legal:** Corporate structure, material agreements, pending disputes, GDPR compliance

Sixteen-section detailed checklist available. Applied as part of NBI's game investment advisory (/gi) service. [source: chatgpt_6866b4be]

## Deal Case Studies

### Grand Games -- $103M in 2 Years (Hybrid Casual Mobile, Istanbul)

$70M Series B (May 2026) led by Balderton Capital Growth Fund. Total: $103M across three rounds in under two years ($3M pre-seed 2024, $30M Series A Jan 2025). ~75 people, Istanbul. Six live hybrid casual puzzle games, 50M+ downloads, 200+ countries. Simultaneous #1 and #2 on US iOS most-downloaded. 5x YoY revenue growth. Approaching unicorn status.

**Pitch structure:** Team/ownership philosophy (five autonomous studios, creator ownership), proof-of-execution (chart positions, downloads, revenue multiple), market timing (hybrid casual IAP 100% YoY growth), capital allocation to marketing and pipeline.

**Key lesson:** Enter a funding round with chart proof, not projections. Organisational model (five autonomous studios) pitched as the structural moat. Repeat-investor lead (Balderton backed both A and B) removes credibility question. [source: web_2026-06-22_grand-games-hybrid-casual-balderton-103m]

### Dead Astronauts -- EUR 4M Oversubscribed Seed (Pre-Prototype, Feb 2025)

Oversubscribed EUR 4M seed led by Behold Ventures (led by former DICE/Battlefield studio head). Co-investors: Lifelike Capital, 1Up Ventures, Acequia Capital. Angels included former senior executives from major studios. No prototype existed at funding. Four founders from Massive Entertainment (Ubisoft), AAA credits on Avatar: Frontiers of Pandora.

**Key lesson:** Domain-specialist investors are more likely to fund on team credentials than generalist VCs. Investor selection (domain specialist, not generalist) was as important as pitch content. Two-stage approach: personal network angels first, then institutional with angel names as social proof. [source: web_2026-06-15_dead-astronauts-seed-round-oversubscribed]

### Homa Games -- $50M Series A (Platform-Over-Games Thesis)

Platform-over-games thesis positioning. Relevant as a structural comparison to Grand Games' later proof-of-execution approach. Both succeeded but at different stages with different evidence bases. [source: web_2026-06-22_grand-games-hybrid-casual-balderton-103m]

## Funding Gaps and Alternative Paths

### The $500K-$3M Dead Zone

Studios with proven concept (D30 retention 8-10%, $50K/month early revenue) but unable to raise conventional equity at acceptable dilution face a structural funding gap. Traditional VCs want more proof; the studio's valuation is too uncertain for equity negotiation.

**Grey Zone Convertible Debt Instrument (proposed framework):**
- Fund structure: $1-3M facility, 12-15% PIK coupon (compounding into principal, preserving cash), senior liquidation preference, 5% warrants
- Qualification: D30 8-10% AND $50K/month revenue AND functioning core loop
- Intended providers: regional development bodies, strategic studios, credit-focused investors, family offices -- NOT traditional gaming VCs
- Pitch is a repayment thesis, not a growth thesis: different audience, different deck structure

**3-Stage Mobile Funding Path:**
- Stage 1: $500K seed equity (soft launch + D30 8-10%)
- Stage 2: $1-3M grey zone convertible debt (build-out to $300K/month revenue)
- Stage 3: Cohort debt / AR factoring (LTV > CAC proven, UA scaling)

[source: web_2026-06-22_3stage-capital-mobile-studio-funding-path]

### UK Games Fund Prototype Grants

Up to GBP 100,000 for prototype/early development. Expressions of interest open for next round as of June 2026. Best fit: UK-based studios, pre-vertical-slice.

**Strategic use:** Cornerstone funding for a pre-VS studio. Grant buys time to build VS without burning founder capital. Combination: UK Games Fund prototype grant, then VS completion, then private investment/publisher deal.

[source: 26f722af_uk_games_fund]

### Vertical Slice as Dual-Purpose Asset

VS serves two purposes beyond the game: (1) investor proof of concept -- demonstrates the team can build, iterate, and ship; (2) studio proof it can build a game -- many first-large-project studios have never completed a full production cycle. The VS is the first time the full studio completes an estimate, builds to it, and ships a result. This is emotional proof ("we can build a game") that directly affects morale and retention. [source: 4e3e7bfd_vs_dual_purpose]
