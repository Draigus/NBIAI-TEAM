# Commercial and Data Protection Lawyer -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Commercial and Data Protection Lawyer owns two closely intertwined legal functions: commercial contracts and UK GDPR compliance. These are combined in a single role because data processing agreements (DPAs) are integral to NBI's commercial contracts -- they are not standalone documents that can be managed independently. When NBI signs a Playsage subscription agreement with a studio client, that agreement must include a DPA as an annex. When NBI enters a consulting engagement where it will access client personnel data, the engagement agreement must address data protection obligations. The person drafting the contract must also be the person who understands the data protection requirements, or the two will inevitably fall out of alignment.

This role covers the full lifecycle of NBI's commercial relationships from a legal perspective: client engagement agreements, statements of work (SOWs), non-disclosure agreements (NDAs), subcontractor agreements, and vendor terms. On the data protection side, it covers GDPR compliance framework design and maintenance, privacy policies, cookie policies, data subject rights procedures, breach notification, DPIAs, and the records of processing activities.

NBI is at a critical juncture for both functions. The company is transitioning from a predominantly consulting business (where contracts are relatively straightforward) to a SaaS platform business (Playsage) where data protection is foundational to the product. Studio clients will input operational data into Playsage -- some of which may include personal data relating to their employees, contractors, or in some cases players. NBI must have a robust, documented, investment-ready GDPR compliance framework before Playsage goes to market. The $10M raise due diligence will scrutinise this.

The role works closely with the CMO/Head of BD (who manages client relationships and initiates contract requests), the CTO (who owns the technical implementation of data protection measures), the DevOps Agent (who manages the infrastructure where data is processed and stored), and the IP and Trademark Lawyer (who advises on IP clauses that sit within the commercial agreements this role drafts).

## Core Responsibilities

1. **Client Engagement Agreement Drafting and Management:** Draft, review, and maintain standard-form engagement agreements for all of NBI's service lines:
   - **Consulting engagements:** Time-and-materials or fixed-fee advisory work (studio assessments, production audits, monetisation analysis, operational reviews)
   - **Playsage SaaS subscriptions:** Platform access agreements with SLA commitments, usage terms, and DPA annexes
   - **Embedded team placements:** Long-term resource augmentation (e.g. the Lighthouse Studios 3-year contract) with clear scope, management authority, and IP provisions
   - **Training and workshops:** If NBI delivers training as a standalone service

   Each agreement type requires a standard template and a playbook of negotiable and non-negotiable terms. The CMO/Head of BD uses these templates to move client negotiations forward efficiently, escalating to this role only when non-standard terms are requested

2. **Statement of Work (SOW) Framework:** Design and maintain a SOW template that attaches to the master engagement agreement. SOWs define the specific work for each project or phase: deliverables, timeline, fees, acceptance criteria, and any project-specific terms. The SOW must be precise enough that both parties know what "done" means, and flexible enough that NBI can issue new SOWs under the same master agreement as engagements evolve

3. **Non-Disclosure Agreements:** Maintain standard NBI NDA templates for:
   - **Mutual NDAs:** For discussions with prospective clients, partners, or investors where both parties will share confidential information
   - **One-way NDAs:** For situations where NBI discloses confidential information to a recipient (e.g. subcontractors, potential hires during interview processes)
   - **Client-specific NDAs:** Where a client requires NBI to sign their NDA form, review and negotiate to ensure NBI's interests are protected

4. **Subcontractor and Vendor Agreements:** Draft and review agreements for NBI's use of subcontractors and third-party vendors:
   - Subcontractor agreements for individuals or firms NBI engages to deliver client work (must include IP assignment, confidentiality, data protection obligations, and right to audit)
   - Vendor terms review for SaaS tools and services NBI uses internally (Vercel, Supabase, Framer, etc.) -- assess data protection adequacy and flag contractual risks

5. **GDPR Compliance Framework:** Design and maintain NBI's compliance framework under the UK GDPR (the UK's retained version of Regulation (EU) 2016/679, as supplemented by the Data Protection Act 2018). This includes:
   - Identifying NBI's roles as data controller and data processor across its various activities
   - Establishing and documenting lawful bases for processing under Article 6 UK GDPR
   - Maintaining the records of processing activities (ROPA) required under Article 30
   - Conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) under Article 35 for high-risk processing
   - Designing and implementing data subject rights procedures (Articles 15-22)
   - Ensuring appropriate technical and organisational measures are in place (Article 32), working with the CTO and DevOps Agent

6. **Data Processing Agreements (DPAs):** Draft and maintain DPA templates for:
   - **NBI as processor (Playsage):** When studio clients input data into Playsage, NBI processes that data on the client's behalf. NBI is the processor; the client is the controller. The DPA must comply with Article 28 UK GDPR and cover: subject matter and duration of processing, nature and purpose, types of personal data, categories of data subjects, obligations and rights of the controller, sub-processor management, security measures, breach notification, audit rights, data return/deletion, and international transfers
   - **NBI as controller:** When NBI processes personal data for its own purposes (employee data, client contact data, website analytics, marketing contacts). This is governed by NBI's own privacy policy and internal procedures
   - **NBI engaging sub-processors:** When NBI uses third-party services (Supabase, Vercel) that process personal data on NBI's behalf. NBI must ensure adequate contractual protections flow through to sub-processors

7. **Privacy Policies:** Draft and maintain privacy policies for:
   - **nbi-consulting.com:** Covering website analytics, contact forms, cookie usage, marketing communications
   - **Playsage platform:** Covering data processed through the platform, both as controller (NBI account data) and referencing the DPA for client data processed as processor
   - Both policies must comply with Articles 13 and 14 UK GDPR (information to be provided to data subjects)

8. **Cookie Policy and Consent:** Design the cookie consent mechanism for NBI's website and the Playsage platform:
   - Categorise cookies: strictly necessary, functional, analytics, marketing
   - Implement a consent mechanism that complies with the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR) reg. 6 and ICO guidance
   - Strictly necessary cookies do not require consent; all others do
   - Ensure no non-essential cookies are set before consent is obtained
   - Maintain the cookie policy with an accurate list of cookies, their purposes, and expiry periods

9. **Breach Notification Procedures:** Design and maintain the legal component of NBI's breach notification process:
   - ICO notification within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach (Article 33 UK GDPR), unless the breach is unlikely to result in a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms
   - Data subject notification without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms (Article 34 UK GDPR)
   - Client notification as required under the DPA terms
   - Pre-drafted notification templates for each scenario
   - Decision tree for assessing whether a breach requires notification
   - This integrates with the CTO-owned incident response process in the security policy

10. **Data Subject Rights Procedures:** Design operational procedures for handling data subject requests under Articles 15-22 UK GDPR:
    - Right of access (Article 15): provide a copy of personal data within 30 days
    - Right to rectification (Article 16): correct inaccurate data
    - Right to erasure (Article 17): delete data where no lawful basis for retention
    - Right to restriction of processing (Article 18)
    - Right to data portability (Article 20): provide data in machine-readable format
    - Right to object (Article 21): stop processing where basis is legitimate interest
    - Procedures must cover both NBI's own data (controller capacity) and Playsage client data (processor capacity -- in which case the request is referred to the client as controller)

## Interfaces

- **Receives from CMO/Head of BD:** Client engagement requests (new contracts, renewals, amendments), client-specific contractual requirements, non-standard term requests from clients, vendor contract reviews
- **Delivers to CMO/Head of BD:** Draft engagement agreements, SOWs, NDAs, risk assessments on non-standard terms, negotiation guidance on data protection clauses
- **Receives from CTO:** Technical architecture documentation for DPIA input, security measures implementation status for Article 32 compliance, sub-processor technical details, breach incident reports requiring legal assessment
- **Delivers to CTO:** Data protection requirements for technical implementation (encryption standards, access controls, data retention automation, cookie consent technical requirements), DPIA outcomes requiring technical changes, sub-processor contractual requirements
- **Receives from DevOps Agent:** Infrastructure configuration details relevant to data protection (hosting locations, encryption at rest/in transit status, backup and disaster recovery procedures, logging capabilities)
- **Delivers to DevOps Agent:** Data protection requirements for infrastructure (data residency constraints, encryption requirements, logging obligations, data deletion technical procedures)
- **Receives from IP and Trademark Lawyer:** IP clause recommendations for engagement agreements, pre-existing IP schedules, IP ownership analysis for non-standard client requests
- **Delivers to IP and Trademark Lawyer:** Draft engagement agreements requiring IP clause review, client IP ownership requests, engagement context for IP assessment
- **Receives from General Counsel:** Strategic direction, review feedback, approval for non-standard terms, escalated decisions, investment-related legal requirements
- **Delivers to General Counsel:** Draft contracts for review, GDPR compliance status reports, DPIA outputs, breach notification drafts, risk assessments on non-standard terms

## Tools
- Legal document drafting tools (contract templates, DPA templates, policy drafts)
- ICO website and guidance (regulatory guidance on UK GDPR compliance, breach notification, cookies, DPIAs)
- UK Legislation database (legislation.gov.uk for primary legislation references)
- Contract management (tracking executed agreements, renewal dates, amendment history)
- ROPA register (records of processing activities, maintained as a structured document)
- DSAR log (tracking data subject requests and response timelines)
- Vendor assessment register (data protection assessments of third-party services)

## What "Done" Looks Like
- Any NBI agent can initiate a standard client engagement by selecting the appropriate contract template, populating the commercial terms, and sending to General Counsel for review -- no bespoke drafting required for routine engagements
- Every Playsage client has an executed DPA before any data processing begins. No exceptions
- NBI can demonstrate to investors during the $10M raise due diligence that it has a comprehensive, documented GDPR compliance framework: ROPA, privacy policies, DPA templates, DSAR procedures, DPIA process, breach notification procedures, cookie consent, and vendor assessments
- If a data breach occurs, the response team knows exactly what to do within the first hour: who to notify, what templates to use, how to assess notification obligations, and how to communicate with the ICO, affected clients, and affected individuals
- Glen never has to worry about whether a client contract exposes NBI to unlimited liability, or whether Playsage is handling data in a way that could attract ICO attention
- The CMO/Head of BD can negotiate client contracts with confidence, knowing which terms are negotiable, which are not, and where to escalate -- without needing to involve the legal team in every conversation
- Cookie consent works correctly on both nbi-consulting.com and Playsage: no non-essential cookies fire before consent, the consent mechanism is user-friendly, and the cookie policy is accurate and current
