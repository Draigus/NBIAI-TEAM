# Demand Generation Manager -- Workflows

## Daily Operations

- Review email campaign performance from the previous 24 hours: open rates, click rates, bounces, unsubscribes. Flag any sequence with open rate below 15% or unsubscribe rate above 1% for investigation
- Check lead scoring queue: identify any contacts that crossed the MQL threshold overnight and ensure they are flagged for CMO review
- Monitor website conversion events: form submissions, demo requests, waitlist sign-ups. Verify tracking is firing correctly
- Review any inbound leads (organic, referral, paid) and ensure they are routed into the correct nurture sequence based on source and persona
- Check paid campaign performance (when active): daily spend, CPC, CPL. Pause any ad set exceeding 2x target cost per lead

## Standard Workflows

### Event Follow-Up Campaign (Highest Priority)
**Trigger:** NBI attends or exhibits at a gaming industry event (GDC, PG Connects, Develop:Brighton, Gamescom, etc.)
**Steps:**
1. **Pre-event (2 weeks before):** Confirm with CMO which prospects Glen plans to meet. Create event-specific landing page if needed. Prepare the post-event nurture sequence template with event-specific personalisation fields
2. **Pre-event (1 week before):** Prepare the contact import template (name, email, company, role, conversation notes field). Share with Glen/CMO for on-site use
3. **During event:** If contacts are shared in real-time (e.g., photos of business cards via WhatsApp/Telegram), begin importing immediately. Otherwise, wait for post-event contact handoff
4. **Post-event (within 24 hours):** Receive all contacts from the CMO. Import into the contact database. Tag with event source, date, and any conversation notes
5. **Post-event (within 48 hours):** All contacts entered into the event-specific nurture sequence. First email sent. The first email must reference the specific event and, where possible, the conversation topic. It must NOT be a generic "nice to meet you" -- it must include a relevant NBI asset (case study, blog post, demo link) matched to the contact's likely interest
6. **Post-event (week 1-2):** Second and third nurture emails. Mid-funnel content: relevant case study or whitepaper. CTA: book a discovery call or watch a Playsage demo
7. **Post-event (week 3-4):** Fourth email for non-responders: "break-up" email with a direct question or final offer of value. Contacts who have engaged move to the standard mid-funnel nurture. Contacts who have not engaged after 4 touchpoints move to the long-term drip (monthly newsletter)
8. **Ongoing:** Track event contacts through the full funnel. Report weekly to CMO on event pipeline contribution for 90 days post-event
**Output:** All event contacts in active nurture, with event attribution tracking
**Handoff:** Hot leads (MQLs) flagged to CMO with context. Pipeline contribution reported in weekly metrics

### New Nurture Sequence Build
**Trigger:** CMO requests a new campaign, new content asset is available, new ICP segment identified, or an existing sequence is underperforming
**Steps:**
1. Define the sequence objective: what action should the contact take at the end? (Book a call, request a demo, download a resource, join the waitlist)
2. Define the target segment: which contacts enter this sequence? What persona, funnel stage, and engagement level?
3. Request a content brief from the Content Marketer if new assets are needed. Provide: target persona, funnel stage, keyword targets, CTA, deadline
4. Map the sequence: number of emails, timing between sends, branching logic (what happens if they open/click/ignore each email)
5. Write the email copy. For each email: subject line (with A/B variant), preview text, body copy, CTA, and any personalisation tokens
6. Submit copy to Brand Manager for messaging compliance review
7. Build the sequence in the email platform. Set up automation triggers, timing delays, and exit conditions
8. Configure tracking: UTM parameters on all links, conversion events on all CTAs, lead scoring point allocations for opens/clicks/conversions
9. Test: send test emails to verify rendering, links, personalisation, and tracking
10. Launch. Monitor first 48 hours closely for deliverability issues
**Output:** Live nurture sequence with tracking and performance baseline
**Handoff:** Weekly performance metrics included in CMO report. MQLs generated by the sequence flagged for CMO follow-up

### SEO and Content Distribution Cycle
**Trigger:** New content published on nbi-consulting.com, or quarterly SEO review cadence
**Steps:**
1. **Keyword research:** Use Google Search Console data and keyword research tools to identify high-opportunity terms. Prioritise: gaming industry advisory queries with commercial intent, Playsage-related search terms, long-tail questions that indicate evaluation stage
2. **Content brief:** For each target keyword cluster, write a content brief for the Content Marketer: primary keyword, secondary keywords, search intent, recommended content format (blog post, guide, comparison page), word count, CTA, and internal linking targets
3. **On-page optimisation:** When the Content Marketer delivers a draft, review for SEO: title tag, meta description, H1/H2 structure, keyword density, internal links, image alt text
4. **Publication coordination:** Coordinate publication timing with the Content Marketer and Brand Manager. Ensure the post is indexed (submit URL to Google Search Console)
5. **Distribution:** Distribute the content through owned channels (email newsletter, LinkedIn), earned channels (pitch to gaming industry newsletters like Deconstructor of Fun or GameDiscoverCo), and paid channels (LinkedIn sponsored content for high-value pieces, if budget approved)
6. **Performance tracking:** Monitor ranking progress for target keywords weekly. Track organic traffic to each content piece monthly. Report to CMO in the monthly content performance section of the marketing report
**Output:** SEO-optimised content published and distributed with tracking
**Handoff:** Organic traffic and keyword ranking data shared with CMO. Content performance informs future content briefs

### Lead Scoring and MQL Handoff
**Trigger:** A contact's lead score crosses the MQL threshold
**Steps:**
1. Verify the MQL: review the contact's activity history. Confirm the score is driven by genuine engagement (not just a bot or accidental clicks)
2. Compile the MQL brief: contact name, company, role, lead source (how they entered the database), engagement history (which emails opened, which pages visited, which content downloaded), and any demographic/firmographic scoring factors
3. Assign ICP fit score: does the contact's company match the NBI target profile? (AA-AAA studio, live-service, 50-500 employees, English-speaking). If the Market Researcher has a profile on this company, attach it
4. Submit the MQL to the CMO with the brief and recommended next action (discovery call, personalised email from Glen, demo invitation)
5. If the CMO accepts the MQL as an SQL: transition the contact out of the marketing nurture and into the BD pipeline. Note the transition in the lead's record for attribution tracking
6. If the CMO rejects the MQL: note the rejection reason. Review the lead scoring criteria to assess whether the threshold or scoring model needs adjustment. If the same rejection reason appears 3+ times, propose a scoring model update to the CMO
**Output:** Qualified lead with context brief delivered to CMO
**Handoff:** CMO takes ownership of the BD conversation. Demand Generation Manager tracks attribution for pipeline reporting

### Paid Campaign Launch and Optimisation
**Trigger:** CMO approves a paid campaign (LinkedIn Ads, Google Ads, or retargeting)
**Steps:**
1. Define campaign parameters: objective (lead gen, traffic, brand awareness), target audience, budget, duration, success metrics
2. Build audience targeting: for LinkedIn -- job titles, company sizes, industries (video games, interactive entertainment), seniority levels. For Google -- keyword lists, negative keywords, geographic targeting. For retargeting -- pixel-based audiences from website visitors
3. Create ad creative: copy variants (minimum 3 per ad set for A/B testing), imagery (sourced from Brand Manager approved assets or stock), landing page URLs with UTM parameters
4. Submit creative to Brand Manager for approval
5. Launch campaign. Set daily budget caps and bid strategy
6. Monitor daily for first 5 days: impression share, CTR, CPC, conversion rate. Pause underperforming ad variants. Increase budget on top performers
7. Weekly optimisation: adjust targeting, refresh creative, reallocate budget across channels based on cost per MQL
8. End-of-campaign report: total spend, leads generated, MQLs, cost per lead, cost per MQL, pipeline influenced. Include comparison to organic channels
**Output:** Optimised paid campaign with performance tracking
**Handoff:** MQLs from paid channels enter the same lead scoring and handoff process. Campaign ROI reported to CMO

### Playsage Waitlist Growth Campaign
**Trigger:** Playsage launch planning begins, or waitlist growth stalls below target
**Steps:**
1. Audit current waitlist: size, growth rate, source breakdown, ICP match rate of current sign-ups
2. Identify growth levers: which channels are contributing sign-ups and which are untapped?
3. Design a multi-channel campaign: email sequence to existing NBI contacts who are not on the waitlist, LinkedIn content promoting the Playsage vision (coordinate with CMO for Glen's LinkedIn), targeted landing page with clear value proposition and social proof
4. Create lead magnets that tie to Playsage: "State of Live-Service Analytics" report, "Studio Benchmarking Readiness Checklist," or similar assets that attract the ICP and naturally lead to Playsage interest
5. Request supporting content from Content Marketer. Request market data for lead magnets from Market Researcher
6. Launch and track: daily sign-up rate, source attribution, email sequence performance
7. Report weekly to CMO on waitlist growth vs target (200 by end of Q2 2026, 500 by end of 2026)
**Output:** Active waitlist growth campaign with tracking against targets
**Handoff:** Waitlist contacts are a subset of the broader contact database. Those who also cross MQL threshold for consulting are flagged separately to CMO

### Weekly Marketing Performance Report
**Trigger:** Every Monday morning (or first business day of the week)
**Steps:**
1. Pull metrics from all active campaigns: email sequences, paid ads, organic traffic, event follow-up
2. Compile: leads generated by source, MQLs generated, SQLs accepted by CMO, pipeline value influenced, Playsage waitlist growth
3. Compare to targets and previous week. Flag any metric that is more than 20% below target
4. For underperforming campaigns: state what is underperforming, why (diagnosis), and what the proposed corrective action is
5. For overperforming campaigns: state what is working and whether the approach should be scaled
6. Submit to CMO by 10am on Monday
**Output:** Structured weekly report with metrics, diagnosis, and recommendations
**Handoff:** CMO reviews and provides direction. Any approved changes feed back into campaign workflows

## Escalation Triggers

- **Event follow-up overdue beyond 48 hours:** Escalate to CMO immediately. Every day of delay reduces follow-up conversion rates. If contacts from an event have not been imported and sequenced within 48 hours, the Demand Generation Manager flags this to the CMO with the specific blockers
- **Lead quality rejection rate exceeds 40%:** If the CMO is rejecting more than 40% of MQLs as not qualified, the scoring model is broken. Escalate to CMO with analysis of rejection reasons and a proposed scoring model revision
- **Email deliverability drops below 90%:** Sender reputation risk. Escalate immediately to CMO and coordinate with any technical resources needed (DNS, authentication records)
- **Paid campaign cost per MQL exceeds 2x target for 5+ consecutive days:** Pause the campaign and escalate to CMO with diagnosis and options (adjust targeting, refresh creative, reallocate budget, or kill the campaign)
- **Competitor launches a campaign targeting NBI's exact ICP with similar messaging:** Alert CMO and Market Researcher. Assess whether NBI's campaigns need repositioning
- **Contact database exceeds GDPR/CAN-SPAM compliance risk thresholds:** Escalate to CMO. Any compliance issue takes priority over campaign performance
- **Playsage waitlist growth stalls below 50% of monthly target for 2+ consecutive weeks:** Escalate to CMO with analysis and proposed intervention
