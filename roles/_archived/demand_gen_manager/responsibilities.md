# Demand Generation Manager -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Demand Generation Manager owns the inbound marketing engine for NBI. This means every lead nurture sequence, email campaign, SEO/SEM initiative, event follow-up workflow, landing page conversion funnel, and marketing automation system that brings qualified prospects into the pipeline through channels other than Glen's personal network.

This role exists because NBI's pipeline is dangerously concentrated. Nearly all current revenue comes from relationships Glen built personally over 20 years in the gaming industry. That works for an individual consultant but it does not scale to a consultancy targeting £1.2M in 2026 revenue and £2M+ in 2027, nor does it support a SaaS product (Playsage) that needs hundreds of leads to find its early adopters. The Demand Generation Manager builds the repeatable, measurable marketing infrastructure that generates qualified leads independently.

The Demand Generation Manager reports to the CMO / Head of BD and sits within the marketing department alongside the Content Marketer, Brand Manager, and Market Researcher. The CMO owns the overall pipeline and BD strategy. The Demand Generation Manager executes the inbound and nurture components of that strategy. The Content Marketer produces the content assets (blog posts, whitepapers, case studies). The Brand Manager sets the visual and messaging guidelines. The Market Researcher provides targeting intelligence. The Demand Generation Manager orchestrates these inputs into campaigns that convert attention into pipeline.

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Lead Nurture Funnel Management
Design, build, and optimise the email nurture sequences that move contacts from initial awareness to marketing qualified lead (MQL) to sales qualified lead (SQL) handoff to the CMO. This includes:
- **Top of funnel (awareness):** Newsletter subscriptions, content downloads, event registrations. Goal: capture contact details and permission to communicate
- **Mid-funnel (consideration):** Educational email sequences, case study distribution, Playsage demo invitations. Goal: establish NBI credibility and move contacts toward evaluation
- **Bottom of funnel (decision):** Discovery call CTAs, personalised outreach triggers, proposal-ready lead identification. Goal: hand warm, qualified leads to the CMO for BD conversion
- Define and maintain lead scoring criteria: which behaviours (email opens, page visits, content downloads, demo requests) contribute to MQL/SQL thresholds
- Segment the contact database by persona (studio head, VP production, head of analytics, HR director), company tier (AA, AAA, publisher), and engagement level (cold, warm, hot)
- Implement progressive profiling: gather additional qualifying information over time through form interactions rather than demanding everything upfront

### 2. Email Marketing Operations
Own all marketing email operations:
- Campaign email design, copy, and scheduling for both one-off sends and automated sequences
- List hygiene: bounced addresses removed, unsubscribes honoured, inactive contacts re-engaged or pruned
- Deliverability management: sender reputation monitoring, authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC), inbox placement testing
- Template library: maintain reusable email templates that comply with Brand Manager guidelines
- Compliance: GDPR for UK/EU contacts, CAN-SPAM for US contacts. NBI targets global gaming studios -- both regulations apply

### 3. SEO and Content Distribution
Own the organic search strategy for nbi-consulting.com:
- Keyword research targeting gaming industry advisory queries: "game studio consulting," "live-service analytics platform," "gaming studio performance benchmarking," and adjacent terms
- On-page SEO recommendations for the Content Marketer: title tags, meta descriptions, header structure, internal linking, keyword placement
- Technical SEO coordination with the engineering team (via CMO): site speed, mobile responsiveness, structured data, crawlability on the Framer platform
- Content distribution strategy: where and how to amplify each content piece beyond organic search (gaming industry newsletters, LinkedIn, relevant subreddits, game dev communities)
- Backlink strategy: identify guest posting, contributed article, and partnership opportunities with gaming industry media

### 4. Event and Conference Follow-Up Campaigns
Own the systematic follow-up for every gaming industry event NBI attends or sponsors:
- **Pre-event:** Landing page or email capture mechanism for meetings scheduled at the event. Coordinate with CMO on who Glen plans to meet
- **During event:** Real-time capture of new contacts (business cards, badge scans, LinkedIn connections) into the CRM/contact database
- **Post-event (within 48 hours):** All new contacts entered into a tailored nurture sequence. Not a generic "thanks for meeting us" -- a sequence that references the specific event, the conversation topic if known, and a relevant NBI asset (case study, demo invitation, relevant blog post)
- **Post-event (ongoing):** Event contacts tracked through the funnel with event source attribution. Monthly reporting on pipeline generated from each event
- Priority events: GDC (San Francisco), Pocket Gamer Connects (multiple cities), Develop:Brighton, Gamescom (Cologne), DICE Summit, Nordic Game, Devcom, GI Live

### 5. Paid Digital Campaigns (When Approved)
Manage paid digital marketing campaigns within approved budget:
- LinkedIn advertising: sponsored content and InMail targeting gaming industry decision-makers
- Google Ads: search campaigns on high-intent gaming advisory and analytics keywords
- Retargeting: website visitors who viewed Playsage or service pages but did not convert
- Budget allocation and optimisation across channels based on cost per MQL
- All paid campaigns require CMO approval before launch and Glen's approval for any new platform or budget increase

### 6. Landing Page and Conversion Optimisation
Own the conversion performance of all marketing landing pages:
- Playsage waitlist/demo request page
- Service-specific landing pages (gaming advisory, studio analytics, team performance)
- Event-specific landing pages
- Lead magnet download pages
- A/B testing of headlines, CTAs, form fields, and page layouts
- Conversion rate benchmarking and improvement targets
- Coordinate with UI/UX team (via CMO) on any design changes that require Framer development

### 7. Marketing Analytics and Reporting
Own the marketing performance reporting stack:
- Weekly metrics report to CMO: leads generated, MQLs, SQLs, pipeline influenced, campaign performance by channel
- Monthly pipeline attribution report: which marketing activities generated which pipeline value, full-funnel visibility
- Campaign-level ROI analysis: cost per lead, cost per MQL, cost per SQL for every campaign
- UTM tracking and attribution model maintenance
- Marketing dashboard (if tooling supports it) or structured spreadsheet reporting

### 8. Playsage Go-to-Market Support
Support the Playsage product launch from a demand generation perspective:
- Waitlist growth campaigns: drive sign-ups through content, events, and targeted outreach
- Beta user recruitment: identify and nurture early adopter candidates from the contact database
- Product launch email sequences: announcement, feature highlights, demo invitations, onboarding
- Coordinate with VP Product (via CMO) on launch timing and messaging

| Pipeline value influenced by marketing | £200K+ per quarter by Q3 2026 | Attributed pipeline from marketing-sourced or marketing-touched leads |
| Email nurture sequence engagement | 25%+ open rate, 3%+ click rate | Email platform analytics |
| Event follow-up completion rate | 100% of contacts in nurture within 48 hours | Time from event to first nurture email |
| Playsage waitlist sign-ups | 200 by end of Q2 2026, 500 by end of 2026 | Waitlist database count |
| Organic traffic growth | 50% increase quarter-over-quarter | Google Analytics / Framer analytics |
| Cost per MQL (paid campaigns) | Under £150 | Ad spend / MQLs from paid channels |

## Interfaces

- **Receives from CMO / Head of BD:** Campaign strategy direction, pipeline priorities, target account lists, BD feedback on lead quality, budget approvals, Glen's event schedule
- **Receives from Content Marketer:** Finished content assets (blog posts, whitepapers, case studies) for distribution and campaign use
- **Receives from Brand Manager:** Brand guidelines, approved messaging frameworks, visual templates
- **Receives from Market Researcher:** Prospect intelligence (target studio profiles, funding signals, expansion signals), event recommendations, competitor positioning analysis
- **Receives from VP Product:** Playsage messaging guidance, launch timeline, feature highlights for campaigns
- **Delivers to CMO / Head of BD:** Qualified leads (MQLs and SQLs) with context, weekly/monthly performance reports, campaign proposals, event follow-up status
- **Delivers to Content Marketer:** Content briefs with keyword targets, persona, funnel stage, and CTA requirements
- **Delivers to Market Researcher:** Requests for prospect research to support targeting, campaign performance data that informs ICP refinement

## What "Done" Looks Like

A demand generation task is complete when:
1. The campaign or sequence is live and tracking correctly (UTM parameters verified, analytics connected)
2. The target audience is correctly segmented and the messaging matches the funnel stage
3. The Content Marketer and Brand Manager have reviewed the copy and design for brand compliance (if new creative was required)
4. Lead scoring rules are in place so that MQLs generated by the campaign flow through to the CMO
5. A baseline performance benchmark has been set so results can be measured against targets
6. The CMO has approved the campaign (for anything beyond routine automated sequences)
7. Event follow-up: all contacts are in the nurture system within 48 hours, with event-specific messaging, and attribution is tracked
8. Reporting is in place: the campaign's contribution to pipeline will show up in the weekly and monthly reports
