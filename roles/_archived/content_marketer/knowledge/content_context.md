## NBI's Brand Voice

### Glen's Personal Voice (for LinkedIn posts and thought leadership attributed to him)
- **Direct.** Says what he means. Does not hedge. "Most studios fail at GTM because they start too late and plan too little" not "GTM can be challenging for many studios"
- **Experience-led.** Speaks from having done the work, not from having read about it. "When I was at Blizzard..." or "I worked with a studio that..." or "In my experience shipping [X] games..."
- **Opinionated.** Has strong views backed by two decades of results. This is not a consultant playing it safe -- this is someone who has sat in the room and made the calls
- **Studio-native.** Uses the language of game development: ship, sprint, milestone, greenlight, live ops, day-one patch, retention loop, FTUE, ARPPU, LTV, whale, progression system. Never "deliverable optimisation" or "strategic value creation"
- **Practical.** Every insight has an implied "here is what you should actually do about it." Not theoretical frameworks -- actionable advice

### NBI Company Voice (for the company LinkedIn page and website)
- **Authoritative but not arrogant.** The track record speaks for itself when presented properly. No need to oversell
- **Evidence-based.** Every claim is backed by a specific outcome, client name, or proof point. "We have worked with 100+ clients including Blizzard, Xbox, and EA" not "We are a leading advisory firm"
- **Gaming-first.** Everything is framed through the lens of game studios. Human capital content is reframed as "studio health" and "team performance." Tom's work is positioned as solving problems studio heads actually have, not as organisational psychology theory
- **Results-focused.** Client outcomes over process descriptions. "18% retention lift" over "we conducted a thorough analysis and developed recommendations"

### Language Standards
- **British English always.** Colour, organisation, analyse, centre, programme (except in proper nouns like "Program Manager")
- **Never use em dashes.** Use commas, semicolons, colons, or -- (double hyphen) where needed
- **No corporate jargon.** Ban list: leverage (as a verb), synergy, holistic, paradigm, best-in-class, world-class (unless directly quoting a testimonial), cutting-edge, innovative (show innovation through specifics instead), thought leader (demonstrate it, do not claim it), stakeholder (say who you mean -- the studio head, the publisher, the producer)
- **No fluff.** Every sentence carries information. If a sentence can be removed without losing meaning, remove it

## Content Type Specifications

### Case Study Format
- **Length:** 400-800 words for the full website version. 150-250 words for the LinkedIn adaptation
- **Structure:** Problem -- Approach -- Outcome (+ Testimonial if available)
- **Tone:** Client-centric. The client is the hero of the story, not NBI. NBI is the expert who helped them succeed
- **Proof:** At least one hard metric per case study where available. If no hard metric exists, use a specific qualitative outcome confirmed by Glen. Never say "significant improvement" without defining what improved and by how much
- **Anonymisation:** If a client cannot be named, anonymise but keep the specifics. "A AAA live-service studio with 200+ developers" is usable. "A large gaming company" is not

### LinkedIn Post Format
- **Length:** 150-300 words for Glen's personal posts. 100-200 words for company page posts
- **Hook:** First line must stop the scroll. Specific, provocative, or counterintuitive. "The last three studios I worked with all made the same mistake in their live ops plan" beats "Thoughts on live ops strategy"
- **Structure:** Hook -- Insight (1-3 short paragraphs) -- CTA or question
- **Hashtags:** Only if CMO specifically requests. Default is no hashtags
- **Images/graphics:** Flag to CMO if a post would benefit from visual support. Content Marketer produces text only

### Thought Leadership Article Format
- **Length:** 800-1500 words
- **Structure:** Hook -- Problem statement -- Glen's perspective (with examples) -- Practical takeaway
- **Tone:** Expert practitioner sharing insights, not consultant marketing services
- **Sources:** All claims backed by Glen's confirmed experience, publicly available industry data, or Tom's published work. No unsourced statistics
- **CTA:** Soft. The article should make the reader want to work with NBI by demonstrating expertise, not by asking them to book a call

### Website Copy Format
- **Length:** Varies by page. Service pages: 300-600 words. Homepage: 200-400 words
- **Structure:** Problem the audience faces -- How NBI solves it -- Proof (case study excerpt or stat) -- CTA
- **SEO consideration:** Use natural language that gaming executives would actually search for. "Games industry consultant" not "strategic advisory services provider"
- **Proof density:** Every page section must include at least one specific proof point. No section should be pure claim without evidence
