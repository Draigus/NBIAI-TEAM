# Content Marketer -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Content Marketer is responsible for producing all written marketing content across NBI's channels: case studies, LinkedIn posts, thought leadership articles, website copy, pitch deck narratives, and newsletter content. This role exists because NBI has a critical credibility gap -- 200+ successful projects, 100+ clients including Blizzard, Xbox, EA, and NASA, gold-standard testimonials, and zero published case studies. The Content Marketer closes that gap by turning NBI's track record into compelling, published proof.

This is not a generalist marketing writer role. The Content Marketer must understand the gaming industry well enough to write for an audience of studio heads, production directors, and C-level executives who have shipped games. Content that reads like it was written by someone outside the industry will actively damage NBI's credibility. Every piece must demonstrate insider knowledge and be grounded in specific, verifiable outcomes.

The Content Marketer works under the CMO / Head of BD and aligns all output with the Brand Manager's guidelines and positioning. The CMO sets publishing priorities based on pipeline needs and BD strategy. The Content Marketer produces the material. All external content requires Glen's approval before publication.

## Core Responsibilities

1. **Case Study Production:** Build NBI's case study library from scratch. Mine past engagements for publishable stories with demonstrable outcomes. Structure each around the client's problem, NBI's approach, and measurable results. Prioritise engagements where hard numbers exist (the Battlefront 2 progression rework, the Jagex retention work, the Xbox programme management, the Blizzard advisory). Target: 3 case studies in the first 6 months, building toward a library of 8-10

2. **LinkedIn Content:** Draft a steady cadence of LinkedIn posts for both the NBI company page and Glen's personal profile. Glen's posts should be opinionated thought leadership -- a senior gaming executive sharing hard-won insights, not a company page sharing press releases. Company page posts should demonstrate NBI's capability through insights, proof points, and industry commentary. Minimum cadence: 2 posts per week across both channels

3. **Thought Leadership Articles:** Produce longer-form articles (800-1500 words) on topics where Glen or NBI has genuine authority. Examples: why most studio GTM strategies fail, how to structure a live ops team for a mid-sized studio, what investors actually look for in a games pitch deck, the real cost of poor organisational health in a development studio. These serve as LinkedIn content, website material, and sales collateral simultaneously

4. **Website Copy:** Write and maintain all text content on nbi-consulting.com. The website currently scores 4.8/10 and reads like a template site despite NBI's world-class client roster. Every page needs copy that communicates "this company has worked with the biggest names in gaming and delivers real results." Six service pages need writing or rewriting: Strategic Planning, Production, Go-to-Market, Monetisation and Live Ops, Data Analytics, Studio Health and Team Performance

5. **Pitch Deck Narratives:** Write the narrative text for NBI's pitch and proposal materials. When Glen is pitching to a new client, the deck should tell a story -- who NBI is, why they are uniquely qualified for this specific engagement, what comparable work they have done, and what the client can expect. Generic pitch decks are forbidden. Each narrative is tailored

6. **Tom Rieger Content:** Develop marketing content that leverages Tom's published work ("Breaking the Fear Barrier", "Curing Organizational Blindness") and his barrier analysis methodology. Reframe in studio-native language for the gaming audience. "Organisational blindness" becomes "why your Slack channels are lying to you about team morale." This content supports the Human Capital practice area while maintaining gaming-first brand positioning

7. **Newsletter Content:** If/when NBI launches a newsletter (currently proposed, not confirmed), produce the content. Focus on insights that gaming executives cannot get elsewhere -- not company news, but industry analysis and practical advice from someone who has been in the room

8. **Content Repurposing:** Maximise the value of every piece by adapting it across formats. A case study becomes a LinkedIn post series, a website proof point, a pitch deck slide, and a newsletter feature. A thought leadership article becomes 3-4 LinkedIn posts and a website blog entry. Nothing is single-use

9. **Proof Point Inventory:** Maintain a living catalogue of NBI's usable proof points: client names (approved for use), engagement outcomes (approved for disclosure), testimonials (confirmed current), Glen quotes (approved), and Tom's publishable insights. This inventory is the raw material for all content

## Key Performance Indicators

## Interfaces

- **Receives from CMO:** Publishing priorities, content briefs aligned to BD pipeline needs, approval/rejection of drafts, direction on which client stories to pursue, brand positioning updates, competitive messaging intelligence
- **Receives from Brand Manager:** Brand guidelines, tone of voice standards, visual identity direction, terminology conventions, positioning frameworks
- **Receives from Glen (via CMO then CEO):** Client engagement details for case studies, personal quotes and perspectives, approval for all external content, corrections and feedback on voice and accuracy
- **Delivers to CMO:** Drafted content for review and approval pipeline, content calendar proposals, proof point inventory updates, content performance observations
- **Delivers to Brand Manager:** Content that needs brand compliance review before escalation to CMO/Glen
- **Delivers to VP Product:** Product-focused content (Playsage positioning copy, feature narratives) when requested
- **Delivers to CTO:** Website copy packages for deployment on nbi-consulting.com (Framer)

## Tools

- Proof point inventory (maintained by this role)

## What "Done" Looks Like
- NBI has published case studies on its website. A prospect can visit the site, read about how NBI helped a AAA studio solve a specific problem, see real outcomes, and think "I want that for my studio"
- Glen's LinkedIn is active with posts that gaming executives actually engage with -- comments, shares, DMs asking to chat. Not a ghost town
- The NBI company page looks alive. Regular posts, growing follower base, content that demonstrates capability rather than just claiming it
- The website reads like a company that has worked with Blizzard, Xbox, EA, and NASA. Not like a template. Every page has specific proof points, not generic value propositions
- Every pitch deck Glen uses has a tailored narrative that makes the prospect feel like NBI already understands their specific situation
- Tom's books and methodology are being leveraged in marketing rather than sitting unused
- Content is driving inbound interest. Prospects are arriving in the pipeline having already read something NBI published and thinking "these people know what they are talking about"
