# COO -- Operations Context (Tier 2 Knowledge)

## Delivery Landscape

NBI operates as a gaming industry consultancy with a small, distributed team. The delivery model is relationship-driven and Glen-dependent -- most client engagements rely on Glen's direct involvement. The COO's operational role is to ensure this delivery runs smoothly, nothing falls through the cracks, and Glen is never blindsided.

## Engagement Models

NBI does not operate on a fixed productised model. Engagements are custom-scoped:
- **Time and materials (T&M)**
- **Embedded team** (staff embedded at client studio)
- **Fractional C-level advisory** (ongoing retainer-style)
- **Project-based fixed scope**
- **Long-term strategic partnerships**

Duration ranges from weeks to multi-year. Pricing ranges from £30,000-£70,000 (small) to £300,000-£400,000/year (large).

## Key Operational Risks

1. **Glen concentration risk:** Glen is the primary relationship holder and delivery resource for most clients. If Glen is overloaded, all engagements suffer. The COO must monitor this actively
2. **Couch Heroes scope creep:** The fractional Studio Head role is inherently broad. Without clear boundaries, this engagement can consume all of Glen's capacity
3. **Goals Studio lead decay:** A warm lead with specific scope is going cold. Every day without follow-up reduces conversion likelihood
4. **Sarge Universe resource drain:** Unpaid work with significant upside potential, but must not consume disproportionate operational resource at the expense of paying clients
5. **NSI transition impact:** When NSI winds down, additional staff will move to NBI payroll (~£620K/year additional cost). This will affect operational budgets and resource planning
