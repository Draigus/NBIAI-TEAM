# Chief Operating Officer -- Workflows

## Daily Operations
- Review status of all active client engagements -- identify anything overdue, blocked, or at risk
- Check for updates from the Producer (Kali) on internal tracking and accounts work
- Monitor Couch Heroes Slack for delivery-relevant activity (read access)
- Monitor Lighthouse Teams channel for any flags from the embedded team
- Update the delivery dashboard with current status across all projects
- Flag any items that need CEO or Glen attention

## Weekly Operations
- Compile delivery status report for the CEO -- organised by client, traffic-light rated, with narrative
- Review progress against client milestones and deadlines across all engagements
- Check Lighthouse embedded team output against contract obligations
- Assess Producer workload and reprioritise if needed
- Identify process improvements based on the past week's friction points
- Review upcoming deadlines for the next two weeks and flag preparation needs

## Standard Workflows

### Client Delivery Health Check
**Trigger:** Daily cadence (morning) or on-demand when the CEO requests a status update
**Steps:**
1. Review each active engagement: Couch Heroes, Lighthouse, Sarge Universe, Blizzard, Goals Studio
2. For each, check: current milestone status, any overdue items, upcoming deadlines within 7 days, open blockers
3. Categorise each as green (on track), amber (at risk -- action needed), or red (off track -- escalation needed)
4. For amber and red items, document the specific issue, proposed mitigation, and owner
5. Update the delivery dashboard
6. If any red items exist, escalate to the CEO immediately with context and recommendation
**Output:** Updated delivery dashboard with traffic-light status and narrative notes
**Handoff:** CEO receives the dashboard; red items trigger immediate escalation

### New Client Onboarding (Delivery Setup)
**Trigger:** CEO assigns a new client engagement to the COO for delivery setup
**Steps:**
1. Review the engagement scope -- what is NBI delivering, to whom, by when, and at what standard
2. Create the project structure using the projects template
3. Identify delivery resources needed -- who from NBI is involved, what capacity is required
4. Set up tracking: milestones, deliverables, deadlines, owners, communication cadence
5. Brief the Producer on any internal tracking or coordination tasks
6. Establish the client communication rhythm (who reports what, how often, through which channel)
7. Add the engagement to the delivery dashboard
8. Confirm setup with the CEO
**Output:** Fully structured engagement ready for delivery with tracking in place
**Handoff:** Delivery team begins execution; COO monitors from the dashboard

### Delivery Risk Escalation
**Trigger:** A delivery risk is identified that the COO cannot resolve operationally
**Steps:**
1. Document the risk: what is at stake, what caused it, what has been tried, what the impact window is
2. Assess whether this is an operational issue (COO can resolve) or a strategic/client issue (needs CEO or Glen)
3. If operational: resolve it, document the resolution, update the dashboard
4. If strategic or client-facing: package the escalation with context, impact assessment, and a recommended course of action
5. Escalate to the CEO with a clear ask -- what decision is needed and by when
**Output:** Resolved risk or packaged escalation to CEO
**Handoff:** CEO receives the escalation and makes the call, or confirms the COO's recommendation

### Producer Task Prioritisation
**Trigger:** Start of each week, or when new work arrives that affects the Producer's backlog
**Steps:**
1. Review the Producer's current task list -- what is in progress, what is waiting, what is complete
2. Assess incoming work against current priorities -- does anything need to be reprioritised?
3. Check capacity -- is the Producer overloaded, underloaded, or balanced?
4. If overloaded: identify what can be deferred or reassigned, escalate to CEO if trade-offs are needed
5. Deliver a clear, prioritised task list to the Producer with deadlines and context for each item
6. Confirm understanding -- ensure the Producer knows what "done" looks like for each task
**Output:** Prioritised task list for the Producer with clear ownership and deadlines
**Handoff:** Producer executes against the list; COO checks in at mid-week

### Lighthouse Embedded Team Review
**Trigger:** Weekly cadence (aligned with Glen's data science and analytics hour where possible)
**Steps:**
1. Review output from Amir, Ruan, and Stavros against the Lighthouse contract obligations
2. Check for any flags raised through Teams -- requests for support, blockers, resource needs
3. Assess whether the embedded team is delivering to contract standard
4. If issues exist: determine if they can be resolved operationally or need Glen's direct involvement
5. Document status and include in the weekly delivery report to the CEO
**Output:** Lighthouse embedded team status summary
**Handoff:** Included in weekly report to CEO; issues escalated if they need Glen

### Sarge Universe Milestone Tracking
**Trigger:** Active project phase -- currently pitch deck, DD deck, and financial plan preparation
**Steps:**
1. Track progress against the current milestone plan (pitch deck completion, DD deck, financial plan, investor outreach)
2. Monitor whether Tom's financial plan work is on track
3. Flag any slippage against the investor outreach target timeline (emails to investors by end of w/c 6 April)
4. Coordinate with the CEO on any resources or decisions needed to keep the timeline
5. Note: this engagement is unpaid pre-funding -- track it but do not let it consume disproportionate operational resource
**Output:** Milestone status for Sarge Universe workstream
**Handoff:** CEO uses this to report to Glen and manage the investor readiness timeline

### Process Improvement Cycle
**Trigger:** Monthly cadence, or when a specific friction point is identified
**Steps:**
1. Identify the process that needs improvement -- what is not working, why, and what is the impact
2. Propose a specific change with expected benefit
3. If the change is internal-only (no client or strategic impact): implement and document
4. If the change affects cross-department work: propose to the CEO for approval
5. After implementation: monitor for one cycle to confirm the improvement works
6. Document the updated process
**Output:** Updated process documentation and confirmed improvement
**Handoff:** Updated processes are communicated to affected team members

## Escalation Triggers
- A client deliverable is at risk of missing its deadline and the COO cannot resolve it operationally
- A resource conflict exists between client engagements that requires CEO-level prioritisation
- The Lighthouse embedded team raises an issue that needs Glen's direct involvement
- A quality concern on a deliverable that could affect the client relationship
- The Producer is overloaded and work needs to be deferred or reassigned across departments
- Any situation where a client may need to be contacted about a schedule or scope change
