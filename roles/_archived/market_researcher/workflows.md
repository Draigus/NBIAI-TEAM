# Market Researcher -- Workflows

## Daily Operations

- Scan gaming industry news sources for commercially relevant signals: studio funding announcements, layoffs, expansions, leadership changes, new title announcements, and M&A activity. Sources: GamesIndustry.biz, PocketGamer.biz, Deconstructor of Fun, GameDiscoverCo, VGInsights, Crunchbase alerts
- Check Crunchbase/PitchBook alerts for funding rounds involving studios on the target account list or studios that match the NBI ICP
- Monitor LinkedIn for leadership changes at target accounts (VP/C-level hires in analytics, strategy, operations, or production roles signal buying intent)
- Log any actionable signals in the prospect signal tracker and flag urgent ones to the CMO immediately (do not wait for the weekly report)

## Standard Workflows

### TAM/SAM/SOM Calculation
**Trigger:** Initial build, quarterly refresh, new data source becomes available, or investor material preparation requires updated figures
**Steps:**
1. Define the market being sized. For NBI there are two: (a) gaming advisory/consulting services and (b) gaming analytics SaaS platforms. These are separate TAMs
2. Gather input data:
   - Total number of game studios globally by tier (AA, AAA, mega-publisher). Source: Newzoo, PitchBook, LinkedIn company search, industry directories
   - Average annual advisory spend per studio tier. Source: PitchBook deal data for consulting engagements, public earnings reports from listed studios, NBI's own contract data as a baseline
   - Average annual analytics platform spend per studio. Source: vendor pricing pages, industry surveys, NBI's Playsage pricing tiers as a reference point
3. Calculate TAM: total studios x average spend. Break down by tier, geography, and category
4. Calculate SAM: filter to NBI's addressable segment -- English-speaking studios, 50-500 employees, at least one live-service title, headquartered in regions NBI can serve
5. Calculate SOM: based on NBI's current capacity (number of concurrent engagements), average contract value, and realistic win rate. Cross-check against the 2026 revenue target (£1.2M) and 2027 target (£2M+)
6. Document every assumption, every source, and every calculation step in a methodology appendix
7. Produce three outputs: (a) executive summary with headline numbers for CMO/Glen, (b) detailed methodology for CFO/investor scrutiny, (c) data slides formatted for pitch deck insertion
8. Submit to CMO for review. If intended for investor materials, escalate to Glen for assumption validation
**Output:** Sourced, defensible TAM/SAM/SOM with methodology documentation
**Handoff:** CMO uses for strategy. CFO uses for financial models. Glen uses for pitch deck. Update quarterly

### Monthly Market Dynamics Brief
**Trigger:** First business day of each month
**Steps:**
1. Review the signal log accumulated over the previous month: funding rounds, layoffs, expansions, M&A, product launches, regulatory changes
2. Categorise signals by commercial relevance to NBI:
   - **Direct opportunities:** Studios that just raised funding, are expanding, or publicly expressed pain points NBI can address
   - **Market shifts:** Trends affecting the advisory/analytics market (platform fee changes, live-service adoption trends, indie-to-AA graduation patterns)
   - **Competitive moves:** Competitor launches, pivots, hires, or positioning changes
   - **Risk signals:** Changes that could affect existing NBI clients or NBI's market position
3. For each category, state the signal, the source, the date, and the "so what" -- what NBI should consider doing in response
4. Highlight the top 3 most commercially significant developments with specific recommended actions
5. Include an updated view of the target account list: any additions, removals, or priority changes based on the month's signals
6. Format as a concise brief (maximum 3 pages). Executive summary first, detail below
7. Deliver to CMO by the 5th business day of the month
**Output:** Monthly market dynamics brief with actionable implications
**Handoff:** CMO reviews and incorporates into BD strategy. Demand Generation Manager uses target account updates for campaign targeting. Urgent signals flagged immediately (do not wait for the monthly brief)

### Prospect Research Profile
**Trigger:** CMO requests a profile on a specific studio, Demand Generation Manager needs targeting data, or Market Researcher identifies a studio matching the ICP through signal monitoring
**Steps:**
1. Gather company data:
   - Company name, headquarters, headcount (current and 12-month trend), founding date
   - Ownership structure: independent, publisher-owned, PE-backed, publicly listed
   - Funding history: rounds, amounts, investors, dates (Crunchbase/PitchBook)
   - Revenue indicators: public revenue data if listed; otherwise estimated from headcount, title portfolio, and market position
2. Map the game portfolio:
   - Active titles: name, platform, genre, live-service status (yes/no), estimated monthly active users if available
   - Titles in development: any publicly announced projects
   - Historical performance: any notable successes or failures
3. Identify key decision-makers:
   - CEO/studio head, VP/Head of Production, VP/Head of Analytics or Data, VP/Head of Operations, Head of HR/People (for Human Capital practice relevance)
   - LinkedIn profiles where available. Note any connections to Glen's network
4. Assess pain points and buying signals:
   - Job postings: are they hiring for analytics, data, operations, or strategy roles? (Signals internal capability gaps)
   - Public statements: conference talks, interviews, blog posts that reveal strategic priorities or challenges
   - Layoffs or restructuring: recent headcount changes that might signal cost pressure or strategic pivot
   - Funding recency: studios within 12 months of a funding round are often in expansion/investment mode
5. Evaluate NBI fit:
   - Which NBI services are relevant? (Gaming advisory, Playsage, studio health/team performance)
   - How strong is the fit? Score 1-5 across: size match, live-service relevance, buying signal strength, accessibility (can NBI reach the decision-maker?), and timing
   - Recommended approach: should CMO reach out directly, should the studio be added to nurture campaigns, or should NBI wait for a stronger signal?
6. Compile into a structured profile template. Maximum 2 pages per studio
**Output:** Structured prospect profile with fit score and recommended approach
**Handoff:** CMO receives for BD prioritisation. Demand Generation Manager receives for campaign targeting. Profile added to the target account database

### Event Strategy and Evaluation
**Trigger:** Annual event calendar planning (typically Q4 for following year), new event opportunity identified, or CMO requests evaluation of a specific event
**Steps:**
1. Compile the candidate event list for the planning period. Sources: industry event calendars, gaming trade association announcements, competitor exhibit lists from previous years
2. For each event, assess:
   - **ICP overlap:** What percentage of attendees/exhibitors match the NBI target profile? Check past attendee lists, exhibitor directories, speaker lists
   - **Decision-maker density:** Are attendees senior enough to make or influence buying decisions? Or is it primarily developers/junior staff?
   - **Speaking opportunities:** Does the event accept speaker proposals? What topics align with NBI's positioning? When is the CFP deadline?
   - **Competitor presence:** Which NBI competitors exhibit at or sponsor this event? What can be inferred about event quality from their investment?
   - **Cost:** Registration, travel, accommodation, booth/sponsorship tiers, marketing collateral. Request budget estimates from CMO
   - **Historical performance:** Has NBI attended before? What pipeline resulted? If first time, what do peer companies report?
3. Score each event on a weighted matrix: ICP overlap (30%), decision-maker density (25%), speaking opportunity (20%), cost efficiency (15%), competitor benchmarking (10%)
4. Categorise: must attend, should attend, consider, skip
5. For "must attend" events: identify specific speaking topics Glen should propose, specific prospects Glen should aim to meet, and specific intelligence objectives
6. Produce the annual event calendar with budget estimates. Submit to CMO for review
**Output:** Scored and prioritised event calendar with speaking recommendations
**Handoff:** CMO approves events and budget with Glen. Demand Generation Manager uses the calendar to plan pre/post-event campaigns. Glen receives speaking topic recommendations

### Competitor Positioning Analysis
**Trigger:** Quarterly refresh, significant competitor move detected, or CMO requests analysis for a specific competitive situation
**Steps:**
1. Define the competitor set. Two categories:
   - **Consulting competitors:** Firms offering gaming advisory services (boutique gaming consultancies, management consulting gaming practices, publisher advisory arms)
   - **Platform competitors:** SaaS platforms offering analytics, intelligence, or operational tools to game studios (GameAnalytics, deltaDNA/Unity, Amplitude gaming, Newzoo Intelligence, Sensor Tower)
2. For each competitor, analyse their marketing positioning (NOT their product features -- that is VP Product's domain):
   - **Website messaging:** What do they claim on their homepage, about page, and service/product pages? What outcomes do they promise? What language do they use?
   - **Case studies and social proof:** Which clients do they name? What results do they claim? How do they present testimonials?
   - **Pricing positioning:** What pricing information is public? Do they lead with enterprise sales, self-serve, or freemium?
   - **Content marketing:** What do they publish? Blog topics, whitepapers, reports. What keywords are they targeting?
   - **Event presence:** Where do they exhibit, sponsor, or speak? What messaging do they use at events?
   - **LinkedIn and social:** How active are they? What themes do they push? How large is their following?
3. Map the positioning landscape: plot competitors on a 2x2 matrix (e.g., consulting-vs-SaaS on one axis, gaming-specialist-vs-generalist on the other). Identify where NBI sits and where gaps exist
4. Identify NBI's unique positioning angles that no competitor claims:
   - Glen's 20-year operating experience at Blizzard, Xbox, EA/DICE, Jagex, Build A Rocket Boy
   - Dual model: consulting + SaaS platform (Playsage)
   - Multi-module intelligence platform vs single-purpose analytics tools
   - Focus on AA-AAA live-service (not indie, not hyper-casual)
5. Produce positioning gap analysis: where is NBI under-messaging vs competitors? Where is NBI's messaging strongest?
6. Deliver to CMO with recommendations for messaging adjustments
**Output:** Competitor positioning analysis with NBI gap analysis and recommendations
**Handoff:** CMO uses to refine NBI messaging. Brand Manager uses for messaging guidelines. Content Marketer uses to inform content strategy. Demand Generation Manager uses to sharpen campaign angles

### Investor Intelligence Package
**Trigger:** Playsage fundraising preparation, investor meeting scheduled, or CFO requests market data for financial model
**Steps:**
1. Update TAM/SAM/SOM (see TAM/SAM/SOM workflow if refresh is needed)
2. Comparable company analysis:
   - Identify 8-12 gaming SaaS/analytics companies that have raised Series A or Series B in the past 3 years
   - For each: company name, product category, raise amount, valuation (if public), revenue at time of raise (if known), key investors, growth metrics cited
   - Identify the range of revenue multiples and valuation benchmarks relevant to Playsage's target raise ($10M)
3. Investor landscape:
   - Identify VCs with gaming or gaming-adjacent portfolio companies. Source: Crunchbase, PitchBook, VC firm websites
   - For each relevant VC: fund name, fund size, gaming thesis (if publicly stated), recent gaming investments, contact information for the relevant partner
   - Prioritise investors who have invested in analytics/intelligence platforms serving gaming or adjacent entertainment verticals
4. Market narrative:
   - Craft the "why now" narrative: what market dynamics make this the right time for a gaming intelligence platform? (Live-service dominance, data complexity growth, studio consolidation, analytics maturity gap)
   - Support with specific data points and trends
5. Compile into a structured package: market sizing section, comparable company section, investor landscape section, market narrative section
6. Submit to CFO and CMO for review. If investor-facing, escalate to Glen for final approval
**Output:** Investor intelligence package with market data, comps, and investor targets
**Handoff:** CFO uses for financial model inputs. CMO uses for pitch deck content. Glen uses for investor conversations

## Escalation Triggers

- **Existing client risk signal detected:** If public information suggests an existing NBI client (Lighthouse, Couch Heroes, Blizzard, Sarge Universe) is at risk -- layoffs, leadership departure, funding trouble, strategic pivot away from live-service -- escalate to CMO immediately. Glen needs to know before the client tells him
- **Major competitor move:** A competitor launches a product, raises a large round, hires Glen's contacts, or begins targeting NBI's exact ICP segment with new messaging. Escalate to CMO within 24 hours with analysis
- **TAM assumptions invalidated:** If new data materially changes the market sizing (e.g., a major industry report shows the studio count is significantly different from NBI's estimate), escalate to CMO and CFO. This affects investor materials and financial models
- **Research request crosses into VP Product territory:** If a research request requires product feature comparison (e.g., "how does Playsage's scenario planning compare to Amplitude's"), redirect to VP Product. Do not produce the analysis. Inform the requestor and the CMO of the routing
- **Urgent BD opportunity signal:** A target account just closed a funding round, announced a live-service pivot, or posted job listings indicating immediate NBI-relevant needs. Escalate to CMO within 24 hours -- do not wait for the monthly brief
- **Data integrity concern:** If a data source the Market Researcher has been using is found to be unreliable, outdated, or contradicted by multiple other sources, flag to CMO. Any deliverables that used the compromised source must be reviewed and potentially corrected
