## The Gaming Advisory and Intelligence Market

### Market Structure
The market for external advisory and intelligence services to game studios is fragmented and poorly defined. There is no single "gaming consulting" category tracked by market research firms. The Market Researcher must construct NBI's TAM from component data rather than referencing a single industry report.

The addressable market sits at the intersection of:
- **Management consulting** (gaming vertical practices at McKinsey, Bain, Deloitte, PwC, plus boutique gaming consultancies)
- **Production and operations consulting** (studios hire external expertise for scaling live-service operations, production methodology, tooling strategy)
- **Analytics and business intelligence platforms** (SaaS tools that studios subscribe to for player analytics, financial analytics, market intelligence)
- **Organisational and talent consulting** (studio health, team performance, leadership coaching -- this is Tom Rieger's domain)

### Market Size Inputs

The Market Researcher should maintain and refine these baseline data points. All figures require source citation and regular updates.

**Studio Count Estimates (as of mid-2025 data):**
- Total game studios globally: ~12,000-15,000 (Source: various industry estimates; Newzoo, LinkedIn company search, Steam developer data)
- AA studios (50-200 employees, $10M-$50M budget per title): ~800-1,200 globally
- AAA studios (200-2,000 employees, $50M-$200M+ budget per title): ~150-250 globally
- Mega-publishers with multiple studios: ~20-30 (EA, Ubisoft, Activision Blizzard, Take-Two, etc.)
- Studios with at least one live-service title: estimated 40-60% of AA+ studios

**Advisory Spend Estimates:**
- Average annual external consulting/advisory spend for an AA studio: estimated $200K-$800K (wide range; depends on whether studio is scaling, launching, or in maintenance)
- Average annual external consulting/advisory spend for a AAA studio: estimated $500K-$2M
- These figures include all advisory categories, not just what NBI offers. NBI's addressable share is a subset

**Analytics Platform Spend Estimates:**
- Average annual analytics platform spend for an AA studio: estimated $50K-$200K across all analytics tools
- Average annual analytics platform spend for a AAA studio: estimated $200K-$1M
- This includes player analytics, financial reporting tools, BI platforms, market intelligence subscriptions, and custom analytics infrastructure

### Market Dynamics

**Tailwinds for NBI:**
- Live-service model dominance increasing the operational complexity of running a game studio
- Data maturity gap: most studios acknowledge they need better analytics but lack in-house capability
- Studio consolidation creating larger, more complex organisations that need external advisory
- VC investment in gaming continuing (though more selective post-2022 correction), creating funded studios that need scaling advice
- Platform and regulatory complexity (App Store changes, EU Digital Markets Act, loot box regulation) increasing the need for strategic advisory

**Headwinds for NBI:**
- Tech layoffs and studio closures reducing the total buyer population in the short term
- Cost pressure on studios leading to advisory budget scrutiny ("can we do this in-house?")
- Free and low-cost analytics tools (Google Analytics, Unity Analytics, Firebase) meeting basic needs for smaller studios
- Macro uncertainty (interest rates, funding environment) making studios cautious on discretionary spend
- Competition from management consulting firms entering gaming as it becomes a larger entertainment vertical

## Prospect Research Framework

### ICP Fit Scoring Methodology
When profiling a prospect studio, score across five dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | 5 (Strong Fit) | 3 (Moderate Fit) | 1 (Weak Fit) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Size match | 25% | 100-500 employees, AA-AAA titles | 50-100 or 500-1000 employees | <50 or >1000 (indie or mega-publisher) |
| Live-service relevance | 25% | Active live-service titles generating revenue | Live-service title in development | No live-service titles, no plans |
| Buying signal strength | 20% | Recent funding, hiring in analytics/ops, public pain point statements | General growth, industry-standard hiring | Layoffs, cost-cutting, no expansion signals |
| Accessibility | 15% | Decision-maker in Glen's network or 1 degree away; attended same events | Decision-maker identifiable on LinkedIn; studio active at NBI target events | No identifiable decision-maker; studio not present in NBI's channels |
| Timing | 15% | Within 12 months of funding round; launching live-service title; publicly seeking advisory | Stable operations, no urgent trigger | Recently laid off staff; just completed major restructuring; in "maintenance mode" |

**Score interpretation:**
- 4.0-5.0: Priority target. CMO should pursue direct outreach
- 3.0-3.9: Strong candidate. Add to active nurture campaigns. Monitor for signal upgrades
- 2.0-2.9: Watch list. Add to monthly newsletter. Re-evaluate quarterly
- Below 2.0: Does not fit ICP. Do not pursue
