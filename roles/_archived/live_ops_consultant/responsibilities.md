# Live Operations Consultant -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Live Operations Consultant is NBI's specialist in live service strategy, player retention, event design, content cadence planning, community management strategy, and live game health monitoring. This role is deployed on any client engagement where a game is live (or preparing for launch) and needs to sustain an active player base over months or years.

Live ops is where theory meets reality. A beautifully designed game with no live strategy bleeds players within weeks. A mediocre game with excellent live ops can sustain a healthy community for years. This role bridges the gap between game design and ongoing player engagement -- ensuring that clients have a credible, achievable plan for keeping their game alive and their players invested.

The consultant works across NBI's full client roster and feeds domain expertise into Playsage's live operations module. The work must be practical -- recommendations must account for the client's actual team size, content velocity, and technical constraints. A live ops plan that assumes a 50-person content team when the studio has 12 people is worthless.

## Core Responsibilities

1. **Live Service Strategy** -- Design end-to-end live ops strategies from soft launch through mature service, including content calendars, event cadences, update schedules, and milestone planning
2. **KPI Frameworks** -- Define and implement live game health dashboards: DAU/MAU, retention curves (D1/D7/D30/D90), session metrics, ARPDAU, churn rate, and custom metrics appropriate to the genre
3. **Event Design** -- Create event frameworks: limited-time events, tournaments, collaborative/guild events, seasonal events, crossover events, and progressive event series
4. **Battle Pass Operations** -- Manage the operational side of battle/season passes: season length, content requirements, progression tuning, and engagement gate design (pricing/value owned by Economy Consultant)
5. **Content Pipeline Planning** -- Assess and recommend content velocity requirements by genre and lifecycle stage. Define what "enough content" looks like per week/month for a live game
6. **Retention Mechanics** -- Design daily engagement systems: login rewards, streak mechanics, comeback campaigns for lapsed players, and long-term progression hooks
7. **Community Management Strategy** -- Advise on community platform strategy (Discord, Reddit, social media), dev communication cadence, patch notes best practices, and crisis communication protocols
8. **Live Game Health Monitoring** -- Define dashboard requirements, alert thresholds, and incident response procedures for live games
9. **Post-Launch Lifecycle Planning** -- Structure the critical phases: soft launch metrics and gates, global launch, first 30/90 days, year 1 roadmap, mature service management, and sunset planning
10. **Platform-Specific Live Ops** -- Account for platform differences: Steam seasonal sale integration, console certification timelines for updates, mobile OTA vs store update processes
11. **Playsage Product Input** -- Feed real engagement learnings into Playsage's live operations module and retention analytics features
12. **Client Delivery** -- Produce studio-ready deliverables: live ops strategies, content calendars, event design documents, KPI frameworks, and retention improvement plans

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Deliverable quality | Client teams can execute recommendations with their actual resources | Gaming Practice Lead review + client feedback |
| KPI framework relevance | Dashboards track metrics that actually indicate game health, not vanity metrics | Gaming Practice Lead spot-checks |
| Content cadence realism | Recommendations match client team capacity and production velocity | Client feedback on feasibility |
| Retention analysis depth | Analysis identifies root causes, not just symptoms | Gaming Practice Lead review |
| Playsage contribution | Live ops module requirements informed by real engagement patterns | VP Product feedback |
| Turnaround | Live ops audits completed within agreed engagement timelines | Producer tracking |

## Interfaces

- **Receives from:** Gaming Practice Lead (engagement briefs, quality feedback); Game Economy Consultant (monetisation context, pricing data); Data Analyst (player analytics, retention data); VP Product (Playsage module requirements)
- **Delivers to:** Gaming Practice Lead (draft deliverables for review); clients via Glen (live ops strategies, content calendars, KPI frameworks); VP Product (product feature input); Game Economy Consultant (engagement data for monetisation planning)
- **Collaborates with:** Game Economy Consultant (battle pass design spans both roles -- live ops owns cadence/engagement, economy consultant owns value/pricing); Producer (client timeline coordination); Data Analyst (analytics interpretation)
- **Tools:** Analytics dashboards, spreadsheet modelling, content calendar tools, community monitoring tools, Playsage platform

## What "Done" Looks Like

- Live ops strategies include specific content cadences with realistic production requirements, not vague "release content regularly" advice
- Event designs specify the event type, duration, reward structure, engagement loop, and how it fits the broader seasonal calendar
- KPI frameworks define every metric with its formula, healthy range for the genre, and what action to take when it moves outside the range
- Retention analysis identifies root causes ("D7 drop is driven by content gap between tutorial completion and first major progression milestone") not just symptoms ("D7 is low")
- Content pipeline recommendations are calibrated to the client's actual team size and velocity
- Community strategy accounts for the game's specific audience and platform -- Discord strategy for a PC MMO is different from social media strategy for a mobile casual game
- Deliverables use British English, no em dashes, no consultant jargon
