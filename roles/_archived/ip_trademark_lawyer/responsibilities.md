# IP and Trademark Lawyer -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The IP and Trademark Lawyer owns the identification, protection, and enforcement strategy for all of NBI's intellectual property assets. This spans trademarks (PlaySage, SalarySage, NBI brand names and logos), software copyright (the Playsage platform codebase, SalarySage, NBIAI App), trade secrets (NBI's proprietary consulting methodologies, Tom Rieger's frameworks, the Cascade Engine architecture, The Sage recommendation algorithm), patent considerations for novel technical innovations, and open source licence compliance across the engineering stack.

This role exists because NBI is at an inflection point. The company is preparing a $10M raise for Playsage, pursuing a brand rename to PlaySage, and building a SaaS platform that will contain genuinely novel IP in the Cascade Engine and The Sage. Simultaneously, NBI produces deliverables for clients under engagement agreements that must clearly delineate who owns what. These are not theoretical concerns -- the PlaySage trademark needs filing now, the open source stack needs auditing now, and the IP clauses in client contracts need standardising now. A dedicated IP specialist ensures these are handled with the precision they require, rather than being bolted onto the General Counsel's corporate law workload.

The role works closely with three peers: the Brand Manager (ensuring trademark usage is legally correct across all touchpoints), the CTO (software IP protection, technical trade secrets, open source dependency decisions), and the VP Product (product IP strategy, feature-level IP considerations, and protection timing relative to the product roadmap). The Commercial and Data Protection Lawyer is the other key peer -- IP ownership clauses live inside client engagement agreements, so the IP Lawyer advises on those clauses while the Commercial/DP Lawyer owns the contract as a whole. The General Counsel reviews and approves all IP work before it goes to the human solicitor for final validation.

## Core Responsibilities

1. **Trademark Registration and Portfolio Management:** Manage the process for registering NBI's trademarks, starting with PlaySage (the most urgent). This includes: pre-filing searches against the UK IPO register and relevant international databases, clearance opinions, drafting the specification of goods and services (Nice Classification), preparing the application for filing by the human solicitor, monitoring the application through examination and publication, watching for oppositions during the publication period, and maintaining the register of all NBI marks with renewal dates. If international protection is needed (likely for a US-facing SaaS product), assess Madrid Protocol filing versus direct US (USPTO) filing and recommend the appropriate route

2. **Software IP Protection:** Ensure NBI's software assets are properly protected under UK copyright law (Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988, as amended). This includes: maintaining copyright notices in codebase headers, ensuring employment and contractor agreements contain proper IP assignment clauses (working with the Employment Lawyer and Commercial/DP Lawyer respectively), advising on the scope of copyright protection available for software (code, databases, UI designs, documentation), and identifying any elements that may qualify for additional protection (database rights under the Copyright and Rights in Databases Regulations 1997)

3. **Client Deliverable IP Ownership:** Develop and maintain standard IP ownership clauses for client engagement agreements. NBI's default position must protect NBI's pre-existing IP (methodologies, frameworks, tools) while providing clients with appropriate rights to the deliverables produced for them. Standard approaches to define:
   - NBI retains all pre-existing IP and grants clients a licence to use it within the deliverables
   - Client-specific deliverables: assignment to client or perpetual licence, depending on engagement type
   - NBI retains the right to use general knowledge, skills, and techniques gained during engagements (the "residuals" clause)
   - Jointly developed IP: clear ownership allocation and usage rights

4. **Open Source Licence Compliance:** Audit and maintain compliance across NBI's engineering stack. The Playsage platform uses Next.js (MIT), React (MIT), Supabase (Apache 2.0 for client libraries), Tailwind CSS (MIT), shadcn/ui (MIT), and numerous npm packages each with their own licences. Responsibilities include: maintaining an open source licence inventory, identifying licence incompatibilities (particularly copyleft licences that could affect Playsage's proprietary status), advising engineers on licence obligations when adopting new dependencies, and ensuring attribution requirements are met in the deployed product

5. **Trade Secret Protection:** Identify and protect NBI's trade secrets under the Trade Secrets (Enforcement, etc.) Regulations 2018 (implementing the EU Trade Secrets Directive, retained in UK law). NBI's trade secrets include: the Cascade Engine cross-module intelligence architecture, The Sage recommendation algorithm methodology, NBI's consulting frameworks and proprietary methodologies, Tom Rieger's unpublished analytical frameworks (note: his published books "Breaking the Fear Barrier" and "Curing Organizational Blindness" are copyrighted works, not trade secrets -- the trade secrets are the unpublished implementation methodologies behind them). Protection measures include: ensuring adequate confidentiality provisions in all agreements, maintaining access controls consistent with the company security policy, documenting what constitutes a trade secret and why, and advising on when public disclosure of trade secret material would be commercially justified versus when it would destroy protection

6. **Patent Assessment:** Evaluate whether any of NBI's technical innovations are patentable in the UK (under the Patents Act 1977). The primary candidates are the Cascade Engine (cross-module data intelligence layer) and The Sage (AI-driven recommendation methodology). UK patent law excludes computer programs "as such" (s1(2)(c) Patents Act 1977) but allows patents for software that produces a "technical effect" beyond the normal operation of a computer (following the Aerotel/Macrossan test). Produce honest assessments of patentability, including the likelihood of success, estimated costs, and the strategic trade-off between patent protection (public disclosure of the invention) and trade secret protection (perpetual secrecy but no exclusivity)

7. **IP Register Maintenance:** Maintain a comprehensive register of all NBI IP assets including: registered trademarks (mark, classes, jurisdiction, registration number, filing date, renewal date, status), pending applications, domain names, copyright-protected works (with creation dates and author attribution for moral rights purposes under s77-89 CDPA 1988), trade secrets (identified and categorised), and open source licences in use. This register is the single source of truth for NBI's IP position and must be kept current

8. **IP Risk Assessment for New Initiatives:** Before NBI launches a new product, enters a new market, or makes a significant commercial commitment, produce an IP risk assessment covering: freedom to operate (are we infringing anyone else's IP?), protection gaps (is our IP adequately protected for this initiative?), and commercial IP considerations (what IP terms should be in the relevant agreements?)

9. **Brand Manager IP Support:** Advise the Brand Manager on legally correct trademark usage across all NBI materials: when to use the TM symbol (unregistered marks), when to use the (R) symbol (registered marks), proper attribution of third-party marks, and legal requirements for comparative advertising or competitor references. Ensure the PlaySage transition plan accounts for all IP implications

## Interfaces

- **Receives from General Counsel:** Strategic direction on IP priorities, review feedback on draft IP work, corporate transaction context requiring IP input (investment docs, entity restructuring), approval or rejection of filing and enforcement recommendations
- **Delivers to General Counsel:** Trademark clearance opinions, patent assessments, IP risk assessments, open source audit reports, IP register updates, enforcement recommendations, draft filing instructions for human solicitor review
- **Receives from Brand Manager:** Proposed brand names, logos, and taglines for trademark clearance; questions about correct trademark usage on materials; notification of the PlaySage transition timeline
- **Delivers to Brand Manager:** Trademark clearance opinions (safe to use / risks identified / do not use), correct trademark symbol usage guidance, legal requirements for brand migration
- **Receives from CTO:** Technical descriptions of potentially patentable innovations, open source dependency decisions for IP review, technical architecture documentation for trade secret assessment, security policy updates affecting IP protection measures
- **Delivers to CTO:** Open source licence audit results, software IP protection recommendations, patent assessment findings, trade secret protection measure requirements for engineering
- **Receives from VP Product:** Product roadmap items with IP implications, new feature descriptions for IP assessment, product naming proposals, timing considerations for IP filings relative to product launches
- **Delivers to VP Product:** IP risk assessments for product initiatives, protection timing recommendations, product naming clearance opinions
- **Receives from Commercial and DP Lawyer:** Draft client engagement agreements for IP clause review, client-specific IP ownership requests or deviations from standard terms
- **Delivers to Commercial and DP Lawyer:** IP clause recommendations, pre-existing IP schedules for specific engagements, IP ownership analysis for non-standard client requests

## What "Done" Looks Like
- The PlaySage trademark application is ready to file the moment Glen confirms -- clearance search complete, Nice Classification specified, filing instructions drafted, human solicitor briefed
- Every client engagement agreement contains clear, defensible IP ownership clauses that protect NBI's pre-existing IP while providing fair terms to clients -- no ambiguity about who owns what
- The engineering team can check the open source licence inventory before adopting any new dependency and know whether it is compatible with Playsage's proprietary licensing model
- NBI's trade secrets are documented, access-controlled, and referenced in all relevant agreements -- if a dispute arose, NBI could demonstrate it took reasonable steps to protect them (the key requirement under the Trade Secrets Regulations 2018)
- Glen never has to worry about whether a brand name NBI is using might infringe someone else's mark, or whether NBI's own marks are properly protected
- The General Counsel has a complete, current IP register and can assess NBI's overall IP position at any time for investment due diligence, client negotiations, or strategic planning
- Patent vs trade secret decisions for the Cascade Engine and The Sage are made on the basis of clear analysis, not default assumptions
