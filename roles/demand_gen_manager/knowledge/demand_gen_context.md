## NBI's Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP: Gaming Advisory Services
- **Company type:** Game development studio
- **Studio tier:** AA to AAA (50-500 employees). Not indie (<50) and not mega-publishers (>5,000) unless engaging a specific studio within the publisher
- **Business model:** At least one live-service title (GaaS) -- in operation or in development
- **Geography:** English-speaking markets primarily (UK, US, Canada, Australia, Nordics, Netherlands). Expandable to Western Europe
- **Pain points:** Scaling live-service operations, player analytics maturity, production efficiency, team performance at scale, strategic planning for portfolio diversification
- **Budget:** Ability to commit £100K-£500K annually for advisory services
- **Decision-makers:** CEO/Studio Head, VP Production, VP Analytics/Data, Head of Operations

### Secondary ICP: Playsage SaaS Platform
- Same studio profile as above, plus:
- **Tech maturity:** Currently using spreadsheets, basic BI tools, or siloed analytics rather than a unified intelligence platform
- **Immediate need:** Cross-functional visibility (production, finance, player data, market intelligence in one place)
- **Price tolerance:** $1,500/mo (Starter) to $20,000/mo (Enterprise) depending on studio size and module usage

### Disqualifying Signals
- Studio primarily focused on hyper-casual or casual mobile games (different advisory needs, lower budgets)
- Studio in active distress (major layoffs in past 3 months, leadership exodus, funding runway under 6 months) -- not a good time to sell advisory
- Studio already locked into a competing platform with a multi-year contract
- Publisher-owned studio with no independent budget authority

## Email Marketing Best Practices for Gaming B2B

### What Works
- **Short, direct emails.** Gaming executives are busy. Get to the point in the first sentence. If the email requires scrolling on mobile, it is too long
- **Personalisation beyond {first_name}.** Reference the studio's games, their recent news, or the event where they were met. "I noticed Lighthouse just launched their live-service update" is better than "Dear Head of Studio"
- **Single CTA per email.** Do not ask them to read a blog post AND book a call AND sign up for a webinar. One action per email
- **Social proof from the gaming industry.** A testimonial from a studio head is worth ten from generic B2B companies. NBI's lack of published case studies is a critical gap
- **Timing.** Gaming studios run on irregular schedules (crunch, launch windows, post-launch recovery). Tuesday-Thursday sends between 9am-11am local time perform best for B2B. Avoid major game launch windows when the industry is distracted

### What Does Not Work
- **Generic B2B messaging.** "Unlock your team's potential with data-driven insights" reads as spam. Gaming people can smell corporate marketing from a mile away
- **Heavy sales pressure early in the funnel.** Do not ask for a meeting in the first email to a cold contact. Provide value first
- **Long-form content in emails.** Link to it. Do not paste it into the email body
- **Over-automated sequences.** If someone replies to a nurture email, they should be pulled out of the sequence and handled as a human conversation (flagged to CMO)
- **Ignoring reply context.** If a prospect replies asking a specific question, the answer must be human (or human-reviewed), not the next automated email in the sequence

## GDPR and Email Compliance Context

NBI targets studios globally but is a UK entity. Both GDPR (UK/EU) and CAN-SPAM (US) apply.

### GDPR Requirements (UK/EU Contacts)
- **Lawful basis for email marketing:** Consent (opt-in) or legitimate interest. Event contacts can be emailed under legitimate interest for a limited window (the "soft opt-in" for related services), but must include an easy unsubscribe
- **Data minimisation:** Only collect data you need. Progressive profiling over time rather than long forms upfront
- **Right to erasure:** Contacts can request deletion. The system must support this
- **Data processing records:** Maintain records of where each contact's data was collected and under what legal basis

### CAN-SPAM Requirements (US Contacts)
- **Unsubscribe mechanism:** Every marketing email must include a working unsubscribe link. Process within 10 business days
- **Physical address:** Every marketing email must include NBI's physical postal address
- **No deceptive headers or subject lines:** Subject lines must reflect the email content
- **Identification as advertising:** If it is a marketing email, it should be identifiable as such

### Practical Implementation
- Every email template includes: unsubscribe link, NBI physical address, and clear sender identification
- Every contact record tracks: acquisition source, consent status, consent date, and communication preferences
- Quarterly list hygiene: remove bounced addresses, honour unsubscribes, prune contacts with no engagement for 6+ months (re-engage campaign first, then remove)
