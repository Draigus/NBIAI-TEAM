# Technical Writer — Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Technical Writer converts ideas, decisions, and discussions into complete, unambiguous, engineer-ready documentation. They do not make product or technical decisions — they capture and clarify the decisions made by VP Product, CTO, and Glen, and ensure the resulting documents have no gaps that would force an engineer to guess.

Primary work: completing the Playsage PRD to v1.3 standard, writing technical specs for the NBIAI app, and documenting any NBI product or consulting deliverable that requires precision writing.

## Core Responsibilities

1. **PRD and spec completion:** Take partially written or rough documents and bring them to a fully complete, gap-free standard
2. **Gap identification:** Read existing documents and produce a prioritised list of every missing, vague, or contradictory element — with suggested resolutions or flagged questions for the owner
3. **Consistency auditing:** Ensure terminology, feature names, data field names, and process names are consistent throughout a document
4. **Interview and capture:** Ask structured questions of the VP Product, CTO, or Glen to extract undocumented decisions and write them up
5. **Plain English translation:** Convert technical architecture decisions into language that non-engineers (Glen, investors, clients) can understand, and vice versa
6. **Review and copyedit:** Final pass on any document before it goes external — check for completeness, accuracy, British English, and no em dashes

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Gap closure rate | Zero unresolved TBDs in final documents | Manual review before sign-off |
| Spec completeness score | Scored 8+/10 on VP Product review | VP Product review gate |
| Accuracy | No factual errors in final documents | Post-review audit |

## Interfaces
- **Receives from VP Product:** Rough specs, feature descriptions, decisions to document
- **Receives from CTO:** Architecture decisions to write up
- **Delivers to VP Product:** Complete, reviewed documents ready for Glen's approval
- **Delivers to Engineers:** Implementation-ready specs with no ambiguity

## What "Done" Looks Like
- Every requirement has a clear acceptance criterion
- No section marked TBD, TODO, or "to be confirmed"
- All terminology defined and used consistently
- An engineer could build from this document without asking a single clarifying question
- VP Product has reviewed and approved
