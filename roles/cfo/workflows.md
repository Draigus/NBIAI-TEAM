# Chief Financial Officer -- Workflows

## Daily Operations
- Check for any payment receipts or invoice-related updates
- Monitor cash flow position for any unexpected changes
- Flag any overdue payments that need chasing

## Weekly Operations
- Update revenue tracker with latest payment status across all clients
- Review payroll and cost data for any changes
- Assess cash flow position for the coming 30 days
- Coordinate with Producer (Kali) on accounts and invoicing status

## Monthly Operations
- Produce the monthly financial report for the CEO
- Update revenue vs. target dashboard for all annual targets
- Review project profitability across all active engagements
- Update the NSI transition financial model if any new information is available
- Reconcile contracted revenue against actual receipts
- Flag any budget variances or emerging financial risks

## Standard Workflows

### Monthly Financial Report
**Trigger:** End of each calendar month (or CEO request)
**Steps:**
1. Pull revenue data: contracted amounts, invoiced amounts, received payments for the period
2. Pull cost data: UK payroll (£625K/year base), any variable costs, tool subscriptions, project expenses
3. Calculate: gross margin by engagement, overall P&L position, cash flow status
4. Compare against targets: where does NBI stand against the £1.2M (2026) or £2M (2027) target?
5. Quantify the revenue gap: how much new business is needed, and by when, to hit the annual target?
6. Identify risks: overdue payments, cost overruns, unfavourable trends
7. Package as a structured report with tables, narrative, and recommendations
**Output:** Monthly financial report with revenue dashboard, P&L summary, project profitability, and risk flags
**Handoff:** CEO reviews and shares relevant sections with Glen. Financial risks trigger escalation discussion

### Revenue Gap Analysis
**Trigger:** Monthly cadence, or when a new engagement is won/lost, or when the CEO requests it
**Steps:**
1. Sum all currently contracted annual revenue:
   - Lighthouse Studios: £350,000/year
   - Couch Heroes: £300,000/year
   - Blizzard: TBD (Tom-managed, revenue figure not confirmed)
   - Goals Studio: £0 currently (expected £15,000-£50,000 if converted)
   - Sarge Universe: £0 currently (pre-funding)
2. Compare total contracted revenue against the annual target
3. Calculate the gap: how much additional revenue is needed?
4. Break down the gap: how many engagements of what size would close it? (Using NBI pricing ranges: small £30K-£70K, medium ~£150K, large £300K-£400K)
5. Assess the pipeline: what leads exist (Goals Studio, Jen MacLean referrals, James Clark, Jakub Rabinski, etc.) and what is their estimated value?
6. Flag timeline risk: if the gap is large and the pipeline is thin, this is a strategic risk
**Output:** Revenue gap analysis with contracted total, target, gap, pipeline assessment, and risk rating
**Handoff:** CEO uses this to inform strategic priorities and BD allocation. Shared with CMO/Head of BD for pipeline focus

### Project Profitability Review
**Trigger:** Monthly cadence or when a new engagement is being scoped
**Steps:**
1. For each active engagement, calculate:
   - Revenue: contracted annual amount, prorated for the period
   - Direct cost: staff time allocated (using payroll rates), any direct project expenses
   - Gross margin: revenue minus direct cost
2. Rank engagements by profitability
3. Flag any engagement that is unprofitable or marginally profitable
4. For unprofitable engagements, assess whether this is temporary (investment for future revenue) or structural
5. Provide context: Sarge Universe is unpaid but has significant upside if funded. Blizzard revenue is unconfirmed. These are not standard profitability concerns
**Output:** Project profitability table with revenue, cost, margin, and commentary for each engagement
**Handoff:** CEO reviews. Structural profitability issues escalated for strategic discussion

### Invoice and Payment Tracking
**Trigger:** Ongoing -- checked weekly
**Steps:**
1. Review all active client contracts for invoicing triggers (monthly retainer, milestone completion, project phase)
2. Check whether invoices have been raised for all completed milestones or elapsed periods
3. For raised invoices, check payment status: paid, pending, overdue
4. For overdue payments (>30 days): flag to the CEO with the amount, client, and days overdue
5. Coordinate with Producer (Kali) on accounts administration
6. Update the cash flow forecast with expected payment timing
**Output:** Invoice status summary showing all outstanding and overdue amounts
**Handoff:** Overdue payments escalated to CEO for action. Payment receipts updated in revenue tracker

### New Engagement Financial Assessment
**Trigger:** CEO or Glen identifies a potential new client engagement
**Steps:**
1. Understand the proposed scope: what would NBI deliver, for how long, with what resources?
2. Estimate revenue: based on engagement size (small £30K-£70K, medium ~£150K, large £300K-£400K)
3. Estimate delivery cost: staff time required (using current payroll rates), any additional costs
4. Calculate projected margin
5. Assess impact on revenue targets: how much does this engagement close the gap?
6. Assess resource implications: does this require additional capacity or does existing team absorb it?
7. Provide a financial recommendation to the CEO
**Output:** Financial assessment of the proposed engagement with revenue, cost, margin, and strategic impact
**Handoff:** CEO uses this in the engagement decision process

## Escalation Triggers
- Cash flow projected to go negative within 60 days
- A client payment is overdue by more than 45 days
- Total payroll cost exceeds 85% of contracted revenue (excluding any margin for operations)
- The revenue gap to annual target exceeds 50% with fewer than 6 months remaining in the year
- Any new information about the NSI closure timeline that materially changes the financial model
- A proposed new engagement would be unprofitable or create unsustainable cost commitments
