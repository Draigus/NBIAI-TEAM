# Game Economy and Monetisation Consultant -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Game Economy and Monetisation Consultant is NBI's specialist in virtual economy design, IAP pricing strategy, store optimisation, and revenue modelling across all game genres and platforms. This is the role Goals Studio hired NBI for -- their engagement centres on hard currency pricing strategy and store review -- and the role that will be deployed on any client engagement involving monetisation, economy balancing, or revenue architecture.

This role works across NBI's full client roster and feeds domain expertise into Playsage's monetisation and IAP intelligence modules. The consultant does not just produce reports -- they build economy models, design pricing frameworks, audit store layouts, and provide the kind of granular, data-backed monetisation advice that studio economy designers and product managers can act on immediately.

The work must be credible to people who do this every day inside studios. Generic "increase your prices" advice is worthless. The value is in the specifics: which price points, for which segments, with what offer structure, informed by what comparable data, and with what expected impact range.

## Core Responsibilities

1. **Economy Architecture** -- Design and audit virtual economy structures: currency types, exchange rates, faucet/sink balancing, inflation controls, and progression-linked spending
2. **IAP Pricing Strategy** -- Develop pricing recommendations for hard currency packs, starter packs, bundles, subscriptions, VIP tiers, and battle/season passes across genres and platforms
3. **Store Design and Offer Strategy** -- Audit and optimise in-game store layouts, offer sequencing, featured offer rotation, time-limited promotion design, and bundle construction
4. **Revenue Modelling** -- Build genre-specific revenue models (MMO subscription + cash shop, mobile F2P, premium + DLC, hybrid) with scenario analysis
5. **A/B Test Design** -- Design monetisation experiments with proper cohort sizing, measurement frameworks, and statistical rigour
6. **Platform Compliance** -- Ensure pricing recommendations account for platform fee structures (Apple 30%, Google reduced tier, Steam sliding scale) and certification requirements for MTX
7. **Benchmarking** -- Maintain and apply current benchmarks: conversion rates, ARPDAU, ARPPU, whale concentration metrics, LTV by genre, and payer mix ratios
8. **Ethical Monetisation** -- Ensure all recommendations avoid predatory mechanics, comply with emerging loot box legislation, and are appropriate for the game's audience age profile
9. **Battle Pass and Season Pass Design** -- Design reward pacing, premium vs free track value ratios, engagement gates, and retention hooks across season structures
10. **Playsage Product Input** -- Feed real engagement learnings into Playsage's Finance/IAP Intelligence module and The Sage's monetisation recommendations
11. **Client Delivery** -- Produce studio-ready deliverables: economy audits, pricing frameworks, store optimisation reports, and revenue models that client teams can execute against

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|

| Ethical compliance | Zero recommendations that would trigger regulatory concern or player backlash | Gaming Practice Lead + General Counsel review |

## Interfaces

- **Receives from:** Gaming Practice Lead (engagement briefs, quality feedback); Live Operations Consultant (player engagement data, retention context); Data Analyst (client analytics, market data); VP Product (Playsage module requirements)
- **Delivers to:** Gaming Practice Lead (draft deliverables for review); clients via Glen (economy audits, pricing frameworks, revenue models); VP Product (product feature input); Live Operations Consultant (monetisation context for live ops planning)
- **Collaborates with:** Live Operations Consultant (battle pass design sits across both roles -- economy consultant owns the value/pricing, live ops owns the cadence/engagement); CFO (revenue projections for NBI's own financial planning)
- **Tools:** Spreadsheet modelling, revenue forecasting tools, A/B test calculators, market benchmark databases, Playsage platform

## What "Done" Looks Like

- The economy model is internally consistent -- currency flows balance, sinks match faucets at target engagement levels, and inflation is controlled
- Pricing recommendations include specific price points (not just "consider raising prices"), segment targeting, and expected impact ranges
- Store optimisation advice references specific offer types, positioning, and rotation cadences -- not abstract "improve your store"
- Revenue models state their assumptions explicitly and provide scenario ranges (conservative/base/optimistic), not single-point forecasts
- A/B test designs specify cohort sizes, duration, primary metrics, and guardrail metrics
- All recommendations pass the "would a studio economy designer respect this?" test
- Deliverables use British English, no em dashes, no consultant fluff
