## Key UK Employment Legislation

### Employment Rights Act 1996 (ERA 1996)
The primary statute governing individual employment rights in the UK. Key provisions for NBI:

- **s.1 -- Written statement of employment particulars:** Every employee and worker is entitled to a written statement on or before their first day of employment (amended by the Employment Rights (Miscellaneous Amendments) Regulations 2019, effective 6 April 2020). The statement must include: employer and employee names, date employment began, date continuous employment began, remuneration and payment intervals, hours of work, holiday entitlement, place of work, job title or description, notice periods, expected duration (if fixed-term), collective agreements, pension, training entitlement, disciplinary and grievance procedures
- **s.86 -- Minimum notice periods:** After one month's service, the employer must give at least 1 week's notice. After 2 years' service: 1 week per year of service, up to a maximum of 12 weeks. Employee must give at least 1 week's notice after one month's service (contractual notice may be longer)
- **s.94 -- Right not to be unfairly dismissed:** Requires 2 years' qualifying service (s.108). Five potentially fair reasons for dismissal: capability, conduct, redundancy, statutory illegality, and some other substantial reason (SOSR)
- **s.98 -- Determining fairness:** Even if a fair reason exists, the dismissal must be fair in all the circumstances, including the procedure followed. Band of reasonable responses test (Iceland Frozen Foods v Jones [1983])
- **s.99 -- Automatically unfair dismissals:** Dismissal for pregnancy, maternity leave, making a flexible working request, whistleblowing, asserting a statutory right, trade union membership/activities, TUPE-connected (without ETO reason), and others. No qualifying service required
- **s.104 -- Asserting a statutory right:** Dismissal for asserting a statutory right (e.g., requesting written particulars, claiming minimum wage, asserting working time rights) is automatically unfair regardless of length of service
- **s.135-165 -- Redundancy:** Right to redundancy pay after 2 years' service. Statutory calculation formula. Duty to consult
- **Part IVA (ss.43A-43L) -- Whistleblowing:** Protected disclosures. Workers who make qualifying disclosures about wrongdoing are protected from detriment and dismissal. Compensation for whistleblowing dismissal is uncapped

### Equality Act 2010
Consolidates all anti-discrimination law. Key provisions for NBI:

- **Protected characteristics (s.4):** Age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage and civil partnership, pregnancy and maternity, race, religion or belief, sex, sexual orientation
- **Types of discrimination:** Direct (s.13), indirect (s.19), harassment (s.26), victimisation (s.27). Indirect discrimination: a provision, criterion, or practice that puts persons sharing a protected characteristic at a particular disadvantage. Can be justified if a proportionate means of achieving a legitimate aim
- **Employer liability (s.109):** Employer is vicariously liable for discrimination by employees in the course of employment, unless the employer can show it took all reasonable steps to prevent the discrimination. This is the legal basis for NBI needing equal opportunities and anti-harassment policies
- **Reasonable adjustments (s.20-21):** Duty to make reasonable adjustments for disabled employees. Failure to make reasonable adjustments is discrimination. Relevant for NBI: remote working may itself be a reasonable adjustment, but NBI should have a process for identifying when further adjustments are needed
- **Equal pay (s.66):** Right to equal pay for equal work. Relevant if NBI has employees doing similar roles at different pay levels
- **Recruitment (s.39):** Discrimination in recruitment is prohibited. NBI's hiring processes (even for small-scale hires) must not discriminate
- **Compensation:** Discrimination compensation is uncapped. Injury to feelings awards follow the Vento bands (updated annually by Presidential Guidance): lower band (less serious cases), middle band (serious cases), upper band (the most serious cases). Currently: lower GBP 1,100-GBP 11,200, middle GBP 11,200-GBP 33,700, upper GBP 33,700-GBP 56,200 (figures from 6 April 2023 -- check for annual updates)

### Working Time Regulations 1998 (WTR 1998)
Implements the EU Working Time Directive in UK law. Key provisions:

- **Reg. 4 -- Maximum weekly working time:** 48 hours averaged over a 17-week reference period. Workers can opt out in writing. The opt-out must be voluntary -- no detriment for refusing to sign
- **Reg. 10 -- Daily rest:** 11 consecutive hours' rest in each 24-hour period
- **Reg. 11 -- Weekly rest:** 24 consecutive hours' rest per 7-day period (or 48 hours per 14-day period)
- **Reg. 12 -- Rest breaks:** 20-minute break where the working day exceeds 6 hours. Must be taken during the shift, not at the start or end
- **Reg. 13 -- Annual leave:** 4 weeks (20 days for a 5-day week) -- this is the WTD entitlement. Holiday pay for these 4 weeks must include regular overtime and commission (Bear Scotland v Fulton [2015], Lock v British Gas [2014])
- **Reg. 13A -- Additional leave:** 1.6 weeks (8 days for a 5-day week) -- the UK domestic addition. Holiday pay for these days can be at basic rate only
- **Night work (Reg. 6):** 8 hours per 24-hour period on average. Night workers are entitled to free health assessments. Unlikely to be directly relevant to NBI's consulting workforce but worth noting
- **NBI-specific concern:** Remote working makes it harder to monitor compliance with rest breaks and maximum hours. NBI should have a policy requiring employees to record their hours and confirming that NBI does not expect employees to work beyond the WTR limits (unless they have opted out of the 48-hour maximum)

### Transfer of Undertakings (Protection of Employment) Regulations 2006 (TUPE)
Protects employees' rights when the business they work for transfers to a new owner. Key provisions:

- **Reg. 3 -- Relevant transfer:** Applies to transfers of an undertaking (or part) and service provision changes. The undertaking must be "situated in the United Kingdom immediately before the transfer" -- this is why TUPE does not apply to the NSI wind-down (NSI is a US LLC)
- **Reg. 4 -- Effect of transfer:** Employees transfer automatically to the new employer on their existing terms and conditions. The new employer inherits all rights, powers, duties, and liabilities connected with the employment
- **Reg. 7 -- Dismissal by reason of transfer:** Automatically unfair unless there is an economic, technical, or organisational (ETO) reason entailing changes in the workforce
- **Reg. 13-16 -- Information and consultation:** Both transferor and transferee must inform and consult with appropriate representatives of affected employees. Failure to consult: compensation of up to 13 weeks' pay per affected employee
- **Service provision changes (Reg. 3(1)(b)):** Relevant for NBI's consulting engagements -- if NBI provides a dedicated team to a client (like the Lighthouse embedded team) and that contract transfers to another provider, TUPE may apply, and the team may transfer to the new provider. Conversely, if NBI wins a contract with a dedicated team from the outgoing provider, those staff may transfer to NBI

### Immigration, Asylum and Nationality Act 2006 / Immigration Act 2014
Right to work checks. Key provisions:

- **s.15 IAN Act 2006:** Civil penalty for employing a person who does not have the right to work in the UK. Penalty: up to GBP 45,000 per illegal worker (first offence), GBP 60,000 (repeat offence). The penalty applies regardless of knowledge -- strict liability unless the employer conducted the prescribed right to work checks
- **s.21 Immigration Act 2014:** Criminal offence of employing a person knowing or having reasonable cause to believe they are disqualified from employment by reason of their immigration status. Penalty: up to 5 years' imprisonment
- **The statutory excuse:** Employers establish a "statutory excuse" against civil penalties by conducting the prescribed right to work checks before employment starts. The checks must be conducted in accordance with the Home Office Code of Practice (currently the "right to work checks: an employer's guide" document)
- **For NBI:** The NSI transition staff (Tom, Bryan, Jeff, Jessica) are US nationals. US nationals do not have automatic right to work in the UK. If they are to be employed by NBI Analytics Ltd and work in the UK, they need valid immigration permission. If they will continue to work from the US, the right to work check position may differ, but there are tax and social security implications. **This requires immigration law specialist input via the GC**

## IR35 Assessment Framework -- Detailed Indicators

### Indicators of Employment (Inside IR35)

| Factor | Description | NBI Relevance |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| No substitution right | The engager expects the specific individual to do the work. There is no right in the contract to send someone else, or the right exists on paper but has never been exercised and would not be accepted in practice | Amir, Ruan, Stavros are named individuals embedded in the Lighthouse team. Substitution seems unlikely in practice |
| Control over how work is done | The engager (or end client) directs not just what work is done but how it is done -- methods, processes, standards | Lighthouse likely controls day-to-day methods for its embedded analytics team |
| Control over when and where | The contractor works the client's hours at the client's location (or follows the client's remote working schedule) | Embedded full-time suggests working Lighthouse's schedule |
| Mutuality of obligation | The engager must provide work; the worker must accept it. Both parties are obliged to maintain the relationship | A 3-year contract suggests ongoing mutual obligation |
| Part and parcel | The contractor is integrated into the client's organisation -- attends team meetings, has a company email, is listed on the org chart, has line management responsibilities | "Embedded" strongly suggests integration |
| Exclusivity | The contractor works only for this one client and has no other clients | Full-time embedded suggests exclusivity |
| Length of engagement | Long-term continuous engagements look more like employment | 3 years is very long for a genuine contractor engagement |
| Payment structure | Paid a regular salary-like amount rather than per project or deliverable | Verify: are Amir, Ruan, Stavros paid monthly fixed amounts or per deliverable? |

### Indicators of Self-Employment (Outside IR35)

| Factor | Description | NBI Relevance |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Genuine substitution right | The worker can send a qualified substitute and the engager will accept them. This right has been exercised or is genuinely available | Does the NBI-Lighthouse contract allow substitution? Have substitutes ever been sent? |
| Financial risk | The worker bears genuine financial risk -- own equipment, own insurance, risk of non-payment for defective work | Do Amir, Ruan, Stavros provide their own equipment? Do they carry PI insurance? |
| Opportunity to profit | The worker can profit from efficient working or managing their own business | Unlikely in an embedded full-time arrangement |
| Multiple clients | The worker has other clients simultaneously | Unlikely if embedded full-time |
| Own business infrastructure | The worker has their own business: office, website, marketing, other employees | Verify: do these three have their own businesses or PSCs? |
| Project-based engagement | Engaged for a specific project with a defined deliverable and end point | A 3-year rolling engagement is not project-based |

### Assessment Conclusion Framework
- If the balance of indicators heavily favours employment: determination is INSIDE IR35. Advise restructuring
- If the balance of indicators heavily favours self-employment: determination is OUTSIDE IR35. Document the basis
- If borderline: escalate to GC with full analysis. Recommend external counsel review given the financial exposure. A wrong determination on three people over three years could represent substantial back-tax, NI, and penalties

## TUPE Triggers -- What to Watch For

TUPE may be triggered in NBI's business in the following scenarios:

1. **NBI loses a client contract with a dedicated team:** If NBI's contract with Lighthouse Studios ends and another provider takes over, and the same team was dedicated to the Lighthouse work, this is potentially a service provision change under TUPE Reg. 3(1)(b). The employees may transfer to the incoming provider
2. **NBI wins a contract with a dedicated team from an outgoing provider:** The reverse -- NBI may inherit employees from the outgoing provider
3. **NBI acquires a business or team:** If NBI acquires another consultancy or its clients, TUPE may apply
4. **Playsage spin-out:** If the Playsage business is transferred to PlaySage USA LLC and employees are dedicated to Playsage, this could trigger TUPE (though the cross-border element adds complexity -- TUPE applies to undertakings "situated in the United Kingdom")
5. **NSI wind-down:** As documented above, TUPE does not apply because NSI is not a UK entity and the undertaking is not situated in the UK. But this assessment must be documented

- Social security: which country's social security system applies? (US-UK Totalization Agreement may be relevant)

- US employment law termination: ending the NSI employment arrangements in the US. US employment is generally at-will, but there may be contractual protections, COBRA obligations, and state-specific requirements
- Entity liability: ensuring NSI's employment liabilities are properly discharged before dissolution (final pay, accrued vacation payout under NC law, health insurance continuation)
