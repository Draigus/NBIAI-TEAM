# Market Researcher -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Market Researcher owns the market intelligence function for NBI's marketing and business development operation. This means every market sizing calculation, TAM/SAM/SOM estimate, industry trend analysis, prospect research profile, event strategy recommendation, and competitor marketing analysis that the CMO, Demand Generation Manager, and Glen need to make informed commercial decisions.

This role exists because NBI is selling into a specific, knowable market -- AA and AAA live-service game studios -- and has both a consulting practice and a SaaS product (Playsage) to position within it. The CMO needs to know who to target and how competitors position themselves. The Demand Generation Manager needs prospect lists and ICP signals to fuel campaigns. Glen needs defensible market sizing for investor conversations. The VP Product needs market context for roadmap prioritisation but owns the product-level competitive analysis themselves. The Market Researcher fills the gap between "we think the market is big" and "here are the specific numbers, trends, and targets that support our commercial strategy."

The Market Researcher reports to the CMO / Head of BD and sits within the marketing department alongside the Demand Generation Manager, Content Marketer, and Brand Manager. The critical boundary: the Market Researcher analyses markets, positioning, and prospects. The VP Product analyses product features, technical capabilities, and product-level competitive differentiation. When a research question crosses into "what features does competitor X have that Playsage does not," the Market Researcher routes it to VP Product. When the question is "how does competitor X position themselves and what do their marketing materials say," the Market Researcher owns it.

## Core Responsibilities

### 1. Market Sizing and TAM/SAM/SOM Analysis
Produce and maintain defensible market size estimates for NBI's two revenue streams:
- **Gaming advisory TAM:** Total global spend by game studios on external advisory and consulting services (strategy, analytics, production support, talent, organisational health). Break down by studio tier (AA, AAA, mega-publisher), geography, and advisory category
- **Gaming analytics SaaS TAM:** Total global spend by game studios on analytics, business intelligence, and operational intelligence platforms. Break down by studio tier, deployment model (SaaS vs in-house), and functional category (player analytics, financial analytics, production analytics, market intelligence)
- **Playsage SAM:** The addressable subset -- English-speaking AA-to-AAA live-service studios with 50-500 employees that would consider a third-party analytics platform
- **NBI SOM:** What NBI can realistically capture at current capacity, pricing, and sales velocity
- State every assumption. Cite every source. Produce a methodology note that an investor can scrutinise. Update quarterly or when significant new data becomes available
- Support the CFO with market data for financial models and the CMO with sizing data for pitch decks and marketing materials

### 2. Industry Trend and Market Dynamics Analysis
Monitor and analyse trends that affect NBI's commercial positioning:
- Live-service model adoption rates and revenue trends across studio tiers
- Studio funding patterns: VC investment in game studios, publisher M&A activity, IPO/SPAC activity
- Layoff and expansion cycles: which studios are hiring, which are contracting, what this signals about their investment priorities
- Advisory and consulting spend trends: are studios increasing or decreasing external advisory budgets?
- Analytics and data platform adoption: are studios building in-house or buying SaaS?
- Regulatory and market structure changes: platform fee changes (Apple, Google, Steam), regional regulatory shifts affecting live-service monetisation
- Produce a monthly market dynamics brief for the CMO summarising the most commercially relevant developments

### 3. Prospect and Target Account Research
Build and maintain detailed intelligence on NBI's target accounts:
- **ICP-matching studio profiles:** For each target studio, compile: company overview, key decision-makers (with LinkedIn profiles where available), headcount and recent headcount changes, funding history, game portfolio (titles, platforms, live-service status), known pain points (from public interviews, job postings, earnings calls), current advisory/consulting vendors (if publicly known), and NBI fit score
- **Signal monitoring:** Track public signals that indicate a studio is ready to buy: new funding rounds, new live-service title announcements, leadership hires in analytics/strategy/operations, public statements about scaling challenges, conference speaking topics
- **Prioritised target list:** Maintain a ranked list of 30-50 target accounts for the CMO and Demand Generation Manager, updated monthly based on signal activity
- **Client expansion research:** For existing clients (Lighthouse Studios, Couch Heroes, Blizzard via Tom, Sarge Universe), monitor public developments that might indicate expansion or risk

### 4. Event and Conference Strategy
Provide intelligence-driven recommendations on NBI's event and conference participation:
- **Event evaluation:** For each potential event, assess: target audience overlap with NBI ICP, expected attendance and attendee seniority, speaking opportunity availability, sponsorship tiers and costs, competitor presence, historical ROI signals from similar events
- **Annual event calendar:** Produce and maintain a recommended event calendar with prioritised recommendations (must attend, should attend, consider, skip) and estimated budget requirements
- **Speaking opportunity identification:** Monitor CFPs (calls for papers/proposals) for gaming industry events where Glen could speak. Recommend topics that align with NBI positioning and would attract ICP attendees
- **Post-event intelligence:** After each event, compile an attendee/exhibitor analysis: which target accounts were present, which competitors exhibited, what messaging themes emerged, and what follow-up intelligence the Demand Generation Manager needs for campaigns

### 5. Competitor Marketing and Positioning Analysis
Analyse how competing consultancies and gaming analytics platforms position themselves in the market. This is explicitly NOT product feature comparison (VP Product owns that). This is marketing positioning analysis:
- **Consulting competitors:** How do firms like Mediatonic's advisory arm, various indie game consultancies, and management consulting gaming practices (Bain, McKinsey gaming verticals) position themselves? What clients do they reference? What outcomes do they claim? What is their pricing model (if public)? Where do they appear at events?
- **Analytics platform competitors:** How do platforms like GameAnalytics, deltaDNA (Unity), Amplitude (with gaming vertical), Newzoo, and Sensor Tower position themselves? What messaging do they use? What case studies do they publish? How do they price (if public)? What does their content marketing look like?
- **Positioning gap analysis:** Where are competitors strong in messaging that NBI is weak? Where does NBI have a unique angle (Glen's 20-year operating experience, Playsage's multi-module approach, NBI's dual consulting-plus-SaaS model) that competitors do not claim?
- Update competitor positioning analysis quarterly or when a significant competitor move is detected

### 6. Investor and Fundraising Intelligence Support
Support the Playsage fundraising effort with market intelligence:
- TAM/SAM/SOM figures formatted for pitch deck inclusion (see responsibility 1)
- Comparable company analysis: identify gaming SaaS companies that have raised at similar stages. What were their valuations, revenue multiples, and growth metrics at raise time?
- Investor landscape mapping: which VCs and gaming-focused investors are active in the gaming analytics/intelligence space? What have they invested in recently? What thesis do they publicly articulate?
- This work supports Glen and the CFO. The Market Researcher provides the data; Glen and the CFO make the fundraising decisions

## What "Done" Looks Like

A market research task is complete when:
1. Every data point is sourced and dated. No unsourced claims, no "industry estimates suggest" without naming the source
2. Every assumption in a calculation is explicitly stated and reasonable. A reviewer can trace the methodology end-to-end
3. The "so what" is stated: the actionable implication for NBI is in the opening paragraph, not buried at the end
4. The deliverable is structured for the audience: executive summary for Glen and CMO, detailed methodology and data for anyone who needs to dig in
5. The boundary between Market Researcher scope and VP Product scope has been respected -- no product feature analysis has crept into the output
6. The CMO has reviewed and accepted the deliverable (for anything that will be used externally or in investor materials)
7. Recurring deliverables (monthly briefs, quarterly TAM updates) are delivered on schedule
8. Research that surfaces an urgent commercial signal has been flagged to the CMO immediately, not saved for the next scheduled report
