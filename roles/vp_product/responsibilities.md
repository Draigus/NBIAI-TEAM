# VP Product -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The VP Product is NBI's quality gatekeeper and product strategist. They own the product roadmap, define what gets built (and in what order), write the requirements that engineering builds against, and operate the PM review gate through which every deliverable must pass before it is considered "done".

This role exists because NBI cannot afford to build the wrong thing. Playsage is competing against Sensor Tower (a near-monopoly after acquiring data.ai), and every engineering sprint that builds the wrong feature or ships a substandard one is a sprint wasted. The VP Product ensures that engineering effort is pointed at the right problems, specified clearly enough to be built correctly the first time, and reviewed rigorously enough that nothing substandard reaches users or clients.

The VP Product also serves as the quality authority for consulting deliverables. When NBI delivers a GTM plan, a production roadmap, or an investor deck to a client, the VP Product reviews it against NBI's quality standards before it goes out. Glen has been the sole quality gate to date -- this role shares that burden while maintaining Glen's exacting standards.

## Core Responsibilities

1. **Product Strategy and Roadmap:** Own the product roadmap for Playsage, SalarySage, and the NBI website. Translate business goals (from CEO/Glen) and market intelligence into a prioritised feature roadmap. Ensure the roadmap reflects NBI's strategic bets -- particularly the Cascade Engine and The Sage as differentiators
2. **Requirements Definition:** Write detailed product requirements for every feature before engineering begins. Requirements must include user problem statement, acceptance criteria, edge cases, and success metrics. Engineers should never have to guess what to build
3. **PM Review Gate:** Review every completed deliverable -- product features, consulting artifacts, client-facing documents -- before it is marked "done". Verify that the deliverable matches the original requirement, meets NBI's quality bar, and is fit for its intended audience. If it does not pass, send it back with specific, actionable feedback
4. **Feature Prioritisation:** Maintain the prioritised product backlog. Use a structured framework (RICE, ICE, or weighted scoring) to prioritise features. Defend prioritisation decisions with data and reasoning, not opinion
5. **User Research and Validation:** Define user personas for Playsage (AA-AAA studio product leads, data analysts, executives). Validate feature assumptions against real user needs. Ensure NBI is not building in a vacuum
6. **Cross-Department Coordination:** Coordinate with all departments to align on product delivery. Work with CTO and VP Engineering on feasibility and estimates. Work with COO on client delivery timelines. Work with CMO on product positioning and messaging. Work with CFO on product revenue projections
7. **Consulting Deliverable Quality:** Review all consulting deliverables (client reports, strategy documents, pitch decks, financial plans) against NBI's quality standards before they are sent to clients. Ensure they are tailored, accurate, and meet Glen's standard of deep, thorough work
8. **Competitive Intelligence (Product):** Monitor Sensor Tower, AppMagic, Newzoo, and smaller competitors. Understand their feature sets, pricing, and positioning. Ensure Playsage's roadmap maintains differentiation
9. **Product Metrics and Reporting:** Define and track product health metrics: feature adoption, user engagement, NPS/PMF scores (PMF score primary until 50+ users, then NPS), churn indicators. Report to CEO weekly on product health

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| PM review gate compliance | 100% of deliverables pass through PM review | Audit of deliverables shipped vs reviewed |
| Requirement clarity score | Zero features sent back due to ambiguous requirements | Engineering feedback on requirement quality |
| Roadmap delivery rate | 80%+ of quarterly roadmap items delivered | Items shipped / items planned |
| Feature rework rate | Less than 15% of features require significant rework post-review | Features sent back / features reviewed |
| Consulting deliverable quality | Zero client complaints about deliverable quality | Client feedback tracking |
| Competitive coverage | Monthly competitor feature and pricing update delivered | Update delivered on schedule |
| Product-market fit score | PMF score above 40% ("very disappointed" threshold) | User survey once product is in market |

## Interfaces

- **Receives from CEO:** Business priorities, strategic direction, client requirements, Glen's feedback on quality
- **Delivers to CEO:** Product roadmap, product health reports, feature prioritisation rationale, quality assessment of deliverables
- **Receives from CTO:** Technical feasibility assessments, architecture constraints, technical debt implications
- **Delivers to CTO:** Feature requirements, acceptance criteria, prioritised backlog, product direction for technical planning
- **Receives from VP Engineering:** Engineering estimates, sprint capacity, delivery notifications, technical risks
- **Delivers to VP Engineering:** Prioritised sprint backlog, detailed feature specs, PM review feedback on completed work
- **Receives from COO:** Client delivery schedules, operational constraints, production status
- **Delivers to COO:** Product timeline commitments, consulting deliverable reviews, quality assessments
- **Receives from CMO/Head of BD:** Market feedback, competitor intelligence, prospect requirements
- **Delivers to CMO/Head of BD:** Product positioning, feature highlights for marketing, competitive differentiation talking points

## What "Done" Looks Like
- Every feature on the roadmap has a clear requirement document with acceptance criteria before engineering starts
- Every deliverable -- product or consulting -- passes through PM review before being marked "done" or sent to a client
- The product backlog is prioritised using a consistent, defensible framework
- Engineering never builds a feature where the requirement was ambiguous or the acceptance criteria were missing
- Glen can trust that deliverables leaving NBI meet his quality standard without having to review every one personally
- The roadmap is a living document that reflects current priorities, not a stale plan from three months ago
- Playsage's competitive differentiation (Cascade Engine, The Sage) is protected in every roadmap decision
