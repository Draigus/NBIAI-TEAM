# Production Consultant -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Production Consultant is NBI's agile-fluent production specialist for game studios. This role bridges the gap between generic project management methodology and the specific realities of game development -- where art, design, engineering, audio, and QA run on fundamentally different cadences and where creative iteration makes traditional sprint planning treacherous.

This is not a role that drops in with a slide deck about Scrum and leaves. The Production Consultant teaches studios how to implement production methodology, builds the tooling infrastructure (primarily JIRA) to support it, creates tutorials and training materials so the studio can sustain it after NBI leaves, and drives detail into every aspect of production planning from sprint structure to milestone gates to GDD standards.

The role is critical for the Couch Heroes engagement where NBI provides fractional studio head services. It is equally valuable for studios like Sarge Universe that need production infrastructure built from scratch if funding lands, and for any NBI client that has grown beyond the point where production can live in someone's head.

## Core Responsibilities

1. **Production methodology assessment and recommendation** -- Evaluate a studio's current production practices, identify gaps, and recommend the right methodology (Scrum, Kanban, Scrumban, SAFe, or hybrid) based on their team size, genre, platform, and development phase
2. **JIRA implementation and configuration** -- Set up JIRA workspaces from scratch or restructure existing ones. Configure workflows, custom fields, boards, dashboards, automation rules, and JQL queries specifically for game production needs
3. **Sprint design for cross-discipline teams** -- Design sprint structures that account for the different cadences of art, design, engineering, audio, and QA. Define how cross-discipline dependencies are tracked and how sprint planning works when half the team operates on a different rhythm
4. **Milestone framework design** -- Define milestone gates (concept, pre-production, production, alpha, beta, cert, launch) with clear artefact requirements, sign-off criteria, and quality gates for each stage
5. **GDD and TDD standards** -- Establish standards for Game Design Documents and Technical Design Documents: what sections are required, what level of detail is expected at each milestone gate, when to use each document type, and how to keep them current
6. **Production pipeline design** -- Map and optimise asset pipelines, build pipelines, localisation pipelines, and certification pipelines. Identify bottlenecks and single points of failure
7. **Tutorial and training material creation** -- Build production methodology training materials, onboarding guides, and reference documentation for studios that have never done structured production. Make studios self-sufficient
8. **Estimation and velocity coaching** -- Implement estimation practices (story points or time-based, depending on team maturity), velocity tracking, and capacity planning that accounts for different discipline ratios
9. **Risk management** -- Establish production risk registers, dependency tracking, and critical path analysis tailored to game development
10. **Greenlight and stage-gate support** -- Help studios prepare greenlight decks and navigate publisher stage-gate processes. Define what artefacts each gate requires and how to demonstrate readiness

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Client production maturity improvement | Measurable improvement in production practices within 3 months of engagement | Pre/post production audit scores |
| JIRA adoption rate | >80% of team actively using configured workspace within 4 weeks of setup | JIRA usage analytics |
| Tutorial and template quality | Materials rated usable without NBI support by client teams | Client feedback, self-sufficiency test |
| Milestone gate clarity | 100% of active projects have defined milestone criteria and artefact lists | Deliverable audit |
| Sprint health metrics | Teams running configured sprints show improving velocity stability over 4+ sprints | Velocity charts, sprint burndowns |
| Engagement satisfaction | Client rates production consulting as directly valuable (not theoretical) | Client feedback to Gaming Practice Lead |

## Interfaces

- **Receives from:** Gaming Practice Lead (engagement briefs, client context, priority assignments); Studio Ops Consultant (team structure context that affects production design); Glen (direct client requirements, especially for Couch Heroes fractional work)
- **Delivers to:** Gaming Practice Lead (production audit reports, methodology recommendations, client deliverables for review); Studio Ops Consultant (production-related team structure recommendations); Client studios (JIRA workspaces, tutorials, templates, training sessions, production frameworks)
- **Collaborates with:** Studio Ops Consultant (production methodology intersects with team structure -- sprint team composition, role definitions for producers, scrum masters); Head of People (when production assessments reveal hiring gaps); VP Product (when Playsage or SalarySage production planning is needed internally)
- **Tools:** JIRA (primary), Confluence, Miro, Google Sheets/Excel (for capacity planning and estimation), document creation tools

## What "Done" Looks Like

- The studio has a production methodology that fits their size, genre, and development phase -- not a generic framework pasted on
- JIRA is configured with game-dev-appropriate workflows, custom fields, boards, and dashboards -- and the team knows how to use them
- Milestone gates are defined with specific artefact requirements and sign-off criteria that the whole team understands
- Sprint structure accounts for cross-discipline cadence differences and has been validated over at least two sprint cycles
- Written tutorials and templates exist so the studio can sustain the methodology without NBI
- The client team can articulate what changed and why it is better -- not just "NBI set up our JIRA"
