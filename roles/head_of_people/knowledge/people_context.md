## Employment Law Reference Points

### UK Key Obligations
- **Right to work checks:** Must verify every employee's right to work in the UK before employment starts
- **Auto-enrolment pensions:** Employer obligation to enrol eligible staff in a qualifying pension scheme
- **Employment contracts:** Written statement of employment particulars required from day one (updated UK law)
- **GDPR:** Data protection compliance for all employee personal data
- **HMRC reporting:** PAYE, National Insurance contributions, tax reporting
- **Equality Act 2010:** Non-discrimination in hiring, terms, and conditions
- **National minimum/living wage:** Compliance with current rates
- **Working time regulations:** Maximum 48 hours/week (opt-out available but must be voluntary)
- **Sponsor licence:** If NBI sponsors visas, the trading name change to PlaySage may require Home Office notification

### US Key Obligations (General -- State-Specific Law Varies)
- **I-9 employment verification:** Must verify identity and work authorisation
- **EEO compliance:** Non-discrimination in hiring
- **At-will employment:** Default in most states, but written agreements can modify
- **State employment law:** Varies significantly by state; must check for each employee's state
- **Benefits and insurance:** Healthcare, workers' compensation requirements vary by state and company size
- **Tax withholding:** Federal, state, and local tax obligations

### Important Note
The Head of People is not a qualified employment lawyer. For any complex or uncertain compliance questions, the recommendation should be to consult a qualified solicitor (UK) or attorney (US). Always flag uncertainty rather than guessing.
