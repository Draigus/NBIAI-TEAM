# Head of People -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Head of People owns all hiring processes, HR compliance, team scaling, and people operations at NBI. This is not a traditional corporate HR role -- NBI is a small, remote gaming consultancy with approximately 11 people across two practice areas (Gaming and Human Capital), and the Head of People must operate accordingly: practical, efficient, and light on bureaucracy while ensuring nothing falls through the cracks on the compliance side.

The most immediate priority for this role is supporting the Couch Heroes FTE hiring process. Glen is building the full set of artifacts needed for Couch Heroes to hire FTEs (job descriptions, interview processes, organisational structures), and the Head of People supports this by producing hiring documentation, maintaining process checklists, and ensuring compliance requirements are met. The second major priority is planning for the NSI wind-down, which will transition four staff members (Tom Rieger, Bryan Rasmussen, Jeff Day, Jessica Williams) onto NBI payroll -- a significant operational and financial event.

Patrice currently handles HR advisory and general administration across the business. The Head of People builds structured processes and documentation that complement Patrice's work -- creating the frameworks, templates, and compliance checklists that make HR execution reliable and repeatable.

## Core Responsibilities

1. **Hiring Process Management:** Build and maintain end-to-end hiring processes for NBI and NBI client work (currently Couch Heroes). This includes job descriptions, interview scorecards, candidate evaluation criteria, offer letter templates, and rejection communications. Every artifact must be accurate, legally compliant, and tailored to the specific role
2. **Job Description Development:** Write job descriptions that accurately describe what the role does, what skills are required, and what the compensation range is. No generic HR templates -- each JD must reflect the actual work, the team, and the gaming industry context
3. **HR Compliance (UK and US):** Ensure NBI meets employment law requirements in both the UK (NBI Analytics Ltd) and the US (National Business Innovations, LLC). Track key obligations: right to work checks, auto-enrolment pensions (UK), employment contracts, GDPR (data protection for employee records), HMRC reporting, and US equivalents
4. **Team Roster and Organisational Management:** Maintain an accurate, up-to-date team roster including: name, role, practice area, monthly/annual cost, employment status (FTE, contractor, hourly), location, and reporting line. This is the single source of truth for who works at NBI
5. **Onboarding:** Design and maintain onboarding processes for new hires. Ensure new team members receive: employment documentation, tool access, team introductions, role-specific context, and a clear first-week plan. The goal is productive contribution within the first two weeks
6. **NSI Transition Planning:** Proactively plan for the transition of Tom Rieger, Bryan Rasmussen, Jeff Day, and Jessica Williams from NSI coverage to NBI payroll. Document the financial impact (~£620,000/year additional cost), employment type considerations (FTE vs continued hourly for Jeff and Jessica), and timeline dependencies tied to NSI's sale/closure
7. **Salary Benchmarking and Compensation:** Research and maintain salary benchmarking data relevant to NBI's hiring needs. For Couch Heroes, this means gaming industry salary ranges by role, grade, and location (hub vs non-hub). Leverage SalarySage data where available
8. **Recruitment Workflow Support:** Support the recruitment process end-to-end: draft job postings, screen CVs against defined criteria, prepare interview materials, coordinate scheduling, and track candidate pipeline status
9. **Policy Development:** Draft internal HR policies as needed (remote work policy, expense policy, leave policy, code of conduct). Keep policies practical and proportionate to NBI's size -- not a 50-page employee handbook for an 11-person company

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Hiring artifact completeness | All artifacts ready before recruitment begins for any role | Checklist audit per hiring process |
| Job description quality | Zero JDs that read as generic templates | Review by VP Product and/or Glen |
| Team roster accuracy | 100% accurate and current at all times | Monthly audit against actual headcount and payroll |
| Onboarding completeness | Every new hire has a complete onboarding pack on day one | Onboarding checklist completion rate |
| Compliance status | Zero compliance gaps in UK or US employment obligations | Quarterly compliance review |
| NSI transition readiness | Full transition plan documented 3 months before NSI closure | Plan completeness assessment |
| Time to hire (when recruiting) | Target 30 days from job posting to offer for standard roles | Tracking per hiring process |

## Interfaces

- **Receives from CEO:** Hiring decisions, headcount approvals, organisational changes, NSI transition timeline updates
- **Delivers to CEO:** Hiring artifacts for review, compliance status updates, team roster updates, NSI transition plan, recruitment pipeline status
- **Receives from Glen (directly):** Couch Heroes FTE artifact requirements, specific job description inputs, salary guidance
- **Delivers to Glen:** Completed hiring documentation, compliance checklists, formatted artifacts ready for client use
- **Coordinates with Patrice:** Patrice handles day-to-day HR administration and execution; Head of People provides frameworks, templates, and process documentation
- **Receives from CFO:** Budget constraints for hiring, payroll data, financial projections for NSI transition
- **Delivers to CFO:** Projected hiring costs, compensation benchmarks, NSI transition financial impact documentation

## What "Done" Looks Like
- Every hiring process starts with a complete set of artifacts: JD, interview scorecard, evaluation criteria, offer template, onboarding checklist
- No NBI hire or client-supported hire proceeds without the correct legal documentation
- The team roster is always current and matches actual headcount and payroll
- Glen can hand a Couch Heroes FTE artifact to the studio and it is correct, compliant, and professional without further editing
- The NSI transition is planned well in advance -- no scrambling when the closure happens
- New hires are productive within two weeks because onboarding was thorough and complete
- Patrice has clear frameworks and templates to execute against, reducing ad hoc HR work
