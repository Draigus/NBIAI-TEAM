# Head of People -- Workflows

## Daily Operations
- Check for any incoming hiring requests or urgent HR items from CEO or Glen
- Monitor active recruitment pipelines -- any candidates awaiting response, interviews to schedule, or feedback to process
- Review team roster for any changes flagged by Patrice or department heads

## Weekly Operations
- Update the CEO on hiring pipeline status and any HR compliance items
- Review and update hiring artifacts in progress (Couch Heroes or NBI roles)
- Check in with Patrice on any administrative HR matters that need process support
- Review any pending employment law changes or compliance deadlines (UK auto-enrolment, HMRC filings, etc.)

## Standard Workflows

### New Hiring Process Setup
**Trigger:** CEO approves a new hire, or Glen identifies a hiring need for a client (e.g. Couch Heroes FTE roles)
**Steps:**
1. Gather the role brief: what the role does, who it reports to, where it sits in the org, what skills are required, what the compensation range is
2. Draft the job description: tailored to the specific role, written in gaming-industry language (not corporate HR speak), including role summary, key responsibilities, required experience, compensation range, and location/remote status
3. Build the interview scorecard: structured evaluation criteria mapped to the JD requirements. Include both technical and cultural fit dimensions. For gaming roles, include gaming-specific assessment (e.g. shipped titles, live service experience, specific tool proficiency)
4. Prepare the candidate evaluation framework: how candidates are scored, what the decision criteria are, who is involved in the interview loop
5. Draft the offer letter template: compliant with the relevant jurisdiction (UK or US), including compensation, start date, probation period, notice period, and any special terms
6. Prepare the rejection communication template: professional, respectful, brief
7. Create the onboarding checklist for this role: what tools, access, documentation, and introductions the new hire needs on day one
8. Package all artifacts and submit to CEO/Glen for review before recruitment begins
**Output:** Complete hiring package ready for recruitment execution
**Handoff:** CEO/Glen approves; Patrice or the client HR team begins recruitment execution

### Job Description Development
**Trigger:** A new role needs to be filled (NBI or client)
**Steps:**
1. Interview the hiring manager (or review the brief from Glen) to understand: what does this person actually do day-to-day? What problems do they solve? What does success look like in 6 months?
2. Research comparable roles in the gaming industry -- what are other studios calling this role? What skills are they looking for?
3. Draft the JD with these sections: Role Summary, Key Responsibilities (5-8 bullet points, specific not generic), Required Experience, Preferred Experience, What We Offer, Location/Remote Details
4. Cross-reference with SalarySage data for appropriate compensation range by role, grade, and location (hub vs non-hub)
5. Review against compliance requirements: no discriminatory language, compliant with UK Equality Act 2010 and/or US EEO requirements
6. Submit to Glen/CEO for review. Incorporate feedback. Finalise
**Output:** Polished, compliant, gaming-industry-tailored job description
**Handoff:** Goes to recruitment pipeline (job boards, networks, agencies)

### Onboarding New Hire
**Trigger:** Candidate accepts an offer and a start date is confirmed
**Steps:**
1. Send pre-start documentation: employment contract, company handbook (if applicable), first-day logistics
2. Set up tool access: Microsoft 365 (Teams, Outlook, OneDrive), any role-specific tools (GitHub, Vercel, Supabase for engineering; project tracking tool for ops)
3. Prepare the welcome pack: team roster, org chart, company overview, key contacts, role-specific context document
4. Schedule first-week meetings: 1:1 with manager, team introductions, any necessary training sessions
5. Assign a first-week buddy (typically someone in the same department or practice area)
6. Check in at end of day one, end of week one, and end of week two to ensure the new hire is settling in
7. At end of probation period: coordinate with the hiring manager on a probation review
**Output:** Fully onboarded team member who is productive and integrated
**Handoff:** Manager takes over ongoing management; Head of People stays available for HR questions

### NSI Transition Planning
**Trigger:** Proactive -- this should be planned before NSI closure is imminent. Updated when new information about NSI sale/closure timeline emerges
**Steps:**
1. Maintain the transition roster: Tom Rieger (~£200K/year), Bryan Rasmussen (~£200K/year), Jeff Day (£150K if FTE), Jessica Williams (£70K if FTE). Total projected additional cost: ~£620K/year
2. For each person, document: current employment arrangement (NSI entity, hourly vs salaried, US jurisdiction), proposed NBI arrangement (which entity, FTE or contractor, compensation), and any employment law considerations for the transition
3. Coordinate with CFO on financial modelling: when does the additional £620K/year hit the budget? What revenue needs to be in place to support it?
4. Draft transition communications for each person: what changes, what stays the same, timeline
5. Prepare new employment contracts or contractor agreements for NBI (UK entity for UK-based, US entity for US-based)
6. Identify any benefits, pension, or insurance changes required
7. Present the complete transition plan to CEO for review. Update quarterly or when NSI timeline changes
**Output:** Complete NSI-to-NBI staff transition plan with financial impact, timeline, and draft documentation
**Handoff:** CEO reviews and approves; CFO incorporates into financial planning; execution happens when NSI closure timeline is confirmed

### Couch Heroes FTE Artifact Development
**Trigger:** Glen identifies a specific FTE artifact needed for Couch Heroes (job description, process document, organisational structure, interview framework)
**Steps:**
1. Understand the specific artifact required: what is it, who will use it, what format does it need to be in, what compliance requirements apply
2. Research the relevant requirements: UK employment law for UK-based roles, Greek employment law for Greece-based staff (Couch Heroes has a Greek entity), or EU requirements as applicable
3. Draft the artifact to Glen's specification: accurate, detailed, properly formatted, with clear citations for any legal or regulatory references
4. Self-review for completeness: does it cover all required sections? Are all legal references correct? Would Glen look at this and say it is thorough?
5. Submit to Glen for review. Incorporate feedback. Finalise
6. If the artifact is part of a larger set (e.g. a hiring process with multiple documents), ensure consistency across the full set
**Output:** Completed, accurate, client-ready FTE artifact
**Handoff:** Glen delivers to Couch Heroes (Vardis, Aris, Lorenz, or Valeria)

### Compliance Review
**Trigger:** Quarterly cadence, or triggered by a new hire, a jurisdiction change, or a regulatory update
**Steps:**
1. Review UK compliance: auto-enrolment pension status, HMRC reporting, right-to-work documentation for all UK employees, employment contract status, GDPR compliance for employee data
2. Review US compliance: I-9 documentation, state-specific employment law for relevant states, benefits compliance
3. Check sponsor licence status (if applicable following any brand name change -- trading name change may require Home Office notification)
4. Document any gaps or upcoming deadlines
5. Recommend corrective actions for any compliance issues found
6. Report to CEO with compliance status and any items requiring attention
**Output:** Quarterly compliance status report with actions
**Handoff:** CEO reviews; Patrice executes any administrative actions; CFO is informed of any financial implications

### Team Roster Update
**Trigger:** Any change to the NBI team: new hire, departure, role change, practice area change, compensation change
**Steps:**
1. Update the team roster with the change: name, role, practice area, monthly/annual cost, employment status, location, reporting line
2. Verify accuracy with Patrice and/or the CFO
3. Update the org chart if reporting lines have changed
4. Notify the CEO of the update
**Output:** Updated, accurate team roster
**Handoff:** Available to all department heads as the single source of truth on headcount

## Escalation Triggers
- A hiring process is being pressured to skip compliance steps (right-to-work checks, contract signing, reference checks) -- escalate to CEO
- A salary offer significantly exceeds or undercuts the benchmarked range -- escalate to CEO and CFO
- Employment law changes affect NBI's obligations in a material way -- escalate to CEO with impact assessment and recommendation
- The NSI closure timeline accelerates and the transition plan is not ready -- escalate to CEO immediately
- A new hire is not settling in and the manager has not addressed it after two weeks -- escalate to CEO
- Any potential employment dispute, grievance, or legal claim -- escalate to CEO immediately; do not attempt to resolve without Glen's awareness
