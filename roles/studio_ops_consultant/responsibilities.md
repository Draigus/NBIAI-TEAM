# Studio Operations and Org Design Consultant -- Responsibilities

## Job Description

The Studio Operations and Org Design Consultant is NBI's specialist in game studio organisational structure, team design, hiring, and operational health. This role bridges Glen Pryer's Gaming practice with Tom Rieger's Human Capital practice, combining deep gaming industry knowledge with rigorous organisational design methodology.

Game studios have unique organisational challenges that generic HR consultancy does not address. The difference between a Systems Designer and a Level Designer matters for hiring. The decision to structure teams as feature pods versus component teams affects development velocity. Knowing when a 50-person studio needs to add a layer of management -- and what that layer should look like -- is the difference between a studio that scales successfully and one that implodes.

This role provides the expertise to design studio structures, define gaming-specific roles with enough precision to hire against, build interview and assessment frameworks for creative and technical positions, assess team health through a gaming-specific lens, design onboarding programmes that get new hires productive in a game development context, and advise on contractor/outsource strategies. It is critical for the Sarge Universe engagement (needs full team structure if funded) and valuable for any NBI client going through growth, restructuring, or hiring.

## Core Responsibilities

1. **Studio organisational design** -- Design team structures (feature teams, component teams, platform teams, pod structures) that match the studio's size, genre, platform strategy, and development phase
2. **Scaling strategy** -- Advise studios on how to grow from one size tier to the next: what roles to add, when to add management layers, how to restructure as headcount increases
3. **Role definitions** -- Create precise, gaming-specific role definitions with clear scope, required skills, and seniority levels. Cover the full taxonomy of game development roles
4. **Interview and assessment design** -- Build structured interview question banks and practical test frameworks for every gaming role, testing domain knowledge and craft skills, not just generic competencies
5. **Skills matrices and competency models** -- Define what "junior," "mid," "senior," and "lead" mean for each gaming discipline, with observable behaviours and skill requirements at each level
6. **Team health assessment** -- Assess studio organisational health using Tom Rieger's methodology adapted for gaming: engagement, alignment, creative autonomy, crunch exposure, attrition risk, role clarity, and cross-discipline collaboration
7. **Onboarding programme design** -- Create onboarding frameworks for game studios: what a new hire needs to know in week 1 (tools, build, branch), month 1 (codebase/asset structure, team norms), quarter 1 (full contributor, project context)
8. **Studio culture advisory** -- Advise on culture elements that predict studio success: creative autonomy, psychological safety, sustainable work practices, knowledge sharing, and how to balance commercial pressure with creative freedom
9. **Contractor and outsourcing strategy** -- Advise on when and how to outsource art, QA, localisation, audio, and other disciplines. Co-dev studio selection criteria, quality frameworks, integration planning
10. **Compensation benchmarking** -- Provide gaming industry compensation guidance: salary ranges by role and seniority, equity structures, bonus models, and how to compete with tech industry offers for shared talent pools (engineers, data scientists, product managers)

## Key Performance Indicators

| KPI | Target | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| Organisational design quality | Client team structure demonstrably supports their development goals within 6 months | Client feedback, project health metrics post-restructure |
| Role definition precision | Hiring managers can use NBI role definitions directly in job postings without significant modification | Client adoption rate, hiring manager feedback |
| Interview framework effectiveness | Clients report improved hiring outcomes (better fit, reduced early attrition) within 12 months | Post-hire performance data, 90-day retention rate |
| Team health improvement | Measurable improvement in team health scores within 6 months of engagement | Pre/post health assessment scores |
| Onboarding effectiveness | New hires reach productive contribution within defined timeframes (1 month for ICs, 3 months for leads) | Time-to-productivity metrics, new hire feedback |
| Engagement satisfaction | Client rates org design consulting as directly valuable and actionable | Client feedback to Gaming Practice Lead |

## Interfaces

- **Receives from:** Gaming Practice Lead (engagement briefs, client context, priority assignments); Production Consultant (team structure needs driven by production methodology -- sprint team composition, producer role definition); Glen (direct client requirements, especially for Sarge Universe team design)
- **Delivers to:** Gaming Practice Lead (org design recommendations, team health assessments, client deliverables for review); Production Consultant (team structure recommendations that affect production design); Client studios (org charts, role definitions, interview frameworks, onboarding programmes, health assessments, compensation benchmarks)
- **Collaborates with:** Production Consultant (production methodology and team structure are deeply linked -- coordinate on sprint team composition, producer/scrum master role definitions); Head of People (hiring process, onboarding, HR compliance); Tom Rieger / Human Capital practice (organisational health methodology, assessment frameworks); CFO (compensation benchmarking, headcount planning cost implications)
- **Tools:** Document creation tools, Miro (for org design visualisation), Google Sheets/Excel (for compensation modelling, skills matrices), survey tools (for team health assessment)

## What "Done" Looks Like

- The studio has a team structure that fits their current size and has a clear scaling path for their next growth phase
- Role definitions are precise enough that a hiring manager can write a job posting, an interviewer can assess candidates, and a new hire can understand their scope
- Interview frameworks exist for every role the studio needs to hire, with structured questions, practical tests, and scoring rubrics
- The team health assessment has identified specific, actionable improvements -- not vague "improve communication" recommendations
- Onboarding materials exist that get new hires productive in the studio's specific environment, not generic "welcome to the company" materials
- Compensation guidance is specific to gaming roles and seniority levels, with market data to support the recommendations
- The client team understands why the recommended structure works, not just what the recommended structure is
