4. Key Learnings & Insights

Studio Transformation

[Apr 13] Nia Kiigan's independent assessment validated Glen's internal audit exactly.
Team believed mid-production; Glen confirmed pre-production. Art significantly ahead creates "illusion of a full game" while core systems, design, and tech lag. Studio built components out of sequence; prototype mode persisted too long.
[Apr 7] 50%+ of features have missing documentation; existing docs often not applicable.
Most design team members never worked on games before (except recent hires). Too much time spent explaining basics vs executing. Design team identified as weakest department across all assessments.
[Mar 25] Studio workflow completely backwards.
Lore created after art completion, art created without game design input, characters completed before game mechanics defined. Robin unaware of completed goblin assets until December. 600-page GDD too unwieldy for practical use.
[Apr 17] "The studio's problem isn't bad people, it's invisible systems."
Root cause analysis reveals process failures, not individual failures. Portal Peak instancing discussed for months but poor communication flow. Only Portal Peak has proper documentation with Google art boards; other zones have never had detailed briefs.
[May 15] 3-month timeline was premature sharing, not a commitment.
Art leads panicked hearing "3 months" without context of planned scope cuts, headcount additions, and outsourcing. Incomplete information creates anxiety faster than no information.

People & Management

[Apr 24] Mustafa breakthrough: isolation and lack of feedback is the root cause.
"I don't have anyone to talk to" — the admission. Regular 1:1s and direct communication fix engagement. Glen's silence wasn't patience — it was enabling drift. But by May 21, pattern continued: failed documentation sign-off for 2 weeks. "One thread away" from termination.
[Mar 25] Addressing interpersonal conflicts increased team productivity 35-40%.
Clear boundaries and consequences for toxic behaviour had measurable impact. Dragon terminated, Clara and Ella identified as underperforming.
[Apr 8] Lost critical code review workflow migrating GitHub/DevOps to Perforce.
Previously Rainer approved all commits. Now anyone can push directly without review. Rainer "made it clear he's not the person to go to anymore" after losing lead. Mustafa "runs around in panic mode." ~10% build failure rate.

Hiring Patterns

[Mar-May] Most successful hires came through direct sourcing, not the agency.
Simon, Gary, Graham — all direct. Jack (Skill Search) actively damaged the pipeline: shared negative feedback with candidates, told candidates CH "has zero idea what it's doing."
[Apr 9] 300K+ candidates rarely accept startup risk even with equity.
Neil (Epic, ~300K) interested but financial downside too large. The 220-240K ceiling is real unless someone cashed out elsewhere (Maurizio model).
[Apr 17] EMI options structurally impossible — affects ALL senior hires.
20+ vehicle corporate structure with Cyprus-based shareholders. Connected persons hold >50% control. Only unapproved options available (~25% income tax on exercise). 3% effectively worth ~1.5% after tax. Dual class/weighted voting rights could potentially solve — needs legal investigation.
[Apr 17] "Avengers team" hiring is a fundraising asset, not just a production asset.
Gary (23yr Blizzard), Charlie/Tanya (AC/Epic), Simon (Sea of Thieves) — recognisable AAA talent assembling at a startup signals quality to investors.

Business & Industry

[May 4] AI knowledge management system IS the scalability framework.
Zero-code implementation: client KBs, BD pipelines, delivery templates, reporting automation. Methodology that replicates without additional senior headcount.
[Apr 8] AI implementation failure market: $10-20B potential.
MIT study: 95% AI implementation failure rate. Failure modes: poor planning (40%), change management (30%), vendor overselling (20%), tech issues (10%). Enterprise spending $50B+ annually. NBI positioning: vendor-neutral consulting.
[May 8] Industry follows 7-year expansion/contraction cycles, potentially extending to 11-12 years due to AI.
Publishers squeeze successful studios until destruction, cycle repeats. Roblox under threat: takes 70% revenue (vs Steam 30%), 200+ child safety cases annually.
[Mar 31] PlayGOALS: ~50% D1 retention on PlayStation. Launch May 14, multi-platform.
400,000 players tested since 2022. Monetisation is the unproven element — pricing architecture needs external validation.
[Apr 16] Simon Woodroffe: "Social games are 8x more sticky due to friend connections."
Sea of Thieves anchor handbrake turn: 0.5 days coding, 99.5 days art polish — illustrates production reality. Players choose games based on which friends are available, not traditional "quality."
