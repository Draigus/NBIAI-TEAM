# Client Delivery Pipeline

## Purpose
Defines how NBI onboards, runs, and closes out client engagements. The COO owns delivery health. Glen remains the relationship owner and primary point of contact for all clients.

---

## Engagement Types

| Type | Description | Current examples |
|---|---|---|
| Fractional C-Level | Glen embedded as senior leader at the studio | Couch Heroes (Fractional Studio Head) |
| Embedded Team | NBI staff embedded full-time at client | Lighthouse Studios (Amir, Ruan, Stavros) |
| Project-Based | Fixed-scope deliverable | Sarge Universe (pitch deck, DD deck, financial plan) |
| Ongoing Advisory | Regular reporting or ongoing strategic support | Blizzard (Tom-managed) |

## Onboarding Workflow

**Trigger:** New engagement agreed and signed (or informal agreement in place for fractional/unpaid work)
**Owner:** COO + Producer

### Steps
1. **Create project folder** in `projects/` using the `_template/` structure
2. **Populate project brief** with: client name, Glen's role, scope, team assigned, communication channel, key contacts, timeline, and success criteria
3. **Populate Tier 3 knowledge** with: client background, current titles/products, recent news, key contacts and their roles, any constraints or sensitivities
4. **Confirm communication channels** — which tool NBI uses with this client (Slack, Teams, Telegram, email)
5. **Set up reporting cadence** — how often COO reviews delivery health
6. **Brief the delivery team** — whoever is working on the engagement gets the project brief and Tier 3 knowledge
7. **Confirm first milestone** — what is the first deliverable and when is it due

## Active Delivery Management

**Owner:** COO (oversight), Producer (day-to-day tracking)

### Weekly Rhythm
- Producer reviews all active tasks across each engagement
- Any blockers or at-risk milestones flagged to COO same day
- COO updates delivery health (Green/Amber/Red) for each client
- Weekly client delivery report produced (see reporting pipeline)

### Escalation Triggers
- Milestone at risk of slipping → COO flags to CEO and Glen immediately
- Client expresses dissatisfaction → COO escalates to Glen same day
- Scope is expanding beyond agreed terms → COO flags to Glen before any commitment is made
- Embedded team member has issues → COO coordinates with Glen and Head of People

### Communication with Clients
- All client-facing communications drafted by the relevant agent
- Approval gate: Glen reviews and approves before sending (see approval_gates.md)
- Glen sends the communication — agents do not send directly to clients

## Milestone and Deliverable Management

**Owner:** Producer

- Each active engagement has a milestone list in its project folder
- Producer tracks milestone status and alerts COO when milestones are at risk
- Deliverables requiring client delivery go through PM review (VP Product) then Glen approval
- Producer confirms delivery after Glen sends

---

## Engagement Close-Out

**Trigger:** Engagement reaches natural end or client gives notice

**Owner:** COO + CMO

### Steps
1. **Produce final deliverable** (if applicable) — reviewed by VP Product and approved by Glen
2. **Document learnings** — what worked, what was difficult, outcome metrics if available
3. **CMO captures case study** — Glen confirms what can be shared publicly
4. **Head of People** — if embedded staff return, COO coordinates transition
5. **CFO confirms** — all invoices settled, engagement closed in financial records
6. **CMO updates** — client carousel on website, past clients list, BD learnings
7. **CEO flags** — renewal or expansion opportunity to Glen if applicable

---

## Delivery Principles

- **Glen is always the relationship owner.** Agents support delivery; they do not manage client relationships directly
- **Surface problems early.** An Amber flag given early is far better than a Red flag given late
- **Scope changes require Glen's input.** Any expansion of what NBI is doing for a client must be explicitly acknowledged and agreed — never drift into extra work without a conversation
- **Quality over speed.** NBI's reputation is its only moat. A missed deadline is survivable. A poor deliverable is not
