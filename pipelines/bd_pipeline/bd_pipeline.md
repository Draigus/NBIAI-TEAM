# Business Development Pipeline

## Purpose
Defines how NBI identifies, qualifies, pursues, and converts new client leads. All outreach drafts require Glen's approval before sending. The CMO owns this pipeline; the COO ensures delivery capacity exists before commitments are made.

---

## Pipeline Stages

### Stage 0 — Lead Identification
**Owner:** CMO
**Trigger:** New contact from event (e.g. GDC), referral, inbound enquiry, or proactive research
**Actions:**
- Log the lead in the client tracker with: name, company, role, source, date, initial context
- Assess fit against target client profile (AA/AAA studio, already funded, C-level buyer)
- Flag to CEO if high-priority or time-sensitive

**Disqualify if:** pre-revenue indie with no funding, not a C-level contact, geography with no engagement model fit

---

### Stage 1 — Research and Qualification
**Owner:** CMO + Data Analyst (for market/company research)
**Trigger:** Lead passes initial fit check
**Actions:**
- Research the studio: size, funding status, titles in development or live, key contacts
- Identify the pain point most likely to resonate (production crisis, GTM planning, analytics gap, fundraising)
- Identify the right NBI service angle (fractional advisory, embedded analytics, investor readiness, etc.)
- Prepare a one-page lead brief: company background, opportunity thesis, proposed angle, suggested next step

**Output:** Lead brief reviewed by CEO and CMO
**Handoff:** CEO approves pursuit → move to Stage 2

---

### Stage 2 — Outreach Preparation
**Owner:** CMO
**Trigger:** CEO approves pursuit
**Actions:**
- Draft personalised outreach message (email or LinkedIn DM) tailored to the lead's specific situation
- Reference concrete NBI proof points relevant to their context (e.g. "we restructured Star Wars Battlefront 2 progression and lifted performance 40%")
- Use gaming-native language — no consultant-speak
- Include a clear, low-friction call to action (15-minute call, not "get started")
- Present draft to Glen for approval with: recipient, channel, full message, and context

**Approval gate:** Glen approves the outreach before it is sent
**Output:** Approved outreach message

---

### Stage 3 — First Contact
**Owner:** Glen (sends), CMO (prepares)
**Trigger:** Glen approves outreach
**Actions:**
- Glen sends the outreach via the appropriate channel (email from Outlook, LinkedIn, etc.)
- CMO logs the send date and sets a follow-up reminder (5 business days if no response)
- If response received: brief CEO and Glen, prepare for discovery call

---

### Stage 4 — Discovery Call
**Owner:** Glen
**Trigger:** Lead agrees to a call
**Actions (pre-call, CMO):**
- Prepare call brief: what we know about the studio, likely pain points, questions to ask, NBI services to mention
- Include relevant proof points and past client analogues
- Brief Glen on the lead before the call

**Actions (post-call, CMO):**
- Capture call notes from Glen
- Summarise: what they need, fit assessment, proposed next step, budget signals
- Update lead status and CRM
- Identify follow-up actions and draft any follow-up communications for Glen's approval

---

### Stage 5 — Proposal / Scoping
**Owner:** Glen + COO
**Trigger:** Discovery call confirms genuine opportunity
**Actions:**
- COO assesses delivery capacity (can NBI take this on given current workload?)
- CMO drafts the proposal structure/outline
- Glen defines the scope and commercial terms
- COO validates the resourcing model
- CFO reviews financial terms (pricing, payment structure, profitability)
- Proposal drafted for Glen's review and sign-off
- Glen sends the proposal

**Approval gate:** Glen approves proposal before it goes to client

---

### Stage 6 — Negotiation and Close
**Owner:** Glen
**Actions (CMO supports):**
- Track negotiation status and any outstanding questions from the prospect
- Prepare any revised versions of the proposal for Glen's review
- Update pipeline status and revenue forecast

---

### Stage 7 — Won / Onboarding
**Owner:** COO
**Trigger:** Engagement agreed and signed
**Actions:**
- COO initiates client onboarding workflow (see pipelines/client_delivery/onboarding.md)
- CMO updates the pipeline, marks lead as Won, adds client to the active clients list
- CFO logs contracted revenue
- CMO documents the win: what angle worked, what objections were raised, for future BD learning

---

### Stage 8 — Lost / Nurture
**Owner:** CMO
**Trigger:** Lead declines or goes cold
**Actions:**
- Log the reason (no budget, wrong timing, chose competitor, no response)
- Determine: nurture (keep warm for future) or archive (genuinely not a fit)
- For nurture leads: schedule quarterly check-in or add to content/newsletter list when active
- Never hard close a gaming industry relationship — the industry is small

## BD Principles

- **Glen's relationships are the primary trust signal.** Personal introductions from Glen convert faster than cold outreach. Prioritise warm paths
- **The industry is small.** Never burn a contact. Even a "no" today is a "maybe" next year
- **Specificity wins.** Generic "we help game studios" outreach gets ignored. Reference their specific game, their recent news, their pain
- **Testimonials are weapons.** Paul Sams (COO, Blizzard), J Allen Brack (President, Blizzard), Justin Logan (Xbox) — these names open doors
- **Events are key moments.** GDC, Gamescom, and other industry events are the highest-density BD opportunities. Capitalise before and after
