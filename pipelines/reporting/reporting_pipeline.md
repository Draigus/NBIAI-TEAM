# Reporting Pipeline

## Purpose
Defines the cadence, format, and ownership of all internal and external reports. Reporting is proactive — agents do not wait to be asked. Glen should never have to chase for a status update.

---

## Report Types

### 1. Weekly Company Status Report
**Owner:** CEO
**Audience:** Glen
**Cadence:** Weekly, delivered proactively (not waiting to be asked)
**Format:** Use templates/status_report.md
**Contents:**
- All active projects and clients: what was done, what is next, what is blocked
- Items needing Glen's attention with context and recommendations
- Risks and flags
- Upcoming decisions and deadlines

**How it is assembled:**
1. Each department head delivers their section to the CEO by end of week
2. CEO compiles into a single report
3. CEO delivers to Glen

---

### 2. Client Delivery Report
**Owner:** COO + Producer
**Audience:** CEO (internal), optionally Glen
**Cadence:** Weekly per active client (more frequently if delivery is at risk)
**Format:** Per-client status — Red/Amber/Green health, what was delivered, what is next, blockers
**Key clients:**
- Couch Heroes (heavy workload — weekly minimum)
- Lighthouse Studios (light — bi-weekly or on milestone)
- Sarge Universe (project-phase dependent)
- Goals Studio / Blizzard (as needed per engagement activity)

---

### 3. Financial Report
**Owner:** CFO
**Audience:** CEO, Glen
**Cadence:** Monthly
**Contents:**
- Revenue vs target (month and YTD)
- Payroll and cost summary
- Cash flow position
- Invoices issued and outstanding
- Pipeline revenue forecast
- NSI transition scenario update
- Any financial risks or anomalies

---

### 4. BD Pipeline Report
**Owner:** CMO
**Audience:** CEO, Glen
**Cadence:** Weekly
**Contents:**
- All active leads with stage, last contact, next action, and priority
- Leads moved forward or lost this week
- Outreach sent (awaiting Glen's approval if not yet sent)
- Upcoming BD opportunities (events, warm introductions)

---

### 5. Engineering Sprint Report
**Owner:** VP Engineering
**Audience:** CTO, CEO
**Cadence:** Per sprint (typically bi-weekly)
**Contents:**
- Sprint goal and completion rate
- Stories completed vs planned
- Bugs filed and resolved
- Blockers encountered
- Next sprint priorities

---

### 6. Product Roadmap Review
**Owner:** VP Product
**Audience:** CEO, Glen
**Cadence:** Monthly
**Contents:**
- Roadmap progress (milestones hit vs planned)
- Feature completions and PM review results
- Upcoming features and prioritisation decisions
- Any scope changes or trade-offs needed

---

## Reporting Principles

- **Proactive, not reactive.** Reports are delivered on schedule without being requested
- **Action-oriented.** Every report answers "what needs to happen next?" not just "what happened?"
- **Flag early.** If something is going wrong, surface it before it becomes a crisis. Amber is better than surprise Red
- **Needs Glen's attention items must be clear.** Never bury a required decision inside a long report. Put it in a visible "Needs Glen's Attention" section with context and a recommendation
- **British English. No em dashes. Bullet points with narrative.** Follow Glen's communication preferences in all reports

---

## Escalation from Reports

If a report reveals a critical issue:
1. Do not wait for the next scheduled report — escalate immediately
2. Use the standard escalation protocol (see company/policies/escalation_rules.md)
3. Include: what the issue is, what has been tried, what is recommended, what Glen needs to decide
